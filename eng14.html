Oral English: Emphatic Stress

JAMB Emphatic Stress: Complete Mastery Guide

2025/2026 UTME Ready – 30 Real JAMB-Style Examples + Golden Rule

What is Emphatic Stress?

Emphatic stress (also called contrastive stress or sentence stress) is the extra loud stress placed on one whole word in a sentence to:

  • Show importance
  • Correct a wrong idea
  • Show contrast

In JAMB, the stressed word is always written in CAPITAL LETTERS.

Example: The teacher wants MY pen.

This means: “Not his pen… MY pen!” (correcting someone who thought it was another person’s pen).

Difference from Word Stress

  • Word stress = stress on a syllable inside one word (e.g., COMmercial, deCIDE)
  • Emphatic stress = stress on the whole word in the sentence (e.g., MY, BOUGHT, YESTERDAY)

JAMB only tests emphatic stress in this section.

Exact JAMB Question Format

“In each of the following questions, the word in capital letters has the emphatic stress. Choose the option to which the given sentence relates.”

The Golden Rule (Never Forget This!)

The correct option is the one that contradicts or replaces the CAPITALISED word.

  • The answer will NOT contain the exact capitalised word
  • It usually replaces the stressed word with a different word (often an opposite or different item)
  • Your sentence becomes the answer that corrects the question

Quick Test Method

  1. Spot the CAPITALISED word
  2. Read the 4 options
  3. Ask: “Which option changes exactly that word?”
  4. That is your answer

Master It with Real JAMB-Style Examples

These are grouped into the three most common types JAMB tests: stress on subject, verb, and object/adverbial/adjective. Each group has 10 fresh examples with full options, correct answer, and clear explanation.

Type 1: Emphatic Stress on the SUBJECT (10 Examples)

1. JOHN bought a new car yesterday.
A. Did Peter buy a new car yesterday?
B. Did John sell a new car yesterday?
C. Did John buy an old car yesterday?
D. Did John buy a new car today?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “JOHN” with “Peter”
2. MY mother cooked the rice.
A. Did your father cook the rice?
B. Did your mother eat the rice?
C. Did your mother cook the beans?
D. Did your sister cook the rice?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “MY mother” with “your father”
3. THE accused has been tried.
A. Has the witness been tried?
B. Has the accused been sentenced?
C. Has the judge been tried?
D. Has the accused been released?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “THE accused” with “the witness”
4. EMEKA is a lawyer.
A. Is Chinedu a lawyer?
B. Is Emeka a doctor?
C. Was Emeka a lawyer?
D. Is Emeka the judge?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “EMEKA” with “Chinedu”
5. ADAMU is leaving a car behind.
A. Is Musa leaving a car behind?
B. Is Adamu driving a car?
C. Is Adamu leaving a house behind?
D. Is Adamu taking a car?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “ADAMU” with “Musa”
6. HIS wife works in a bank.
A. Does her husband work in a bank?
B. Does his sister work in a bank?
C. Does his wife work in a school?
D. Does his mother work in a bank?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “HIS wife” with “her husband”
7. THE Nigerian football team has many foreign players.
A. Does the Ghanaian football team have many foreign players?
B. Does the Nigerian volleyball team have many foreign players?
C. Does the Nigerian football team have local players?
D. Does the Nigerian basketball team have many foreign players?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “THE Nigerian” with “Ghanaian”
8. SHE works at the hospital.
A. Does he work at the hospital?
B. Does she work at the school?
C. Does she teach at the hospital?
D. What does she do at the hospital?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “SHE” with “he”
9. THE fire destroyed many lives.
A. Did the flood destroy many lives?
B. Did the fire destroy few lives?
C. Did the fire save many lives?
D. Did the accident destroy many lives?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “THE fire” with “the flood”
10. DADDY plays tennis on Saturday afternoons.
A. Does mummy play tennis on Saturday afternoons?
B. Does daddy play football on Saturday afternoons?
C. Does daddy play tennis on Sunday afternoons?
D. Does uncle play tennis on Saturday afternoons?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “DADDY” with “mummy”

Type 2: Emphatic Stress on the VERB (10 Examples)

1. John BOUGHT a new car yesterday.
A. Did John sell a new car yesterday?
B. Did John buy an old car yesterday?
C. Did Peter buy a new car yesterday?
D. Did John wash a new car yesterday?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “BOUGHT” with “sell”
2. The accused hasn’t even been TRIED.
A. Was the accused sentenced?
B. Has the accused been released?
C. Has the suspect been apprehended?
D. Was the accused handed over to the police?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “TRIED” with “sentenced”
3. John must PAY for the broken glass immediately.
A. Must John repair the broken glass immediately?
B. Must Jane pay for the broken glass immediately?
C. Must John pay for the broken window?
D. Must John steal the broken glass?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “PAY” with “repair”
4. The workers agreed to forgo their December SALARIES.
A. Did the workers agree to claim their December salaries?
B. Did the workers refuse to forgo their December salaries?
C. Did the workers agree to forgo their March salaries?
D. Did the employers agree to forgo their December salaries?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “forgo” with “claim”
5. The cat DEVOURED the rat.
A. Did the cat pet the rat?
B. Did the rat devour the cat?
C. Did the dog devour the rat?
D. Did the cat chase the rat?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “DEVOURED” with “pet”
6. She WORKS at the hospital.
A. Does she teach at the hospital?
B. Does she work at the school?
C. Does he work at the hospital?
D. What does she do at the hospital?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “WORKS” with “teach”
7. The landlord REFUSED to eject the tenant.
A. Did the landlord agree to eject the tenant?
B. Did the caretaker refuse to eject the tenant?
C. Did the landlord refuse to accept the tenant?
D. Did the landlord eject the guest?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “REFUSED” with “agree”
8. Adon stated she had a RIGHT to her privacy.
A. Did Adon state she had no right to her privacy?
B. Did Adon state she had a duty to her privacy?
C. Did Kella state she had a right to her privacy?
D. Did Adon state she had a right to her money?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “RIGHT” with “no right”
9. The accountant PAID the workers July salary in September.
A. Did the cashier pay the workers July salary in September?
B. Did the accountant receive the workers July salary in September?
C. Did the accountant pay the workers August salary in September?
D. Did the accountant pay the workers July salary in October?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “PAID” with “receive” (but mainly the doer change)
10. The car CRUSHED him.
A. Did the car hit him?
B. Did the lorry crush him?
C. Did the car miss him?
D. Did the car kill him?
Answer: A
Explanation: Contradicts “CRUSHED” with “hit”
