📚 Chapter 2: Scalars & Vectors

🎯 Learning Objectives: Master scalar vs vector quantities, vector addition, components, position, distance, displacement, and measurement concepts for JAMB success.

Part (a): SCALARS AND VECTORS

1️⃣ Definitions 📖

Concept Definition Key Characteristic
Scalar Physical quantity with magnitude (size) only Described by number + unit
Vector Physical quantity with magnitude and direction Requires number, unit, AND direction

2️⃣ Examples Classification 📝

Scalars (Magnitude Only):

  • Mass (5 kg), Time (10 s), Temperature (25°C)
  • Speed (20 m/s), Distance (100 m), Energy (50 J)
  • Volume, Density, Pressure, Electric current

Vectors (Magnitude + Direction):

  • Velocity (20 m/s north), Displacement (50 m east)
  • Force (10 N downward), Acceleration (9.8 m/s² down)
  • Weight, Momentum, Electric field, Gravitational field
💡 Memory Tip: Vectors need "where to" — if you can add "to the left/up/north," it's a vector!

3️⃣ Vector Representation 🎯

Graphical Representation:

  • Drawn as an arrow (→)
  • Length of arrow = magnitude (to scale)
  • Arrowhead = direction

Mathematical Representation:

• Bold letters: F (force vector)
• Arrow notation: F⃗ or v⃗
• Component form: F⃗ = (Fx, Fy) or Fxî + Fyĵ

4️⃣ Vector Operations ➕

A. Vector Addition (Resultant)

Method 1: Parallelogram Law

If two vectors act at a point, their resultant is the diagonal of the parallelogram formed by them.

R = √(A² + B² + 2ABcosθ)

Where θ = angle between vectors

Method 2: Triangle Law

Place vectors head-to-tail; resultant joins tail of first to head of second.

Method 3: Component Method (Most Important for JAMB)

  1. Break each vector into x and y components
  2. Add all x-components: Rx = Ax + Bx + Cx + ...
  3. Add all y-components: Ry = Ay + By + Cy + ...
  4. Resultant magnitude: R = √(Rx² + Ry²)
  5. Direction: tan α = Ry/Rx → α = tan⁻¹(Ry/Rx)

🔢 Example 1: Resultant of Two Perpendicular Forces

Question: Two forces of 3N and 4N act at right angles. Find the resultant force.

Solution:
Using Pythagoras (θ = 90°, cos 90° = 0):

R = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5 N

Direction: tan θ = 4/3 → θ = tan⁻¹(1.333) = 53.1°
Answer: 5 N at 53.1° to the 3N force

🔢 Example 2: Vector Components

Question: A displacement vector of 100 m makes 30° with horizontal. Find horizontal and vertical components.

Solution:
• Horizontal: dx = 100cos(30°) = 100 × 0.866 = 86.6 m
• Vertical: dy = 100sin(30°) = 100 × 0.5 = 50 m
Answers: 86.6 m (horizontal), 50 m (vertical)

🔢 Example 3: Adding Multiple Vectors

Question: Find resultant of: A = 5 m at 0°, B = 3 m at 90°, C = 4 m at 180°

Component Table:
Vector A: Vx = 5cos(0°) = 5, Vy = 5sin(0°) = 0
Vector B: Vx = 3cos(90°) = 0, Vy = 3sin(90°) = 3
Vector C: Vx = 4cos(180°) = -4, Vy = 4sin(180°) = 0

Sum: Rx = 5 + 0 - 4 = 1, Ry = 0 + 3 + 0 = 3

R = √(1² + 3²) = √10 = 3.16 m
θ = tan⁻¹(3/1) = 71.6°
Answer: 3.16 m at 71.6° from positive x-axis

🔢 Example 4: Relative Velocity (JAMB Favorite!)

Question: A boat moves at 5 m/s in still water, heads north across a river flowing east at 3 m/s. Find resultant velocity.

Solution:
• Boat velocity (north): v⃗b = 5ĵ m/s
• River velocity (east): v⃗r = 3î m/s

Resultant: v⃗ = 3î + 5ĵ
Magnitude: v = √(3² + 5²) = √34 = 5.83 m/s
Direction: θ = tan⁻¹(5/3) = 59.0° north of east
Answer: 5.83 m/s at 59.0° north of east

5️⃣ Special Cases of Vector Addition 📐

Angle Between Vectors Resultant Formula Special Condition
0° (same direction) R = A + B Maximum resultant
180° (opposite) R = |A - B| Minimum resultant
90° (perpendicular) R = √(A² + B²) Pythagorean theorem
θ (general) R = √(A² + B² + 2ABcosθ) Parallelogram law
Part (b): MEASUREMENT, POSITION, DISTANCE & DISPLACEMENT

1️⃣ Fundamental Concepts 📏

A. Measurement in Physics

Physical Quantity

Anything that can be measured (e.g., length, time, mass)

Types:

  • Fundamental (Basic): Cannot be defined in terms of other quantities
    • Length (m), Mass (kg), Time (s), Current (A), Temperature (K), Amount (mol), Luminous intensity (cd)
  • Derived: Obtained from fundamental quantities
    • Area (m²), Volume (m³), Velocity (m/s), Acceleration (m/s²), Force (N)

2️⃣ Position, Distance, and Displacement 🗺️

Concept Definition Nature Symbol/Unit
Position Location relative to reference point Vector r⃗ (m)
Distance Total path length traveled Scalar d or s (m)
Displacement Change in position (final - initial) Vector s⃗ or Δx⃗ (m)

Key Differences:

Scenario: Walking 3m east, then 4m north

Distance = 3m + 4m = 7m (total path)
Displacement = √(3² + 4²) = 5m northeast (straight line start to finish)

3️⃣ Coordinate Systems 📍

1-Dimension (Linear):
• Position: x (can be + or -)
• Displacement: Δx = xf - xi

2-Dimension (Plane):
• Position: (x, y)
• Displacement: Δr⃗ = (xf - xi)î + (yf - yi)ĵ

🔢 Example 5: Distance vs Displacement

Question: A student walks 400 m east, then 300 m north, then 400 m west, then 300 m south. Find: (a) Total distance (b) Displacement.

Solution:
(a) Distance = 400 + 300 + 400 + 300 = 1400 m

(b) Displacement:
• East-West: +400 - 400 = 0 m
• North-South: +300 - 300 = 0 m
• Displacement = 0 m
Answers: (a) 1400 m (b) 0 m (returned to start!)

🔢 Example 6: Displacement in 2D

Question: A particle moves from A(2, 3) to B(5, 7). Find displacement vector and magnitude.

Solution:
Displacement components:
• Δx = 5 - 2 = 3 m
• Δy = 7 - 3 = 4 m

Displacement vector: s⃗ = 3î + 4ĵ m
Magnitude: s = √(3² + 4²) = 5 m
Direction: θ = tan⁻¹(4/3) = 53.1°
Answer: 5 m at 53.1°

🔢 Example 7: Average Speed vs Average Velocity

Question: A car travels 60 km north in 1 hour, then 80 km east in 2 hours. Calculate: (a) Average speed (b) Average velocity.

Solution:
(a) Average Speed = Total distance/Total time = (60+80)/(1+2) = 140/3 = 46.7 km/h

(b) Average Velocity:
• Displacement = √(60² + 80²) = 100 km
• Direction: tan⁻¹(80/60) = 53.1° east of north
• Velocity = 100 km/3 h = 33.3 km/h at 53.1° east of north
Answers: (a) 46.7 km/h (b) 33.3 km/h at 53.1° east of north

🔢 Example 8: Unit Conversion

Question: Convert 72 km/h to m/s.

Solution:
72 km/h = 72 × (1000 m)/(3600 s) = 72 × (5/18) = 20 m/s
Answer: 20 m/s
💡 Quick Trick: To convert km/h to m/s, multiply by 5/18!

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

  1. Always check if answer requires direction — if vector, direction matters!
  2. Draw diagrams — vector problems are easier with a sketch
  3. Watch for negative components — left/down vectors are negative
  4. Distance vs Displacement — distance is total path (scalar), displacement is shortest path (vector)
  5. Component method is safest — for 3+ vectors, always use components
