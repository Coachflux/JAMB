CHEMISTRY

Oxidation and Reduction (Redox) 🚀

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry (Pages 200–206 & 219–221)

Definition of Environmental Pollution

Environmental pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or energy into the environment by human activities, causing adverse effects on living organisms and the ecosystem.

Step 1: Classical Definitions (Addition/Removal of Oxygen)

Oxidation is the addition of oxygen to a substance.

  • Example: 2Mg(s) + O₂(g) → 2MgO(s)

Reduction is the removal of oxygen from a compound (often by hydrogen).

  • Example: CuO(s) + H₂(g) → Cu(s) + H₂O(l)

Step 2: Teach Loss and Gain of Electrons Step by Step (Modern Definition)

Oxidation = loss of electrons (OIL – Oxidation Is Loss)

Reduction = gain of electrons (RIG – Reduction Is Gain)

Step-by-step teaching (exactly as Ababio explains):

  1. Write the reaction: e.g. 2Na(s) + Cl₂(g) → 2NaCl(s)
  2. Split into half-reactions:
    Na → Na⁺ + e⁻ (oxidation – loss of 1 electron per Na)
    Cl₂ + 2e⁻ → 2Cl⁻ (reduction – gain of 2 electrons)
  3. Check: total electrons lost = total electrons gained (balance by multiplying)
  4. Combine: the species that loses electrons is oxidised; the one that gains is reduced.

Another example: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

  • Mg: 0 → +2 (loss of 2e⁻ per Mg) → oxidised
  • O: 0 → –2 (gain of 2e⁻ per O) → reduced

Step 3: Oxidation Numbers & Change in ON

Oxidation = increase in oxidation number
Reduction = decrease in oxidation number

Rules (Ababio’s exact 6 rules):

  • Free elements = 0
  • Group 1 = +1, Group 2 = +2
  • H = +1 (except hydrides = –1)
  • O = –2 (except peroxides = –1)
  • Sum in neutral compound = 0
  • Sum in ion = charge on ion

Example: In KMnO₄, Mn = +7

Step 4: Balancing Redox Equations Using Oxidation Numbers

Ababio’s method (step by step):

  1. Assign ON to changing atoms
  2. Note total change (electrons lost = electrons gained)
  3. Balance the rest by inspection

Worked example: MnO₂ + HCl → MnCl₂ + Cl₂ + H₂O

Balanced: MnO₂ + 4HCl → MnCl₂ + Cl₂ + 2H₂O

Step 5: IUPAC Nomenclature Using Oxidation Number

Calculate ON first, then name with Roman numerals and oxo- prefixes.

  • KMnO₄ → potassium tetraoxomanganate(VII)
  • K₂Cr₂O₇ → potassium heptaoxodichromate(VI)
  • FeCl₂ → iron(II) chloride

Step 6: Tests for Oxidising and Reducing Agents

Reagent Ability Test Observation
Acidified KMnO₄ Oxidising agent Purple → colourless
Acidified K₂Cr₂O₇ Oxidising agent Orange → green
H₂S Reducing agent Blackens lead acetate paper
SO₂ Reducing agent Decolourises KMnO₄

6 JAMB Examples with Step-by-Step Solutions

Example 1: Identify Oxidation/Reduction (Classical)

Question: In CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O, which substance is oxidised?

Solution:
Step 1: CuO loses oxygen → reduced
Step 2: H₂ gains oxygen → oxidised
Answer: H₂ is oxidised

Example 2: Loss & Gain of Electrons

Question: Show electron transfer in 2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl.

Solution:
Step 1: Na → Na⁺ + e⁻ (loss – oxidation)
Step 2: Cl₂ + 2e⁻ → 2Cl⁻ (gain – reduction)
Step 3: Multiply Na by 2 to balance electrons
Answer: Na is oxidised, Cl₂ is reduced

Example 3: Oxidation Number Change

Question: In H₂S + Cl₂ → 2HCl + S, which element is oxidised?

Solution:
Step 1: S in H₂S = –2 → 0 in S (increase)
Step 2: Cl in Cl₂ = 0 → –1 in HCl (decrease)
Answer: Sulphur is oxidised

Example 4: Balancing Using ON Method

Question: Balance: MnO₂ + HCl → MnCl₂ + Cl₂ + H₂O

Solution:
Step 1: Mn +4 → +2 (decrease 2)
Step 2: Cl –1 → 0 (increase 1 per Cl, need 2 Cl)
Step 3: Multiply HCl by 4
Balanced: MnO₂ + 4HCl → MnCl₂ + Cl₂ + 2H₂O

Example 5: IUPAC Naming

Question: Give IUPAC name for K₂Cr₂O₇.

Solution:
Step 1: Cr oxidation number = +6
Step 2: Two Cr, seven O → heptaoxodichromate(VI)
Answer: potassium heptaoxodichromate(VI)

Example 6: Identifying Agents

Question: In the reaction 2KMnO₄ + 16HCl → products, which is the oxidising agent?

Solution:
Step 1: Mn +7 → +2 (decrease) → MnO₄⁻ is reduced
Step 2: Reducing agent = HCl (Cl⁻ oxidised to Cl₂)
Answer: KMnO₄ is the oxidising agent
