1️⃣ Definition of Environmental Pollution

Environmental pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or energy into the environment by human activities, causing adverse effects on living organisms and the ecosystem.

Pollution can be from smoke, gases, waste, oil spills, and chemicals
🧠 Exam wording

Harmful substance/energy + human activities + adverse effects.

3️⃣ Why Air Composition Varies

Factors responsible

  • Respiration: uses O₂, produces CO₂
  • Photosynthesis: uses CO₂, releases O₂
  • Combustion: uses O₂, produces CO₂/CO + smoke
  • Industries/vehicles: add CO₂, NOₓ, dust
  • Volcanoes/decay: increase CO₂ and H₂S
  • Location: near sea → more water vapour; industrial areas → more pollutants
Photosynthesis reduces CO₂; respiration/combustion increase CO₂

4️⃣ Air as a Mixture + Separation + Uses of Noble Gases

Why air is a mixture

  • Composition varies from place to place and time to time
  • Components separated by physical methods
  • Properties are average of constituents

Separation principle

Fractional distillation of liquid air (air is cooled to liquefy, then separated by boiling points).

Fractional distillation separates N₂, O₂, Ar from liquid air

Uses of noble gases

Noble gas Main use Reason
Argon (Ar) Fill electric bulbs/fluorescent tubes Inert (prevents filament from burning)
Neon (Ne) Advertising signs/glow lamps Gives red-orange light in discharge tube

5️⃣ Air Pollution (Pollutants, Sources & Effects)

Air pollutants: CO, SO₂, NOₓ, H₂S, CFCs, dust/particulates.

Smog is linked to NOₓ and particulates from vehicles and industries
Pollutant Main sources Harmful effects
CO Incomplete combustion; car exhaust Binds haemoglobin → less O₂ transport → headache, dizziness, death
SO₂ Burning coal/fuel oil; volcanoes Acid rain; respiratory diseases; corrosion of buildings
NOₓ Car engines; power stations Acid rain; photochemical smog; lung irritation
H₂S Decay of organic matter; oil refineries Poisonous; damages respiratory system; rotten-egg smell
CFCs Old fridges; aerosols; foams Ozone depletion → more UV → skin cancer
Dust/particulates Construction; bush burning; factories Respiratory diseases; reduces visibility

  • Burning coal → SO₂ → acid rain

6️⃣ Water Pollution

Main types

  • Sewage/industrial waste/detergents: increases BOD → less dissolved oxygen; can cause eutrophication; spreads diseases (cholera, typhoid)
  • Oil pollution: tanker accidents/pipeline leaks → oil layer blocks oxygen → kills fish and birds
Oil spills block oxygen transfer and harm aquatic life

7️⃣ Soil Pollution + Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable

Soil pollution (common cause)

Oil spillage (pipeline leaks/vandalism, tanker accidents) kills soil microorganisms, destroys crops, and contaminates underground water.

Oil on soil reduces fertility and can contaminate groundwater

Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable

Type Broken down by microbes? Examples
Biodegradable Yes Sewage, animal dung, dead plants, paper
Non-biodegradable No Plastics, metal cans, pesticides, oil, glass

8️⃣ Control of Environmental Pollution

Measures (know at least 5)

  • Use catalytic converters in cars
  • Use unleaded petrol and CNG
  • Afforestation: plant trees
  • Treat sewage before discharge
  • Proper disposal/recycling of plastics and safe handling of oil
  • Government laws and enforcement (e.g., NESREA in Nigeria)
Afforestation helps reduce CO₂ and improves air quality

📝 3 JAMB-Style Examples (Quick)

Example 1: Air composition

Question: Which gas is about 78% of dry air?

Answer: Nitrogen (N₂)

Example 2: Source & effect

Question: Which pollutant from burning coal causes acid rain?

Answer: Sulphur dioxide (SO₂)

Example 3: Biodegradable vs non

Question: Classify plastics and sewage.

Plastics = non-biodegradable

Sewage = biodegradable
