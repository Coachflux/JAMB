Environmental Pollution 🚀

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry

Definition of Environmental Pollution

Environmental pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or energy into the environment by human activities, causing adverse effects on living organisms and the ecosystem.

Step 1: Air – Natural Gaseous Constituents and Proportions

Air is not a compound — it is a mixture of gases.

The natural (dry) air composition by volume is:

Gas Chemical Formula Approximate Percentage (%)
Nitrogen N₂ 78.0
Oxygen O₂ 21.0
Argon (noble gas) Ar 0.93
Carbon(IV) oxide CO₂ 0.03 – 0.04
Neon (noble gas) Ne 0.0018
Other noble gases — traces
Water vapour H₂O(g) 0 – 4 (variable)

Step 2: Factors Responsible for Variation of Components of Air

  • Respiration (by humans, animals and plants): consumes O₂, produces CO₂
  • Photosynthesis (by green plants): consumes CO₂, releases O₂
  • Combustion (burning fuels, bush burning): consumes O₂, produces CO₂, CO, smoke
  • Industrial activities and vehicles: add CO₂, oxides of nitrogen, dust
  • Volcanic eruptions and decay of organic matter: increase CO₂, H₂S
  • Location: air near sea has more water vapour; industrial areas have more pollutants

Step 3: Air as a Mixture + Separation + Uses of Noble Gases

Why air is a mixture:

  • Its composition varies from place to place and time to time
  • Components can be separated by physical methods only
  • The properties of air are the average of the properties of its constituents

Principle of separation: Fractional distillation of liquid air.

Uses of noble gases:

  • Argon: used to fill electric bulbs and fluorescent tubes (inert, prevents filament from burning)
  • Neon: used in advertising signs and glow lamps (gives red-orange light)

Step 4: Air Pollution

Examples of air pollutants: H₂S, CO, SO₂, NOₓ, CFCs, dust/particulates.

Sources and effects of pollutants:

Pollutant Main Sources Harmful Effects
Carbon monoxide (CO) Incomplete combustion of petrol/diesel, car exhaust Combines with haemoglobin → reduces O₂ transport → headache, dizziness, death
Sulphur dioxide (SO₂) Burning coal, fuel oil, volcanic eruptions Acid rain (corrodes buildings), respiratory diseases
Oxides of nitrogen (NOₓ) Car engines, power stations Acid rain, photochemical smog, lung irritation
Hydrogen sulphide (H₂S) Decay of organic matter, oil refineries Rotten-egg smell, poisonous, damages respiratory system
Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) Old refrigerators, aerosols, foams Depletes ozone layer → more UV radiation → skin cancer
Dust/particulates Construction, bush burning, factories Causes respiratory diseases, reduces visibility

Step 5: Water Pollution

  • Sewage pollution: human waste, industrial waste, detergents → increases BOD, eutrophication, spreads diseases (cholera, typhoid)
  • Oil pollution: oil tanker accidents, pipeline leaks → forms layer on water surface, kills fish and birds

Step 6: Soil Pollution

Oil spillage: pipeline vandalism, tanker accidents (very common in Nigeria) → kills soil microorganisms, destroys crops, contaminates underground water.

Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable pollutants:

Type Can be broken down by microbes? Examples
Biodegradable Yes Sewage, animal dung, dead plants, paper
Non-biodegradable No Plastics, metal cans, pesticides, oil, glass

Step 7: Measures for Control of Environmental Pollution

  • Use catalytic converters in cars
  • Use unleaded petrol and CNG
  • Plant trees (afforestation)
  • Treat sewage before discharge
  • Proper disposal of plastics and oil
  • Government laws (NESREA in Nigeria)

3 JAMB-Style Examples & Explanations

Example 1: Air Composition

JAMB Question: Which gas makes up about 78% of dry air?

Explanation: Nitrogen (N₂). JAMB always tests the exact percentages from the table.

Example 2: Sources & Effects

JAMB Question: Which pollutant causes acid rain and is produced by burning coal?

Explanation: Sulphur dioxide (SO₂). Know the table — it appears every year.

Example 3: Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable

JAMB Question: Classify plastics and sewage as biodegradable or non-biodegradable.

Explanation: Plastics = non-biodegradable; sewage = biodegradable. JAMB tests this classification directly.
