📚 Chapter 4: Gravitational Field

🎯 Learning Objectives: Master Newton's law of gravitation, gravitational field strength, potential, escape velocity, satellite motion, and Kepler's laws for JAMB success.

1️⃣ Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation 🍎

Core Concept

Every particle in the universe attracts every other particle with a force that is:

  • Directly proportional to the product of their masses (m₁ × m₂)
  • Inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them (r²)
The Formula:
F = Gm₁m₂/r²

Where:
• F = Gravitational force (N)
• G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²kg⁻²
• m₁, m₂ = Masses (kg)
• r = Distance between centers (m)

🔢 Example 1: Force Between Two Masses

Question: Calculate the gravitational force between two spheres of mass 100 kg and 200 kg with centers 2 m apart.

Solution:
F = (6.67 × 10⁻¹¹)(100)(200)/2²
F = (6.67 × 10⁻¹¹)(20,000)/4
F = (1.334 × 10⁻⁶)/4
F = 3.335 × 10⁻⁷ N
Answer: F = 3.335 × 10⁻⁷ N
Key Insight: Gravitational forces are extremely weak for everyday objects—we only notice them with planetary masses!

2️⃣ Gravitational Field Strength (g) 📊

Definition

The gravitational field strength at a point is the force per unit mass experienced by a small test mass placed at that point.

g = F/m = GM/r²

On Earth's Surface:
M_E = 6.0 × 10²⁴ kg, R_E = 6.4 × 10⁶ m
g = (6.67 × 10⁻¹¹)(6.0 × 10²⁴)/(6.4 × 10⁶)² ≈ 9.8 ms⁻²

🔢 Example 2: Field Strength at Height

Question: Calculate g at height of 640 km above Earth's surface.

Solution (Ratio Method - Easier!):
New distance: r = 6400 + 640 = 7040 km = 7.04 × 10⁶ m

g_h/g_s = (R_E/(R_E+h))² = (6400/7040)² = (10/11)² = 100/121
g_h = 9.8 × 100/121 = 8.1 ms⁻²
Answer: g_h = 8.1 ms⁻²

3️⃣ Gravitational Potential (V) ⚡

Definition

The gravitational potential at a point is the work done in bringing a unit mass from infinity to that point.

V = -GM/r

The negative sign indicates:
• Work is done by the field when mass falls
• Potential at infinity is zero (reference point)
• Potential increases (less negative) as you move away

🔢 Example 3: Gravitational Potential

Question: Calculate the gravitational potential at Earth's surface.

Solution:
V = -(6.67 × 10⁻¹¹)(6.0 × 10²⁴)/(6.4 × 10⁶)
V = -(4.002 × 10¹⁴)/(6.4 × 10⁶)
V = -6.25 × 10⁷ Jkg⁻¹ = -62.5 MJkg⁻¹
Answer: V = -62.5 MJkg⁻¹

4️⃣ Escape Velocity 🚀

Definition

The minimum velocity needed for an object to escape from a planet's gravitational field without further propulsion.

Derivation (Energy Conservation):
At surface: Total energy = ½mv² - GMm/R
At infinity: Total energy = 0

½mv_esc² = GMm/R

v_esc = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR)

🔢 Example 4: Earth's Escape Velocity

Question: Calculate the escape velocity from Earth.

Solution:
v_esc = √[2(6.67×10⁻¹¹)(6.0×10²⁴)/(6.4×10⁶)]
v_esc = √[(8.004×10¹⁴)/(6.4×10⁶)]
v_esc = √(1.25×10⁸)
v_esc = 11,200 ms⁻¹ = 11.2 kms⁻¹
Answer: v_esc ≈ 11.2 km/s
💡 Memory Tip: Earth's escape velocity ≈ 11 km/s (about 25,000 mph)

5️⃣ Satellite Motion (Orbital Mechanics) 🛰️

Key Principle

For a satellite in circular orbit, gravitational force provides centripetal force:

GMm/r² = mv²/r

Orbital Velocity: v = √(GM/r)

Period (Kepler's Third Law):
T² = 4π²r³/GM
or T² ∝ r³

🔢 Example 5: Geostationary Satellite

Question: Calculate the orbital radius of a geostationary satellite (period = 24 hours).

Solution:
T = 24 × 3600 = 86,400 s

From T² = 4π²r³/GM:
r³ = GMT²/4π² = (6.67×10⁻¹¹)(6.0×10²⁴)(86,400)²/4π²
r³ = (2.99×10²⁴)/39.48 = 7.57×10²²
r = ∛(7.57×10²²) = 4.23×10⁷ m = 42,300 km

Height above surface: 42,300 - 6,400 = 35,900 km
Answer: r = 42,300 km (height = 35,900 km)

6️⃣ Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion 🌌

Law Statement Physics Meaning
First Law Planets move in elliptical orbits with Sun at one focus Orbits not perfect circles (often approximated as such)
Second Law Line joining planet to Sun sweeps equal areas in equal times Planets move faster when closer to Sun (conservation of angular momentum)
Third Law T² ∝ r³ Ratio T²/r³ is constant for all planets orbiting same central body

📊 Comprehensive Formula Summary

Concept Formula Units Key Notes
Newton's Law F = Gm₁m₂/r² N Force always attractive
Field Strength g = GM/r² N/kg or m/s² At surface: g = GM/R²
Gravitational Potential V = -GM/r J/kg Negative; zero at infinity
Potential Energy U = -GMm/r J U = mV
Escape Velocity v_esc = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR) m/s Independent of projectile mass
Orbital Velocity v = √(GM/r) m/s Circular orbits only
Orbital Period T = 2π√(r³/GM) s Kepler's Third Law
Relation g and G g = GM/R² m/s² Connects local and universal gravity

7️⃣ Important Relationships & Proportionality Tricks 📈

If Distance Doubles:

Quantity Effect
Gravitational Force Becomes 1/4
Field Strength (g) Becomes 1/4
Gravitational Potential Becomes 1/2 (less negative)

Variation of g:

At height h above surface:
g_h = GM/(R+h)² ≈ g(1 - 2h/R) for small h

At depth d below surface:
g_d = g(1 - d/R)

Key Point: g is maximum at surface, decreases both upward and downward!

8️⃣ JAMB-Style Practice Problems 📝

Problem 1: Proportionality (Quick MCQ)

Question: The gravitational force between two masses is F. If the distance is halved, what is the new force?

Solution:
F ∝ 1/r², so if r → r/2, then F → 4F
Answer: 4F

Problem 2: Energy Calculation

Question: Calculate energy required to move a 1000 kg satellite from Earth's surface to orbit of radius 2R_E.

Solution:
Energy needed = Change in potential energy
W = GMm(1/R - 1/2R) = GMm/2R = ½mgR
W = ½(1000)(9.8)(6.4×10⁶) = 3.14×10¹⁰ J
Answer: W = 3.14 × 10¹⁰ J

Problem 3: Binary System

Question: Two stars of mass 4M and M rotate about common COM. If distance between them is d, find period of rotation.

Solution:
Both stars have same angular velocity ω and period T.
For star with mass 4M at distance r₁ from COM:
G(4M)(M)/d² = 4Mω²r₁

Since 4Mr₁ = Mr₂ and r₁ + r₂ = d:
r₁ = d/5, r₂ = 4d/5

4GM²/d² = 4M(2π/T)²(d/5)
T² = 4π²d³/5GM

9️⃣ Common Mistakes to Avoid ⚠️

Mistake Correction
Using diameter instead of radius Always use distance between centers
Forgetting G is very small (10⁻¹¹) Expect tiny forces between everyday objects
Confusing mass of object with Earth In g = GM/r², M is the central mass
Sign errors with potential Potential is always negative
Thinking escape velocity depends on mass v_esc is same for all objects from same planet
Using altitude instead of distance from center r = R + h, not just h
