(b) Conversion from One Base to Another (including fractional parts)

Key Rules

  • Base b → Base 10: Value = dₙ × bⁿ + dₙ₋₁ × bⁿ⁻¹ + … + d₀ × b⁰ + d₋₁ × b⁻¹ + …
  • Base 10 → Base b (integer): Divide repeatedly by b, record remainders bottom → top.
  • Base 10 → Base b (fraction): Multiply fractional part by b repeatedly, record integer parts top → bottom.
  • Any base ↔ any base: Convert via base 10 first (easiest & safest).

10 Solved Examples – Conversions

Example 1: Convert 110101₂ to base 10.
Step 1: 1 × 2⁵ = 1 × 32 = 32
Step 2: 1 × 2⁴ = 1 × 16 = 16
Step 3: 0 × 2³ = 0 × 8 = 0
Step 4: 1 × 2² = 1 × 4 = 4
Step 5: 0 × 2¹ = 0 × 2 = 0
Step 6: 1 × 2⁰ = 1 × 1 = 1
Total = 32 + 16 + 0 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 53₁₀
Example 2: Convert 53₁₀ to base 2.
Step 1: 53 ÷ 2 = 26 remainder 1
Step 2: 26 ÷ 2 = 13 remainder 0
Step 3: 13 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 1
Step 4: 6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0
Step 5: 3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1
Step 6: 1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1
Read remainders from bottom to top: 110101₂
Example 3: Convert 234₅ to base 10.
Step 1: 2 × 5² = 2 × 25 = 50
Step 2: 3 × 5¹ = 3 × 5 = 15
Step 3: 4 × 5⁰ = 4 × 1 = 4
Total = 50 + 15 + 4 = 69₁₀
Example 4: Convert 69₁₀ to base 5.
Step 1: 69 ÷ 5 = 13 remainder 4
Step 2: 13 ÷ 5 = 2 remainder 3
Step 3: 2 ÷ 5 = 0 remainder 2
Read remainders from bottom to top: 234₅
Example 5: Convert 157₈ to base 10.
Step 1: 1 × 8² = 1 × 64 = 64
Step 2: 5 × 8¹ = 5 × 8 = 40
Step 3: 7 × 8⁰ = 7 × 1 = 7
Total = 64 + 40 + 7 = 111₁₀
Example 6: Convert 111₁₀ to base 8.
Step 1: 111 ÷ 8 = 13 remainder 7
Step 2: 13 ÷ 8 = 1 remainder 5
Step 3: 1 ÷ 8 = 0 remainder 1
Read remainders from bottom to top: 157₈
Example 7: Convert 0.1011₂ to base 10.
Step 1: 1 × 2⁻¹ = 1 × 0.5 = 0.5
Step 2: 0 × 2⁻² = 0 × 0.25 = 0
Step 3: 1 × 2⁻³ = 1 × 0.125 = 0.125
Step 4: 1 × 2⁻⁴ = 1 × 0.0625= 0.0625
Total = 0.5 + 0 + 0.125 + 0.0625 = 0.6875₁₀
Example 8: Convert 0.6875₁₀ to base 2.
Step 1: 0.6875 × 2 = 1.375 → integer 1, fraction 0.375
Step 2: 0.375 × 2 = 0.75 → integer 0, fraction 0.75
Step 3: 0.75 × 2 = 1.5 → integer 1, fraction 0.5
Step 4: 0.5 × 2 = 1.0 → integer 1, fraction 0.0
Read integer parts top to bottom: 0.1011₂
Example 9: Convert 3.25₁₀ to base 4.
Integer part:
3 ÷ 4 = 0 remainder 3 → 3₄
Fractional part:
0.25 × 4 = 1.0 → integer 1, fraction 0.0
→ 3.1₄ (Proof: 3 + 1/4 = 3.25)
Example 10: Convert 0.375₁₀ to base 8.
0.375 × 8 = 3.0 → integer 3, fraction 0.0
→ 0.3₈ (Proof: 3/8 = 0.375)

(a) Operations in Different Number Bases (2 to 10)

You do exactly the same as in base 10, but carry when sum ≥ base and borrow when needed.

10 Solved Examples – Operations

Example 1 (Addition – Base 2): 1011₂ + 1101₂
1 0 1 1
+ 1 1 0 1
-----------
Rightmost: 1 + 1 = 10₂ → write 0, carry 1
Next: 1 + 0 + 1 = 10₂ → write 0, carry 1
Next: 0 + 1 + 1 = 10₂ → write 0, carry 1
Next: 1 + 1 + 1 = 11₂ → write 1, carry 1
Left carry: 1
Answer: 11000₂
Example 2 (Addition – Base 5): 324₅ + 143₅
3 2 4
+ 1 4 3
---------
Units: 4 + 3 = 7 = 12₅ → write 2, carry 1
Fives: 2 + 4 + 1 = 7 = 12₅ → write 2, carry 1
25s: 3 + 1 + 1 = 5 = 10₅ → write 0, carry 1
Answer: 1022₅
Example 3 (Subtraction – Base 2): 10110₂ – 1011₂
Pad to: 10110₂ – 01011₂
Right: 0 – 1 → borrow → 10₂ – 1 = 1, next 1 becomes 0
Next: 0 – 1 → borrow → 10₂ – 1 = 1, next 1 becomes 0
Next: 0 – 0 → borrow → 10₂ – 0 = 0, next 1 becomes 0
Next: 0 – 1 → borrow → 10₂ – 1 = 1, next 1 becomes 0
Next: 0 – 0 → borrow chain completes to
Answer: 1011₂
Example 4 (Subtraction – Base 8): 472₈ – 165₈
4 7 2
- 1 6 5
---------
Units: 2 – 5 → borrow → 12₈ – 5 = 7, next 7 becomes 6
Tens: 6 – 6 = 0
Hundreds: 3 – 1 = 2 (after borrow from 4)
Answer: 305₈
Example 5 (Multiplication – Base 2): 111₂ × 10₂
Multiplying by 10₂ = shift left by 1 position
Answer: 1110₂
Example 6 (Multiplication – Base 3): 12₃ × 21₃
1 2
× 2 1
-------
12₃ × 1 = 12₃
12₃ × 2 = (2×2=11₃ write 1 carry 1), (2×1+1=3=10₃ write 0 carry 1), carry 1 → 101₃
Add shifted: 12₃
+101₃
-----
1100₃
Answer: 1100₃
Example 7 (Division – Base 2): 1100₂ ÷ 10₂
Long division:
10 into 11 (1 time), 1×10=10, subtract → 01
Bring down 0 → 10, 1 time, 1×10=10, subtract → 0
Bring down 0 → 00, 0
Quotient: 110₂
Example 8 (Division – Base 5): 234₅ ÷ 3₅
Long division in base 5:
3 into 23₅ = 4₅ (4×3=12₅), subtract 23₅–12₅=11₅
Bring down 4 → 114₅
3 into 11₅ = 3₅ (3×3=14₅? Wait 3₅×3₅=14₅ yes), subtract 114₅–14₅=44₅? Wait verified decimal
Quotient: 43₅
Example 9 (Mixed – Base 4): (32₄ + 13₄) × 2₄
Addition: 32₄ + 13₄ = 101₄
Multiplication: 101₄ × 2₄ = 202₄ (1×2=2, 0×2=0, 1×2=2)
Answer: 121₄
Example 10 (Mixed – Base 8): 76₈ – 25₈ = 51₈
Then 51₈ ÷ 3₈
Long division: 3 into 5 = 1, 1×3=3, 5-3=2
Bring down 1 → 21₈, 3 into 21 = 7 (7×3=25₈=21₁₀? 7₈×3=25₈ yes), subtract 21-25 borrow

(c) Modular Arithmetic

Key Rules

  • a mod m = remainder when a divided by m
  • (a + b) mod m = [(a mod m) + (b mod m)] mod m
  • Same for – and ×
  • Division: multiply by modular inverse (if gcd(divisor, m) = 1)

10 Solved Examples – Modular Arithmetic

Example 1: 47 mod 9
47 ÷ 9 = 5 times, 5 × 9 = 45, remainder
2
Example 2: (38 + 55) mod 7
38 ÷ 7 = 5 × 7 = 35, remainder 3
55 ÷ 7 = 7 × 7 = 49, remainder 6
3 + 6 = 9 → 9 mod 7 = 2
Example 3: 81 – 29 mod 6
81 ÷ 6 = 13 × 6 = 78, remainder 3
29 ÷ 6 = 4 × 6 = 24, remainder 5
3 – 5 = –2 → –2 + 6 = 4
Example 4: 17 × 12 mod 5
17 ÷ 5 = 3 × 5 = 15, remainder 2
12 ÷ 5 = 2 × 5 = 10, remainder 2
2 × 2 = 4 mod 5 = 4
Example 5: Find inverse of 3 mod 7 (3x ≡ 1 mod 7)
3 × 1 = 3 ≡ 3
3 × 2 = 6 ≡ 6
3 × 3 = 9 ≡ 2
3 × 4 = 12 ≡ 5
3 × 5 = 15 ≡ 1
Inverse = 5
Example 6: 4 ÷ 3 mod 7
Inverse of 3 mod 7 = 5
4 × 5 = 20
20 mod 7 = 6
Example 7: 25 mod 12
25 ÷ 12 = 2 × 12 = 24, remainder
1
Example 8: (64 × 3 – 19) mod 8
64 ÷ 8 = 8, remainder 0
0 × 3 = 0
19 ÷ 8 = 2 × 8 = 16, remainder 3
0 – 3 = –3 → –3 + 8 = 5
Example 9: 2⁵ mod 9
2⁵ = 32
32 ÷ 9 = 3 × 9 = 27, remainder
5
Example 10: Solve 5x ≡ 10 mod 6
10 mod 6 = 4 → 5x ≡ 4 mod 6
Inverse of 5 mod 6:
5 × 1 = 5 ≡ 5
5 × 2 = 10 ≡ 4
5 × 3 = 15 ≡ 3
5 × 4 = 20 ≡ 2
5 × 5 = 25 ≡ 1
Inverse = 5 → x ≡ 4 × 5 = 20 ≡ 2 mod 6
