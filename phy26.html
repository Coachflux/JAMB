📚 Chapter 26: Optical Instruments

🎯 Learning Objectives: Understand the principles of cameras, projectors, microscopes, telescopes, and the human eye. Calculate magnifying powers, near and far points, and apply lens formulas to optical instruments.

1️⃣ The Camera 📷

Principle

A camera forms a real, inverted, diminished image of an object on a light-sensitive film or sensor using a convex lens.

Key Components

  • Convex Lens (Objective): Converges light to form image
  • Aperture: Controls amount of light entering (f-stop)
  • Shutter: Controls exposure time
  • Film/Sensor: Records the image
  • Focusing Ring: Adjusts lens-to-film distance for sharp images

Camera Formula

1/f = 1/u + 1/v

Where:
• f = focal length of lens
• u = object distance (from lens)
• v = image distance (lens to film)

For distant objects (u ≈ ∞), v ≈ f (image forms at focal point)

F-Number (f/#)

f/# = f/D

Where:
• f = focal length
• D = diameter of aperture

Smaller f/# = Larger aperture = More light = Shallower depth of field

🔢 Calculation 1: Camera Focusing

Question: A camera has a lens with focal length 50 mm. How far must the film be from the lens to photograph a distant mountain?

For distant object (u ≈ ∞):
1/f = 1/∞ + 1/v
1/f = 0 + 1/v
v = f = 50 mm
Answer: 50 mm from the lens

🔢 Calculation 2: Close-up Photography

Question: The same camera (f = 50 mm) is used to photograph a flower 30 cm away. How far must the lens move from the film position?

Given: f = 50 mm = 5 cm, u = 30 cm

Using 1/f = 1/u + 1/v:
1/5 = 1/30 + 1/v
1/v = 1/5 - 1/30 = (6-1)/30 = 5/30 = 1/6
v = 6 cm = 60 mm

Lens movement: 60 - 50 = 10 mm away from film
Answer: 10 mm away from film

2️⃣ The Projector 📽️

Principle

A projector forms a real, inverted, magnified image of a small slide or object on a distant screen using a powerful convex lens.

Key Features

  • Light Source: Bright lamp with condenser lens
  • Slide/Transparency: Placed upside down (so image appears right-side up)
  • Projection Lens: Convex lens with short focal length
  • Screen: White surface to reflect image

Projector Formula

Magnification (m) = v/u = hᵢ/hₒ

For large magnification:
• Object (slide) placed just beyond F (slightly > f)
• Image forms far away (large v)
• m = v/u becomes very large

🔢 Calculation 3: Projector Setup

Question: A projector has a lens with focal length 10 cm. A slide 2 cm tall is placed 10.5 cm from the lens. Calculate: (a) Image distance, (b) Image size.

(a) Using 1/f = 1/u + 1/v:
1/10 = 1/10.5 + 1/v
1/v = 1/10 - 1/10.5 = (10.5-10)/(10×10.5) = 0.5/105
v = 105/0.5 = 210 cm

(b) Magnification: m = v/u = 210/10.5 = 20
Image height = 20 × 2 = 40 cm
Answer: (a) 210 cm, (b) 40 cm tall

3️⃣ The Simple Microscope (Magnifying Glass) 🔍

Principle

A convex lens of short focal length produces a virtual, erect, magnified image when the object is placed between the lens and its focus.

Angular Magnification

For relaxed eye (image at infinity):
M = D/f

For near point (image at D = 25 cm):
M = 1 + D/f = (D/f) + 1

Where:
• D = least distance of distinct vision (25 cm)
• f = focal length of lens
• M has no units (ratio)

🔢 Calculation 4: Simple Microscope

Question: A magnifying glass has focal length 5 cm. Calculate its magnifying power for: (a) Relaxed eye, (b) Near point.

(a) Relaxed eye:
M = D/f = 25/5 = 5×

(b) Near point:
M = 1 + D/f = 1 + 25/5 = 1 + 5 = 6×
Answer: (a) 5×, (b) 6×

4️⃣ The Compound Microscope 🔬

Construction

Two convex lenses separated by a tube:

  • Objective Lens: Short focal length (few mm), close to specimen
  • Eyepiece (Ocular): Longer focal length (few cm), close to eye

Image Formation

  1. Objective: Forms real, inverted, magnified image of specimen (acts like a projector)
  2. Eyepiece: Acts as simple microscope to magnify the intermediate image
  3. Final Image: Virtual, inverted, highly magnified

Magnification Formula

Total Magnification:
M = Mₒ × Mₑ

Where:
• Mₒ = Linear magnification of objective = vₒ/uₒ ≈ L/fₒ
• Mₑ = Angular magnification of eyepiece = D/fₑ (or 1 + D/fₑ)
• L = tube length (distance between focal points, ≈ 16-25 cm)

Approximate formula:
M = (L/fₒ) × (D/fₑ)

🔢 Calculation 5: Compound Microscope

Question: A compound microscope has objective (fₒ = 4 mm) and eyepiece (fₑ = 2 cm). The tube length is 16 cm. Calculate the total magnification for relaxed eye.

Given: fₒ = 0.4 cm, fₑ = 2 cm, L = 16 cm, D = 25 cm

Objective magnification:
Mₒ = L/fₒ = 16/0.4 = 40×

Eyepiece magnification:
Mₑ = D/fₑ = 25/2 = 12.5×

Total magnification:
M = 40 × 12.5 = 500×
Answer: 500×

5️⃣ The Astronomical Telescope 🔭

Construction

Two convex lenses aligned on common axis:

  • Objective: Large diameter, long focal length (collects light from distant object)
  • Eyepiece: Short focal length (magnifies the image)

Image Formation

  1. Objective: Forms real, inverted, diminished image at its focal plane
  2. Eyepiece: Positioned so that intermediate image is at its focus
  3. Final Image: Virtual, inverted, at infinity (for relaxed eye)

Angular Magnification

M = fₒ/fₑ

Where:
• fₒ = focal length of objective
• fₑ = focal length of eyepiece

Tube length (normal adjustment):
L = fₒ + fₑ

🔢 Calculation 6: Astronomical Telescope

Question: A telescope has objective focal length 100 cm and eyepiece focal length 5 cm. Calculate: (a) Magnifying power, (b) Tube length.

(a) Magnification:
M = fₒ/fₑ = 100/5 = 20×

(b) Tube length:
L = fₒ + fₑ = 100 + 5 = 105 cm
Answer: (a) 20×, (b) 105 cm

6️⃣ The Human Eye 👁️

Structure and Function

  • Cornea: Transparent front part, provides most of eye's focusing power
  • Aqueous Humor: Watery fluid between cornea and lens
  • Iris: Colored part, controls pupil size (aperture)
  • Lens: Convex lens that fine-tunes focus (accommodation)
  • Ciliary Muscles: Change lens shape for focusing
  • Retina: Light-sensitive screen (film/sensor)
  • Optic Nerve: Transmits signals to brain

Accommodation

The eye's ability to focus on objects at different distances by changing the lens shape:

  • Near object: Ciliary muscles contract → lens becomes thicker → shorter focal length → more converging power
  • Distant object: Ciliary muscles relax → lens becomes thinner → longer focal length → less converging power

Far Point and Near Point

Term Definition Normal Value
Far Point Farthest distance eye can see clearly (relaxed) Infinity (∞)
Near Point Closest distance eye can see clearly (maximum accommodation) 25 cm (D)

Eye Defects and Corrections

Defect Cause Symptom Correction
Myopia (Short-sighted) Eyeball too long or lens too converging Can see near, not far; Far point closer than ∞ Concave (diverging) lens
Hypermetropia (Long-sighted) Eyeball too short or lens not converging enough Can see far, not near; Near point > 25 cm Convex (converging) lens
Presbyopia Loss of accommodation with age Cannot see near objects Convex lens (reading glasses)
Astigmatism Cornea/lens not perfectly spherical Blurred vision at all distances Cylindrical lens

🔢 Calculation 7: Correcting Myopia

Question: A short-sighted person cannot see objects beyond 2 m clearly. Calculate the power of lens needed to correct this defect.

Far point = 2 m (instead of ∞)
Lens must form virtual image of distant object at 2 m

Using 1/f = 1/v - 1/u:
u = ∞ (object), v = -2 m (virtual image)
1/f = 1/(-2) - 1/∞ = -0.5 - 0 = -0.5
f = -2 m

Power: P = 1/f = -0.5 D
Answer: -0.5 D (concave lens)

🔢 Calculation 8: Correcting Hypermetropia

Question: A long-sighted person has near point at 50 cm. Calculate the power of lens needed to read a book at 25 cm.

Lens must form virtual image at 50 cm when object is at 25 cm

Using 1/f = 1/v - 1/u:
u = 25 cm = 0.25 m, v = -50 cm = -0.5 m
1/f = 1/(-0.5) - 1/0.25 = -2 - 4 = -6
Wait - this gives negative! Let me recalculate correctly:

For corrective lens:
Object at 25 cm, image at near point (-50 cm, same side)
1/f = 1/v - 1/u = 1/(-0.5) - 1/0.25 = -2 - 4 = -6

Actually: 1/f = 1/0.25 + 1/(-0.5) = 4 - 2 = +2 D
f = +0.5 m = +50 cm
Answer: +2 D (convex lens)

📋 Summary Table: Optical Instruments

Instrument Lens Type Image Nature Key Formula
Camera Single convex Real, inverted, diminished 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
Projector Single convex Real, inverted, magnified m = v/u
Simple Microscope Single convex (short f) Virtual, erect, magnified M = D/f or 1 + D/f
Compound Microscope Two convex lenses Virtual, inverted, highly magnified M = (L/fₒ) × (D/fₑ)
Telescope Two convex lenses Virtual, inverted, angular magnification M = fₒ/fₑ

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

  1. Camera vs Projector: Both use single convex lens, but object position differs (camera: u > 2f, projector: f < u < 2f)

  2. Microscope vs Telescope: Microscope for small nearby objects, telescope for distant large objects

  3. Eye defects: Myopia = short sight (correct with concave), Hypermetropia = long sight (correct with convex)

  4. Magnification: Simple microscope M = D/f; Telescope M = fₒ/fₑ (don't confuse!)

  5. Sign conventions: Real is positive for lenses; virtual image v is positive for lenses (opposite to mirrors!)

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Confusing linear magnification (M = v/u) with angular magnification (M = D/f or fₒ/fₑ)
  • ❌ Forgetting that compound microscope produces inverted final image
  • ❌ Using mirror sign conventions for lenses (they're opposite!)
  • ❌ Thinking telescope produces erect image (astronomical telescope gives inverted image)
  • ❌ Forgetting to convert mm to m when calculating power in diopters
