Electrolysis 🚀

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry

Definition of Electrolysis

Electrolysis is the process by which an electric current is passed through an electrolyte (molten or aqueous) causing chemical decomposition (breakdown) of the electrolyte into its constituent elements or compounds at the electrodes.

Step 1: Electrolytes and Non-Electrolytes

Electrolyte = substance that conducts electricity when molten or dissolved in water and is decomposed by the current.

Non-electrolyte = substance that does not conduct electricity or get decomposed.

Type Examples
Strong electrolytes NaCl, HCl, H₂SO₄, NaOH (complete ionisation)
Weak electrolytes CH₃COOH, NH₃(aq) (partial ionisation)
Non-electrolytes Sugar, ethanol, urea, kerosene

Step 2: Faraday’s Laws of Electrolysis

Faraday’s First Law (Denotation):

The mass (m) of a substance liberated at an electrode during electrolysis is directly proportional to the quantity of electricity (Q) passed through the electrolyte.

Denotation & Formula: m ∝ Q  →  m = Z × Q  or  m = Z × I × t

where:
Z = electrochemical equivalent (g/C)
I = current in amperes (A)
t = time in seconds (s)
Q = quantity of electricity (coulombs, C)

Faraday’s Second Law (Denotation):

When the same quantity of electricity is passed through different electrolytes, the masses of substances liberated at the electrodes are directly proportional to their chemical equivalents (equivalent weights).

Denotation & Formula: m₁ / Eq₁ = m₂ / Eq₂  or  m ∝ Eq

Equivalent mass (Eq) = M / n  (M = molar mass, n = number of electrons transferred)

Step 3: Electrolysis of Specific Solutions

Solution Cathode (–) Product Anode (+) Product
Dilute H₂SO₄ (inert electrodes) H₂ gas O₂ gas (volume ratio 2:1)
Aqueous CuSO₄ (inert electrodes) Cu metal (pink deposit) O₂ gas
Concentrated NaCl (brine) H₂ gas Cl₂ gas (greenish-yellow)
Molten NaCl Na metal Cl₂ gas

Step 4: Factors Affecting Discharge of Ions

  • Position in electrochemical series
  • Concentration of ions
  • Nature of electrode (inert or reactive)

Step 5: Uses of Electrolysis

  • Purification of copper (electrolytic refining)
  • Extraction of aluminium (Hall-Héroult process)
  • Production of NaOH, Cl₂, H₂ (chlor-alkali process)
  • Electroplating (e.g. silver, gold, chromium)

Step 6: Electrochemical Cells & Series

Electrochemical series (learn order): K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Sn > Pb > H > Cu > Ag > Au

Step 7: Corrosion & Prevention

Corrosion of iron is an electrolytic process (anodic oxidation, cathodic reduction).

Prevention: painting, galvanising, cathodic protection (Mg/Zn sacrificial anode), grease/oil coating.

6 JAMB Calculation Examples with Step-by-Step Solutions & Formulas

Example 1: Mass Deposited (Faraday 1st Law)

Formula: m = (M × I × t) / (n × F)  F = 96 500 C mol⁻¹

Question: Calculate mass of Cu deposited by 0.5 A for 30 min in CuSO₄ (M = 63.5 g/mol, n = 2).

Step 1: t = 30 × 60 = 1800 s
Step 2: Q = I × t = 0.5 × 1800 = 900 C
Step 3: m = (63.5 × 900) / (2 × 96 500) = 57 150 / 193 000 ≈ 0.296 g
Answer: 0.296 g

Example 2: Time Required

Formula: t = (m × n × F) / (M × I)

Question: How long will 2 A deposit 0.635 g Cu? (M = 63.5, n = 2)

Step 1: t = (0.635 × 2 × 96 500) / (63.5 × 2) = 122 555 / 127 ≈ 965 s
Step 2: 965 s = 16.08 minutes
Answer: 965 seconds (≈ 16 min)

Example 3: Volume of Gas at STP

Formula: Volume (dm³) = (Q × 22.4) / (n × F)  (for 1 mole gas at STP = 22.4 dm³)

Question: Volume of H₂ at STP liberated by 96 500 C (n = 2 for H₂).

Step 1: Moles e⁻ = Q / F = 96 500 / 96 500 = 1 mol
Step 2: Moles H₂ = 1 / 2 = 0.5 mol
Step 3: Volume = 0.5 × 22.4 = 11.2 dm³
Answer: 11.2 dm³

Example 4: E°_cell Calculation

Formula: E°_cell = E°_cathode – E°_anode

Question: Calculate E°_cell for Zn | Zn²⁺ || Cu²⁺ | Cu (E°_Cu = +0.34 V, E°_Zn = –0.76 V).

Step 1: Cu is cathode (+0.34 V), Zn is anode (–0.76 V)
Step 2: E°_cell = 0.34 – (–0.76) = 0.34 + 0.76 = 1.10 V
Answer: 1.10 V

Example 5: Equivalent Mass

Formula: Equivalent mass = M / n

Question: Equivalent mass of Al in Al³⁺ electrolysis (M = 27, n = 3).

Step 1: n = 3 (Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Al)
Step 2: Eq. mass = 27 / 3 = 9 g
Answer: 9 g

Example 6: Faraday’s 2nd Law (Mass Ratio)

Formula: m₁ / Eq₁ = m₂ / Eq₂

Question: Same Q deposits 1.08 g Ag (Eq = 108) and x g Cu (Eq = 31.75). Find x.

Step 1: 1.08 / 108 = x / 31.75
Step 2: x = (1.08 × 31.75) / 108 ≈ 0.3175 g
Answer: 0.3175 g
