Electrolysis 🚀

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry

Definition of Electrolysis

Electrolysis is the process by which an electric current is passed through an electrolyte (molten or aqueous) causing chemical decomposition (breakdown) of the electrolyte into its constituent elements or compounds at the electrodes.

Step 1: Electrolytes and Non-Electrolytes

Electrolyte = substance that conducts electricity when molten or dissolved in water and is decomposed by the current.

Non-electrolyte = substance that does not conduct electricity or get decomposed.

Type Examples
Strong electrolytes NaCl, HCl, H₂SO₄, NaOH (complete ionisation)
Weak electrolytes CH₃COOH, NH₃(aq) (partial ionisation)
Non-electrolytes Sugar, ethanol, urea, kerosene

Step 2: Faraday’s Laws of Electrolysis

Faraday’s 1st Law: Mass liberated (m) ∝ quantity of electricity (Q = I × t).

Formula: m = (M × I × t) / (n × F) where F = 96 500 C mol⁻¹.

Faraday’s 2nd Law: For same Q, masses ∝ chemical equivalents.

Step 3: Electrolysis of Specific Solutions

Solution Cathode Product Anode Product
Dilute H₂SO₄ (inert electrodes) H₂ gas O₂ gas (ratio 2:1)
Aqueous CuSO₄ (inert) Cu metal O₂ gas
Conc. NaCl (brine) H₂ gas Cl₂ gas
Molten NaCl Na metal Cl₂ gas

Step 4: Factors Affecting Discharge of Ions

  • Position in electrochemical series
  • Concentration of ions
  • Nature of electrode

Step 5: Uses of Electrolysis

  • Purification of copper
  • Extraction of aluminium (Hall-Héroult process)
  • Production of NaOH, Cl₂, H₂ (chlor-alkali)
  • Electroplating

Step 6: Electrochemical Cells & Series

Electrochemical series: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Sn > Pb > H > Cu > Ag > Au

Step 7: Corrosion & Prevention

Corrosion of iron is an electrolytic process.

Prevention: painting, galvanising, cathodic protection (Mg/Zn sacrificial anode).

6 JAMB Calculation Examples with Step-by-Step Solutions

Example 1: Mass Deposited (Faraday 1st Law)

Question: Calculate mass of Cu deposited by 0.5 A for 30 min in CuSO₄ (M = 63.5, n=2).

Formula: m = (M × I × t) / (n × F)

Step 1: Q = 0.5 × (30×60) = 900 C
Step 2: m = (63.5 × 900) / (2 × 96500) = 0.296 g
Answer: 0.296 g

Example 2: Time Required

Question: How long will 2 A deposit 0.635 g Cu? (M=63.5, n=2)

Formula: t = (m × n × F) / (M × I)

Step 1: t = (0.635 × 2 × 96500) / (63.5 × 2) = 965 s
Answer: 965 s (16.08 min)

Example 3: Volume of Gas (H₂)

Question: Volume of H₂ at STP liberated by 96500 C (1 mole e⁻ = 11.2 dm³ H₂).

Formula: Volume = (Q × 11.2) / (n × F) for H₂ (n=2)

Step 1: For 96500 C, moles e⁻ = 1
Step 2: Moles H₂ = 0.5, Volume = 0.5 × 22.4 = 11.2 dm³
Answer: 11.2 dm³

Example 4: E°_cell Calculation

Question: Calculate E°_cell for Zn | Zn²⁺ || Cu²⁺ | Cu (E°_Cu = +0.34 V, E°_Zn = –0.76 V).

Formula: E°_cell = E°_cathode – E°_anode

Step 1: Cathode Cu (+0.34), Anode Zn (–0.76)
Step 2: E°_cell = 0.34 – (–0.76) = 1.10 V
Answer: 1.10 V

Example 5: Equivalent Mass

Question: Equivalent mass of Al in Al₂O₃ electrolysis (M=27, n=3).

Formula: Eq. mass = M / n

Step 1: n=3 for Al³⁺
Step 2: Eq. mass = 27 / 3 = 9 g
Answer: 9 g

Example 6: Faraday’s 2nd Law

Question: Same Q deposits 1.08 g Ag (Eq=108) and x g Cu (Eq=31.75). Find x.

Formula: m₁ / Eq₁ = m₂ / Eq₂

Step 1: 1.08 / 108 = x / 31.75
Step 2: x = 0.3175 g
Answer: 0.3175 g
