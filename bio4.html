Definition: Internal Structure of a Flowering Plant

Refers to the microscopic organization of tissues in root, stem, and leaf of angiosperms (flowering plants).

Divided into dicotyledons (dicots: 2 cotyledons) and monocotyledons (monocots: 1 cotyledon).

Key focus: Identify T.S. (transverse section), relate structure to functions (absorption, support, transport, photosynthesis), distribution of supporting tissues.

‚Ä¢ Vascular Bundles ‚Äì Definition & Functions

Definition: Longitudinal strands of conducting tissues (xylem + phloem), often with fibers and bundle sheath.

Functions:

  • Xylem: Transports water + minerals upward (unidirectional; transpiration pull).
  • Phloem: Transports food (sugars, amino acids) bidirectionally (translocation).
  • Mechanical support: Lignified xylem + sclerenchyma fibers prevent collapse.
  • Connects all plant parts for nutrient/water distribution and structural integrity.

‚Ä¢ Xylem Structure (Key Component of Vascular Bundles)

Composed of dead, lignified cells for strength and water conduction.

Main elements:

  • Tracheids: Long, tapered, pointed cells with pits; water passes via bordered pits; common in all vascular plants.
  • Vessels (vessel elements): Wider, shorter, tube-like; joined end-to-end with perforation plates; efficient water flow; mainly in angiosperms (dicots more).
  • Xylem fibers: Sclerenchyma; provide mechanical support.
  • Xylem parenchyma: Living cells; store food, aid lateral transport.

Types of thickening (lignin patterns):

  • Annular/spiral (protoxylem): Early, extensible.
  • Scalariform/pitted/reticulate (metaxylem): Later, rigid.

Function: Water/mineral transport + mechanical rigidity (lignin prevents collapse under tension).

‚Ä¢ i. Root (T.S.)

Dicot Root (e.g. bean)

Epidermis ‚Üí root hairs (absorption) ‚Üí thick cortex (parenchyma, storage) ‚Üí endodermis (Casparian strip regulates entry) ‚Üí pericycle (lateral roots) ‚Üí star-shaped xylem (tetrarch: 2‚Äì6 arms) + phloem between arms ‚Üí small/no pith.

Vascular bundles: Central stele; xylem dominant for support/transport.

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Dicot Root):

  • Epidermis: Outermost layer with root hairs for absorption
  • Cortex: Parenchyma cells for storage
  • Endodermis: Casparian strip controls water/mineral entry
  • Pericycle: Origin of lateral roots
  • Xylem: Star-shaped, transports water upward
  • Phloem: Between xylem arms, transports food

Monocot Root (e.g. maize)

Epidermis ‚Üí large cortex ‚Üí endodermis ‚Üí pericycle ‚Üí polyarch xylem (>6‚Äì20 arms) around large central pith ‚Üí vascular bundles in ring.

Vascular bundles: Scattered around pith; sclerenchymatous conjunctive tissue.

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Monocot Root):

  • Epidermis: Outer protective layer
  • Cortex: Large, storage tissue
  • Endodermis: Regulates flow
  • Pericycle: Lateral root origin
  • Xylem: Polyarch (many arms), central support
  • Pith: Large central storage area

‚Ä¢ ii. Stem (T.S.)

Dicot Stem (e.g. sunflower)

Epidermis (cuticle) ‚Üí cortex (collenchyma hypodermis) ‚Üí vascular bundles in ring (open: xylem inner, phloem outer, cambium between) ‚Üí central pith (storage).

Vascular bundles: Ring arrangement; allow secondary growth.

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Dicot Stem):

  • Epidermis: Protective layer with cuticle
  • Hypodermis: Collenchyma ‚Äì flexible support
  • Cortex: Storage tissue
  • Vascular bundle: Ring arrangement; open (cambium present)
  • Xylem: Inner, water transport & support
  • Phloem: Outer, food transport
  • Pith: Central storage

Monocot Stem (e.g. maize)

Epidermis ‚Üí sclerenchyma hypodermis ‚Üí scattered vascular bundles (closed: no cambium) in ground tissue ‚Üí often hollow center.

Vascular bundles: Scattered; bundle sheath + sclerenchyma caps.

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Monocot Stem):

  • Epidermis: Outer protection
  • Hypodermis: Sclerenchyma ‚Äì rigid support
  • Ground tissue: Storage & support
  • Vascular bundle: Scattered, closed (no cambium)
  • Bundle sheath: Surrounds vascular tissue

‚Ä¢ iii. Leaf (T.S.)

Dicot Leaf (dorsiventral, e.g. bean)

Upper epidermis (thick cuticle, few stomata) ‚Üí palisade mesophyll (columnar, chloroplast-rich) ‚Üí spongy mesophyll (air spaces, gas exchange) ‚Üí lower epidermis (many stomata) ‚Üí veins (xylem upper, phloem lower).

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Dicot Leaf):

  • Upper epidermis: Thick cuticle, fewer stomata
  • Palisade mesophyll: Columnar cells, max photosynthesis
  • Spongy mesophyll: Air spaces for gas exchange
  • Lower epidermis: Many stomata
  • Vein: Xylem (upper), phloem (lower)

Monocot Leaf (isobilateral, e.g. maize)

Both epidermises similar (stomata on both) ‚Üí undifferentiated mesophyll ‚Üí scattered vascular bundles with bundle sheath ‚Üí bulliform cells (rolling).

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Monocot Leaf):

  • Epidermis: Stomata on both sides
  • Mesophyll: Undifferentiated
  • Vascular bundle: Scattered with bundle sheath
  • Bulliform cells: Help in leaf rolling

‚Ä¢ Stomata Structure (in Leaf Epidermis)

Definition: Pores in epidermis for gas exchange and transpiration.

Structure:

  • Two kidney-shaped guard cells (living, chloroplast-containing) surround stomatal pore.
  • Thick inner walls (thicker toward pore); thin outer walls.
  • Subsidiary cells (epidermal cells around guard cells; help regulate).
  • Sub-stomatal cavity below pore connects to air spaces in mesophyll.

Short Notes on Labeled Parts (Stomata):

  • Guard cells: Control pore opening/closing
  • Stomatal pore: Gas exchange & transpiration
  • Subsidiary cells: Assist in regulation
  • Sub-stomatal cavity: Connects to mesophyll air spaces

‚Ä¢ Supporting Tissues Summary

  • Collenchyma: Living, flexible; corners thickened; dicot stems/petiole.
  • Sclerenchyma: Dead, rigid; fibers/sclereids; monocot stems, bundle caps.
  • Xylem: Lignified; mechanical support + water transport.
  • Phloem fibers: Sclerenchymatous; extra support near phloem.

Definition: Internal Structure of a Mammal

Arrangement, appearance, and position of major organs in thoracic/abdominal cavities (dissection view).

Diaphragm separates thoracic (heart, lungs) from abdominal.

General Arrangement

Thoracic cavity: Heart (central, pericardium), lungs (lateral).

Abdominal cavity: Liver (upper right, reddish-brown), stomach (left, J-shaped), intestines (coiled), kidneys (posterior, bean-shaped), spleen (left), reproductive organs (pelvis).

Digestive Organs

Esophagus ‚Üí stomach (left upper) ‚Üí small intestine (central, long/coiled) ‚Üí large intestine (frame-like) ‚Üí rectum.

Liver (large, right), pancreas (below stomach).

Reproductive Organs

Male: Testes (scrotum), vas deferens, prostate.

Female: Ovaries (small, lower abdomen), uterus (central, pear-shaped), vagina.

Position: Lower abdomen/pelvis.

Excretory Organs

Kidneys (reddish-brown, posterior; right slightly lower) ‚Üí ureters ‚Üí bladder (pelvis) ‚Üí urethra.
