Measures of Dispersion: Easy-to-Understand Guide (JAMB Style)

Measures of dispersion (also called measures of spread or variation) tell us how spread out the data is.
Mean/median/mode tell us the “centre” or typical value.
Dispersion tells us: “Are the numbers close together or scattered far apart?”
Imagine two classes with the same average score of 50:
Class A: all students scored exactly 50 → very little spread.
Class B: scores from 10 to 90 → very big spread.
Dispersion helps us see the difference.

We will cover four main measures for both ungrouped data (raw list of numbers) and grouped data (frequency table).

  • 1. Range (Simplest)
    Formula (ungrouped): Range = Highest value – Lowest value
    For grouped data: Range = Upper boundary of highest class – Lower boundary of lowest class
    (Example: classes 10–19 and 50–59 → range = 59 – 10 = 49)
    Why useful? Quick idea of total spread. Very sensitive to outliers.
  • 2. Mean Deviation (M.D. or Average Deviation)
    It is the average distance of each value from the mean (we ignore + or – by using absolute value | |).
    Ungrouped formula: M.D. = Σ |x – x̄| / n
    (where x̄ = mean, n = number of items)
    Grouped formula: M.D. = Σ f |m – x̄| / N
    (m = class midpoint, f = frequency, N = total frequency)
  • 3. Variance
    It is the average of the squared distances from the mean. Squaring removes the +/– problem.
    Ungrouped formula (JAMB uses population formula): Variance (σ²) = Σ (x – x̄)² / n
    (Shortcut: Σx²/n – (x̄)² ← very useful in exams!)
    Grouped: Variance = Σ f (m – x̄)² / N
  • 4. Standard Deviation (S.D. or σ)
    Square root of variance. It is in the same units as the original data (most useful).
    Formula: S.D. = √Variance

Note for JAMB: They almost always use division by n (not n–1).
Answers are often left in √ form or to 2 decimal places.

Step-by-Step Calculation Tips (Easy Method)

Ungrouped data (quick table method):
Find mean (x̄).
Make columns: x | x – x̄ | |x – x̄| | (x – x̄)²
Sum the columns you need.

Grouped data:
Find class midpoints (m).
Calculate mean first: x̄ = Σ(f × m) / N
Then use the absolute or squared columns.

10 JAMB-Style & Past Questions with Full Solutions

Range Examples

Q1 (JAMB-style): Find the range of: 2, 4, 5, 9
Step 1: Identify highest = 9, lowest = 2.
Step 2: Range = 9 – 2 = 7.
Answer: 7
Q2 (JAMB-style): The scores of 10 students: 15, 18, 22, 25, 30, 35, 40, 45, 50, 55. Find the range.
Step 1: Highest = 55, lowest = 15.
Step 2: Range = 55 – 15 = 40.
Answer: 40
Q3 (Grouped – JAMB-style): Marks of students:
0–9 (f=5), 10–19 (f=15), 20–29 (f=20), 30–39 (f=10)
Range = ?
Step 1: Lowest class lower boundary = 0, highest class upper boundary = 39.
Step 2: Range = 39 – 0 = 39.
Answer: 39

Mean Deviation Examples

Q4 (Real JAMB 2008): Find the mean deviation of 2, 4, 5, 9.
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (2+4+5+9)/4 = 20/4 = 5.
Step 2: Absolute deviations: |2-5|=3, |4-5|=1, |5-5|=0, |9-5|=4.
Step 3: Sum = 3+1+0+4 = 8.
Step 4: M.D. = 8/4 = 2.
Answer: 2
Q5 (Real JAMB 2007): What is the mean deviation of 3, 5, 8, 11, 12, 21?
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (3+5+8+11+12+21)/6 = 60/6 = 10.
Step 2: Abs dev: |3-10|=7, |5-10|=5, |8-10|=2, |11-10|=1, |12-10|=2, |21-10|=11.
Step 3: Sum = 7+5+2+1+2+11 = 28.
Step 4: M.D. = 28/6 ≈ 4.67 (or 4.7).
Answer: 4.67
Q6 (Real JAMB 2002): Calculate the mean deviation of 7, 3, 14, 9, 7, 8.
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (7+3+14+9+7+8)/6 = 48/6 = 8.
Step 2: Abs dev: |7-8|=1, |3-8|=5, |14-8|=6, |9-8|=1, |7-8|=1, |8-8|=0.
Step 3: Sum = 1+5+6+1+1+0 = 14.
Step 4: M.D. = 14/6 = 7/3 ≈ 2.33.
Answer: 7/3
Q7 (Real JAMB 2000): If the mean of 0, (x+2), (3x+6), (4x+8) is 4, find their mean deviation.
Step 1: Sum = 0 + (x+2) + (3x+6) + (4x+8) = 8x + 16 = 4 × 4 = 16 → 8x + 16 = 16 → 8x = 0 → x = 0.
Step 2: Data: 0, 2, 6, 8. Mean = 16/4 = 4.
Step 3: Abs dev: |0-4|=4, |2-4|=2, |6-4|=2, |8-4|=4.
Step 4: Sum = 12, M.D. = 12/4 = 3.
Answer: 3
Q8 (JAMB-style): Find M.D. of 10, 12, 15, 18, 20 (n=5)
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (10+12+15+18+20)/5 = 75/5 = 15.
Step 2: Abs dev: |10-15|=5, |12-15|=3, |15-15|=0, |18-15|=3, |20-15|=5.
Step 3: Sum = 16.
Step 4: M.D. = 16/5 = 3.2.
Answer: 3.2

Variance & Standard Deviation Examples

Q9 (Real JAMB 2024): Calculate the standard deviation of 5, 4, 6, 7, 8.
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (5+4+6+7+8)/5 = 30/5 = 6.
Step 2: (x – x̄)²: (5-6)²=1, (4-6)²=4, (6-6)²=0, (7-6)²=1, (8-6)²=4.
Step 3: Sum = 1+4+0+1+4 = 10.
Step 4: Variance = 10/5 = 2.
Step 5: S.D. = √2 ≈ 1.41 (or √2).
Answer: √2
Q10 (Real JAMB 2010): Find the standard deviation of 2, 3, 5, 6.
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (2+3+5+6)/4 = 16/4 = 4.
Step 2: (x – x̄)²: (2-4)²=4, (3-4)²=1, (5-4)²=1, (6-4)²=4.
Step 3: Sum = 10.
Step 4: Variance = 10/4 = 2.5.
Step 5: S.D. = √2.5 = √(5/2) ≈1.58.
Answer: √(5/2)
Q11 (Real JAMB 1993): Calculate the standard deviation of 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13.
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (7+8+9+10+11+12+13)/7 = 70/7 = 10.
Step 2: (x – x̄)²: (7-10)²=9, (8-10)²=4, (9-10)²=1, 0, 1, 4, 9.
Step 3: Sum = 28.
Step 4: Variance = 28/7 = 4.
Step 5: S.D. = √4 = 2.
Answer: 2
Q12 (Real JAMB 2000 – Variance): The variance of x, 2x, 3x, 4x, 5x is
Step 1: Mean x̄ = (x+2x+3x+4x+5x)/5 = 15x/5 = 3x.
Step 2: (x – 3x)² = (–2x)² = 4x², (2x–3x)²= x², 0, x², 4x².
Step 3: Sum = 4x² + x² + 0 + x² + 4x² = 10x².
Step 4: Variance = 10x² / 5 = 2x².
Answer: 2x²
Q13 (Grouped – JAMB-style):
Class: 0–4 (f=2), 5–9 (f=5), 10–14 (f=8), 15–19 (f=5) (N=20)
Midpoints (m): 2, 7, 12, 17
Mean x̄ = (2×2 + 5×7 + 8×12 + 5×17)/20 = (4+35+96+85)/20 = 220/20 = 11
Now Variance:
f(m–11)²:
(2–11)²×2 = 81×2 = 162
(7–11)²×5 = 16×5 = 80
(12–11)²×8 = 1×8 = 8
(17–11)²×5 = 36×5 = 180
Sum = 162+80+8+180 = 430
Variance = 430/20 = 21.5
S.D. = √21.5 ≈ 4.64
Answer: Variance 21.5, S.D. ≈4.64
Q14 (Simple Variance from S.D.): If S.D. = 12.34, find variance.
Step 1: Variance = (S.D.)² = (12.34)² = 152.2756.
Answer: 152.2756
