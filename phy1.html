1️⃣ Length, Area, and Volume 📐

Length is the measure of distance between two points. The SI unit is the metre (m).

Measuring Instruments:

Instrument What It Measures Precision/Accuracy
Metre Rule Length up to 1m ± 0.1 cm (1 mm)
Vernier Calipers Length, internal/external diameters ± 0.01 cm (0.1 mm)
Micrometer Screw Gauge Small lengths (wire thickness) ± 0.001 cm (0.01 mm)
Measuring Cylinder Volume of liquids/irregular solids ± 1 cm³
🔧 DETAILED CALCULATION EXAMPLES

2️⃣ Vernier Calipers 📏

How to Read:
Main Scale Reading (MSR) + (Vernier Scale Division × Least Count)
Least Count = 0.01 cm = 0.1 mm

Example 1: Basic Reading

Question: Main scale reading is 3.2 cm and the 4th vernier division coincides. Calculate total reading.

Solution:
• MSR = 3.2 cm
• Vernier coincidence = 4
• Least count = 0.01 cm
• Vernier reading = 4 × 0.01 = 0.04 cm
• Total reading = 3.2 + 0.04 = 3.24 cm
Answer: 3.24 cm

Example 2: Zero Error Correction

Question: A vernier caliper has zero error of +0.03 cm. Main scale reads 2.5 cm and 6th vernier division coincides. Find correct diameter.

Solution:
• Observed reading = 2.5 + (6 × 0.01) = 2.56 cm
• Zero error = +0.03 cm (positive error is subtracted)
• Correct reading = 2.56 - 0.03 = 2.53 cm
Answer: 2.53 cm

Example 3: Volume Calculation

Question: Calculate volume of cylinder: length = 5.45 cm, diameter = 2.16 cm.

Solution:
• Radius r = 2.16 ÷ 2 = 1.08 cm
• Volume V = πr²h = (22/7) × (1.08)² × 5.45
• V = (22/7) × 1.1664 × 5.45
• V = 19.98 cm³ ≈ 20.0 cm³
Answer: 20.0 cm³

3️⃣ Micrometer Screw Gauge 🔩

How to Read:
Main Scale Reading (MSR) + (Thimble Scale Reading × Least Count)
Least Count = 0.01 mm = 0.001 cm
One complete rotation = 0.5 mm

Example 4: Basic Reading

Question: Sleeve reading shows 5.5 mm and thimble reading is 28 divisions. Calculate measurement.

Solution:
• Sleeve reading = 5.5 mm
• Thimble reading = 28 × 0.01 = 0.28 mm
• Total reading = 5.5 + 0.28 = 5.78 mm = 0.578 cm
Answer: 5.78 mm (0.578 cm)

Example 5: Zero Error Correction

Question: A micrometer has zero error of -0.04 mm. It reads 3.25 mm when measuring a wire. Find correct thickness.

Solution:
• Observed reading = 3.25 mm
• Zero error = -0.04 mm (negative error is added)
• Correct reading = 3.25 + 0.04 = 3.29 mm
Answer: 3.29 mm

Example 6: Error Analysis

Question: Five measurements of wire diameter: 2.14, 2.16, 2.15, 2.17, 2.13 mm. Calculate: (a) Mean (b) Absolute errors (c) Mean absolute error.

Solution:
(a) Mean = (2.14+2.16+2.15+2.17+2.13) ÷ 5 = 10.75 ÷ 5 = 2.15 mm

(b) Absolute errors: |2.14-2.15|=0.01, |2.16-2.15|=0.01, |2.15-2.15|=0, |2.17-2.15|=0.02, |2.13-2.15|=0.02

(c) Mean absolute error = (0.01+0.01+0+0.02+0.02) ÷ 5 = 0.012 mm
Final result = 2.15 ± 0.01 mm

4️⃣ Metre Rule & Measuring Cylinder 📏

Example 7: Percentage Error

Question: A block measures 8.5 cm × 3.2 cm × 1.5 cm. Calculate: (a) Volume (b) Percentage error if each measurement has ±0.1 cm error.

Solution:
(a) Volume = 8.5 × 3.2 × 1.5 = 40.8 cm³

(b) Relative error = (0.1/8.5) + (0.1/3.2) + (0.1/1.5)
= 0.0118 + 0.0313 + 0.0667 = 0.1098
Percentage error = 10.98% ≈ 11.0%
Answers: (a) 40.8 cm³ (b) 11.0%

Example 8: Volume by Displacement

Question: Measuring cylinder contains 45 cm³ water. When stone is added, level rises to 62 cm³. Calculate stone's volume.

Solution:
• Initial volume V₁ = 45 cm³
• Final volume V₂ = 62 cm³
• Stone volume = V₂ - V₁ = 62 - 45 = 17 cm³
Answer: 17 cm³

5️⃣ Standard Form & Error Analysis 🔢

Example 10: Standard Form

Question: Express in standard form to 3 significant figures: (a) 456789 (b) 0.00003456 (c) 299792458

Solution:
(a) 4.57 × 10⁵
(b) 3.46 × 10⁻⁵
(c) 3.00 × 10⁸

Example 11: Uncertainty Expression

Question: A student measures length as 25.4 cm with uncertainty of ±0.2 cm. Express properly and calculate percentage error.

Solution:
• Expression: 25.4 ± 0.2 cm
• Percentage error = (0.2/25.4) × 100 = 0.79% ≈ 0.8%
Answer: 0.8%

6️⃣ Area and Volume Calculations 📐

Example 12: Wire Volume

Question: A wire has length 50 cm and diameter 0.50 mm. Calculate its volume.

Solution:
• Radius r = 0.25 mm = 0.025 cm
• Length h = 50 cm
• Volume = πr²h = (22/7) × (0.025)² × 50
= (22/7) × 0.000625 × 50 = 0.0982 cm³ ≈ 9.82 × 10⁻² cm³
Answer: 9.82 × 10⁻² cm³

Example 13: Sphere Surface Area

Question: Calculate surface area of sphere with radius 3.5 cm.

Solution:
Surface area = 4πr² = 4 × (22/7) × (3.5)²
= 4 × (22/7) × 12.25 = 154 cm²
Answer: 154 cm²

7️⃣ Dimensional Analysis Examples 🔬

Example 14: Checking Consistency

Question: Check dimensional consistency of s = ut + ½at²

Solution:
• [s] = [L]
• [ut] = [LT⁻¹][T] = [L]
• [at²] = [LT⁻²][T²] = [L]
• All terms have dimension [L]
Equation is dimensionally consistent ✓

Example 15: Verifying Formula

Question: Verify dimensions of T = 2π√(l/g) for simple pendulum period.

Solution:
• [T] = [T]
• [l/g] = [L]/[LT⁻²] = [T²]
• √[T²] = [T]
Dimensions match: [T] = [T] ✓

Example 16: Finding Dimensions

Question: If x = a + bt + ct², where x is distance, find dimensions of a, b, and c.

Solution:
• [a] = [x] = [L]
• [bt] = [L] → [b] = [L]/[T] = [LT⁻¹]
• [ct²] = [L] → [c] = [L]/[T²] = [LT⁻²]

8️⃣ Scalar vs Vector Quantities ↔️

Type Definition Examples
Scalar Has magnitude only Mass, time, distance, speed, energy, temperature, density
Vector Has magnitude and direction Displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, momentum, electric field
Vector Addition:
• Parallelogram law: R = √(P² + Q² + 2PQcosθ)
• If θ = 90°: R = √(P² + Q²)

🔢 Example 17: Position and Displacement

Question: A particle moves from A(2, 3) to B(5, 7). Find displacement and distance.

Solution:
• Displacement vector = (5-2, 7-3) = (3, 4) m
• Distance = √(3² + 4²) = 5 m
• Direction: tan⁻¹(4/3) = 53.1° above x-axis
Answers: Displacement = (3,4) m, Distance = 5 m, Direction = 53.1°

🎯 JAMB Exam Focus - Key Objectives

You must be able to:

  • Identify units of length, area, and volume
  • Use metre rules, vernier calipers, micrometers, and measuring cylinders
  • Determine dimensions of physical quantities
  • Test homogeneity of equations using dimensional analysis
  • Estimate errors and express answers in standard form
  • Distinguish between distance and displacement
  • Locate positions using Cartesian coordinates

💡 Study Tips for JAMB Success

  1. Practice reading vernier calipers and micrometers - JAMB loves these questions
  2. Master dimensional analysis - Use it to check if equations make sense
  3. Always include units in calculations - Dimensional consistency prevents errors
  4. Remember significant figures - Don't lose marks for over-precision
  5. Use standard form for very large/small numbers
  6. Know your instruments - Least count, range, and zero error corrections
  7. Memorize dimensions of common quantities - Speeds up problem-solving
🧠 Memory Aid: "Positive error subtract, Negative error add" (PESA) for zero error corrections!
