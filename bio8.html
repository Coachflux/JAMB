Definition of Excretion

Excretion is the biological process by which organisms remove metabolic waste products (toxic or excess substances like nitrogenous wastes, CO‚ÇÇ, excess water/salts) to maintain homeostasis and prevent toxicity.

Significance: Regulates body fluid composition, osmotic pressure, pH, electrolyte balance; prevents cellular damage/death from waste accumulation.

a. Types of Excretory Structures

  • Contractile vacuole: Unicellular freshwater protozoa (e.g., Amoeba). Collects excess water/wastes ‚Üí contracts to expel via membrane. Prevents osmotic bursting.
  • Flame cell (protonephridia): Flatworms (e.g., Planaria). Ciliated tubules filter body fluids; wastes exit pores. Simple filtration + osmoregulation in aquatic forms.
  • Nephridium: Annelids (e.g., earthworm). Coiled tubules per segment; filter coelomic fluid, reabsorb useful substances, excrete via pores. Excretion + osmoregulation in moist habitats.
  • Malpighian tubule: Insects/arachnids. Blind tubules at gut junction; secrete uric acid/salts ‚Üí water reabsorbed in rectum. Water conservation in terrestrial life.
  • Kidney: Vertebrates. Nephrons filter blood; excrete urea/ammonia/uric acid. Advanced waste removal + osmoregulation/ion balance.
  • Stoma & Lenticel: Plants. Stomata (leaves) diffuse CO‚ÇÇ/O‚ÇÇ/water vapor; lenticels (stems) allow gas exchange. Gaseous waste removal via diffusion.

b. Excretory Mechanisms

Kidneys

Main vertebrate organs. Structure: Cortex/medulla/pelvis; nephron is functional unit.

Detailed nephron function (human/mammal example):

  • Glomerulus + Bowman's capsule: Ultrafiltration ‚Üí glomerular filtrate (water, urea, glucose, salts).
  • Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT): Reabsorbs ~65-80% (glucose, amino acids, Na‚Å∫, water osmotically).
  • Loop of Henle: Countercurrent multiplier ‚Üí descending limb (water out), ascending limb (Na‚Å∫/Cl‚Åª out) ‚Üí hyperosmotic medulla.
  • Distal convoluted tubule (DCT) + collecting duct: Hormone-regulated (aldosterone: Na‚Å∫ reabsorption; ADH: water reabsorption via aquaporins); secretion of H‚Å∫/wastes.

‚Üí Urine formation; maintains blood volume, pressure, pH, electrolytes.

Diseases & treatments: Kidney stones (lithotripsy/surgery); CKD/AKI (dialysis/transplant); infections (antibiotics).

Lungs

Diseases & treatments: COPD/asthma (inhalers, steroids); pneumonia (antibiotics, ventilation).

Comparison: Excretion in Fish vs Birds

Fish (e.g., bony): Ammonotelic (ammonia waste, toxic but diluted). Freshwater: dilute urine + salt uptake; marine: concentrated urine + gill salt excretion. Gills aid ion/ammonia exchange.

Birds: Uricotelic (uric acid paste, conserves water). Kidneys produce hypertonic urine; no bladder; cloaca precipitates uric acid. Adapted for arid/flight (water retention).

Similarities: Kidney-based filtration/reabsorption; habitat-linked osmoregulation.

Excretion in Earthworm

Nephridia (paired/segment): Septal, integumentary, pharyngeal types.

Mechanism: Coelomic fluid enters nephrostome ‚Üí reabsorption in tubule ‚Üí dilute urine (ammonia/urea) via nephridiopores.

Functions: Waste removal + osmotic balance (prevents dehydration in moist soil). Chloragogen cells aid waste storage/conversion.

Osmoregulation in Amphibians

Dual life (aquatic larva ‚Üí terrestrial adult).

In water: Permeable skin absorbs excess water ‚Üí dilute urine; active salt uptake via skin/gills.

On land: Risk dehydration ‚Üí concentrated urine (ADH-like); mucus reduces evaporation; behavioral (burrowing/nocturnal).

Ureotelic (urea waste).

Osmoregulation in Reptiles

Fully terrestrial/adapted to arid/marine habitats; minimize water loss.

Key adaptations:

  • Thick, impermeable skin ‚Üí reduces evaporative loss.
  • Uricotelic excretion ‚Üí uric acid (insoluble, semi-solid paste) excreted with minimal water via cloaca (no bladder in most).
  • Kidneys: Metanephric; limited hyperosmotic urine production (no mammalian loop of Henle); rely on uric acid precipitation.
  • Extrarenal salt glands (marine/desert species): Nasal (lizards/snakes), lingual (crocodiles), orbital (sea turtles) ‚Üí excrete concentrated NaCl solution (up to 4-5x plasma) with little water. Eliminate excess salts (e.g., seawater).
  • Behavioral: Nocturnal, burrowing, reduced heat activity.

Overall: Efficient water/salt balance; salt glands vital for marine forms.
