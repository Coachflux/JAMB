(a) Limit of a Function

What is it?
Imagine x is walking closer and closer to a number “a”. The limit is the value your function f(x) is heading towards (getting very close to) when x is extremely close to “a”. We write it as lim_{x→a} f(x).

Easy ways to find it (JAMB style):

  • Direct substitution – Just plug in the value of x = a. If you get a number, that’s the answer.
  • If you get 0/0 (indeterminate), factor and cancel common terms.
  • If there is √, sometimes multiply by conjugate (top and bottom).

Simple classroom examples first:
lim_{x→3} (2x + 5) = 2(3) + 5 = 11 → just plug in.
lim_{x→2} (x² - 4)/(x - 2) = 0/0 → factor: (x-2)(x+2)/(x-2) = x+2 → plug x=2 → 4.

That’s all you need for JAMB limits! Now the 10 examples.

10 JAMB Examples on Limits with Solutions

JAMB 1992: Evaluate lim_{x→2} \frac{(x-2)(x^2 + 3x - 2)}{x^2 - 4}
Step 1: Plug x=2 → 0/0 indeterminate.
Step 2: Denominator = x² - 4 = (x-2)(x+2).
Step 3: Numerator = (x-2)(x² + 3x - 2).
Step 4: Cancel (x-2).
Step 5: lim_{x→2} \frac{x^2 + 3x - 2}{x+2} = \frac{4 + 6 - 2}{2+2} = 8/4 = 2.
Answer: 2
JAMB 2021: Find lim_{x→1} \frac{x^3 + 6x - 7}{x-1}
Step 1: Plug x=1 → 0/0.
Step 2: Use synthetic division for numerator x³ + 0x² + 6x - 7 divided by (x-1).
Step 3: 1 | 1 0 6 -7
| 1 1 7
----------
1 1 7 0
Step 4: Quotient = x² + x + 7.
Step 5: Limit = 1² + 1 + 7 = 9.
Answer: 9
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3}
Step 1: Plug x=3 → 0/0.
Step 2: Numerator = (x-3)(x+3).
Step 3: Cancel (x-3).
Step 4: lim_{x→3} (x + 3) = 3 + 3 = 6.
Answer: 6
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→2} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{x^2 - 2x}
Step 1: Plug x=2 → 0/0.
Step 2: Numerator = (x+6)(x-2), denominator = x(x-2).
Step 3: Cancel (x-2).
Step 4: lim_{x→2} (x+6)/x = (2+6)/2 = 8/2 = 4.
Answer: 4
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→1} \frac{1 - x}{x^2 - 3x + 2}
Step 1: Plug x=1 → 0/0.
Step 2: Numerator = 1 - x = -(x-1).
Step 3: Denominator = (x-1)(x-2).
Step 4: Cancel (x-1): -1/(x-2).
Step 5: lim_{x→1} -1/(x-2) = -1/(1-2) = -1/(-1) = 1.
Answer: 1
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→0} \frac{x^2 + 5x}{x}
Step 1: Simplify = x + 5 (for x≠0).
Step 2: lim_{x→0} (x + 5) = 5.
Answer: 5
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→-1} \frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 1}
Step 1: Plug x=-1 → 0/0.
Step 2: Denominator = (x-1)(x+1).
Step 3: Cancel (x+1).
Step 4: lim_{x→-1} 1/(x-1) = 1/(-1-1) = 1/(-2) = -1/2.
Answer: -1/2
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→4} \frac{x^2 - 16}{x - 4}
Step 1: 0/0.
Step 2: Numerator (x-4)(x+4).
Step 3: Cancel (x-4).
Step 4: x+4 at x=4 = 8.
Answer: 8
Typical JAMB (product form): lim_{x→2} (x^3 - 2x^2 + 6x - 12)(x - 2)
Step 1: Plug x=2: first part 8 - 8 + 12 - 12 = 0, times 0 = 0.
Step 2: Limit = 0 (direct, no indeterminate).
Answer: 0
Typical JAMB: lim_{x→5} \frac{x^2 - 25}{x - 5}
Step 1: 0/0.
Step 2: (x-5)(x+5)/(x-5) = x+5.
Step 3: At x=5 = 10.
Answer: 10

Tip: Always check if you can plug in first. If 0/0, factor immediately!

(b) Differentiation of Explicit Algebraic and Simple Trigonometrical Functions

What is differentiation?
It is finding the “derivative” (written dy/dx or f'(x)). It tells you the slope or rate of change at any point.

Super simple rules you MUST know (JAMB only uses these):

  • Algebraic (power rule): y = x^n → dy/dx = n x^{n-1}
  • Constant → derivative = 0
  • Sum/difference → differentiate each term
  • Product rule (for two things multiplied): (uv)' = u'v + uv'
  • Quotient not common in simple JAMB.
  • Chain rule (for inside function): derivative of outside × derivative of inside.
  • Trigonometric (must memorise exactly):
    d(sin x)/dx = cos x
    d(cos x)/dx = -sin x
    d(tan x)/dx = sec² x

Examples to understand first:
y = 3x² + 5x - 7 → dy/dx = 6x + 5
y = sin x → dy/dx = cos x
y = 5 cos(2x) → chain rule: 5 × (-sin 2x) × 2 = -10 sin 2x

10 JAMB Examples on Differentiation with Solutions

JAMB 2008: Differentiate sin x - x cos x
Step 1: Derivative of sin x = cos x.
Step 2: Derivative of x cos x (product rule): 1·cos x + x·(-sin x) = cos x - x sin x.
Step 3: Total: cos x - (cos x - x sin x) = cos x - cos x + x sin x = x sin x.
Answer: x sin x
JAMB 2004: Differentiate \frac{x^7 - x^5}{x^4}
Step 1: Simplify first: x^7 / x^4 - x^5 / x^4 = x^3 - x.
Step 2: Derivative = 3x² - 1.
Answer: 3x² - 1
JAMB-style: If y = 2x²(2x - 1), find dy/dx at x = -1
Step 1: Expand: 2x² · 2x - 2x² · 1 = 4x³ - 2x².
Step 2: dy/dx = 12x² - 4x.
Step 3: At x=-1: 12(1) - 4(-1) = 12 + 4 = 16.
Answer: 16
JAMB-style: Differentiate (2 + 3x)(1 - x)
Step 1: Expand: 2(1 - x) + 3x(1 - x) = 2 - 2x + 3x - 3x² = 2 + x - 3x².
Step 2: dy/dx = 1 - 6x.
Answer: 1 - 6x
JAMB-style: If y = 3 sin(-4x), find dy/dx
Step 1: Chain rule: derivative of sin(u) = cos u × du/dx, u = -4x.
Step 2: 3 cos(-4x) × (-4) = -12 cos(-4x).
Step 3: cos(-4x) = cos 4x (even function).
Answer: -12 cos 4x
JAMB-style: Differentiate y = sin²(5x)
Step 1: Let u = sin 5x, y = u².
Step 2: dy/du = 2u.
Step 3: du/dx = 5 cos 5x.
Step 4: dy/dx = 2 sin 5x × 5 cos 5x = 10 sin 5x cos 5x.
Answer: 10 sin 5x cos 5x
JAMB-style: y = x² - 1/x, find dy/dx
Step 1: 1/x = x^{-1}.
Step 2: dy/dx = 2x - (-1)x^{-2} = 2x + 1/x².
Answer: 2x + 1/x²
JAMB-style: Differentiate y = 3 cos(x/3) at x = 3π/2
Step 1: Chain: 3 (-sin(x/3)) × (1/3) = - sin(x/3).
Step 2: At x = 3π/2: - sin((3π/2)/3) = - sin(π/2) = -1.
Answer: -1
JAMB-style: Differentiate tan x
Step 1: Direct formula: sec² x.
Answer: sec² x
JAMB-style: If y = 4x³ + 2 sin x - 5 cos x, find dy/dx
Step 1: d(4x³) = 12x².
Step 2: d(2 sin x) = 2 cos x.
Step 3: d(-5 cos x) = -5 (-sin x) = 5 sin x.
Step 4: Total: 12x² + 2 cos x + 5 sin x.
Answer: 12x² + 2 cos x + 5 sin x

Tip: Always simplify algebraic expressions first (makes life easy!). For trig, just replace sin → cos, cos → -sin, tan → sec². Chain rule only when there is “inside” function (like sin(2x)).
