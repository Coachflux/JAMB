1️⃣ TRIGONOMETRIC RATIOS

Core Ratios (SOH CAH TOA) 📐

💡 SOH CAH TOA:
Sin = Opposite/Hypotenuse | Cos = Adjacent/Hypotenuse | Tan = Opposite/Adjacent
Reciprocal Ratios:
csc θ = 1sin θ | sec θ = 1cos θ | cot θ = 1tan θ

🔢 Example 1: Finding Ratios

Opposite = 3cm, Adjacent = 4cm. Find sin θ, cos θ, tan θ.

Step 1: Find hypotenuse
hyp = √(3² + 4²) = √25 = 5cm

Step 2: Calculate ratios
sin θ = 35 = 0.6
cos θ = 45 = 0.8
tan θ = 34 = 0.75

3️⃣ ELEVATION & DEPRESSION

Angles of Elevation & Depression 📏

Elevation: Angle UP from horizontal to object
Depression: Angle DOWN from horizontal to object
Key: Angle of elevation = Angle of depression (alternate angles)

🔢 Example 3: Angle of Elevation

From 50m from base of tower, elevation to top is 30°. Find height.

tan 30° = height50

height = 50 × tan 30° = 50 × √33 = 50√33 ≈ 28.87m

🔢 Example 4: Angle of Depression

From top of 100m cliff, depression to boat is 45°. How far is boat from base?

Since depression = 45°, elevation from boat = 45°

tan 45° = 100distance
1 = 100distance
distance = 100m
4️⃣ BEARINGS

True & Compass Bearings 🧭

Compass True Bearing
NθE θ°
NθW 360° - θ
SθE 180° - θ
SθW 180° + θ

🔢 Example 5: Bearings Problem

Ship sails 20km on bearing 060°, then 15km on bearing 150°. Find distance from start.

First leg (060° = N60°E):
North: 20 cos 60° = 20 × 0.5 = 10km N
East: 20 sin 60° = 20 × √32 = 10√3 km E

Second leg (150° = S30°E):
South: 15 cos 30° = 15 × √32 = 7.5√3 km S
East: 15 sin 30° = 15 × 0.5 = 7.5km E

Total displacement:
N-S: 10 - 7.5√3 ≈ -2.99km (2.99km S)
E-W: 10√3 + 7.5 ≈ 24.82km E

d = √[(2.99)² + (24.82)²] ≈ 25km
5️⃣ TRIGONOMETRIC IDENTITIES

Fundamental Identities 🆔

Pythagorean:
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

Quotient:
tan θ = sin θcos θ | cot θ = cos θsin θ

Compound Angle Formulas

sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B
cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B
tan(A ± B) = tan A ± tan B1 ∓ tan A tan B

Double Angle Formulas

sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A
cos 2A = cos²A - sin²A = 2cos²A - 1 = 1 - 2sin²A
tan 2A = 2 tan A1 - tan²A

🔢 Example 6: Proving Identity

Prove: 1 - cos 2θsin 2θ = tan θ

LHS: 1 - cos 2θsin 2θ

Using cos 2θ = 1 - 2sin²θ:
= 1 - (1 - 2sin²θ)2 sin θ cos θ
= 2 sin²θ2 sin θ cos θ
= sin θcos θ = tan θ = RHS

🔢 Example 7: Using Compound Angles

Find sin 75° without calculator.

sin 75° = sin(45° + 30°)
= sin 45° cos 30° + cos 45° sin 30°
= √22 · √32 + √22 · 12
= √64 + √24 = √6 + √24
6️⃣ SINE & COSINE RULES

When to Use 📐

Rule Use When Formula
Sine Rule ASA, AAS, SSA asin A = bsin B = csin C
Cosine Rule SAS, SSS a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos A
Area Two sides + included angle 12ab sin C

🔢 Example 8: Sine Rule

In ΔABC, A = 30°, B = 45°, a = 10cm. Find side b.

asin A = bsin B

10sin 30° = bsin 45°
100.5 = b√22
20 = 2b√2
b = 10√2 ≈ 14.14cm

🔢 Example 9: Cosine Rule

In ΔABC, a = 5cm, b = 7cm, C = 60°. Find side c.

c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C
c² = 25 + 49 - 2(5)(7)cos 60°
c² = 74 - 70(0.5) = 74 - 35 = 39
c = √39 ≈ 6.24cm

🔢 Example 10: Area Calculation

Find area of triangle with sides 8cm and 10cm, included angle 30°.

Area = 12 × 8 × 10 × sin 30°
= 12 × 80 × 0.5 = 20cm²
7️⃣ TRIGONOMETRIC EQUATIONS

General Solutions 📊

Equation General Solution (degrees)
sin x = sin α x = 180°n + (-1)ⁿα
cos x = cos α x = 360°n ± α
tan x = tan α x = 180°n + α

🔢 Example 11: Basic Equation

Solve for 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°: 2 sin x - 1 = 0

2 sin x = 1
sin x = 12

Reference: sin⁻¹(12) = 30°

sin positive: Quadrants I and II
x = 30° or x = 180° - 30° = 150°
x = 30°, 150°

🔢 Example 12: Quadratic Trig Equation

Solve: 2 cos²x - 3 cos x + 1 = 0 for 0° ≤ x ≤ 360°

Let y = cos x:
2y² - 3y + 1 = 0
(2y - 1)(y - 1) = 0

y = 12 or y = 1

Case 1: cos x = 12 → x = 60°, 300°
Case 2: cos x = 1 → x = 0°, 360°
x = 0°, 60°, 300°, 360°

🎯 JAMB EXAM TIPS

4. Rationalize denominators containing surds

5. Show all working - JAMB awards method marks
