📚 Chapter 16: Gas Laws

🎯 Learning Objectives: Understand Boyle's law, Charles' law, Pressure law, absolute zero, general gas equation, ideal gas equation, and Van der Waals equation.

1️⃣ Boyle's Law (Pressure-Volume) 🫧

Statement: The pressure of a given mass of gas is inversely proportional to its volume when temperature is kept constant.

Mathematical Expression:
P ∝ 1/V   or   PV = constant

Working Formula:
P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
  • Temperature must remain constant (isothermal process)
  • Graph of P vs V is a hyperbola
  • Graph of P vs 1/V is a straight line through origin

2️⃣ Charles' Law (Volume-Temperature) 🌡️

Statement: The volume of a given mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature when pressure is kept constant.

Mathematical Expression:
V ∝ T   or   V/T = constant

Working Formula:
V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

Important: Temperature must be in Kelvin (K)
Conversion: K = °C + 273

3️⃣ Pressure Law (Gay-Lussac's Law) 🔥

Statement: The pressure of a given mass of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature when volume is kept constant.

Mathematical Expression:
P ∝ T   or   P/T = constant

Working Formula:
P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂

4️⃣ Absolute Zero of Temperature ❄️

  • Definition: The theoretical temperature at which a gas would have zero volume and zero pressure
  • Value: 0 K = -273°C (or -273.15°C precisely)
  • Significance: Lowest possible temperature; molecular motion ceases

5️⃣ General Gas Equation (Combined Gas Law) 🔄

Combining all three laws for a fixed mass of gas:

General Gas Equation:
P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂

Used when pressure, volume, AND temperature all change

6️⃣ Ideal Gas Equation ⚛️

For n moles of gas:

PV = nRT

Where:
• P = Pressure (Pa or N/m²)
• V = Volume (m³)
• n = Number of moles
• R = Universal gas constant = 8.314 J/(mol·K) or 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K)
• T = Temperature (K)

Alternative form: PV = (m/M)RT
Where m = mass of gas, M = molar mass

7️⃣ Van der Waals Equation (Real Gases) 📊

Corrects the ideal gas equation for real gas behavior:

(P + an²/V²)(V - nb) = nRT

Where:
• a = correction for intermolecular forces
• b = correction for volume of gas molecules

🔢 7 Essential Calculations for JAMB

Calculation 1: Boyle's Law Application

Question: A gas occupies 200 cm³ at 760 mmHg. What will be its volume if pressure increases to 950 mmHg at constant temperature?

Given:
P₁ = 760 mmHg, V₁ = 200 cm³
P₂ = 950 mmHg, V₂ = ?

Using P₁V₁ = P₂V₂:
760 × 200 = 950 × V₂
V₂ = (760 × 200)/950
V₂ = 152,000/950
Answer: V₂ = 160 cm³

Calculation 2: Charles' Law Application

Question: A gas occupies 500 cm³ at 27°C. At what temperature will it occupy 600 cm³ at constant pressure?

Given:
V₁ = 500 cm³, T₁ = 27°C = 300 K
V₂ = 600 cm³, T₂ = ?

Using V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂:
500/300 = 600/T₂
T₂ = (600 × 300)/500
T₂ = 360 K = 360 - 273
Answer: 87°C (or 360 K)

Calculation 3: Pressure Law Application

Question: A gas exerts 80 cmHg at 27°C. What will be its pressure at 127°C if volume is constant?

Given:
P₁ = 80 cmHg, T₁ = 27°C = 300 K
T₂ = 127°C = 400 K, P₂ = ?

Using P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂:
80/300 = P₂/400
P₂ = (80 × 400)/300
P₂ = 32,000/300
Answer: 106.67 cmHg

Calculation 4: General Gas Equation

Question: A gas occupies 300 cm³ at 27°C and 750 mmHg. Calculate its volume at -23°C and 600 mmHg.

Given:
P₁ = 750 mmHg, V₁ = 300 cm³, T₁ = 300 K
P₂ = 600 mmHg, T₂ = -23°C = 250 K, V₂ = ?

Using P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂:
(750 × 300)/300 = (600 × V₂)/250
750 = 600V₂/250
750 × 250 = 600V₂
V₂ = 187,500/600
Answer: 312.5 cm³

Calculation 5: Ideal Gas Equation (STP)

Question: Calculate the volume occupied by 2 moles of ideal gas at STP (273 K, 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa).

Given:
n = 2 mol, T = 273 K
P = 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa, R = 8.314 J/(mol·K)

Using PV = nRT:
V = nRT/P
V = (2 × 8.314 × 273)/(1.01 × 10⁵)
V = 4,539.44/101,000
Answer: 0.0449 m³ ≈ 44.9 dm³

Calculation 6: Finding Mass Using Ideal Gas

Question: What is the mass of O₂ in a 10 dm³ container at 25°C and 200 kPa? (M = 32 g/mol)

Given:
V = 10 dm³ = 0.01 m³, T = 298 K
P = 200 kPa = 200,000 Pa, M = 32 g/mol

Using PV = (m/M)RT:
m = PVM/(RT)
m = (200,000 × 0.01 × 32)/(8.314 × 298)
m = 64,000/2,477.57
Answer: 25.83 g

Calculation 7: Absolute Zero Determination

Question: A gas occupies 400 cm³ at 0°C. If volume decreases by 1.5 cm³ per 1°C drop, find absolute zero.

Given:
At 0°C (273 K), V = 400 cm³
Rate of decrease = 1.5 cm³/°C

For zero volume:
Temperature drop = 400/1.5 = 266.67°C
Absolute zero = 0°C - 266.67°C

(Note: Theoretical value is -273°C; this is experimental approximation)
Answer: ≈ -267°C or 6 K

📝 JAMB Exam Tips

  • Always convert temperature to Kelvin before calculations
  • Check units: Pressure (Pa, atm, mmHg, cmHg), Volume (m³, dm³, cm³)
  • STP: 273 K and 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa (or 760 mmHg)
  • Real gases behave like ideal gases at low pressure & high temperature
  • Van der Waals equation is mainly qualitative for JAMB

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting to convert °C to K (add 273!)
  • ❌ Mixing up pressure units (mmHg vs Pa vs atm)
  • ❌ Using wrong formula when multiple variables change
  • ❌ Confusing absolute zero (-273°C) with 0°C
