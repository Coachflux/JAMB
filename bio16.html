– JAMB Topic 3: Natural Habitats Made Super Easy

1. What are Natural Habitats?

A habitat is the natural home or environment where an organism lives and obtains everything it needs (food, water, shelter, mates).

JAMB divides habitats into two main types:

  • Aquatic (water-based)
  • Terrestrial / Arboreal (land-based or tree-based)

2. Aquatic Habitats

Types of Aquatic Habitats

  • Freshwater: ponds, streams, lakes
  • Marine / Brackish: seashores, mangrove swamps

Common Organisms & Their Adaptations

Organism Habitat Key Adaptive Features
Water lily / Water hyacinth Ponds, lakes Floating leaves with air spaces, stomata on upper surface
Tilapia / Catfish Streams, ponds Gills for oxygen extraction, streamlined body
Crabs / Mudskippers Mangrove swamps Strong legs for walking on mud
Red mangrove Mangrove swamps Prop roots / pneumatophores

3. Terrestrial / Arboreal Habitats

Types

  • Tree-tops (arboreal)
  • Dry grassy field (savanna)
  • Burrow / hole

Common Organisms & Their Adaptations

Organism Habitat Key Adaptive Features
Oil palm / Iroko tree Tree-tops Tall trunk, broad leaves
Monkey / Parrot Tree-tops Grasping hands/feet
Elephant grass Savanna Deep roots
Grasshopper / Antelope Savanna Camouflage colour
Earthworm / Termite Burrow Moist skin
