Organic Compounds

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry

1. What Are Organic Compounds?

Organic compounds are compounds of carbon (except a few like CO₂, CO, carbonates).

Examples

  • Methane
  • Ethanol
  • Benzene
  • Glucose
  • Proteins

Why Carbon Forms Many Compounds

Carbon forms millions of compounds because of:

  • Tetravalency: Carbon forms 4 covalent bonds. Example: CH₄
  • Catenation: Carbon atoms form chains or rings. Example: Straight, branched, ring structures

5. Empirical & Molecular Formula

Empirical formula: simplest atom ratio. Example: CH₂O

Molecular formula = Empirical × n. Example: (CH₂O) × 6 = C₆H₁₂O₆

6. Aliphatic Hydrocarbons

Open-chain hydrocarbons: Alkanes, Alkenes, Alkynes.

Alkanes

  • Saturated hydrocarbons
  • General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂
  • Examples: CH₄, C₂H₆, C₃H₈, C₄H₁₀

Properties & Reactions

  • Physical: low members → gases, medium → liquids, high → solids
  • Insoluble in water, low melting/boiling points
  • Chemical: Substitution (e.g., CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl)
  • Isomerism: structural isomers up to 6 carbons

Petroleum & Petrochemicals

  • Fractional distillation products: Gas, Petrol, Kerosene, Diesel, Lubricating oil, Bitumen
  • Cracking: heavy → light hydrocarbons
  • Reforming: straight → branched chains
  • Petrochemicals: Plastics, Fertilizers, Detergents, Synthetic fibres
  • Octane number: fuel quality indicator

Alkenes

  • Unsaturated, double bond, CₙH₂ₙ
  • Addition reactions, polymerization (plastic bags, bottles)
  • Test: decolourize bromine water
  • Synthetic rubber: vulcanization improves elasticity & heat resistance

Alkynes

  • Triple bond, CₙH₂ₙ₋₂
  • Ethyne from CaC₂ + H₂O → C₂H₂ + Ca(OH)₂
  • Burns with luminous flame, used in oxy-acetylene welding

Aromatic Hydrocarbons

  • Benzene (C₆H₆): stable, aromatic smell
  • Substitution reactions, uses: plastics, dyes, drugs, detergents

Alkanols (Alcohols)

  • Contain –OH, example: Ethanol
  • Types: Primary, Secondary, Tertiary
  • Production: fermentation or hydration of ethene
  • Uses: fuel, solvent, antiseptic, drinks
  • Lucas test distinguishes types

Alkanals & Alkanones

  • Alkanals: –CHO (Ethanal)
  • Alkanones: –CO– (Propanone)
  • Tollens test: aldehydes → silver mirror, ketones → no reaction

Alkanoic Acids & Esters

  • –COOH group, e.g., Ethanoic acid
  • Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
  • Esterification: Acid + Alcohol → Ester + Water (Ethyl ethanoate)
  • Natural esters → fats/oils; saponification → soap & glycerol
  • Margarine: hydrogenated vegetable oils

Amines

  • –NH₂ group, primary: CH₃NH₂, secondary: (CH₃)₂NH, tertiary: (CH₃)₃N
  • Uses: drugs, dyes, plastics

Carbohydrates

  • General formula: Cₙ(H₂O)ₙ
  • Monosaccharides: Glucose, Fructose
  • Disaccharides: Sucrose, Maltose
  • Polysaccharides: Starch, Cellulose
  • Tests: Benedict's → red precipitate, Fehling's → brick red
  • Hydrolysis: Starch → Glucose
  • Uses: alcohol, pharmaceuticals, textiles

Proteins

  • Made from amino acids
  • Hydrolysis → amino acids
  • Tests: Biuret → purple, Ninhydrin → blue/purple, Millon → red, Xanthoproteic → yellow
  • Enzymes: catalysts (Amylase, Pepsin)

Polymers

  • Natural: rubber, starch, cellulose, proteins
  • Synthetic: Nylon, Polythene, PVC
  • Polymerization: Addition (Ethene → Polythene), Condensation (Nylon formation)
  • Thermoplastics: remouldable (PVC), Thermosetting: cannot remould (Bakelite)
