1️⃣ PROPERTIES OF ANGLES AND LINES

Fundamental Angle Relationships 📏

Relationship Definition Rule
Angles on straight line Adjacent angles on a straight line x + y = 180°
Angles at a point Angles meeting at a point Sum = 360°
Vertically opposite Angles opposite intersecting lines ∠A = ∠C, ∠B = ∠D
Complementary Two angles adding to 90° A + B = 90°
Supplementary Two angles adding to 180° A + B = 180°

Parallel Lines & Transversals 🔄

💡 Look for "AB || CD" in diagrams - it activates alternate/corresponding angle rules!
Angle Pair Property Example
Corresponding Equal ∠1 = ∠5, ∠2 = ∠6
Alternate Equal ∠3 = ∠5, ∠4 = ∠6
Interior Sum to 180° ∠3 + ∠6 = 180°

🔢 Worked Example 1: Parallel Lines

Problem: AB || CD, EF is a transversal. If ∠AGE = 70°, find ∠GHD.

A-------E-------B / / C----F--------D
Step 1: ∠AGE and ∠EGB are on straight line
∠EGB = 180° - 70° = 110°

Step 2: ∠EGB and ∠GHD are corresponding angles
Therefore: ∠GHD = 110°
2️⃣ POLYGONS

Triangle Properties 🔺

Angle Sum: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
Exterior Angle: Exterior = Sum of opposite interior angles
Exterior Sum: Always 360°

Types of Triangles:

  • Scalene: All sides/angles different
  • Isosceles: Two equal sides, two equal base angles
  • Equilateral: All sides equal, all angles = 60°
  • Right-angled: One angle = 90° (Pythagoras: a² + b² = c²)

🔢 Worked Example 2: Triangle Angles

Problem: Angles are in ratio 2:3:4. Find the largest angle.

Let angles be 2x, 3x, and 4x

Sum: 2x + 3x + 4x = 180°
9x = 180°
x = 20°

Largest angle = 4x = 80°
Answer: 80°

Quadrilaterals 📊

Quadrilateral Key Properties Angle Sum
Parallelogram Opposite sides parallel & equal; opposite angles equal 360°
Rectangle All angles = 90°; diagonals equal 360°
Square All sides equal; all angles = 90° 360°
Rhombus All sides equal; diagonals bisect at 90° 360°
Trapezium One pair of parallel sides 360°
Kite Two pairs of adjacent sides equal 360°

General Polygons 🛑

Sum of Interior Angles: (n - 2) × 180°
Each Interior Angle (regular): (n - 2) × 180° / n
Sum of Exterior Angles: Always 360°
Each Exterior Angle (regular): 360° / n
Number of Diagonals: n(n - 3) / 2

Where n = number of sides

🔢 Worked Example 3: Regular Polygon

Problem: Find number of sides if interior angle = 156°.

Interior angle = (n - 2) × 180° / n = 156°

(n - 2) × 180 = 156n
180n - 360 = 156n
24n = 360
n = 15 sides
3️⃣ CIRCLES (Most Important!)

⚠️ JAMB Favorite Topic!

Circle theorems appear most frequently in the exam. Master these 9 theorems!

Essential Circle Theorems ⭕

Theorem Statement
1. Angle at center Center = 2 × Circumference (same arc)
2. Same segment Angles subtended by same arc are equal
3. Semicircle Angle in semicircle = 90°
4. Cyclic Quad Opposite angles sum to 180°
5. Exterior angle Exterior = Interior opposite angle
6. Tangent-radius Radius ⊥ tangent at contact point
7. Alternate segment Angle between tangent & chord = angle in alt. segment
8. Intersecting chords PA × PB = PC × PD (inside circle)
9. Secant-tangent PT² = PA × PB (outside circle)

🔢 Worked Example 4: Circle Theorem 1

Problem: Chord AB subtends 70° at circumference. Find angle at center.

Using Theorem 1: Center = 2 × Circumference
Angle at center = 2 × 70° = 140°

🔢 Worked Example 5: Cyclic Quadrilateral

Problem: In cyclic quad ABCD, ∠A = 85° and ∠B = 95°. Find ∠C and ∠D.

Opposite angles sum to 180° (Theorem 4)

∠A + ∠C = 180°
85° + ∠C = 180°
∠C = 95°

∠B + ∠D = 180°
95° + ∠D = 180°
∠D = 85°

🔢 Worked Example 6: Intersecting Chords

Problem: Chords AB and CD intersect at P. PA = 4cm, PB = 9cm, PC = 6cm. Find PD.

Using Theorem 8: PA × PB = PC × PD
4 × 9 = 6 × PD
36 = 6 × PD
PD = 6 cm
Answer: PD = 6 cm

🎯 JAMB Exam Strategy

1. Master circle theorems - highest frequency

2. Draw diagrams - sketch every figure

3. Label unknowns - use x, y for angles

5. Watch for parallel lines - activates alternate/corresponding rules

6. Regular polygons: n = 360°/(exterior angle) for speed

Practice Questions:

Q1: Parallel lines cut by transversal, one angle = 125°. Find x.

Answer: 55° (supplementary) or 125° (corresponding)

Q2: Regular polygon with interior angle 162°. How many sides?

Answer: n = 20 sides

Q3: Chords intersect: segments 3cm, 8cm, 4cm, and x cm. Find x.

Answer: 3 × 8 = 4 × x → x = 6 cm
