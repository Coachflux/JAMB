📘 Chapter 11: Liquids at Rest

Overview: This chapter deals with the behavior of stationary liquids, focusing on density, upthrust, buoyancy, and the principles that explain why objects float or sink. Essential for understanding ship design, hydrometers, and submarines.

Topic 1: Density of Solids and Liquids 🧱

Definition

Density (ρ) is defined as mass per unit volume of a substance.

ρ = m/V

Where:
ρ = density (kg/m³ or g/cm³)
m = mass (kg or g)
V = volume (m³ or cm³)

Key Points

  • SI unit: kg/m³ or g/cm³ (1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³)
  • Density is an intrinsic property — doesn't depend on amount
  • Water density = 1 g/cm³ or 1000 kg/m³ (your reference point!)

Determining Density

For Solids For Liquids
• Measure mass using balance
• Regular shapes: use formulas
• Irregular: water displacement
• Calculate: ρ = m/V		 • Measure empty container
• Measure container + liquid
• Subtract to get liquid mass
• Measure volume, calculate ρ

Topic 2: Relative Density (R.D.) 📊

Definition

Relative Density (Specific Gravity) is the ratio of density of substance to density of water.

R.D. = ρ(substance)/ρ(water)

OR

R.D. = Mass of substance/Mass of equal volume of water

Key Properties:

  • No units — pure ratio
  • For water: R.D. = 1
  • R.D. < 1 → floats in water
  • R.D. > 1 → sinks in water
  • Measured using hydrometer
ρ(substance) = R.D. × ρ(water)

Topic 3: Upthrust (Buoyant Force) ⬆️

Definition

Upthrust is the upward force exerted by a fluid on a body immersed in it.

Causes of Upthrust

  1. Pressure increases with depth (P = ρgh)
  2. Bottom of object experiences greater pressure than top
  3. Pressure difference creates net upward force
U = ρ(fluid) × V(displaced) × g

Where:
U = upthrust (N)
ρ(fluid) = density of fluid (kg/m³)
V(displaced) = volume displaced (m³)
g = 10 m/s² (or 9.8 m/s²)

Topic 4: Archimedes' Principle 🏛️

"When a body is completely or partially immersed in a fluid, it experiences an upthrust equal to the weight of the fluid displaced."

Upthrust = Weight of fluid displaced = ρ(fluid) × V(displaced) × g

Experimental Verification

  1. Weigh object in air → W(air)
  2. Weigh object immersed in water → W(apparent)
  3. Upthrust = W(air) - W(apparent)
  4. Collect & weigh displaced water
  5. Verify: Upthrust = Weight of displaced water ✓

Topic 5: Law of Floatation ⚖️

"A body floats in a fluid when its weight is equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces."

Conditions for Floatation

Condition Result
Weight < Upthrust (max) Body floats
Weight = Upthrust Fully immersed (neutral)
Weight > Upthrust Body sinks
For floating body:
m(body) × g = ρ(fluid) × V(submerged) × g

Fraction submerged = R.D. of body/R.D. of fluid

Example: Ice (R.D. ≈ 0.92) in water:

  • Fraction submerged = 0.92/1.0 = 0.92 (92%)
  • 8% remains above water (the "tip of the iceberg"!)

Applications 🚢

1. Ships

  • Steel has R.D. > 1, but ships have hollow spaces
  • Overall density < water density
  • Displaces large volume → large upthrust
  • Plimsoll lines indicate safe loading

2. Hydrometers

  • Measures relative density of liquids
  • Weighted bulb + graduated stem
  • Floats deeper in less dense, higher in denser
  • Uses: battery acid, milk purity, alcohol content

3. Submarines

  • Ballast tanks fill with water → sink
  • Pump out water → rise
  • Neutral buoyancy: weight = upthrust

4. Hot Air Balloons

  • Hot air less dense than cold air
  • Total weight < weight of air displaced → rises

🧮 9 Worked Examples for JAMB

Example 1: Basic Upthrust & Density

A solid weighs 0.50 N in air and 0.40 N when completely immersed in water. Calculate: (a) Upthrust (b) Volume (c) Density (d) R.D.

Solution:
(a) Upthrust = 0.50 - 0.40 = 0.10 N

(b) V = U/(ρ×g) = 0.10/(1000×10) = 1.0 × 10⁻⁵ m³ = 10 cm³

(c) m = 0.50/10 = 0.05 kg = 50 g
ρ = 50g/10cm³ = 5.0 g/cm³

(d) R.D. = 5.0/1.0 = 5.0

Example 2: Floating Wood

A piece of wood (volume 100 cm³, density 0.6 g/cm³) floats in water. What volume remains above water?

Solution:
Fraction submerged = 0.6/1.0 = 0.6
Volume submerged = 0.6 × 100 = 60 cm³
Volume above = 100 - 60 = 40 cm³

Example 3: Object in Unknown Liquid

An object weighs 2.5 N in air and 2.0 N in liquid of density 800 kg/m³. Find its density.

Solution:
Upthrust = 2.5 - 2.0 = 0.5 N
V = 0.5/(800×10) = 6.25 × 10⁻⁵ m³
m = 2.5/10 = 0.25 kg
ρ = 0.25/(6.25×10⁻⁵) = 4000 kg/m³

Example 4: Hydrometer Problem

A hydrometer (mass 25g) floats in water with 5cm stem above surface (stem area 0.5cm²). How much stem is above in liquid of R.D. 1.2?

Solution:
Vol stem above water = 5 × 0.5 = 2.5 cm³
25g = (V(bulb) + 2.5) × 1 × g
V(bulb) = 22.5 cm³

In liquid R.D. 1.2:
Submerged vol = 25/1.2 = 20.83 cm³
Stem submerged = (20.83 - 22.5) → bulb alone sufficient
Stem above ≈ 13 cm (floats higher!)

Example 5: Finding R.D. of Liquid

A solid weighs 10.0 N in air, 8.0 N in water, and 9.0 N in another liquid. Find the liquid's R.D.

Solution:
Upthrust in water = 10.0 - 8.0 = 2.0 N
Upthrust in liquid = 10.0 - 9.0 = 1.0 N

R.D. = U(liquid)/U(water) = 1.0/2.0 = 0.5

Example 6: Tension in Suspended Object

A metal cube (side 5cm, R.D. 8.0) is suspended with half volume in oil (R.D. 0.8). Find thread tension.

Solution:
V = 125 cm³ = 125 × 10⁻⁶ m³
m = 8000 × 125×10⁻⁶ = 1.0 kg
Weight = 1.0 × 10 = 10 N

V immersed = 62.5 cm³ = 62.5 × 10⁻⁶ m³
Upthrust = 800 × 62.5×10⁻⁶ × 10 = 0.5 N

Tension = Weight - Upthrust = 10 - 0.5 = 9.5 N

Example 7: Maximum Load for Boat

A boat (volume 20 m³, mass 8000 kg). Calculate: (a) Maximum mass to float safely (b) Volume displaced when fully loaded.

Solution:
Max upthrust = 1000 × 20 × 10 = 200,000 N
Weight of boat = 8000 × 10 = 80,000 N
Max load = 200,000 - 80,000 = 120,000 N

(a) Max mass = 120,000/10 = 12,000 kg
(b) Volume displaced = 20 m³ (fully submerged)

Example 8: Iceberg Volume

An iceberg (density 920 kg/m³) has 2000 m³ above sea water. Find total volume. (Sea water = 1030 kg/m³)

Solution:
Fraction submerged = 920/1030 = 0.893
Fraction above = 1 - 0.893 = 0.107

0.107 × V = 2000
V = 2000/0.107 = 18,692 m³ ≈ 18,700 m³

Example 9: Finding Liquid R.D.

A solid weighs 0.080 N in air and 0.060 N in liquid. R.D. of solid is 4.0. Find R.D. of liquid.

Solution:
ρ(solid) = 4000 kg/m³
m = 0.080/10 = 0.008 kg = 8g
V = 0.008/4000 = 2 × 10⁻⁶ m³ = 2 cm³

Upthrust = 0.080 - 0.060 = 0.020 N
ρ(liquid) = 0.020/(2×10⁻⁶×10) = 1000 kg/m³
R.D. = 1000/1000 = 1.0

  • Remember: R.D. < 1 floats, R.D. > 1 sinks
