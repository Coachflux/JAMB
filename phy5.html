Equilibrium of Forces

A body is in equilibrium if it has zero net force (no translation) and zero net torque/moment (no rotation). This applies to particles (point masses) and rigid bodies.

(a) Equilibrium of Particles

Step 1: Equilibrium of coplanar forces - Forces in one plane. Conditions: ΣF_x = 0 (horizontal), ΣF_y = 0 (vertical).

Step 2: Triangle and polygon of forces - In equilibrium, vectors drawn head-to-tail form a closed triangle (3 forces) or closed polygon (more forces).

Step 3: Lami’s theorem - For three concurrent coplanar forces in equilibrium:
F₁ / sin θ₁ = F₂ / sin θ₂ = F₃ / sin θ₃
(where θ are opposite angles).

10 JAMB-Style Examples for (a) with Full Solutions

1. Which of the following conditions must hold for a body acted upon by a system of three coplanar forces in equilibrium?
I. All three forces must be concurrent.
II. The upward force equals the downward force.
III. The algebraic sum of moments about any point is zero.
A. I and II only B. I and III only C. II and III only D. I, II and III
Step 1: For three forces, they must be concurrent (meet at a point) to balance.
Step 2: Up/down balance is part of net force zero, but not complete.
Step 3: Sum of moments zero for no rotation.
Step 4: I and III are key, II is partial.
Step 5: Matches option B.
Answer: B. I and III only
2. Three forces act on a particle in equilibrium. Two are 30 N and 40 N at right angles. The magnitude of the third force is
A. 10 N B. 50 N C. 70 N D. 25 N
Step 1: Resultant of 30 N and 40 N = √(30² + 40²) = √(900 + 1600) = √2500 = 50 N.
Step 2: Third force = equilibrant = 50 N (opposite to resultant).
Step 3: Matches option B.
Answer: B. 50 N

(b) Principles of Moments

Step 1: Moment of a force - M = F × d (d = perpendicular distance from pivot). Unit: N m. Clockwise/anticlockwise.

Step 2: Moment of a couple (torque) - Two equal opposite parallel forces. Torque = F × separation.

Step 3: Applications - Levers, beam balances, see-saw.

10 JAMB-Style Examples for (b) with Full Solutions

1. The moment of a force of 50 N about a point is 200 N m. The perpendicular distance is

Step 1: M = F × d.

Step 3: d = 4 m.
Step 4: Matches option A.
Answer: A. 4 m

(c) Conditions for Equilibrium of Rigid Bodies under Parallel and Non-Parallel Forces

Step 1: Resolution/composition - F_x = F cos θ, F_y = F sin θ. Resultant R = √(ΣF_x² + ΣF_y²).

Step 2: Resultant and equilibrant - Resultant replaces all forces. Equilibrant = -resultant.

(d) Centre of Gravity and Stability

Step 1: Centre of gravity (CG) - Point where weight acts (for uniform body, geometric centre).

Step 2: Stable equilibrium - Slight displacement raises CG → returns (e.g., cone on base, low CG).

Step 3: Unstable equilibrium - Slight displacement lowers CG → falls away (e.g., cone on tip, high CG).

Step 4: Neutral equilibrium - CG height unchanged → stays displaced (e.g., ball on flat surface).
