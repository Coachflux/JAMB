CHEMISTRY

Metals & Their Compounds

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry

1. General Properties of Metals

Metals are elements that lose electrons easily to form positive ions called cations.

Physical Properties

  • Shiny (lustrous)
  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • Malleable (can be beaten into sheets)
  • Ductile (drawn into wires)
  • High melting and boiling points
  • Mostly solid at room temperature (except mercury)

Chemical Properties

  • React with oxygen to form metal oxides
  • React with water to produce hydroxides and hydrogen
  • React with acids to produce salt + hydrogen gas
  • More reactive metals displace less reactive metals

2. Alkali Metals (Group 1)

  • Lithium (Li)
  • Sodium (Na)
  • Potassium (K)

Characteristics:

  • Very reactive
  • Soft metals
  • Low density
  • React violently with water
  • Stored in oil or kerosene

3. Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH)

Also called Caustic soda.

Production

Produced by electrolysis of brine (NaCl solution).

  • Sodium hydroxide
  • Hydrogen gas
  • Chlorine gas

Uses

  • Soap manufacture
  • Paper production
  • Textile processing
  • Petroleum refining

4. Sodium Trioxocarbonate (IV) – Na₂CO₃

Common name: Washing soda.

Production (Solvay Process)

  • Sodium chloride
  • Ammonia
  • Carbon dioxide
  • Limestone

Uses

  • Glass manufacture
  • Soap production
  • Water softening
  • Paper manufacture

5. Sodium Hydrogen Trioxocarbonate (Baking Soda)

  • White crystalline powder
  • Releases CO₂ when heated

Uses

  • Baking powder
  • Fire extinguishers
  • Medicine (antacid)

6. Sodium Chloride (NaCl)

Common salt obtained from sea water or rock salt.

Uses

  • Food seasoning
  • Food preservation
  • Raw material for chemicals

Test for Sodium Ion

Flame test → Bright yellow flame

7. Alkaline Earth Metals (Group 2)

  • Calcium
  • Magnesium
  • Barium

8. Calcium Compounds

Calcium Oxide (Quicklime)

  • Prepared by heating limestone
  • White solid
  • Reacts with water vigorously

Calcium Hydroxide (Slaked Lime)

  • Used in whitewashing
  • Water treatment
  • Bleaching powder manufacture

Calcium Carbonate

Found in:

  • Limestone
  • Chalk
  • Marble

9. Aluminium

Extraction

Obtained from bauxite ore using:

  • Bayer Process (purification)
  • Hall-Héroult Process (electrolysis)

Properties

  • Light metal
  • Corrosion resistant
  • Good conductor

Uses

  • Aircraft manufacture
  • Cooking utensils
  • Electrical cables
  • Packaging foil

10. Tin (Sn)

Main ore: Cassiterite (SnO₂)

Uses

  • Tin plating
  • Food cans
  • Alloy manufacture

11. Transition Metals

Examples: Iron, Copper, Nickel.

Characteristics

  • Variable oxidation states
  • Form coloured ions
  • Form complex ions
  • Act as catalysts

12. Iron

Ores:

  • Haematite (Fe₂O₃)
  • Magnetite (Fe₃O₄)
  • Siderite (FeCO₃)

Extracted in the Blast Furnace.

Forms of Iron

  • Cast iron
  • Wrought iron
  • Steel

13. Copper

Important ore: Copper pyrites (CuFeS₂)

Properties

  • Reddish metal
  • Good conductor
  • Malleable

Uses

  • Electrical wiring
  • Water pipes
  • Alloy production

14. Alloys

An alloy is a mixture of two or more metals.

Alloy Composition Uses
Steel Iron + Carbon Construction
Stainless Steel Iron + Chromium + Nickel Kitchen utensils
Brass Copper + Zinc Musical instruments
Bronze Copper + Tin Statues and medals
Duralumin Aluminium + Copper + Magnesium Aircraft

Advantages of Alloys

  • Stronger
  • Harder
  • More corrosion resistant
  • More durable
