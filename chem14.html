Metals lose e⁻; non-metals gain e⁻. Group 1 reacts violently with water.

1️⃣ General Properties of Metals

Metals are elements that lose electrons easily to form positive ions called cations.

Physical properties

  • Shiny (lustrous)
  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • Malleable (beaten into sheets)
  • Ductile (drawn into wires)
  • High melting and boiling points
  • Mostly solid at room temperature (except mercury)

Chemical properties

  • React with oxygen → metal oxides
  • React with water → hydroxides + hydrogen
  • React with acids → salt + hydrogen gas
  • More reactive metals displace less reactive metals
Metals are lustrous and conduct heat/electricity due to free electrons

2️⃣ Alkali Metals (Group 1)

Group 1 metals: lithium (Li), sodium (Na), potassium (K).

Characteristics

  • Very reactive
  • Soft metals
  • Low density
  • React violently with water
  • Stored in oil/kerosene
Alkali metals are soft and highly reactive, so they are stored in oil

3️⃣ Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) — Caustic Soda

Production

Produced by electrolysis of brine (NaCl solution).

Main products (chlor-alkali)

  • Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)
  • Hydrogen gas (H₂)
  • Chlorine gas (Cl₂)

Uses

  • Soap manufacture
  • Paper production
  • Textile processing
  • Petroleum refining

4️⃣ Sodium Trioxocarbonate(IV) (Na₂CO₃) — Washing Soda

Production (Solvay process)

  • Uses: NaCl + NH₃ + CO₂ + limestone (source of CO₂)

Uses

  • Glass manufacture
  • Soap production
  • Water softening
  • Paper manufacture
🧠 Quick remember

Na₂CO₃ = washing soda = water softening + glass.

5️⃣ Sodium Hydrogen Trioxocarbonate(IV) (NaHCO₃) — Baking Soda

  • White crystalline powder
  • Releases CO₂ when heated

Uses

  • Baking powder
  • Fire extinguishers
  • Medicine (antacid)

6️⃣ Sodium Chloride (NaCl) + Test for Sodium Ion

NaCl is common salt obtained from sea water or rock salt.

Uses

  • Food seasoning
  • Food preservation
  • Raw material for chemicals

Test for sodium ion (Na⁺)

Flame test

  • Bright yellow flame

7️⃣ Alkaline Earth Metals (Group 2)

Examples: calcium, magnesium, barium.

Group 2 metals are reactive and form +2 ions

8️⃣ Calcium Compounds (Very Common)

Calcium oxide (Quicklime) — CaO

  • Prepared by heating limestone (CaCO₃)
  • White solid
  • Reacts vigorously with water

Calcium hydroxide (Slaked lime) — Ca(OH)₂

  • Used in whitewashing
  • Water treatment
  • Bleaching powder manufacture

Calcium carbonate — CaCO₃

Found in

  • Limestone
  • Chalk
  • Marble

9️⃣ Aluminium (Al)

Extraction (from bauxite)

  • Bayer process: purification
  • Hall–Héroult process: electrolysis

Properties

  • Light metal
  • Corrosion resistant
  • Good conductor

Uses

  • Aircraft manufacture
  • Cooking utensils
  • Electrical cables
  • Packaging foil
Aluminium is extracted from bauxite via purification + electrolysis

🔟 Tin (Sn)

Main ore: Cassiterite (SnO₂).

Uses

  • Tin plating
  • Food cans
  • Alloy manufacture

1️⃣1️⃣ Transition Metals

Examples: iron (Fe), copper (Cu), nickel (Ni).

Characteristics

  • Variable oxidation states
  • Form coloured ions
  • Form complex ions
  • Act as catalysts

1️⃣2️⃣ Iron (Fe)

Ores

  • Haematite (Fe₂O₃)
  • Magnetite (Fe₃O₄)
  • Siderite (FeCO₃)

Iron is extracted in the blast furnace.

Forms of iron

  • Cast iron
  • Wrought iron
  • Steel
Blast furnace is used for extraction of iron from its ores

1️⃣3️⃣ Copper (Cu)

Important ore: Copper pyrites (CuFeS₂).

Properties

  • Reddish metal
  • Good conductor
  • Malleable

Uses

  • Electrical wiring
  • Water pipes
  • Alloy production
Copper is widely used for electrical wiring because it conducts well

1️⃣4️⃣ Alloys

An alloy is a mixture of two or more metals.

Alloy Composition Main uses
Steel Iron + carbon Construction
Stainless steel Iron + chromium + nickel Kitchen utensils
Brass Copper + zinc Musical instruments
Bronze Copper + tin Statues and medals
Duralumin Aluminium + copper + magnesium Aircraft
