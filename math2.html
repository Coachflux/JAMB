(a) Fractions and decimals

Key rules

  • Fractions: Add/subtract → same denominator (LCD). Multiply → numerators × numerators, denominators × denominators. Divide → multiply by reciprocal.
  • Decimals: Add/subtract → align decimal points. Multiply → ignore decimals first then count total decimal places. Divide → make divisor whole number by moving decimal.
  • Fraction ↔ decimal: Divide numerator by denominator (or place over 10, 100, etc. and simplify).

10 Examples with solutions

Example 1: ½ + ⅓
Step 1: Find LCD of 2 and 3 = 6
Step 2: ½ = 3/6, ⅓ = 2/6
Step 3: 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6
Answer: 5/6
Example 2: ¾ – ⅕
Step 1: LCD of 4 and 5 = 20
Step 2: ¾ = 15/20, ⅕ = 4/20
Step 3: 15/20 – 4/20 = 11/20
Answer: 11/20
Example 3: ⅔ × ¾
Step 1: Multiply numerators: 2 × 3 = 6
Step 2: Multiply denominators: 3 × 4 = 12
Step 3: 6/12 = ½ (simplify by dividing by 6)
Answer: ½
Example 4: 5/6 ÷ ⅔
Step 1: Change to multiplication by reciprocal: 5/6 × 3/2
Step 2: 5×3 = 15, 6×2 = 12 → 15/12
Step 3: Simplify: 15÷3 = 5, 12÷3 = 4 → 5/4 = 1¼
Answer: 5/4 (or 1¼)
Example 5: 0.45 + 0.75
Step 1: Align decimals: 0.45 + 0.75
Step 2: Add column by column: 5+5=10 (write 0 carry 1), 4+7+1=12
Answer: 1.20
Example 6: 2.35 – 0.8
Step 1: Align: 2.35 – 0.80
Step 2: 5–0=5, 3–8 → borrow → 13–8=5, 1–0=1 (after borrow)
Answer: 1.55
Example 7: 0.3 × 0.25
Step 1: Ignore decimals: 3 × 25 = 75
Step 2: Total decimal places = 1 + 2 = 3
Step 3: 75 with 3 decimal places = 0.075
Answer: 0.075
Example 8: 1.5 ÷ 0.3
Step 1: Move decimal in divisor to make whole: 0.3 → 3 (move 1 place)
Step 2: Move decimal in dividend same places: 1.5 → 15
Step 3: 15 ÷ 3 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 9: Convert 7/8 to decimal
Step 1: 7 ÷ 8
Step 2: 8 into 70 = 8 (64), remainder 6
Step 3: 60 ÷ 8 = 7 (56), remainder 4 → 0.875
Answer: 0.875
Example 10: Convert 0.625 to fraction
Step 1: 0.625 = 625/1000
Step 2: Divide numerator & denominator by 125: 625÷125=5, 1000÷125=8
Answer: 5/8

(b) Significant figures

Key rules

  • Significant figures start from the first non-zero digit.
  • Round by looking at the next digit: ≥5 → round up.
  • Trailing zeros after decimal are significant; leading zeros are not.

10 Examples (round to the number of sig figs given)

Example 1: 3.14159 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First 3 digits: 3.14
Step 2: Next digit 1 < 5 → no round up
Answer: 3.14
Example 2: 0.002345 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First non-zero: 2.34
Step 2: Next 5 = 5 → round up
Answer: 0.00235
