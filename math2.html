1️⃣ FRACTIONS

Types of Fractions 📊

Type Definition Example
Proper Fraction Numerator < Denominator 34, 25
Improper Fraction Numerator ≥ Denominator 74, 53
Mixed Number Whole number + Proper fraction 134, 223
Equivalent Fractions Same value, different forms 12 = 24 = 36

Operations with Fractions

➕ Addition & Subtraction (Same Denominator)

ac + bc = a+bc

🔢 Example 1: 38 + 58

Solution:
= 3+58
= 88
= 1
Answer: 1

➕ Addition & Subtraction (Different Denominators)

⚡ Method: Find LCM of denominators first

🔢 Example 2: 23 + 14

Solution:
LCM of 3 and 4 = 12

= 2×412 + 1×312
= 812 + 312
= 1112
Answer: 1112

✖️ Multiplication

ab × cd = a×cb×d

🔢 Example 3: 25 × 34

Solution:
= 2×35×4
= 620
= 310 (simplified)
Answer: 310

➗ Division (Multiply by Reciprocal)

ab ÷ cd = ab × dc

🔢 Example 4: 34 ÷ 25

Solution:
= 34 × 52
= 158
= 178
Answer: 178

Converting Mixed to Improper Fractions

abc = (a×c) + bc

🔢 Example 5: Convert 235 to improper fraction

Solution:
= (2×5) + 35
= 10 + 35
= 135
Answer: 135

🔢 Example 6: Convert 58 to decimal

Solution:
5 ÷ 8 = 0.625

Converting Decimals to Fractions 📝

Use place value (tenths, hundredths, thousandths).

Examples:

• 0.3 = 310

• 0.25 = 25100 = 14

• 0.125 = 1251000 = 18

➕ Addition/Subtraction: Align decimal points

2.5 × 1.2 = 3.00 = 3

6.25 ÷ 0.5 = 62.5 ÷ 5 = 12.5

3️⃣ APPROXIMATIONS & SIGNIFICANT FIGURES

Rounding to Decimal Places 📍

Number To 1 d.p. To 2 d.p. To 3 d.p.
3.14159 3.1 3.14 3.142
2.71828 2.7 2.72 2.718

⚡ Rounding Rule

Look at the digit after the place you're rounding to:

• If it's 5 or more → round UP

• If it's less than 5 → round DOWN

Significant Figures (s.f.) 🔢

Rules:

1. All non-zero digits are significant

2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant

3. Leading zeros are NOT significant

4. Trailing zeros after decimal ARE significant

Number 1 s.f. 2 s.f. 3 s.f.
45678 50000 46000 45700
0.00456 0.005 0.0046 0.00456
3.050 3 3.1 3.05

% Decimal Divide by 100

🔢 Example 8: Conversion Examples

• 34 = 34 × 100 = 75%

• 0.35 = 0.35 × 100 = 35%

Percentage Calculations

Finding a Percentage of a Quantity 📊

Percentage100 × Whole = Part

🔢 Example 9: What is 15% of 80?

Solution:
= 15100 × 80
= 0.15 × 80
= 12
Answer: 12

Percentage Increase/Decrease 📈📉

Increase: Original × (1 + Rate100)
Decrease: Original × (1 - Rate100)

🔢 Example 10: Increase 200 by 25%

Solution:
= 200 × 1.25
= 250
Answer: 250

🔢 Example 11: Decrease 80 by 20%

Solution:
= 80 × 0.80
= 64
Answer: 64

Finding Original Amount (Reverse Percentage) 🔄

💡 If a price increased by 20% to ₦120, find original:
Original = 1201.20 = ₦100

Profit and Loss 💰

Profit % = SP - CPCP × 100

Loss % = CP - SPCP × 100

🔢 Example 12: Profit Calculation

A trader bought goods for ₦5000 and sold for ₦6500

Solution:
Profit % = 6500 - 50005000 × 100
= 15005000 × 100
= 30%
Answer: 30%
5️⃣ RATIOS AND PROPORTIONS

Simplifying Ratios 📐

Express in lowest terms by dividing by HCF.

🔢 Example 13: Simplify 24:36

Solution:
HCF = 12
Ratio = 2412 : 3612
= 2:3
Answer: 2:3

Dividing in a Given Ratio 🥧

🔢 Example 14: Divide ₦600 in ratio 2:3

Solution:
Total parts = 2 + 3 = 5

First share = 25 × 600 = ₦240
Second share = 35 × 600 = ₦360
Answer: ₦240 and ₦360

Direct Proportion ➡️

If a ∝ b, then a₁b₁ = a₂b₂

🔢 Example 15: Direct Proportion

If 5 books cost ₦250, what do 8 books cost?

Solution:
5250 = 8x

x = 250 × 85
x = ₦400
Answer: ₦400

Answer: 4.0

Solution: 800,000 × (1 - 0.15) = 800,000 × 0.85 = ₦680,000

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

  1. Always simplify fractions to lowest terms in final answers
  2. Check units — ensure consistency before calculating
  3. For approximation questions, identify the required precision first
  4. In percentage problems, identify what is the "base" (100% value)
  5. Practice converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages quickly

Quick Checklist for Chapter 2:

  • Find LCM for different denominator operations
  • Remember: dividing by fraction = multiply by reciprocal
  • Count decimal places carefully in multiplication
  • Leading zeros are NOT significant figures
  • For reverse percentages, divide by (1 ± rate)
  • In ratios, always find total parts first
