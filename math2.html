2. Fractions, Decimals, Approximations and Percentages

This is an important foundation topic in WAEC/JAMB Mathematics. I will teach each subtopic clearly with rules, formulas, and tips, then give exactly 10 worked examples per subtopic (a)–(h) with full step-by-step solutions. All examples use real-life style (especially ₦ for Nigeria context). Practice them in order — they get slightly harder.

(a) Fractions and decimals

Key rules

  • Fractions: Add/subtract → same denominator (LCD). Multiply → numerators × numerators, denominators × denominators. Divide → multiply by reciprocal.
  • Decimals: Add/subtract → align decimal points. Multiply → ignore decimals first then count total decimal places. Divide → make divisor whole number by moving decimal.
  • Fraction ↔ decimal: Divide numerator by denominator.

10 Examples with solutions

Example 1: ½ + ⅓
Step 1: Find LCD of 2 and 3 = 6
Step 2: ½ = 3/6
Step 3: ⅓ = 2/6
Step 4: 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6
Answer: 5/6
Example 2: ¾ – ⅕
Step 1: LCD of 4 and 5 = 20
Step 2: ¾ = 15/20
Step 3: ⅕ = 4/20
Step 4: 15/20 – 4/20 = 11/20
Answer: 11/20
Example 3: ⅔ × ¾
Step 1: Multiply numerators: 2 × 3 = 6
Step 2: Multiply denominators: 3 × 4 = 12
Step 3: 6/12 = ½ (divide numerator and denominator by 6)
Answer: ½
Example 4: 5/6 ÷ ⅔
Step 1: Change to multiplication by reciprocal: 5/6 × 3/2
Step 2: Numerators: 5 × 3 = 15
Step 3: Denominators: 6 × 2 = 12
Step 4: 15/12 = 5/4 (divide by 3)
Answer: 5/4 (or 1¼)
Example 5: 0.45 + 0.75
Step 1: Align decimals
0.45
+ 0.75
Step 2: Hundredths: 5+5=10 (write 0, carry 1)
Step 3: Tenths: 4+7+1=12 (write 2, carry 1)
Step 4: Carry 1 becomes the units digit
Answer: 1.20
Example 6: 2.35 – 0.8
Step 1: Align decimals: 2.35 – 0.80
Step 2: Hundredths: 5 – 0 = 5
Step 3: Tenths: 3 – 8 → borrow from 2 → 13 – 8 = 5, units become 1
Step 4: Units: 1 – 0 = 1
Answer: 1.55
Example 7: 0.3 × 0.25
Step 1: Remove decimals: 3 × 25 = 75
Step 2: Count total decimal places: 1 (0.3) + 2 (0.25) = 3 places
Step 3: Place decimal 3 places from right in 75 → 0.075
Answer: 0.075
Example 8: 1.5 ÷ 0.3
Step 1: Make divisor whole: move decimal 1 place in 0.3 → 3
Step 2: Move same places in dividend: 1.5 → 15
Step 3: 15 ÷ 3 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 9: Convert 7/8 to decimal
Step 1: 7 ÷ 8
Step 2: 8 into 70 = 8 (64), remainder 6 → 0.8
Step 3: 60 ÷ 8 = 7 (56), remainder 4 → 0.87
Step 4: 40 ÷ 8 = 5 (40), remainder 0 → 0.875
Answer: 0.875
Example 10: Convert 0.625 to fraction
Step 1: 0.625 = 625/1000
Step 2: Divide numerator and denominator by 125
Step 3: 625 ÷ 125 = 5, 1000 ÷ 125 = 8
Answer: 5/8

(b) Significant figures

Key rules

  • Significant figures start from the first non-zero digit.
  • Round by looking at the next digit: ≥5 → round up.
  • Trailing zeros after decimal are significant; leading zeros are not.

10 Examples (round to the number of sig figs given)

Example 1: 3.14159 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First three significant digits: 3.14
Step 2: Next digit is 1 < 5 → do not round up
Answer: 3.14
Example 2: 0.002345 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First non-zero digit is 2 → 2.34
Step 2: Next digit is 5 → round up 4 to 5
Answer: 0.00235
Example 3: 4567 to 2 sig figs
Step 1: First two digits: 45
Step 2: Next digit 6 ≥ 5 → round up to 46
Step 3: Write as 4600 or 4.6 × 10³
Answer: 4600 (or 4.6 × 10³)
Example 4: 12.034 to 4 sig figs
Step 1: First four significant digits: 12.03
Step 2: Next digit 4 < 5 → no round up
Answer: 12.03
Example 5: 0.0009876 to 2 sig figs
Step 1: First two non-zero digits: 9.8
Step 2: Next digit 7 ≥ 5 → round up to 9.9
Answer: 0.00099
Example 6: 500 to 1 sig fig
Step 1: First significant digit: 5
Step 2: Write as 5 × 10² (trailing zeros not significant unless specified)
Answer: 5 × 10²
Example 7: 9.8765 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First three digits: 9.87
Step 2: Next digit 6 ≥ 5 → round up 7 to 8
Answer: 9.88
Example 8: 0.05060 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First three significant digits: 5.06
Step 2: Next digit 0 < 5 → no round up
Answer: 0.0506
Example 9: 123456 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First three digits: 123
Step 2: Next digit 4 < 5 → no round up
Answer: 123000
Example 10: 1.005 to 3 sig figs
Step 1: First three digits: 1.00
Step 2: Next digit 5 → round up (but trailing zeros stay significant)
Answer: 1.00

(c) Decimal places

Key rules

  • Count only digits after the decimal point.
  • Round by looking at the next digit: ≥5 → round up.

10 Examples (round to the number of decimal places given)

Example 1: 3.14159 to 2 d.p.
Step 1: Look at third decimal digit: 1
Step 2: 1 < 5 → do not round up
Answer: 3.14
Example 2: 0.98765 to 3 d.p.
Step 1: Third decimal digit: 7
Step 2: 7 ≥ 5 → round up 6 to 7
Answer: 0.988
Example 3: 12.3456 to 1 d.p.
Step 1: First decimal digit: 3
Step 2: Next digit 4 < 5 → no round up
Answer: 12.3
Example 4: 0.00456 to 4 d.p.
Step 1: Fourth decimal digit: 5
Step 2: 5 → round up 5 to 6
Answer: 0.0046
Example 5: 7.899 to 2 d.p.
Step 1: Second decimal digit: 9
Step 2: Next digit 9 ≥ 5 → round up 9 to 10 (carry over to 8.90 → 7.90)
Answer: 7.90
Example 6: 45.6789 to 3 d.p.
Step 1: Third decimal digit: 8
Step 2: Next digit 9 ≥ 5 → round up 8 to 9
Answer: 45.679
Example 7: 0.1234 to 1 d.p.
Step 1: First decimal digit: 1
Step 2: Next digit 2 < 5 → no round up
Answer: 0.1
Example 8: 9.995 to 2 d.p.
Step 1: Second decimal digit: 9
Step 2: Next digit 5 → round up 9 to 10 (carry over → 10.00)
Answer: 10.00
Example 9: 2.005 to 2 d.p.
Step 1: Second decimal digit: 0
Step 2: Next digit 5 → round up 0 to 1
Answer: 2.01
Example 10: 0.000987 to 5 d.p.
Step 1: Fifth decimal digit: 8
Step 2: Next digit 7 ≥ 5 → round up 8 to 9
Answer: 0.00099

(d) Percentage errors

Formula

Percentage error = |Approximate value – Exact value| / Exact value × 100%

10 Examples

Example 1: Exact = 50, Approx = 52
Step 1: |52 – 50| = 2
Step 2: 2 / 50 = 0.04
Step 3: 0.04 × 100 = 4%
Answer: 4%
Example 2: Exact length 2.4 m, measured 2.35 m
Step 1: |2.35 – 2.4| = 0.05
Step 2: 0.05 / 2.4 ≈ 0.020833
Step 3: 0.020833 × 100 ≈ 2.083% → round to 2.1%
Answer: ≈2.1%
Example 3: Exact = 100, Approx = 98
Step 1: |98 – 100| = 2
Step 2: 2 / 100 = 0.02
Step 3: 0.02 × 100 = 2%
Answer: 2%
Example 4: Book costs ₦850 exactly, weighed as ₦860
Step 1: |860 – 850| = 10
Step 2: 10 / 850 ≈ 0.0117647
Step 3: 0.0117647 × 100 ≈ 1.176% → ≈1.18%
Answer: ≈1.18%
Example 5: Exact time 60 s, measured 58 s
Step 1: |58 – 60| = 2
Step 2: 2 / 60 ≈ 0.03333
Step 3: 0.03333 × 100 ≈ 3.33%
Answer: ≈3.33%
Example 6: Exact = 3.14, Approx = 3.1
Step 1: |3.1 – 3.14| = 0.04
Step 2: 0.04 / 3.14 ≈ 0.01274
Step 3: 0.01274 × 100 ≈ 1.27%
Answer: ≈1.27%
Example 7: Exact mass 250 g, measured 248 g
Step 1: |248 – 250| = 2
Step 2: 2 / 250 = 0.008
Step 3: 0.008 × 100 = 0.8%
Answer: 0.8%
Example 8: Exact = 5000, Approx = 5100
Step 1: |5100 – 5000| = 100
Step 2: 100 / 5000 = 0.02
Step 3: 0.02 × 100 = 2%
Answer: 2%
Example 9: Exact = 1.25, Approx = 1.3
Step 1: |1.3 – 1.25| = 0.05
Step 2: 0.05 / 1.25 = 0.04
Step 3: 0.04 × 100 = 4%
Answer: 4%
Example 10: Exact population 1,200,000, estimated 1,180,000
Step 1: |1,180,000 – 1,200,000| = 20,000
Step 2: 20,000 / 1,200,000 ≈ 0.016667
Step 3: 0.016667 × 100 ≈ 1.67%
Answer: ≈1.67%

(e) Simple interest

Formula

I = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100
Amount = Principal + I

10 Examples (use ₦)

Example 1: ₦10,000 at 5% for 2 years
Step 1: I = (10,000 × 5 × 2) / 100
Step 2: = 100,000 / 100 = 1,000
Interest = ₦1,000
Example 2: ₦20,000 at 8% for 3 years
Step 1: I = (20,000 × 8 × 3) / 100
Step 2: = 480,000 / 100 = 4,800
Interest = ₦4,800
Example 3: Find rate if ₦5,000 becomes ₦5,800 in 2 years
Step 1: I = 5,800 – 5,000 = 800
Step 2: R = (800 × 100) / (5,000 × 2)
Step 3: = 80,000 / 10,000 = 8%
Rate = 8%
Example 4: ₦15,000 at 6% for 18 months (1.5 years)
Step 1: I = (15,000 × 6 × 1.5) / 100
Step 2: = 135,000 / 100 = 1,350
Interest = ₦1,350
Example 5: How long for ₦8,000 at 10% to earn ₦2,000?
Step 1: T = (2,000 × 100) / (8,000 × 10)
Step 2: = 200,000 / 80,000 = 2.5 years
Time = 2.5 years
Example 6: ₦50,000 at 4% for 5 years
Step 1: I = (50,000 × 4 × 5) / 100
Step 2: = 1,000,000 / 100 = 10,000
Interest = ₦10,000
Example 7: Amount after ₦12,000 at 7% for 4 years
Step 1: I = (12,000 × 7 × 4) / 100 = 3,360
Step 2: Amount = 12,000 + 3,360
Amount = ₦15,360
Example 8: Principal that gives ₦3,000 interest at 12% in 2.5 years
Step 1: P = (3,000 × 100) / (12 × 2.5)
Step 2: = 300,000 / 30 = 10,000
Principal = ₦10,000
Example 9: ₦25,000 at 9% for 8 months (2/3 year)
Step 1: I = (25,000 × 9 × 2/3) / 100
Step 2: = (25,000 × 6) / 100 = 1,500
Interest = ₦1,500
Example 10: Find time for ₦40,000 at 5% to double (I = ₦40,000)
Step 1: T = (40,000 × 100) / (40,000 × 5)
Step 2: = 4,000,000 / 200,000 = 20 years
Time = 20 years

(f) Profit and loss percent

Formulas

Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
Loss % = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100
Selling Price = CP ± Profit/Loss

10 Examples

Example 1: CP ₦200, SP ₦250
Step 1: Profit = 250 – 200 = 50
Step 2: Profit % = (50 / 200) × 100
Answer: 25%
Example 2: CP ₦1,200, SP ₦1,080
Step 1: Loss = 1,200 – 1,080 = 120
Step 2: Loss % = (120 / 1,200) × 100
Answer: 10%
Example 3: CP ₦800, profit 15%
Step 1: Profit = (15 / 100) × 800 = 120
Step 2: SP = 800 + 120
Answer: ₦920
Example 4: SP ₦3,450, loss 8%
Step 1: Let CP = x
Step 2: x – (8/100)x = 3,450
Step 3: 0.92x = 3,450 → x = 3,450 / 0.92 = 3,750
Answer: CP = ₦3,750
Example 5: CP ₦5,000, sold at 20% profit
Step 1: Profit = 20% of 5,000 = 1,000
Step 2: SP = 5,000 + 1,000
Answer: ₦6,000
Example 6: Bought for ₦400, sold for ₦350
Step 1: Loss = 400 – 350 = 50
Step 2: Loss % = (50 / 400) × 100
Answer: 12.5%
Example 7: Profit 30% on ₦2,500
Step 1: Profit = 30% of 2,500 = 750
Step 2: SP = 2,500 + 750
Answer: ₦3,250
Example 8: Loss of ₦600 is 12%
Step 1: Loss % = Loss / CP × 100
Step 2: 12 = 600 / CP × 100
Step 3: CP = (600 × 100) / 12 = 5,000
Answer: CP = ₦5,000
Example 9: CP ₦1,500, SP ₦1,800
Step 1: Profit = 1,800 – 1,500 = 300
Step 2: Profit % = (300 / 1,500) × 100
Answer: 20%
Example 10: SP ₦9,000 after 25% loss
Step 1: SP = CP × (1 – 25/100) = CP × 0.75
Step 2: 9,000 = CP × 0.75
Step 3: CP = 9,000 / 0.75 = 12,000
Answer: CP = ₦12,000

(g) Ratio, proportion and rate

Key rules

  • Ratio a:b simplified by dividing by HCF.
  • Direct proportion: increase one → increase other.
  • Inverse proportion: increase one → decrease other.
  • Rate = quantity / time (e.g. speed = distance/time)

10 Examples

Example 1: Simplify 12:18
Step 1: Find HCF of 12 and 18 = 6
Step 2: 12÷6 = 2, 18÷6 = 3
Answer: 2:3
Example 2: Divide ₦1,200 in ratio 3:5
Step 1: Total parts = 3 + 5 = 8
Step 2: Value of 1 part = 1,200 / 8 = 150
Step 3: 3 parts = 3×150 = 450, 5 parts = 5×150 = 750
Answer: ₦450 : ₦750
Example 3: If 4 men build wall in 10 days, how long for 8 men?
Step 1: Inverse proportion: more men → less time
Step 2: 4 men → 10 days
Step 3: 8 men → (4×10)/8 = 5 days
Answer: 5 days
Example 4: 50 km in 2 hours
Step 1: Rate = distance / time
Step 2: 50 / 2 = 25
Answer: 25 km/h
Example 5: a/b = 3/5, a=12 → find b
Step 1: 12 / b = 3 / 5
Step 2: Cross multiply: 3b = 60
Step 3: b = 60 / 3 = 20
Answer: b = 20
Example 6: ₦2,400 shared 5:3
Step 1: Total parts = 5 + 3 = 8
Step 2: 1 part = 2,400 / 8 = 300
Step 3: 5 parts = 1,500, 3 parts = 900
Answer: ₦1,500 : ₦900
Example 7: 6 workers, 15 days; 10 workers?
Step 1: Inverse proportion: more workers → less time
Step 2: 6 × 15 = 90 worker-days
Step 3: Time = 90 / 10 = 9 days
Answer: 9 days
Example 8: Speed 60 km/h for 3 hours → distance?
Step 1: Distance = speed × time
Step 2: 60 × 3 = 180
Answer: 180 km
Example 9: Ratio 2:3:5, total ₦20,000
Step 1: Total parts = 2+3+5 = 10
Step 2: 1 part = 20,000 / 10 = 2,000
Step 3: Shares = 4,000 : 6,000 : 10,000
Answer: ₦4,000 : ₦6,000 : ₦10,000
Example 10: Petrol 45 litres for 300 km
Step 1: Consumption rate = litres / km
Step 2: 45 / 300 = 0.15 litres/km
Step 3: Or km/litre = 300 / 45 ≈ 6.67 km/litre
Answer: 6.67 km/litre

(h) Shares and Value Added Tax (VAT)

Nigeria note: Standard VAT rate is 7.5% on most goods/services (essentials often exempt).

Shares: Profit/dividend shared in ratio of number of shares held.

VAT = 7.5% of selling price (usually exclusive). Total = Price + VAT.

10 Examples (use ₦ and 7.5% VAT)

Example 1: VAT on ₦10,000 item
Step 1: VAT = 7.5% of 10,000 = 0.075 × 10,000
Step 2: = 750
Step 3: Total = 10,000 + 750
Answer: ₦750 VAT, Total ₦10,750
Example 2: Total paid ₦21,500 includes VAT. Find price before VAT
Step 1: Price + 7.5% VAT = 21,500
Step 2: Price × 1.075 = 21,500
Step 3: Price = 21,500 / 1.075 = 20,000
Answer: ₦20,000
Example 3: Company profit ₦50,000, A has 200 shares, B 300 (total 500)
Step 1: A’s share = 200/500 = 2/5
Step 2: A gets (2/5) × 50,000 = 20,000
Step 3: B gets (3/5) × 50,000 = 30,000
Answer: A ₦20,000, B ₦30,000
Example 4: VAT on ₦40,000 phone
Step 1: VAT = 0.075 × 40,000 = 3,000
Step 2: Total = 40,000 + 3,000
Answer: ₦3,000 VAT, Total ₦43,000
Example 5: Total capital ₦1,000,000 divided into 10,000 shares of ₦100 each. You buy 400
Step 1: Value of your shares = 400 × ₦100
Answer: ₦40,000
Example 6: Dividend ₦120,000 shared by 800 : 400 shares
Step 1: Total parts = 800 + 400 = 1,200
Step 2: Ratio 800:400 = 2:1
Step 3: 2 parts = ₦80,000, 1 part = ₦40,000
Answer: ₦80,000 and ₦40,000
Example 7: VAT-inclusive price ₦5,375 → price before VAT
Step 1: Price × 1.075 = 5,375
Step 2: Price = 5,375 / 1.075
Answer: ₦5,000
Example 8: Company issues 5,000 shares. You own 15%
Step 1: 15% of 5,000 = 0.15 × 5,000
Answer: 750 shares
Example 9: VAT on ₦8,000 service
Step 1: VAT = 0.075 × 8,000 = 600
Step 2: Total = 8,000 + 600
Answer: ₦600 VAT, Total ₦8,600
Example 10: Profit ₦90,000 shared in ratio of shares 250:350:400 (total 1,000)
Step 1: 1 part = 90,000 / 1,000 = 90
Step 2: 250 parts = 22,500
Step 3: 350 parts = 31,500
Step 4: 400 parts = 36,000
Answer: ₦22,500 : ₦31,500 : ₦36,000
