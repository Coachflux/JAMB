Quick Overview

JAMB tests grammar heavily in Lexis & Structure. Master these 7 topics and you will score 9–10/10 every year.

  • Mood (subjunctive is king)

  • Degree (comparison)

Read one section daily. Practice the 10 examples each time.

1. MOOD

Rule (Step-by-step): Mood shows the speaker’s attitude.

  • Indicative: facts/questions
  • Imperative: commands
  • Subjunctive (most tested): wishes, suggestions, “as if”, “it is time/high time”, “suggest/demand that” → uses base form of verb (be, go, have – no -s/-ed). “Were” for all subjects in unreal situations.

10 JAMB-style examples with step-by-step solutions:

The Principal suggested that the unruly student _______ expelled immediately.
A. is B. was C. be D. should be
Step 1: After “suggested that” we use subjunctive mood.
Step 2: Base form of the verb is required.
Step 3: “be” is the base form.
Answer: C. be
Maryam treats people as if she _______ better than they are.
A. has B. were C. is D. was
Step 1: “as if” + unreal situation = subjunctive.
Step 2: Use “were” for all subjects in unreal condition.
Answer: B. were
It is high time we _______ the meeting.
A. have left B. leave C. left D. had left
Step 1: “It is high time” + past subjunctive.
Step 2: Past form of “leave” = left.
Answer: C. left
It is high time you _______ visiting that friend of yours.
A. stopped B. should stop C. stop D. must stop
Step 1: “high time” requires past subjunctive.
Step 2: “stopped” is the past form.
Answer: A. stopped
If I _______ you, I would forgive the boy.
A. am B. was C. were D. be
Step 1: Unreal conditional → subjunctive “were” for all subjects.
Answer: C. were
The teacher demanded that every student _______ present at the meeting.
A. is B. was C. be D. should be
Step 1: After “demanded that” use subjunctive base form.
Answer: C. be
She wishes she _______ rich.
A. is B. was C. were D. be
Step 1: Wish + unreal situation = subjunctive “were”.
Answer: C. were
It is essential that he _______ on time.
A. is B. was C. be D. should be
Step 1: After “essential that” use subjunctive base form.
Answer: C. be
The committee recommended that the report _______ before the decision.
A. is submitted B. was submitted C. be submitted D. should submit
Step 1: After “recommended that” use subjunctive.
Answer: C. be submitted
I would rather you _______ the truth now.
A. tell B. told C. tells D. have told
Step 1: “would rather” + past subjunctive.
Answer: B. told

2. TENSE

Rule (Step-by-step): Tense shows time. Match with time words (yesterday = past, now = present, by tomorrow = future perfect). Sequence: past perfect before another past action.

10 JAMB-style examples with step-by-step solutions:

I had hardly _______ down to sleep when I heard the gunshot.
A. lay B. lied C. lain D. laid
Step 1: “had hardly” = past perfect → past participle needed.
Step 2: Lie (recline) past participle = lain.
Answer: C. lain
By 11:00 a.m., I _______ writing the test for two hours.
A. will have been B. am C. shall have been D. have been
Step 1: “By 11:00 a.m.” + duration = future perfect continuous.
Answer: A. will have been
When I entered the room, they _______ dinner.
A. are eating B. were eating C. have eaten D. had eaten
Step 1: Past simple “entered” interrupted ongoing action → past continuous.
Answer: B. were eating
She _______ to school every day.
A. go B. goes C. going D. went
Step 1: Habit + 3rd person singular = “goes”.
Answer: B. goes
Yesterday, he _______ to the market.
A. is going B. goes C. went D. has gone
Step 1: “Yesterday” = past simple.
Answer: C. went
They _______ the work before the teacher arrived.
A. finish B. finished C. had finished D. have finished
Step 1: Action completed before another past action → past perfect.
Answer: C. had finished
By next year, I _______ in this school for five years.
A. will be B. will have been C. am D. have been
Step 1: “By next year” + duration = future perfect.
Answer: B. will have been
We _______ anything to eat since morning.
A. didn’t eat B. haven’t eaten C. hadn’t eaten D. don’t eat
Step 1: “Since morning” to now = present perfect.
Answer: B. haven’t eaten
Nowadays he _______ to work by bicycle.
A. was going B. is going C. goes D. went
Step 1: “Nowadays” = current habit = present simple.
Answer: C. goes
The boys _______ when the bell rang.
A. play B. played C. were playing D. have played
Step 1: Interrupted ongoing action = past continuous.
Answer: C. were playing

3. ASPECT

Rule (Step-by-step): Aspect shows how the action is viewed (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous). Combine with tense.

10 JAMB-style examples with step-by-step solutions:

The students _______ the lesson for two hours before the break.
A. study B. studied C. have been studying D. had been studying
Step 1: Action ongoing up to a past point → past perfect continuous.
Answer: D. had been studying
She _______ the book since morning.
A. reads B. is reading C. has read D. has been reading
Step 1: Action started in past and still going = present perfect continuous.
Answer: D. has been reading
By the time we arrive, they _______ the match.
A. finish B. will finish C. will have finished D. are finishing
Step 1: Action completed before a future time = future perfect.
Answer: C. will have finished
While I _______ , the phone rang.
A. sleep B. slept C. was sleeping D. have slept
Step 1: Interrupted ongoing action = past continuous.
Answer: C. was sleeping
They _______ the project already.
A. complete B. completed C. have completed D. had completed
Step 1: Completed action with present result = present perfect.
Answer: C. have completed
He _______ in the rain for hours when we found him.
A. walks B. walked C. was walking D. had been walking
Step 1: Ongoing up to a past point = past perfect continuous.
Answer: D. had been walking
I _______ this song before.
A. hear B. heard C. have heard D. am hearing
Step 1: Experience up to now = present perfect.
Answer: C. have heard
The team _______ hard these days.
A. trains B. is training C. has trained D. had trained
Step 1: Temporary action around now = present continuous.
Answer: B. is training
She _______ the letter by tomorrow.
A. writes B. will write C. will have written D. is writing
Step 1: Completed before a future time = future perfect.
Answer: C. will have written
We _______ the news when the announcement came.
A. hear B. heard C. were hearing D. had heard
Step 1: Action completed before another past action = past perfect.
Answer: D. had heard

4. NUMBER (Subject-Verb Concord)

Rule (Step-by-step): Subject and verb must agree in number. Special cases tested heavily in JAMB.

10 JAMB-style examples with step-by-step solutions:

The farmer, with his sons, _______ millet.
A. grows B. grow C. were growing D. have been growing
Step 1: Ignore “with his sons” – main subject “farmer” singular.
Step 2: Singular verb.
Answer: A. grows
One of the ladies who _______ in the premises _______ been asked to withdraw.
A. sells/have B. sell/have C. sells/has D. sell/has
Step 1: “ladies” plural → “sell”.
Step 2: “One” singular → “has”.
Answer: D. sell/has
Either Ekaette or you _______ to go.
A. was B. are C. has D. is
Step 1: Agree with nearer subject “you” plural.
Answer: B. are
The number of accidents _______ increasing.
A. is B. are C. have D. were
Step 1: “The number of” = singular.
Answer: A. is
A number of students _______ absent today.
A. is B. are C. has D. have
Step 1: “A number of” = plural.
Answer: B. are
The committee _______ divided in their opinion.
A. is B. are C. was D. were
Step 1: Collective noun = singular.
Answer: A. is
Neither the teacher nor the students _______ happy.
A. is B. are C. was D. were
Step 1: Agree with nearer plural “students”.
Answer: B. are
Every boy and every girl _______ invited.
A. is B. are C. were D. have
Step 1: “Every” = singular.
Answer: A. is
The boys as well as their teacher _______ coming.
A. is B. are C. was D. were
Step 1: Agree with plural “boys”.
Answer: B. are
One of the schools of thought _______ selective marking.
A. suggest B. suggests C. have suggested D. are suggesting
Step 1: “One of” = singular.
Answer: B. suggests

5. DEGREE (Positive, Comparative, Superlative)

Rule (Step-by-step): Positive: as…as. Comparative: -er/more … than (two things). Superlative: -est/most … of/in/all (three or more). “Of the two” = comparative.

10 JAMB-style examples with step-by-step solutions:

Which of the two questions is _______?
A. most difficult B. the much difficult C. the most difficult D. more difficult
Step 1: “Of the two” = comparative.
Answer: D. more difficult
This is the _______ movie I have ever watched.
A. good B. better C. best D. more good
Step 1: “Ever” = superlative.
Answer: C. best
No other city is _______ Mumbai.
A. as large as B. larger than C. the largest D. largest
Step 1: Positive degree comparison.
Answer: A. as large as
She is _______ than her sister.
A. tall B. taller C. tallest D. most tall
Step 1: Two people = comparative.
Answer: B. taller
Mount Everest is the _______ mountain in the world.
A. high B. higher C. highest D. most high
Step 1: Superlative of all.
Answer: C. highest
Gold is _______ than silver.
A. expensive B. more expensive C. expensiver D. most expensive
Step 1: Long adjective = more.
Answer: B. more expensive
This book is _______ interesting than that one.
A. as B. more C. most D. the most
Step 1: Comparative.
Answer: B. more
She is one of the _______ girls in the class.
A. intelligent B. more intelligent C. most intelligent D. intelligenter
Step 1: Superlative.
Answer: C. most intelligent
No other student is _______ Riya.
A. as intelligent as B. more intelligent than C. the most intelligent D. intelligenter
Step 1: Positive degree.
Answer: A. as intelligent as
He runs _______ than his friend.
A. fast B. faster C. fastest D. most fast
Step 1: Comparative.
Answer: B. faster

6. QUESTION TAGS

Rule (Step-by-step): Positive statement → negative tag; Negative → positive tag. Same auxiliary or “do/does/did”. Special: “I am” → aren’t I?; “let’s” → shall we?

10 JAMB-style examples with step-by-step solutions:

You can travel on your own, _______?
A. can’t you B. can you C. don’t you D. won’t you
Step 1: Positive statement → negative tag.
Answer: A. can’t you
He does not seem to know how to solve the problem, _______?
A. does he B. doesn’t he C. did he D. didn’t he
Step 1: Negative statement → positive tag.
Answer: A. does he
He wrote to you, _______?
A. hadn’t he B. didn’t he C. did he D. hadn’t he
Step 1: Past simple → didn’t he.
Answer: B. didn’t he
You don’t like Mathematics, _______?
A. don’t you B. do you C. did you D. didn’t you
Step 1: Negative → positive tag.
Answer: B. do you
Students will always blame their teachers when results are bad, _______?
A. will they B. won’t they C. do they D. don’t they
Step 1: Positive future → negative tag.
Answer: B. won’t they
We ought to have visited the Governor, _______?
A. oughtn’t we B. shouldn’t we C. did we D. didn’t we
Step 1: “ought to have” → shouldn’t we.
Answer: B. shouldn’t we
One can no longer take for granted what one enjoyed, _______?
A. can one B. can’t one C. could one D. couldn’t one
Step 1: Positive → negative tag.
Answer: A. can one
Let’s go home, _______?
A. shall we B. will we C. don’t we D. aren’t we
Step 1: Special “let’s” rule = shall we.
Answer: A. shall we
You are coming tomorrow, _______?
A. aren’t you B. are you C. isn’t it D. won’t you
Step 1: Positive → negative tag.
Answer: A. aren’t you
She has finished the work, _______?
A. has she B. hasn’t she C. did she D. didn’t she
Step 1: Positive perfect → negative tag.
Answer: B. hasn’t she
