Definition of Respiration

Respiration is the process by which living organisms release energy from food (mainly glucose) in the form of ATP. It involves gaseous exchange and energy production.

Significance: Provides energy for movement (e.g., muscle contraction in humans), growth (e.g., cell division in plants), active transport (e.g., mineral uptake in roots), bioluminescence (e.g., fireflies), heat production (e.g., shivering in mammals), and reproduction (e.g., sperm motility).
