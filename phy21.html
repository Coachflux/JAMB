📚 Chapter 21: Propagation of Sound Wave

🎯 Learning Objectives: Understand the nature and properties of sound waves, calculate speed of sound in different media, apply echo formulas, analyze factors affecting sound propagation, and solve related numerical problems.

1️⃣ Nature and Properties of Sound Waves 🔊

What is Sound?

Sound is a mechanical wave that requires a material medium (solid, liquid, or gas) to travel. It cannot travel through a vacuum.

Production of Sound

Sound is produced by vibrating objects. When an object vibrates, it creates:

  • Compressions (C): Regions of high pressure where particles are crowded together
  • Rarefactions (R): Regions of low pressure where particles are spread apart

Key Properties of Sound

Property Description
Longitudinal wave Particles vibrate parallel to direction of wave propagation
Requires medium Cannot travel through vacuum
Speed varies Fastest in solids, slowest in gases
Frequency range Audible: 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz

2️⃣ Speed of Sound in Different Media ⚡

Formulas

In solids:
v = √(E/ρ)
Where E = Young's modulus, ρ = density

In gases:
v = √(γP/ρ) = √(γRT/M)
Where:
• γ = ratio of specific heats (adiabatic index)
• P = pressure
• ρ = density
• R = gas constant
• T = absolute temperature (K)
• M = molar mass

Standard Values (at 25°C)

Medium Speed (m/s)
Air 340
Water 1,480
Steel 5,000
Glass 4,500
Aluminum 5,100

Temperature Effect

Speed of sound in air increases with temperature:

v = 331 + 0.6T
Where T = temperature in °C

3️⃣ Echo and Reverberation 🔁

Echo

An echo is a reflected sound heard distinctly after the original sound.

  • Condition for echo: Reflecting surface must be at least 17m away
  • Reason: Time interval between original sound and echo must be > 0.1s for human ear to distinguish
Echo Formula:
v = 2d/t

Where:
• v = speed of sound
• d = distance to reflecting surface
• t = time interval
• Factor of 2: Sound travels to surface AND back

Reverberation

Reverberation is the persistence of sound due to multiple reflections in an enclosed space. Unlike echo, the reflections arrive too quickly to be distinguished as separate sounds.

4️⃣ Factors Affecting Speed of Sound 🌡️

Factor Effect
Temperature Speed increases with temperature
Medium density Generally faster in denser media (for same elasticity)
Humidity Speed slightly increases with humidity
Wind Speed increases in the direction of wind

5️⃣ Applications of Sound Propagation 🎯

  1. SONAR (Sound Navigation and Ranging) - used to detect underwater objects
  2. Ultrasound scanning - medical imaging
  3. Echo sounding - depth measurement
  4. Architectural acoustics - designing concert halls

🔢 5 Essential Calculations for JAMB

Calculation 1: Speed of Sound Using Echo

Question: A man stands 85m from a cliff and fires a gun. He hears the echo after 0.5 seconds. Calculate the speed of sound.

Using v = 2d/t:
v = (2 × 85)/0.5
v = 170/0.5 = 340 m/s
Answer: 340 m/s

Calculation 2: Finding Distance (Depth)

Question: A ship sends a sound signal to the seabed and receives the echo after 4 seconds. If speed of sound in water is 1,500 m/s, calculate the depth of the sea.

Using v = 2d/t:
1,500 = 2d/4
2d = 1,500 × 4 = 6,000
d = 3,000 m = 3 km
Answer: 3,000 m (or 3 km)

Calculation 3: Temperature Effect

Question: Calculate the speed of sound in air at 30°C.

Using v = 331 + 0.6T:
v = 331 + 0.6(30)
v = 331 + 18 = 349 m/s
Answer: 349 m/s

Calculation 4: Multiple Echoes (Between Two Cliffs)

Question: A man stands between two parallel cliffs. He hears the first echo after 2 seconds and the second after 3 seconds. If v = 340 m/s, find:
(a) Distance to nearer cliff
(b) Distance to farther cliff
(c) Distance between cliffs

(a) Nearer cliff: d₁ = (v × t₁)/2 = (340 × 2)/2 = 340 m

(b) Farther cliff: d₂ = (v × t₂)/2 = (340 × 3)/2 = 510 m

(c) Distance between cliffs: d₁ + d₂ = 340 + 510 = 850 m
Answer: (a) 340 m, (b) 510 m, (c) 850 m

Calculation 5: Frequency and Wavelength

Question: A sound wave has frequency 500 Hz and travels at 340 m/s. Calculate its wavelength.

Using v = fλ:
λ = v/f = 340/500 = 0.68 m
Answer: 0.68 m (or 68 cm)

📋 Quick Revision Checklist ✅

  • ✅ Sound is a longitudinal mechanical wave
  • ✅ Speed formula: v = fλ
  • ✅ Echo formula: v = 2d/t
  • ✅ Minimum distance for echo: 17m
  • ✅ Temperature formula: v = 331 + 0.6T
  • ✅ Speed order: solids > liquids > gases

📝 Practice Questions for JAMB

✅ Try These:

  1. Why can't sound travel through a vacuum?
    Answer: Sound requires a material medium; vacuum has no particles to vibrate

  2. Calculate the wavelength of a sound wave with frequency 1,000 Hz traveling at 330 m/s.
    Answer: λ = 330/1000 = 0.33 m

  3. A boy stands 680m from a wall. How long after clapping will he hear the echo? (v = 340 m/s)
    Answer: t = (2 × 680)/340 = 4 seconds

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting the factor of 2 in echo calculations (sound travels to surface AND back)
  • ❌ Using Celsius instead of Kelvin in gas formulas (when required)
  • ❌ Confusing echo (distinct reflection) with reverberation (multiple overlapping reflections)
  • ❌ Thinking sound travels faster in gases than solids (opposite is true!)

🎯 Final Tips for Success

  • Master the echo formula: v = 2d/t — this appears frequently in JAMB
  • Remember: Solids > Liquids > Gases for sound speed
  • Temperature increases speed: +0.6 m/s per °C rise
  • Minimum 17m distance needed to hear distinct echo (0.1s time delay)
  • Practice drawing compression/rarefaction diagrams for longitudinal waves
