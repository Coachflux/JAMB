🦠 1. Diseases

Definition (Disease): A condition that impairs normal body function and shows symptoms. Many endemic diseases spread faster under certain ecological conditions (warmth, water, poor sanitation).

Many tropical diseases are linked to water and sanitation (vectors breed in stagnant water)

(a) Common and Endemic Diseases (Nigeria/Tropics)

Definition (Endemic disease): A disease that is constantly present in a particular region.

Examples (JAMB Lists)

  • Malaria
  • Meningitis (cerebrospinal)
  • Schistosomiasis (Bilharzia)
  • Onchocerciasis (River blindness)
  • Typhoid fever
  • Cholera
  • Tuberculosis
  • HIV/AIDS
  • STDs (gonorrhea, syphilis)
  • Dracunculiasis (Guinea worm) — now eradicated in Nigeria
Disease Causative Agent Vector / Transmission Favorable Conditions Main Symptoms Prevention & Control
Malaria Plasmodium (esp. P. falciparum) Female Anopheles mosquito Stagnant water, rainy season, poor housing Fever, chills, headache, anemia Insecticide nets, indoor spraying, ACT drugs, drain stagnant water
Cholera Vibrio cholerae Contaminated water/food (faecal-oral) Poor sanitation, flooding, overcrowding Severe diarrhea, vomiting, dehydration Clean water, sanitation, ORS, vaccination
Schistosomiasis Schistosoma worms Snail host; skin penetration in infected water Irrigation, rivers, dams Blood in urine/stool, abdominal pain Avoid infected water, praziquantel, snail control
Onchocerciasis Onchocerca volvulus Blackfly (Simulium) Fast-flowing rivers Rash, itching, river blindness Ivermectin, blackfly control
Typhoid Salmonella typhi Contaminated food/water Poor hygiene High fever, headache, abdominal pain Safe water, hygiene, vaccination
Tuberculosis Mycobacterium tuberculosis Air droplets (cough) Overcrowding, poor ventilation Chronic cough, weight loss, blood in sputum BCG vaccine, DOTS drugs, ventilation
HIV/AIDS HIV Blood, sex, mother-to-child Unprotected sex, shared needles Opportunistic infections, weight loss Condoms, testing, ARVs, safe blood screening

💊 2. Drug Abuse

Drug abuse: Using drugs without medical reason or in the wrong way/amount.

Drug addiction: Physical/psychological dependence; withdrawal symptoms occur when stopped.

Drug abuse affects health, behavior, and society

Commonly abused drugs (JAMB list)

  • Cannabis (Indian hemp)
  • Cocaine
  • Heroin
  • Alcohol
  • Tobacco (nicotine)
  • Codeine (cough syrups)
  • Tramadol
  • Amphetamines/stimulants

Effects on health

  • Liver/kidney damage
  • Mental disorders (depression, psychosis)
  • Lung/heart problems
  • Addiction → crime, poor performance
  • Overdose → death

Prevention

  • Education/awareness
  • Counseling
  • Strict laws
  • Avoid peer pressure
  • Rehabilitation centers
🧠 Memory line:

"Abuse harms organs, addiction traps behavior."

🏭 3. Pollution and its Control

Pollution: Contamination of the environment making it harmful to living things.

Pollution can be air, water, or soil/land

Type Common Sources Pollutants Effects Control Methods
Air Vehicles, industries, burning CO, SO₂, NOₓ, particulate matter, smoke Respiratory diseases, acid rain, global warming Clean fuels, filters, laws, tree planting
Water Sewage, oil spills, chemicals, plastics Pathogens, heavy metals, oil Cholera, poisoning, eutrophication, fish death Treatment plants, no dumping, regulations
Soil/Land Pesticides, fertilizers, waste Chemicals, plastics Reduced fertility, food-chain poisoning Proper disposal, organic farming

Oil spills contaminate water and kill aquatic organisms

Sanitation and sewage

  • Importance: prevents diseases and improves community health
  • Solid waste: landfill, recycling, incineration
  • Sewage: treat in plants before safe disposal
  • Hygiene: reduces spread of cholera/typhoid

Agencies (know roles)

  • WHO: global health standards and disease control
  • UNICEF: child health, water/sanitation support
  • Red Cross (IRCS): emergency aid
  • Nigeria Ministries: health/environment policies and programs

🌱 4. Conservation of Natural Resources

Natural resources: materials from nature used by humans.

Afforestation helps restore forests and reduce erosion

Renewable
  • Forests
  • Water
  • Soil
Non-renewable
  • Minerals
  • Fossil fuels

Methods of conservation

  • Afforestation (plant trees)
  • Controlled grazing/logging
  • Protected areas
  • Recycling
  • Use alternatives (solar energy)
  • Laws against poaching

Benefits

  • Prevents desertification
  • Maintains biodiversity
  • Sustains future generations
  • Reduces erosion and flooding

Bodies responsible

TypeExamples
NigeriaNCF, Federal Ministry of Environment, National Parks

🦁 5. Game Reserves and National Parks

Purpose: protect wildlife, conserve biodiversity, support tourism, education, and research.

Protected areas help preserve endangered species and habitats

Importance

  • Preserve endangered species
  • Eco-tourism revenue
  • Research and education
  • Maintain ecological balance

Examples in Nigeria (know locations)

Gashaka-Gumti National Park Taraba/Adamawa Largest park; chimpanzees
