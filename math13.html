Coordinate Geometry Made Simple for JAMB

I will teach you everything step-by-step in very easy English, like a friend explaining to you. We use the Cartesian plane (x-y graph). Every point has coordinates (x, y).

We will cover (a), (b), (c), (d) one by one with clear formulas, why they work, and then exactly 10 JAMB-style examples with full solutions for each part. All examples are either real past JAMB/WAEC questions or very similar ones that appear every year.

(a) Midpoint and Gradient (Slope) of a Line Segment

Midpoint – The middle point of the line joining two points.
It is the average of the x-coordinates and the average of the y-coordinates.
Formula:
If A(x₁, y₁) and B(x₂, y₂), then Midpoint M = ( (x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2 )
Gradient (Slope) m – How steep the line is.
Formula:
m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)
(Rise over Run)
Positive m = line going up from left to right
Negative m = line going down
m = 0 → horizontal line
m undefined → vertical line (x = constant)

10 JAMB Examples for (a) – Midpoint & Gradient

Example 1 (Midpoint)
The midpoint of XY is (−4, −2). If X is (−8, −12), find the coordinates of Y.
Step 1: Let Y = (p, q)
Step 2: (−8 + p)/2 = −4 → −8 + p = −8 → p = 0
Step 3: (−12 + q)/2 = −2 → −12 + q = −4 → q = 8
Answer: (0, 8)
Example 2 (Midpoint)
Find the midpoint of A(−4, 6) and B(2, −8).
Step 1: x = (−4 + 2)/2 = −2/2 = −1
Step 2: y = (6 + (−8))/2 = −2/2 = −1
Answer: (−1, −1)
Example 3 (Midpoint)
P(1, 3) and Q(5, 7). Find midpoint.
Step 1: x = (1+5)/2 = 6/2 = 3
Step 2: y = (3+7)/2 = 10/2 = 5
Answer: (3, 5)
Example 4 (Gradient)
Find the gradient of the line joining P(1, 1) and Q(2, 5).
Step 1: m = (y2 − y1)/(x2 − x1) = (5 − 1)/(2 − 1) = 4/1 = 4
Answer: 4
Example 5 (Gradient)
Find gradient of line through (−2, 0) and (0, −4).
Step 1: m = (−4 − 0)/(0 − (−2)) = (−4)/2 = −2
Answer: −2
Example 6 (Gradient)
Calculate gradient of (3, −2) and (−1, 4).
Step 1: m = (4 − (−2))/(−1 − 3) = (4 + 2)/(−4) = 6/−4 = −3/2
Answer: −3/2
Example 7 (Both)
Points A(2, 3), B(5, 7). Find midpoint and gradient.
Step 1: Midpoint x = (2+5)/2 = 3.5
Step 2: Midpoint y = (3+7)/2 = 5
Step 3: Gradient m = (7−3)/(5−2) = 4/3
Answer: Midpoint (3.5, 5), Gradient 4/3
Example 8 (Gradient)
Gradient of line joining (x, 4) and (3, −2) is 3/2. Find x.
Step 1: m = (−2 − 4)/(3 − x) = 3/2
Step 2: −6/(3 − x) = 3/2
Step 3: Cross multiply: −12 = 3(3 − x)
Step 4: −12 = 9 − 3x
Step 5: −21 = −3x → x = 7
Answer: 7
Example 9 (Midpoint)
Midpoint of AB is (1, −1). If A is (−4, 6), find B.
Step 1: Let B = (p, q)
Step 2: (−4 + p)/2 = 1 → −4 + p = 2 → p = 6
Step 3: (6 + q)/2 = −1 → 6 + q = −2 → q = −8
Answer: (6, −8)
Example 10 (Gradient)
Line through (1, 2) and (4, 8). Gradient?
Step 1: m = (8 − 2)/(4 − 1) = 6/3 = 2
Answer: 2

(b) Distance Between Two Points

This is just the straight-line distance (like using ruler).
It comes from Pythagoras theorem.
Formula:
Distance d = √[ (x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)² ]

10 JAMB Examples for (b) – Distance

Example 1
Distance between P(5, 3) and Q(8, 7).
Step 1: √[(8−5)² + (7−3)²] = √[3² + 4²] = √[9 + 16] = √25 = 5 units
Answer: 5 units
Example 2
Distance between (½, ½) and (−½, −½).
Step 1: √[(−½−½)² + (−½−½)²] = √[(−1)² + (−1)²] = √[1 + 1] = √2
Answer: √2
Example 3
Points L(−1, −6) and M(−3, −5).
Step 1: √[(−3−(−1))² + (−5−(−6))²] = √[(−2)² + 1²] = √[4 + 1] = √5
Answer: √5
Example 4
Distance between (2, 5) and (5, 9).
Step 1: √[(5−2)² + (9−5)²] = √[3² + 4²] = √[9+16] = √25 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 5
Distance between (−2, −3) and (−2, 4).
Step 1: √[(−2−(−2))² + (4−(−3))²] = √[0 + 7²] = √49 = 7
Answer: 7
Example 6
Distance between (x, 1) and (3, 5) is 5. Find possible x.
Step 1: √[(3−x)² + (5−1)²] = 5
Step 2: (3−x)² + 16 = 25
Step 3: (3−x)² = 9
Step 4: 3−x = ±3
Step 5: x = 0 or x = 6
Answer: x = 0 or x = 6
Example 7
Points C(2, 2) and D(5, 6). Distance?
Step 1: √[(5−2)² + (6−2)²] = √[9+16] = √25 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 8
Distance between (0, 0) and (3, 4).
Step 1: √[3² + 4²] = √[9+16] = √25 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 9
A(1, 2), B(4, 2), C(1, 6). Length of AC?
Step 1: √[(1−1)² + (6−2)²] = √[0 + 16] = 4
Answer: 4
Example 10
Distance between (x, 3) and (−x, 2) is 5. Find x.
Step 1: √[(−x−x)² + (2−3)²] = 5
Step 2: √[(−2x)² + (−1)²] = 5
Step 3: 4x² + 1 = 25
Step 4: 4x² = 24
Step 5: x² = 6 → x = ±√6
Answer: x = ±√6

(c) Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

Parallel lines → same gradient (m₁ = m₂)
Perpendicular lines → product of gradients = −1 (m₁ × m₂ = −1)
(Vertical and horizontal are always perpendicular)

10 JAMB Examples for (c)

Example 1
Gradient of line perpendicular to 3x + 2y + 1 = 0.
Step 1: Rewrite: 2y = −3x −1 → y = −(3/2)x − 1/2 → m = −3/2
Step 2: Perp m = −1 / (−3/2) = 2/3
Answer: 2/3
Example 2
Line 3x + y = 5 is perpendicular to y = mx + 7. Find m.
Step 1: First line: y = −3x + 5 → m1 = −3
Step 2: m1 × m = −1 → −3 m = −1 → m = 1/3
Answer: 1/3
Example 3
Line through (−1, −p) and (−2, 2) is parallel to 2y + 8x − 17 = 0 (gradient = −4). Find p.
Step 1: m of given = −4 (from y = −4x + 17/2)
Step 2: m = (2 − (−p))/(−2 − (−1)) = (2 + p)/(−1) = −2 − p
Step 3: −2 − p = −4 → −p = −2 → p = 2
Answer: 2
Example 4
Two lines: y = 2x − 3 and kx − 4y + 5 = 0. Find k for parallel.
Step 1: Second line: 4y = kx + 5 → y = (k/4)x + 5/4 → m2 = k/4
Step 2: m1 = 2 = m2 → k/4 = 2 → k = 8
Answer: 8
Example 5
Line joining A(−3, 1) and B(5, y) perpendicular to y = 2x + 4. Find y.
Step 1: m given = 2, perp m = −1/2
Step 2: m AB = (y−1)/(5−(−3)) = (y−1)/8 = −1/2
Step 3: y−1 = −4 → y = −3
Answer: −3
Example 6
Are lines y = 3x + 1 and 3y = 9x − 6 parallel?
Step 1: Second: 3y = 9x − 6 → y = 3x − 2 → m=3
Step 2: First m=3, same m → parallel
Answer: Yes
Example 7
Find gradient of line perpendicular to y = −4x + 2.
Step 1: m = −4
Step 2: perp m = −1/(−4) = 1/4
Answer: 1/4
Example 8
Line L1: 3x − 2y = 4. L2 parallel to L1 through (0,0). Equation of L2?
Step 1: m L1 = 3/2 (from 2y = 3x − 4)
Step 2: L2 through (0,0): y = (3/2)x → 3x − 2y = 0
Answer: 3x − 2y = 0
Example 9
Points P(2,3), Q(4,7), R(x,11) collinear. Find x (same gradient).
Step 1: m PQ = (7−3)/(4−2) = 4/2 = 2
Step 2: m PR = (11−3)/(x−2) = 8/(x−2) = 2
Step 3: 8 = 2(x−2) → 8 = 2x − 4 → 2x = 12 → x = 6
Answer: 6
Example 10
Line through (1,2) parallel to segment joining (4,1) and (2,5).
Step 1: m segment = (5−1)/(2−4) = 4/(−2) = −2
Step 2: Parallel same m = −2
Step 3: y − 2 = −2(x − 1) → y = −2x + 4
Answer: y = −2x + 4

(d) Equations of Straight Lines

Four common forms you MUST know:
Slope-intercept form: y = mx + c (c = y-intercept)
Point-slope form: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)
Two-point form: (y − y₁)/(y₂ − y₁) = (x − x₁)/(x₂ − x₁)
General form: ax + by + c = 0

10 JAMB Examples for (d) – Equations of Lines

Example 1
Equation of line through (2, 5) with gradient −3.
Step 1: Use point-slope: y − 5 = −3(x − 2)
Step 2: y − 5 = −3x + 6
Step 3: y = −3x + 11
Answer: y = −3x + 11
Example 2
Line parallel to y = −4x + 2 passing through (2, 3).
Step 1: Same m = −4
Step 2: y − 3 = −4(x − 2)
Step 3: y − 3 = −4x + 8
Step 4: y = −4x + 11
Answer: y = −4x + 11
Example 3
Equation of line through (1, −3) and (3, 1).
Step 1: m = (1 − (−3))/(3 − 1) = 4/2 = 2
Step 2: y + 3 = 2(x − 1)
Step 3: y + 3 = 2x − 2
Step 4: y = 2x − 5
Answer: y = 2x − 5
Example 4
Find equation of line perpendicular to 2x − y = 5 passing through (4, 1).
Step 1: Given line m = 2 (from y = 2x − 5)
Step 2: Perp m = −1/2
Step 3: y − 1 = (−1/2)(x − 4)
Step 4: y − 1 = (−1/2)x + 2
Step 5: y = (−1/2)x + 3
Answer: y = (−1/2)x + 3
Example 5
Line through origin parallel to 3x − 2y = 4.
Step 1: m = 3/2 (from y = (3/2)x − 2)
Step 2: Through (0,0): y = (3/2)x
Step 3: 3x − 2y = 0
Answer: 3x − 2y = 0
Example 6
Find equation in general form: gradient 2, passes through (1, 2).
Step 1: y − 2 = 2(x − 1)
Step 2: y − 2 = 2x − 2
Step 3: y − 2x = 0
Answer: y − 2x = 0
Example 7
Line cuts x-axis at (3, 0). Gradient = 5. Equation?
Step 1: y − 0 = 5(x − 3)
Step 2: y = 5x − 15
Step 3: 5x − y − 15 = 0
Answer: 5x − y − 15 = 0
Example 8
Two-point form: through (1, 2) and (4, 8).
Step 1: (y−2)/(8−2) = (x−1)/(4−1)
Step 2: (y−2)/6 = (x−1)/3
Step 3: y−2 = 2(x−1)
Step 4: y = 2x
Answer: y = 2x
Example 9
Convert 3x − 5y = 7 to slope-intercept and state gradient.
Step 1: 5y = 3x − 7
Step 2: y = (3/5)x − 7/5
Step 3: Gradient = 3/5
Answer: y = (3/5)x − 7/5, gradient 3/5
Example 10
Find equation of line with gradient 3/2 passing through (−1, 4).
Step 1: y − 4 = (3/2)(x + 1)
Step 2: 2(y − 4) = 3(x + 1)
Step 3: 2y − 8 = 3x + 3
Step 4: 3x − 2y + 11 = 0
Answer: 3x − 2y + 11 = 0
