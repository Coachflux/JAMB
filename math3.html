3. Indices, Logarithms and Surds

Abumchukwu, this topic looks big but we will learn it slowly and easily — like you are just starting SS1. Read one example at a time. Try it on paper first, then check my steps. I will explain every part like I'm your class teacher.

(a) Laws of Indices

Very easy rules to remember

  • When multiplying same base → add powers: 2³ × 2² = 2⁵
  • When dividing same base → subtract powers: 2⁵ ÷ 2² = 2³
  • Power on power → multiply powers: (2³)² = 2⁶
  • Negative power → flip to bottom: 2⁻² = 1/4
  • Fraction power → root: 16^{1/2} = √16 = 4

10 Easy Examples

Example 1: Simplify 32^{3/5} × 2^{-2}
Step 1: 32 is 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 2^5 Step 2: So 32 raised to 3/5 becomes (2^5) raised to 3/5 Step 3: Multiply powers: 5 × 3/5 = 3 → 2^3 = 8 Step 4: Now 8 × 2^{-2} Step 5: 2^{-2} = 1 ÷ 2^2 = 1 ÷ 4 = 0.25 Step 6: 8 × 0.25 = 2
Answer: 2
Example 2: Evaluate 81^{3/4} ÷ 27^{1/3}
Step 1: 81 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 3^4 Step 2: 81 raised to 3/4 = (3^4) raised to 3/4 = 3^3 = 27 Step 3: 27 = 3 × 3 × 3 = 3^3 Step 4: 27 raised to 1/3 = 3^1 = 3 Step 5: 27 ÷ 3 = 9
Answer: 9
Example 3: Simplify (16^{1/2} × 8^{2/3}) ÷ 4^{3/2}
Step 1: 16 = 2^4 → 16^{1/2} = 2^2 = 4 Step 2: 8 = 2^3 → 8^{2/3} = 2^2 = 4 Step 3: 4 = 2^2 → 4^{3/2} = 2^3 = 8 Step 4: (4 × 4) ÷ 8 = 16 ÷ 8 = 2
Answer: 2
Example 4: Evaluate 243^{2/5}
Step 1: 243 = 3^5 (3×3×3×3×3 = 243) Step 2: (3^5) raised to 2/5 = 3^{5×2/5} = 3^2 = 9
Answer: 9
Example 5: Simplify 10^{-4} × 10^{6} × 10^{-1}
Step 1: Same base (10) so add powers: -4 + 6 + (-1) = 1 Step 2: 10^1 = 10
Answer: 10
Example 6: Evaluate (125^{2/3})^{3/2}
Step 1: 125 = 5^3 Step 2: 125 raised to 2/3 = (5^3) raised to 2/3 = 5^2 = 25 Step 3: 25 raised to 3/2 = (5^2) raised to 3/2 = 5^3 = 125
Answer: 125
Example 7: Simplify (a^5 b^{-2})^2 × a^{-3}
Step 1: Raise everything to power 2: a^{10} b^{-4} Step 2: Now multiply by a^{-3}: a^{10-3} b^{-4} = a^7 b^{-4} Step 3: Answer = a^7 / b^4
Answer: a^7 / b^4
Example 8: Evaluate 27^{-2/3}
Step 1: 27 = 3^3 Step 2: (3^3) raised to -2/3 = 3^{-2} Step 3: 3^{-2} = 1/9
Answer: 1/9
Example 9: Simplify 16^{3/4} × 4^{-1/2}
Step 1: 16 = 2^4 → 16 raised to 3/4 = 2^3 = 8 Step 2: 4 = 2^2 → 4 raised to -1/2 = 2^{-1} = 1/2 Step 3: 8 × 1/2 = 4
Answer: 4
Example 10: Express 0.000001 in index form
Step 1: Move decimal point 6 places to the right → 1 Step 2: So 1 × 10^{-6}
Answer: 10^{-6}

(b) Equations involving indices

Easy method: Change both sides to the same base, then make the powers equal and solve for x.

10 Examples with solutions

Example 1: Solve 4^x = 64
Step 1: 64 = 4 × 4 × 4 = 4^3 Step 2: So 4^x = 4^3 Step 3: Powers must be equal → x = 3
Answer: 3
Example 2: Solve 9^{x-1} = 27
Step 1: 9 = 3^2, 27 = 3^3 Step 2: So (3^2)^{x-1} = 3^3 Step 3: 3^{2(x-1)} = 3^3 Step 4: Powers equal → 2(x-1) = 3 Step 5: x-1 = 1.5 → x = 2.5 (which is 5/2)
Answer: 5/2
Example 3: Solve 2^{2x} = 8^{x-1}
Step 1: 8 = 2^3 Step 2: So 2^{2x} = (2^3)^{x-1} = 2^{3(x-1)} Step 3: Powers equal → 2x = 3x - 3 Step 4: Subtract 2x → 0 = x - 3 Step 5: x = 3
Answer: 3
Example 4: Solve 5^{x+1} = 25
Step 1: 25 = 5 × 5 = 5^2 Step 2: 5^{x+1} = 5^2 Step 3: x + 1 = 2 Step 4: x = 1
Answer: 1
Example 5: Solve 8^{2x} = 4^{x+3}
Step 1: 8 = 2^3, 4 = 2^2 Step 2: (2^3)^{2x} = (2^2)^{x+3} Step 3: 2^{6x} = 2^{2x+6} Step 4: 6x = 2x + 6 Step 5: 4x = 6 → x = 3/2 (1.5)
Answer: 3/2
Example 6: Solve 27^x = 9^{x+1}
Step 1: 27 = 3^3, 9 = 3^2 Step 2: 3^{3x} = 3^{2(x+1)} Step 3: 3x = 2x + 2 Step 4: x = 2
Answer: 2
Example 7: Solve 4^{x+1} = 2^{x+5}
Step 1: 4 = 2^2 Step 2: (2^2)^{x+1} = 2^{x+5} Step 3: 2^{2x+2} = 2^{x+5} Step 4: 2x + 2 = x + 5 Step 5: x = 3
Answer: 3
Example 8: Solve 3^{2x-1} = 81
Step 1: 81 = 3^4 Step 2: 3^{2x-1} = 3^4 Step 3: 2x - 1 = 4 Step 4: 2x = 5 Step 5: x = 5/2
Answer: 5/2
Example 9: Solve 16^x = 64
Step 1: 16 = 2^4, 64 = 2^6 Step 2: 2^{4x} = 2^6 Step 3: 4x = 6 Step 4: x = 3/2
Answer: 3/2
Example 10: Solve 7^x = 49^{x-1}
Step 1: 49 = 7^2 Step 2: 7^x = (7^2)^{x-1} = 7^{2(x-1)} Step 3: x = 2(x-1) Step 4: x = 2x - 2 Step 5: x = 2
Answer: 2

JAMB Past Questions for (b) Equations involving indices

JAMB 2010: If 27^x ÷ 81^{x+2y} = 9, and x + 4y = 0, find x, y
Step 1: 27 = 3^3, 81 = 3^4, 9 = 3^2 Step 2: 3^{3x - 4(x+2y)} = 3^2 Step 3: 3x - 4x - 8y = 2 → -x - 8y = 2 Step 4: x + 4y = 0 → x = -4y Step 5: Substitute → x = 1, y = 1/2
Answer: x=1, y=1/2
JAMB 2011: Solve 3^{2x} = 81
Step 1: 81 = 3^4 Step 2: 2x = 4 Step 3: x = 2
Answer: 2
JAMB 2015: If 4^x = 64, find x
Step 1: 64 = 4^3 Step 2: x = 3
Answer: 3

(d) Logarithm Laws

Very easy rules

  • log of multiply = add logs: log(2×3) = log 2 + log 3
  • log of divide = subtract logs: log(6/2) = log 6 - log 2
  • log of power = power × log: log(2^5) = 5 × log 2
  • log of the base itself = 1: log₂ 2 = 1
  • log of 1 = 0: log 1 = 0

10 Easy Examples

Example 1: log₃ 81
Step 1: What power of 3 gives 81? Step 2: 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 81 → 3^4 Step 3: So log₃ 81 = 4
Answer: 4
Example 2: log₁₀ 10000
Step 1: 10 × 10 × 10 × 10 = 10000 → 10^4 Step 2: log₁₀ 10000 = 4
Answer: 4
Example 3: If log₂ y = 6, find y
Step 1: log₂ y = 6 means 2 raised to 6 = y Step 2: 2^6 = 64 Step 3: y = 64
Answer: 64
Example 4: log₅ 1
Step 1: Any number raised to power 0 = 1 Step 2: So log₅ 1 = 0
Answer: 0
Example 5: log₄ 64
Step 1: 4 × 4 × 4 = 64 → 4^3 = 64 Step 2: log₄ 64 = 3
Answer: 3
Example 6: log_b (b^7)
Step 1: b raised to 7 = b^7 Step 2: So log_b (b^7) = 7
Answer: 7
Example 7: log₂ 1/8
Step 1: 1/8 = 1 ÷ 8 = 2^{-3} Step 2: log₂ (2^{-3}) = -3
Answer: -3
Example 8: log₁₀ 0.01
Step 1: 0.01 = 1/100 = 10^{-2} Step 2: log₁₀ (10^{-2}) = -2
Answer: -2
Example 9: If 5^x = 125, find x
Step 1: 125 = 5 × 5 × 5 = 5^3 Step 2: 5^x = 5^3 Step 3: x = 3
Answer: 3
Example 10: log₈ 4
Step 1: 8 = 2^3, 4 = 2^2 Step 2: 8 raised to what power = 4? Step 3: (2^3)^y = 2^2 → 3y = 2 → y = 2/3
Answer: 2/3

(g) Indices-Log Relationship and Applications

Easy connection

If b raised to x = a, then log_b a = x (and the other way round)

10 Easy Examples

Example 1: log₁₀ 2 ≈ 0.3010, log₁₀ 3 ≈ 0.4771. Find log₁₀ 24
Step 1: 24 = 8 × 3 = 2^3 × 3 Step 2: log 24 = log(2^3) + log 3 = 3 × log 2 + log 3 Step 3: 3 × 0.3010 = 0.903 Step 4: 0.903 + 0.4771 = 1.3801
Answer: 1.3801
Example 2: log₁₀ 2 = 0.3010, log₁₀ 5 = 0.6990. Find log₁₀ 0.04
Step 1: 0.04 = 4 / 100 = 2^2 / 10^2 Step 2: log 0.04 = log(2^2) - log(10^2) = 2 log 2 - 2 Step 3: 2 × 0.3010 = 0.602 Step 4: 0.602 - 2 = -1.398
Answer: -1.398
Example 3: Solve log₃ (4x – 5) = 3
Step 1: If log₃ of something = 3, then something = 3^3 = 27 Step 2: 4x – 5 = 27 Step 3: 4x = 32 Step 4: x = 8
Answer: 8
Example 4: log₁₀ 2 = 0.3010, log₁₀ 7 = 0.8451. Find log₁₀ 98
Step 1: 98 = 2 × 49 = 2 × 7^2 Step 2: log 98 = log 2 + log(7^2) = log 2 + 2 log 7 Step 3: 0.3010 + 2 × 0.8451 = 0.3010 + 1.6902 = 1.9912
Answer: 1.9912
Example 5: Solve log₂ (3x – 7) = 5
Step 1: log₂ of something = 5 → something = 2^5 = 32 Step 2: 3x – 7 = 32 Step 3: 3x = 39 Step 4: x = 13
Answer: 13
Example 6: Find number of digits in 8000
Step 1: Number of digits = floor(log₁₀ of number) + 1 Step 2: 8000 = 8 × 10^3 → log₁₀ 8000 ≈ log₁₀ 8 + 3 ≈ 0.903 + 3 = 3.903 Step 3: floor(3.903) = 3 → 3 + 1 = 4 digits
Answer: 4
Example 7: log₅ 7 = log₂ 7 / log₂ 5
Step 1: This is the change of base formula Step 2: Yes, it is true
Answer: True
Example 8: If log_b 64 = 6, find b
Step 1: log_b 64 = 6 means b^6 = 64 Step 2: 64 = 2^6 → b = 2
Answer: 2
Example 9: Evaluate log₃ 9 + log₃ 27
Step 1: log₃ 9 + log₃ 27 = log₃ (9 × 27) Step 2: 9 × 27 = 243 Step 3: 243 = 3^5 → log₃ 243 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 10: Given log₁₀ 3 ≈ 0.4771, find log₁₀ √27
Step 1: √27 = 27 raised to 1/2 = (3^3) raised to 1/2 = 3^{3/2} Step 2: log₁₀ (3^{3/2}) = (3/2) × log₁₀ 3 Step 3: 1.5 × 0.4771 ≈ 0.71565
Answer: 0.71565

(h) Surds

Easy rules

  • Take out perfect squares: √50 = √(25×2) = 5√2
  • √a × √b = √(a×b)
  • Add/subtract only same roots: 3√2 + 5√2 = 8√2
  • To remove root from bottom → multiply by conjugate (opposite sign)

10 Easy Examples

Example 1: Simplify √200
Step 1: 200 = 100 × 2 Step 2: √200 = √100 × √2 = 10√2
Answer: 10√2
Example 2: Rationalise 1 / (√7 – √2)
Step 1: Multiply top and bottom by (√7 + √2) Step 2: Bottom: (√7 – √2)(√7 + √2) = 7 – 2 = 5 Step 3: Top: 1 × (√7 + √2) Step 4: Answer = (√7 + √2)/5
Answer: (√7 + √2)/5
Example 3: Simplify 4√18 – 3√50 + √72
Step 1: √18 = 3√2 → 4 × 3√2 = 12√2 Step 2: √50 = 5√2 → 3 × 5√2 = 15√2 Step 3: √72 = 6√2 Step 4: 12√2 – 15√2 + 6√2 = 3√2
Answer: 3√2
Example 4: Rationalise (√5 + √3) / (√5 – √3)
Step 1: Multiply top and bottom by (√5 + √3) Step 2: Bottom: 5 – 3 = 2 Step 3: Top: (√5 + √3)^2 = 5 + 2√15 + 3 = 8 + 2√15 Step 4: (8 + 2√15)/2 = 4 + √15
Answer: 4 + √15
Example 5: √45 + √20 – √5
Step 1: √45 = 3√5, √20 = 2√5 Step 2: 3√5 + 2√5 – √5 = 4√5
Answer: 4√5
Example 6: (√8 + √2)²
Step 1: √8 = 2√2 Step 2: (2√2 + √2)^2 = (3√2)^2 = 9 × 2 = 18 wait — correct: 8 + 2 + 4√2 = 10 + 4√2
Answer: 10 + 4√2
Example 7: Rationalise 3 / (√6 + √3)
Step 1: Multiply by (√6 – √3) Step 2: Bottom: 6 – 3 = 3 Step 3: Top: 3(√6 – √3) Step 4: √6 – √3
Answer: √6 – √3
Example 8: Simplify √98 – √50 + √32
Step 1: √98 = 7√2, √50 = 5√2, √32 = 4√2 Step 2: 7√2 – 5√2 + 4√2 = 6√2
Answer: 6√2
Example 9: √27 × √12
Step 1: √27 × √12 = √(27 × 12) = √324 Step 2: √324 = 18
Answer: 18
Example 10: Rationalise 6 / (√10 – √6)
Step 1: Multiply by (√10 + √6) Step 2: Bottom: 10 – 6 = 4 Step 3: Top: 6(√10 + √6) Step 4: 6/4 = 3/2 → (3/2)(√10 + √6)
Answer: (3/2)(√10 + √6)
