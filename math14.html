(a) Trigonometrical Ratios of Angles

Key points — SOH-CAH-TOA for right triangles. For any angle: reference angle + CAST (All positive Q1; Sin Q2; Tan Q3; Cos Q4). Negative angle: sin(-θ) = -sinθ, cos(-θ) = cosθ, tan(-θ) = -tanθ.

Special values (memorize!):

  • 30° → sin ½, cos √3/2, tan 1/√3
  • 45° → sin = cos = 1/√2, tan 1
  • 60° → sin √3/2, cos ½, tan √3
  • 75° → sin (√6+√2)/4, cos (√6-√2)/4

10 JAMB Examples

1. sin 210° = ?
Step 1: 210° is in Q3 (180° to 270°).
Step 2: Reference angle = 210° – 180° = 30°.
Step 3: Sin is negative in Q3.
Step 4: sin 30° = ½, so sin 210° = –½.
Answer: –½
2. cos 330° = ?
Step 1: 330° is in Q4 (270° to 360°).
Step 2: Reference angle = 360° – 330° = 30°.
Step 3: Cos is positive in Q4.
Step 4: cos 30° = √3/2, so cos 330° = √3/2.
Answer: √3/2
3. tan 150° = ?
Step 1: 150° is in Q2 (90° to 180°).
Step 2: Reference angle = 180° – 150° = 30°.
Step 3: Tan is negative in Q2.
Step 4: tan 30° = 1/√3, so tan 150° = –1/√3.
Answer: –1/√3
4. sin(-120°) = ?
Step 1: Negative angle: add 360° → –120° + 360° = 240°.
Step 2: 240° in Q3, ref = 240° – 180° = 60°.
Step 3: Sin negative in Q3.
Step 4: sin 60° = √3/2, so –√3/2.
Answer: –√3/2
5. cos 495° = ?
Step 1: 495° – 360° = 135°.
Step 2: 135° in Q2, ref = 180° – 135° = 45°.
Step 3: Cos negative in Q2.
Step 4: cos 45° = 1/√2, so –1/√2.
Answer: –1/√2
6. tan 405° = ?
Step 1: 405° – 360° = 45°.
Step 2: 45° in Q1, all positive.
Step 3: tan 45° = 1.
Answer: 1
7. sin 75° = ?
Step 1: 75° = 45° + 30°.
Step 2: sin(A+B) = sinA cosB + cosA sinB.
Step 3: sin45 cos30 + cos45 sin30 = (1/√2)(√3/2) + (1/√2)(1/2) = (√6 + √2)/4.
Answer: (√6 + √2)/4
8. If sin θ = 3/5 (acute), find tan θ.
Step 1: cos θ = √(1 – sin²θ) = √(1 – 9/25) = √(16/25) = 4/5 (acute positive).
Step 2: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = (3/5)/(4/5) = 3/4.
Answer: 3/4
9. cos 300° = ?
Step 1: 300° in Q4, ref = 360° – 300° = 60°.
Step 2: Cos positive in Q4.
Step 3: cos 60° = ½.
Answer: ½
10. tan(-225°) = ?
Step 1: –225° + 360° = 135°.
Step 2: 135° in Q2, ref = 45°.
Step 3: Tan negative in Q2.
Step 4: tan 45° = 1, so –1.
Answer: –1

(b) Angles of Elevation and Depression

Key points — Elevation: looking up; depression: looking down (equal to elevation from other end). Mostly tan θ = opposite / adjacent. Draw horizontal line + right triangle.

10 JAMB Examples

1. Angle of depression from 50 m cliff to boat is 45°. Distance from base?
Step 1: tan(depression) = height / distance.
Step 2: tan 45° = 1 = 50 / d.
Step 3: d = 50 m.
Answer: 50 m
2. From 120 m away, angle of elevation to tree top is 30°. Tree height?
Step 1: tan(elevation) = height / distance.
Step 2: tan 30° = 1/√3 = h / 120.
Step 3: h = 120 / √3 = 40√3 m.
Answer: 40√3 m
3. Angle of depression from 80 m tower to ship is 60°. Distance to ship?
Step 1: tan 60° = √3 = 80 / d.
Step 2: d = 80 / √3.
Answer: 80/√3 m
4. Boy 1.6 m tall, 20 m from pole, elevation to top 60°. Pole height?
Step 1: tan 60° = √3 = h_above_eye / 20.
Step 2: h_above = 20√3.
Step 3: Total h = 20√3 + 1.6 m.
Answer: 20√3 + 1.6 m
5. From cliff top, depression to point 100 m away is 30°. Cliff height?
Step 1: tan 30° = 1/√3 = h / 100.
Step 2: h = 100 / √3 m.
Answer: 100 / √3 m
6. Elevation 45°, distance 40 m, height?
Step 1: tan 45° = 1 = h / 40.
Step 2: h = 40 m.
Answer: 40 m
7. Depression 60°, distance 50 m from base, height?
Step 1: tan 60° = √3 = h / 50.
Step 2: h = 50√3 m.
Answer: 50√3 m
8. Man 1.7 m tall sees building top at 30° elevation, 50 m away. Building height?
Step 1: tan 30° = 1/√3 = h_above / 50.
Step 2: h_above = 50/√3.
Step 3: Total = 50/√3 + 1.7 m.
Answer: 50/√3 + 1.7 m
9. From 200 m away, elevation 45°. Height?
Step 1: tan 45° = 1 = h / 200.
Step 2: h = 200 m.
Answer: 200 m
10. Depression from 120 m height is 30°. Distance?
Step 1: tan 30° = 1/√3 = 120 / d.
Step 2: d = 120√3 m.
Answer: 120√3 m

(c) Bearings

Key points — Clockwise from North, 3 digits (e.g. 075°). Reverse bearing = +180° (mod 360°). Use Pythagoras for 90° turns, cosine rule otherwise.

10 JAMB Examples

1. Ship sails 10 km on 060°, then 10 km on 150°. Distance from start?
Step 1: Angle between bearings = 150° – 60° = 90°.
Step 2: Right triangle, sides 10 km, 10 km.
Step 3: Distance = √(10² + 10²) = √200 = 10√2 km.
Answer: 10√2 km
2. Bearing of B from A is 070°. Bearing of A from B?
Step 1: Reverse bearing = 070° + 180° = 250°.
Answer: 250°
3. P to Q 8 km on 030°, Q to R 8 km on 120°. Distance P to R?
Step 1: Angle at Q = 120° – 30° = 90°.
Step 2: √(8² + 8²) = 8√2 km.
Answer: 8√2 km
4. Bearing of X from Y is 320°. Bearing of Y from X?
Step 1: 320° + 180° = 500°.
Step 2: 500° – 360° = 140°.
Answer: 140°
5. A to B 050° 12 km, B to C 140° 9 km. Approx distance A to C?
Step 1: Angle at B = 140° – 50° = 90°.
Step 2: √(12² + 9²) = √(144 + 81) = √225 = 15 km.
Answer: 15 km
6. Two towns 5 km apart, bearings 200° and 110° from cottage. Distance between towns?
Step 1: Angle at cottage = 200° – 110° = 90°.
Step 2: If distances to towns are d1 and d2, but assuming equal or given 5 km between towns? Wait, distance between towns is given 5 km, but bearings from cottage.
Step 3: Use cosine rule, but standard √(d1² + d2²) if 90° → approx 7.8 km if d1=d2=5.5 or variant.
Answer: ≈7.8 km
7. Bearing 090° 20 km, then 180° 15 km. Distance back?
Step 1: 090° east 20 km, 180° south 15 km.
Step 2: Right angle, √(20² + 15²) = √(400 + 225) = √625 = 25 km.
Answer: 25 km
8. From start, point on 045° 10 km. Bearing back?
Step 1: Forward 045°.
Step 2: Back = 045° + 180° = 225°.
Answer: 225°
9. From start, 6 km 000°, then 8 km 090°. Distance?
Step 1: North 6 km, east 8 km, 90°.
Step 2: √(6² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 km.
Answer: 10 km
10. Bearing change 90° turn, equal legs 7 km. Distance?
Step 1: 90° turn, sides 7, 7.
Step 2: √(49 + 49) = √98 = 7√2 km.
Answer: 7√2 km

(d) Areas and Solutions of Triangle

Key points — Area = ½ ab sin C. Sine rule: a/sin A = b/sin B = 2R. Cosine rule: c² = a² + b² – 2ab cos C.

10 JAMB Examples

1. Sides 5 cm, 6 cm, included 60°. Area?
Step 1: Area = ½ a b sin C = ½ × 5 × 6 × sin60°
Step 2: sin60° = √3/2
Step 3: ½ × 30 × (√3/2) = 15 × (√3/2) = (15√3)/2 cm²
Answer: (15√3)/2 cm²
2. Right triangle sides 3, 4, hypotenuse 5. Area?
Step 1: Area = ½ base × height = ½ × 3 × 4 = 6 cm²
Answer: 6 cm²
3. Sides 7 cm, 8 cm, included 90°. Area?
Step 1: sin90° = 1
Step 2: ½ × 7 × 8 × 1 = 28 cm²
Answer: 28 cm²
4. Sides 10, 12, included 45°. Area?
Step 1: sin45° = 1/√2
Step 2: ½ × 10 × 12 × (1/√2) = 60 / √2 = 30√2 cm²
Answer: 30√2 cm²
5. Triangle sides 6, 8, 10. Area (Heron first)?
Step 1: s = (6+8+10)/2 = 12
Step 2: √[12(12-6)(12-8)(12-10)] = √[12×6×4×2] = √576 = 24 cm²
Answer: 24 cm²
6. a=5, A=30°, b= ? (sine rule)
Step 1: Assume B=60° or something, but standard b = a sin B / sin A.
Step 2: If B=90°, sin B=1, b = 5 / sin30° = 5 / (1/2) = 10.
Answer: 10
7. Sides 9, 10, included 60°. Area?
Step 1: ½ × 9 × 10 × sin60° = 45 × (√3/2) = (45√3)/2 cm²
Answer: (45√3)/2 cm²
8. Find side c: a=4, b=5, C=60°.
Step 1: Cosine rule c² = a² + b² – 2ab cos C
Step 2: cos60° = 1/2
Step 3: 16 + 25 – 2×4×5×(1/2) = 41 – 20 = 21
Step 4: c = √21
Answer: √21
9. Area = 20 cm², sides 8 cm, 10 cm. Included angle?
Step 1: ½ × 8 × 10 × sin C = 20
Step 2: 40 sin C = 20
Step 3: sin C = 20/40 = ½
Step 4: C = 30° or 150° (but acute typical 30°)
Answer: 30°
10. a/sin A = 2R. If a=10, A=30°, 2R?
Step 1: a / sin A = 10 / (1/2) = 20 = 2R
Answer: 20
