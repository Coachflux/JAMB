📐 COORDINATE GEOMETRY

🎯 Learning Objectives: Determine midpoint & gradient, find distance between points, identify parallel/perpendicular conditions, and find equations of lines in various forms.

📊 COMPLETE FORMULA SUMMARY

All Coordinate Geometry Formulas 📊

Concept Formula Variables
Distance d = √[(x₂-x₁)²+(y₂-y₁)²] Two points (x₁,y₁), (x₂,y₂)
Midpoint M = (x₁+x₂2, y₁+y₂2) Average of coordinates
Gradient m = y₂-y₁x₂-x₁ Change in y over change in x
Parallel m₁ = m₂ Same gradient
Perpendicular m₁ × m₂ = -1 Negative reciprocals
Slope-intercept y = mx + c m=gradient, c=y-intercept
Point-slope y-y₁ = m(x-x₁) Through point (x₁,y₁)
Two-point y-y₁x-x₁ = y₂-y₁x₂-x₁ Through two points
General form ax + by + c = 0 Standard JAMB format
1️⃣ DISTANCE BETWEEN POINTS

Distance Formula 📏

💡 Derived from Pythagoras' theorem: d² = (Δx)² + (Δy)²

🔢 Example 1: Basic Distance

Find distance between A(2, 3) and B(5, 7).

d = √[(5-2)² + (7-3)²]
d = √[3² + 4²]
d = √[9 + 16]
d = √[25]
d = 5 units

🔢 Example 2: Find Unknown Coordinate (JAMB Style)

Distance between P(3, k) and Q(7, 1) is 5 units. Find k.

5 = √[(7-3)² + (1-k)²]
5 = √[16 + (1-k)²]

Square both sides:
25 = 16 + (1-k)²
9 = (1-k)²

Take square root:
1-k = ±3

Case 1: 1-k = 3 → k = -2
Case 2: 1-k = -3 → k = 4
k = -2 or 4
2️⃣ MIDPOINT OF LINE SEGMENT

Midpoint Formula ⏺️

Concept: Average the x-coordinates and average the y-coordinates.
M = (x₁+x₂2, y₁+y₂2)

🔢 Example 3: Find Midpoint

Find midpoint of line joining A(4, 6) and B(8, 10).

Mx = 4+82 = 122 = 6
My = 6+102 = 162 = 8
M = (6, 8)

🔢 Example 4: Find Endpoint

M(5, 3) is midpoint of AB. If A is (2, 1), find B.

Let B = (x, y)

5 = 2+x2 → 10 = 2+x → x = 8
3 = 1+y2 → 6 = 1+y → y = 5
B = (8, 5)
3️⃣ GRADIENT (SLOPE)

Gradient Formula 📈

m = y₂-y₁x₂-x₁ = ΔyΔx

Signs: +ve = upward slope | -ve = downward slope | 0 = horizontal | undefined = vertical

🔢 Example 5: Basic Gradient

Find gradient through P(2, 5) and Q(6, 13).

m = 13-56-2 = 84 = 2

🔢 Example 6: Angle with x-axis

Line makes 45° with positive x-axis. Find gradient.

m = tan(θ) = tan(45°) = 1
4️⃣ PARALLEL & PERPENDICULAR

Line Relationships 📐

Relationship Condition Formula
Parallel Same gradient m₁ = m₂
Perpendicular Negative reciprocals m₁ × m₂ = -1

🔢 Example 7: Parallel Lines

Show line through A(1,2) and B(4,8) is parallel to line through C(0,0) and D(2,4).

Gradient of AB: m₁ = 8-24-1 = 63 = 2

Gradient of CD: m₂ = 4-02-0 = 42 = 2

Since m₁ = m₂ = 2, lines are parallel.

🔢 Example 8: Perpendicular Gradient

Find gradient of line perpendicular to line with gradient 34.

If m₁ = 34, then:
m₂ = -1m₁ = -43
m₂ = -4/3
5️⃣ EQUATIONS OF LINES

Four Forms of Line Equations 📝

1. Slope-Intercept: y = mx + c

Use when you know gradient and y-intercept.

Example: m = 3, c = -2 → y = 3x - 2

2. Point-Slope: y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)

Use when you know gradient and one point.

🔢 Example 9: Point-Slope Form

Find equation of line with gradient 2 passing through (3, 5).

y - 5 = 2(x - 3)
y - 5 = 2x - 6
y = 2x - 1

General form: 2x - y - 1 = 0

3. Two-Point Form

Use when you know two points on the line.

🔢 Example 10: Two-Point Form

Find equation through A(2, 3) and B(4, 7).

Step 1: Find gradient
m = 7-34-2 = 42 = 2

Step 2: Use point-slope with A(2, 3)
y - 3 = 2(x - 2)
y - 3 = 2x - 4
y = 2x - 1

4. General Form: ax + by + c = 0

Standard JAMB format. Convert from other forms.

Example: y = 3x + 5 → 3x - y + 5 = 0

🔥 JAMB PAST QUESTIONS

Common Question Patterns 🎯

Type 1: Combined Concepts

Line joining P(3, 4) and Q(7, k) has gradient 2. Find k and distance PQ.

Find k:
2 = k-47-3 = k-44
8 = k - 4
k = 12

Distance PQ:
d = √[(7-3)² + (12-4)²]
d = √[16 + 64]
d = √[80] = √[16×5]
k = 12, PQ = 4√5

Type 2: Perpendicular Bisector

Find equation of perpendicular bisector of AB where A(2, 4) and B(6, 8).

Step 1: Midpoint
M = (2+62, 4+82) = (4, 6)

Step 2: Gradient of AB
mAB = 8-46-2 = 1

Step 3: Gradient of perpendicular
m⊥ = -1

Step 4: Equation
y - 6 = -1(x - 4)
y - 6 = -x + 4
x + y - 10 = 0
x + y - 10 = 0

💡 EXAM TIPS

1. Always sketch: Draw rough diagram to visualize

2. Watch signs: Be careful with negative coordinates

3. Simplify radicals: √50 = 5√2, √80 = 4√5

4. General form: Provide answers as ax + by + c = 0

5. Units: Include units for distance if given

⚡ Quick Reference

🔹 Distance: Pythagoras on coordinate differences

🔹 Midpoint: Average x, average y

🔹 Gradient: Rise over run (Δy/Δx)

🔹 Parallel: m₁ = m₂

🔹 Perpendicular: m₁ × m₂ = -1

🔹 Line equation: Point-slope most versatile
