1️⃣ Laws of Reflection 🔬

What is Reflection?

Reflection is the bouncing back of light rays when they hit a smooth surface (mirror). The surface that reflects light is called a mirror.

The Two Laws of Reflection

  1. First Law: The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal all lie in the same plane.

  2. Second Law: The angle of incidence (i) equals the angle of reflection (r).
    i = r

Key Terms

  • Incident Ray: The light ray approaching the mirror
  • Reflected Ray: The light ray bouncing off the mirror
  • Normal: Imaginary line perpendicular to the mirror surface at the point where light hits
  • Angle of Incidence (i): Angle between incident ray and normal
  • Angle of Reflection (r): Angle between reflected ray and normal

2️⃣ Plane Mirrors (Flat Mirrors) 🪞

Characteristics of Images in Plane Mirrors

  1. Virtual (cannot be projected on a screen)
  2. Erect/Upright (not inverted)
  3. Same size as the object
  4. Laterally inverted (left appears right, right appears left)
  5. Image distance = Object distance from mirror

Lateral Inversion Explained

When you look in a mirror, your right hand appears as the left hand of your image. This is why "AMBULANCE" is written in reverse on emergency vehicles—drivers see it correctly in their rear-view mirrors.

Multiple Reflections

When two plane mirrors are placed at an angle θ to each other:

n = (360°/θ) - 1

Where n = number of images formed

Special Cases

Angle (θ) Number of Images (n)
Parallel (0°) Infinite images
Perpendicular (90°) n = (360/90) - 1 = 3 images
60° n = (360/60) - 1 = 5 images

🔢 Calculation 1: Multiple Images

Question: Two plane mirrors are inclined at 72°. Calculate the number of images formed.

Using n = (360°/θ) - 1:
n = (360°/72°) - 1
n = 5 - 1 = 4 images
Answer: 4 images

🔢 Calculation 2: Distance in Plane Mirror

Question: A man stands 2m from a plane mirror. He walks 0.5m towards the mirror. Calculate: (a) Initial distance between man and image, (b) New distance after walking.

(a) Initial:
• Object distance = 2m
• Image distance = 2m (behind mirror)
• Distance between man and image = 2 + 2 = 4m

(b) After walking 0.5m:
• New object distance = 2 - 0.5 = 1.5m
• New image distance = 1.5m
• Distance between man and image = 1.5 + 1.5 = 3m
Answer: (a) 4m, (b) 3m

🔢 Calculation 3: Minimum Mirror Height

Question: A man is 1.8m tall with eyes 10cm below the top of his head. Calculate the minimum mirror length required to see his full image.

• Distance from eyes to top = 10cm = 0.1m
• Distance from eyes to feet = 1.8 - 0.1 = 1.7m

Mirror must cover:
• From: 0.1/2 = 0.05m above eye level
• To: 1.7/2 = 0.85m below eye level

Minimum mirror height = 0.05 + 0.85 = 0.9m

Alternative: Height/2 = 1.8/2 = 0.9m
Answer: 90 cm (or 0.9 m)

3️⃣ Curved Mirrors (Spherical Mirrors) 🔭

Types of Curved Mirrors

Concave Mirror (Converging) Convex Mirror (Diverging)
Caved inward (inside of spoon) Curved outward (back of spoon)
Reflecting surface: Inner/concave side Reflecting surface: Outer/convex side
Converges parallel rays to focus Diverges parallel rays from focus behind
Uses: Shaving mirrors, headlights, telescopes Uses: Rear-view mirrors, security mirrors
