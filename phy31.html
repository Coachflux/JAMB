📚 Chapter 31: Current Electricity

🎯 Learning Objectives: Master electric current, potential difference, Ohm's law, resistance, electrical energy and power, circuit analysis, EMF, internal resistance, and electrical measurements for JAMB success.

1️⃣ Electric Current ⚡

Definition

Electric current (I) is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor.

I = Q/t

Where:
• I = Current (Amperes, A)
• Q = Charge (Coulombs, C)
• t = Time (seconds, s)

Types of Current

Type Description Example
Direct Current (DC) Flows in one direction only Battery-powered devices
Alternating Current (AC) Changes direction periodically Mains electricity (50Hz in Nigeria)

🔢 Calculation 1: Calculating Current

Question: A charge of 120 C flows through a conductor in 2 minutes. Calculate the current.

I = Q/t = 120 C / (2 × 60 s) = 120/120 = 1 A
Answer: 1 A

2️⃣ Potential Difference (Voltage) 🔌

Definition

Potential difference (V) is the work done in moving a unit charge from one point to another.

V = W/Q

Where:
• V = Potential difference (Volts, V)
• W = Work done (Joules, J)
• Q = Charge (Coulombs, C)

🔢 Calculation 2: Calculating Voltage

Question: Calculate the potential difference when 500 J of work is done to move 25 C of charge.

V = W/Q = 500 J / 25 C = 20 V
Answer: 20 V

3️⃣ Ohm's Law 📐

Statement

The current flowing through a metallic conductor at constant temperature is directly proportional to the potential difference across its ends.

V = IR

Where:
• V = Potential difference (Volts, V)
• I = Current (Amperes, A)
• R = Resistance (Ohms, Ω)

🔢 Calculation 3: Ohm's Law Application

Question: A resistor has a potential difference of 12 V across it when the current is 3 A. Calculate its resistance.

R = V/I = 12 V / 3 A = 4 Ω
Answer: 4 Ω

4️⃣ Electrical Resistance ⚡

Definition

Resistance is the opposition to the flow of electric current through a conductor.

Factors Affecting Resistance

Factor Relationship Explanation
Length (L) R ∝ L Longer wire = more resistance
Cross-sectional area (A) R ∝ 1/A Thicker wire = less resistance
Resistivity (ρ) R ∝ ρ Material property
Temperature Metals: increases; Semiconductors: decreases Increased vibration impedes flow
R = ρL/A

Where:
• R = Resistance (Ω)
• ρ = Resistivity (Ωm)
• L = Length (m)
• A = Cross-sectional area (m²)

🔢 Calculation 4: Resistance Calculation

Question: A wire of length 2 m and cross-sectional area 5 × 10⁻⁶ m² has resistivity 1.1 × 10⁻⁶ Ωm. Calculate its resistance.

R = ρL/A = (1.1 × 10⁻⁶ × 2) / (5 × 10⁻⁶)
R = (2.2 × 10⁻⁶) / (5 × 10⁻⁶) = 0.44 Ω
Answer: 0.44 Ω

5️⃣ Resistivity and Conductivity 🔬

Quantity Symbol Unit Relationship
Resistivity ρ Ωm ρ = RA/L
Conductivity σ (Ωm)⁻¹ or S/m σ = 1/ρ

6️⃣ Electrical Energy and Power 💡

Electrical Energy

E = VIt = I²Rt = V²t/R

Unit: Joules (J) or kilowatt-hours (kWh)

1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J

Electrical Power

P = VI = I²R = V²/R

Unit: Watts (W)

🔢 Calculation 5: Energy Consumption

Question: An electric heater rated 2000 W operates for 3 hours. Calculate: (a) Energy consumed in kWh, (b) Cost at ₦50 per kWh.

(a) Energy = Power × Time = 2 kW × 3 h = 6 kWh

(b) Cost = 6 kWh × ₦50/kWh = ₦300
Answer: (a) 6 kWh, (b) ₦300

🔢 Calculation 6: Power Calculation

Question: A 60 W bulb operates on 240 V. Calculate: (a) Current drawn, (b) Resistance of the filament.

(a) I = P/V = 60/240 = 0.25 A

(b) R = V/I = 240/0.25 = 960 Ω
Answer: (a) 0.25 A, (b) 960 Ω

7️⃣ Resistors in Circuits 🔗

Series Connection

  • Characteristics: Same current flows through all resistors; Total voltage = sum of individual voltages
  • Formula: Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...

Parallel Connection

  • Characteristics: Same voltage across all resistors; Total current = sum of individual currents
  • Formula: 1/Rtotal = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...
  • For two resistors: Rtotal = R₁R₂/(R₁ + R₂)

🔢 Calculation 7: Series Circuit

Question: Three resistors of 2 Ω, 4 Ω, and 6 Ω are connected in series across a 12 V battery. Calculate: (a) Total resistance, (b) Circuit current, (c) Voltage across the 4 Ω resistor.

(a) Rtotal = 2 + 4 + 6 = 12 Ω

(b) I = V/R = 12/12 = 1 A

(c) V₄ = IR = 1 × 4 = 4 V
Answer: (a) 12 Ω, (b) 1 A, (c) 4 V

🔢 Calculation 8: Parallel Circuit

Question: Two resistors of 6 Ω and 12 Ω are connected in parallel across a 12 V battery. Calculate: (a) Total resistance, (b) Current through each resistor, (c) Total current.

(a) 1/R = 1/6 + 1/12 = (2+1)/12 = 3/12 = 1/4
R = 4 Ω

(b) I₆ = 12/6 = 2 A; I₁₂ = 12/12 = 1 A

(c) Itotal = 2 + 1 = 3 A
Answer: (a) 4 Ω, (b) 2 A and 1 A, (c) 3 A

🔢 Calculation 9: Combined Circuit

Question: Calculate total resistance: 4 Ω and 6 Ω in series, then in parallel with 12 Ω.

Series combination: Rs = 4 + 6 = 10 Ω

Parallel with 12 Ω:
1/Rtotal = 1/10 + 1/12 = (6+5)/60 = 11/60
Rtotal = 60/11 = 5.45 Ω
Answer: 5.45 Ω

8️⃣ Electromotive Force (EMF) and Internal Resistance 🔋

Definitions

  • EMF (E): Total energy supplied per coulomb by a cell (open circuit voltage)
  • Terminal Potential Difference (V): Voltage available at the terminals when current flows
  • Internal Resistance (r): Resistance within the cell itself
E = V + Ir = I(R + r)

Where:
• E = EMF (V)
• V = Terminal p.d. = IR (V)
• I = Current (A)
• r = Internal resistance (Ω)
• R = External resistance (Ω)

🔢 Calculation 10: Internal Resistance

Question: A cell of EMF 12 V and internal resistance 2 Ω is connected to a 10 Ω resistor. Calculate: (a) Circuit current, (b) Terminal potential difference.

(a) I = E/(R + r) = 12/(10 + 2) = 12/12 = 1 A

(b) V = IR = 1 × 10 = 10 V
Or: V = E - Ir = 12 - (1 × 2) = 10 V
Answer: (a) 1 A, (b) 10 V

9️⃣ Cells in Combination 🔗

Series Connection

Etotal = E₁ + E₂ + E₃ + ...
rtotal = r₁ + r₂ + r₃ + ...

Parallel Connection (Identical Cells)

Etotal = E
rtotal = r/n (where n = number of cells)

🔢 Calculation 11: Cells in Series

Question: Three identical cells, each of EMF 1.5 V and internal resistance 0.5 Ω, are connected in series to a 4 Ω resistor. Calculate the current.

Etotal = 1.5 × 3 = 4.5 V
rtotal = 0.5 × 3 = 1.5 Ω

I = Etotal/(R + rtotal) = 4.5/(4 + 1.5) = 4.5/5.5 = 0.818 A
Answer: 0.818 A

  • To voltmeter: Add high resistance (multiplier) in series

1️⃣1️⃣ Kirchhoff's Laws 📐

First Law (Junction/Current Law)

At any junction, the sum of currents entering equals the sum leaving:

ΣIin = ΣIout

Second Law (Loop/Voltage Law)

Around any closed loop, the sum of EMFs equals the sum of potential drops:

ΣE = ΣIR

1️⃣2️⃣ Wheatstone Bridge ⚖️

Principle

When balanced (no current through galvanometer):

P/Q = R/S

Meter Bridge

Uses uniform resistance wire:

R₁/R₂ = l₁/l₂

Where l₁ and l₂ are balancing lengths from either end.

🔢 Calculation 13: Meter Bridge

Question: In a meter bridge experiment, the balance point is at 40 cm from the left end when R₁ = 6 Ω. Calculate R₂.

R₁/R₂ = l₁/l₂
6/R₂ = 40/60 = 2/3
R₂ = 6 × 3/2 = 9 Ω
Answer: 9 Ω

1️⃣3️⃣ Potentiometer 📏

Principle

Compares potential differences without drawing current from the source.

Applications

  1. Comparing EMFs of two cells
  2. Measuring internal resistance of a cell
  3. Measuring small EMFs
E₁/E₂ = l₁/l₂

Where l₁ and l₂ are balancing lengths.

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

  1. Always convert units: Change minutes to seconds, cm to m, etc.

  2. Temperature effect: Remember resistance of metals increases with temperature

  3. Short circuit: When R = 0, current is maximum (I = E/r)

  4. Power rating: Devices are rated at their operating voltage

  5. Maximum power transfer: Occurs when external R = internal r

  6. Efficiency: η = [R/(R+r)] × 100%
