🧠 3-step routine

Mass → moles → divide by dm³ → solubility (mol/dm³).

1️⃣ What is Solubility? (Definition)

Solubility is the maximum amount of solute that can dissolve in 1 dm³ (1000 cm³) of solvent at a given temperature to form a saturated solution.

It is expressed in mol/dm³ (moles per cubic decimetre).

Typical solubility curve: solubility changes with temperature

⚠️ Keyword in the definition

  • Maximum amount + 1 dm³ + given temperature + saturated solution

2️⃣ Types of Solutions

Solutions are grouped by how much solute is dissolved at a given temperature.

Type Meaning Can more solute dissolve? Stability
Unsaturated Less than maximum possible Yes Stable
Saturated Exactly the maximum at that temperature No Stable
Supersaturated More than saturated amount (made by careful cooling) No (unstable) Unstable (crystals form easily)

🧠 Easy memory

Unsaturated = room • Saturated = full • Supersaturated = over-full (crashes out).

3️⃣ Solubility Curves (How to Read Them)

Solubility curves plot solubility (mol/dm³) on the y-axis against temperature (°C) on the x-axis.

Different salts have different solubility curves

What the curve shape means

  • Upward slope (e.g., KNO₃): solubility increases with temperature → dissolution is endothermic
  • Downward slope (e.g., CaSO₄): solubility decreases with temperature → dissolution is exothermic
  • Almost flat (e.g., NaCl): little change with temperature

✅ JAMB deductions you should be able to state

  • Solubility depends on temperature
  • Recrystallization: dissolve in minimum hot solvent, then cool
  • Compare solubility of two salts at the same temperature

4️⃣ Simple Calculations (mol/dm³)

Solubility formula

Solubility (mol/dm³) = moles of solute ÷ volume of solution (dm³)

Example 1 (Mass → mol/dm³)

Question: 20.0 g KNO₃ (Mr = 101) dissolves in 250 cm³ at 40°C. Find solubility.

Moles = 20.0/101 = 0.198 mol

Volume = 250 cm³ = 0.250 dm³

Solubility = 0.198/0.250 = 0.792 mol/dm³

Example 2 (mol/dm³ → mass)

Question: Solubility of NaCl at 25°C is 6.15 mol/dm³. What mass dissolves in 400 cm³? (Mr = 58.5)

Volume = 400 cm³ = 0.400 dm³

Moles = 6.15 × 0.400 = 2.46 mol

Mass = 2.46 × 58.5 = 143.91 g

⚠️ Common mistakes

  • Forgetting to convert cm³ to dm³
  • Using wrong molar mass (Mr)
  • Mixing up “moles = concentration × volume”

5️⃣ Solvents for Stains (Like Dissolves Like)

Rule: substances dissolve best in solvents with similar polarity.

Dry cleaning uses non-polar solvents to remove oil/grease stains

Which solvent for what?

  • Fats/oils (non-polar): petrol, kerosene, CCl₄, dry-cleaning fluid
  • Perspiration stains: water + detergent or alcohol
  • Oil-based paint: turpentine, white spirit, xylene

Stain removal (JAMB favourites)

  • Grease/oil stains → petrol/dry-cleaning solvent then detergent wash
  • Paint stains → turpentine/white spirit (not water)
  • Ball-point ink → methylated spirit

6️⃣ True Solutions vs Suspensions & Colloids

These are separated mainly by particle size and how they behave (settling, filtration, Tyndall effect).
