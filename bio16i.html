BIOLOGY

🇳🇬 Nigeria Vegetation Zones (JAMB)

Rainfall gradient: South (wet forest) → North (dry savanna/sahel) + Highland “islands”

🎯 Overview

Nigeria’s local biomes (vegetation/ecological zones) are mainly determined by rainfall, temperature, soil, and distance from the coast. Rainfall generally decreases from south to north.

Nigeria: coastal south is wetter; far north is drier

JAMB Focus (5 Zones)

  • Tropical rainforest
  • Guinea savanna (southern + northern)
  • Sudan savanna
  • Desert / Sahel-like zone (small area)
  • Montane highlands (Obudu, Jos, Mambilla)
🧠 SOUTH → NORTH ORDER:

Rainforest → Guinea → Sudan → Sahel/Desert (Highlands are “islands” due to altitude)

🌧️ 1. Tropical Rainforest

Location: Southern Nigeria (near coast and Niger Delta; includes mangrove/freshwater swamps in some descriptions, but core is lowland rainforest).

Rainforest: dense layered canopy + high humidity

Key Feature Details
Rainfall Very high (>2000 mm/year; can reach 3000+ mm)
Temperature Hot and humid year-round (~27°C average)
Vegetation Tall evergreen trees (mahogany, iroko, obeche, oil palm), canopy layers, few grasses on forest floor
Special adaptations Buttress roots, epiphytes, lianas (climbers)
Biodiversity Very high: monkeys, birds, insects, leopards, chimpanzees, etc.
Soil Often leached by heavy rain (nutrients washed down)

Buttress roots help tall trees stand firm in wet soils

How to identify: Constant rain + thick forest + high humidity = southern rainforest zone.

🌾 2. Guinea Savanna (Southern & Northern)

Location: Largest zone in Nigeria — central belt (transition between rainforest and Sudan savanna).

Guinea savanna: grasses dominate, trees are scattered

Two Sub-zones

  • Southern Guinea: closer to rainforest (wetter, taller grasses/trees)
  • Northern Guinea: drier transition to Sudan savanna
Key Feature Details
Rainfall Moderate to good (1000–1500 mm/year) with wet and dry seasons
Vegetation Tall grasses (elephant grass, Andropogon) + scattered trees (shea butter, locust bean/Parkia, Isoberlinia, Daniellia)
Fire/season effects Grasses burn in dry season; many trees are deciduous and fire-resistant
Animals Antelopes, birds, rodents; elephants in protected areas

How to identify: Central Nigeria, farming + grazing common, grasses dominate and trees are not too dense.

🌵 3. Sudan Savanna

Location: Northern Nigeria (e.g., Kano, Katsina, Sokoto, Bauchi, parts of Borno).

Sudan savanna: more open, thorny trees like acacia

Key FeatureDetails
RainfallLower (600–1200 mm/year) with longer dry season
VegetationShorter grasses; fewer/smaller trees (acacia, baobab); thorny shrubs common
AnimalsDry-adapted animals; gazelles, birds; camels in fringes
SoilOften sandy, less fertile

← Swipe table to see more →

How to identify: Far north, drier climate, shorter grasses, more open and thorny vegetation; irrigation farming is common.

🏜️ 4. Desert / Sahel-like Zone (Fringes)

Location: Extreme northeast Nigeria (small part near Lake Chad: northern Borno and Yobe). True desert is mostly outside Nigeria, but Sahel-like conditions occur.

Sahel: sparse shrubs and grasses under very low rainfall

Key FeatureDetails
RainfallVery low (<600 mm/year) and often erratic
VegetationSparse grasses, thorny shrubs, occasional acacia/date palms
TemperatureHot days, cooler nights (extreme range)
SoilSandy; prone to desertification
AnimalsDrought-resistant: camels/goats, reptiles

⚠️ JAMB Hot Point

  • This zone is the driest part of Nigeria and is closely linked to desertification.

⛰️ 5. Montane Highlands (Obudu, Jos, Mambilla)

Location: Jos Plateau (Plateau), Obudu Plateau (Cross River), Mambilla Plateau (Taraba).

Highlands: cooler climate due to altitude

Key FeatureDetails
AltitudeUsually 1000–1700 m+ (higher altitude = cooler)
TemperatureCooler (about 15–22°C)
RainfallCan be high in some areas (e.g., Obudu very wet and misty)
VegetationMontane grasslands with patches of forest/woodland
SoilOften rocky/volcanic (Jos)
AnimalsUnique birds/monkeys; some endemic species

How to identify: Cooler highlands + grasslands with forest patches; tourist areas like Obudu (cattle ranch region).

⚡ Tips for JAMB

  • Memorize the south-to-north order: Rainforest → Guinea → Sudan → Sahel
  • Highlands are exceptions (“islands”) due to altitude
  • Match features to region (e.g., high rainfall + dense trees = rainforest)
  • Practice locating zones on a simple Nigeria map sketch
  • Common style: “Which biome is found in Kano?” → Sudan Savanna
