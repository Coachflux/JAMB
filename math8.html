Progressions Made Super Easy for JAMB (Step-by-Step)

Abumchukwu, a progression is just a list of numbers that follows a clear rule. JAMB only tests two types: Arithmetic Progression (AP) → constant difference (called d), Geometric Progression (GP) → constant ratio (called r). Think of AP like adding the same number every time (e.g., 2, 5, 8, 11…). Think of GP like multiplying by the same number every time (e.g., 3, 6, 12, 24…). I will teach each part very slowly and simply, like we are in class together. Read one example at a time. Try it on paper first, then check my steps. You will understand everything fast.

1. Nth Term (How to find any term you want)

AP nth term formula: Tₙ = a + (n – 1)d (a = first term, d = common difference, n = position)
GP nth term formula: Tₙ = a × rⁿ⁻¹ (a = first term, r = common ratio)
Quick tip: Always write the first 2–3 terms to find a and d or r.

2. Sum of First n Terms (Sₙ)

AP sum: Sₙ = n/2 [2a + (n – 1)d] or Sₙ = n/2 (a + l) (l = last term = Tₙ)
GP sum (finite): Sₙ = a(1 – rⁿ)/(1 – r) (when r ≠ 1) (If r > 1, you can also write a(rⁿ – 1)/(r – 1) — same thing)

3. Sum to Infinity (Only for GP)

Only possible when |r| < 1 (the terms get smaller and smaller forever).
S∞ = a / (1 – r)

(i) 10 JAMB Examples on Nth Term of a Progression (AP + GP)

1. (JAMB 2010) The 3rd term of an A.P. is –9 and the 7th term is –29. Find the 10th term.
Step 1: 3rd term: a + 2d = –9
Step 2: 7th term: a + 6d = –29
Step 3: Subtract step 1 from step 2: 4d = –20 → d = –5
Step 4: Substitute d in step 1: a + 2(–5) = –9 → a – 10 = –9 → a = 1
Step 5: 10th term: a + 9d = 1 + 9(–5) = 1 – 45 = –44
Answer: –44
2. (JAMB 2008) The 5th term of an A.P. is 24 and the 11th term is 96. Find the first term.
Step 1: 5th term: a + 4d = 24
Step 2: 11th term: a + 10d = 96
Step 3: Subtract step 1 from step 2: 6d = 72 → d = 12
Step 4: Substitute d in step 1: a + 4×12 = 24 → a + 48 = 24 → a = –24
Answer: –24
3. Find the 19th term of the A.P.: 5/6, 8/6, 11/6, …
Step 1: First term a = 5/6
Step 2: Common difference d = 8/6 – 5/6 = 3/6 = 1/2
Step 3: nth term = a + (n – 1)d
Step 4: T19 = 5/6 + 18 × (1/2) = 5/6 + 9 = 5/6 + 54/6 = 59/6
Answer: 59/6
4. (JAMB 1994) 6th term of an A.P. is 11, first term is 1. Find the 10th term.
Step 1: 6th term: a + 5d = 11
Step 2: 1 + 5d = 11
Step 3: 5d = 10 → d = 2
Step 4: 10th term: 1 + 9×2 = 1 + 18 = 19
Answer: 19
5. The 3rd term of an A.P. is 4x – 2y and the 9th term is 10x – 8y. Find the common difference.
Step 1: 3rd term: a + 2d = 4x – 2y
Step 2: 9th term: a + 8d = 10x – 8y
Step 3: Subtract step 1 from step 2: 6d = 6x – 6y
Step 4: d = x – y
Answer: x – y
6. What is the nth term of the progression 27, 9, 3, …?
Step 1: First term a = 27
Step 2: Common ratio r = 9 / 27 = 1/3
Step 3: Tₙ = a r^{n–1} = 27 × (1/3)^{n–1}
Answer: 27 × (1/3)^{n–1}
7. (JAMB 2003) Three consecutive terms of a G.P. are n–2, n, n+3. Find the common ratio.
Step 1: Ratio: n / (n–2) = (n+3) / n
Step 2: n² = (n–2)(n+3)
Step 3: n² = n² + 3n – 2n – 6
Step 4: 0 = n – 6 → n = 6
Step 5: r = 6 / (6–2) = 6/4 = 3/2
Answer: 3/2
8. The nth term of a sequence is (–1)^{n–2} × 2^{n–1}. Find the 4th term.
Step 1: n = 4
Step 2: (–1)^{4–2} × 2^{4–1} = (–1)^2 × 2^3
Step 3: 1 × 8 = 8
Answer: 8
9. First term of a G.P. is 2, common ratio 3. Find the 6th term.
Step 1: a = 2, r = 3
Step 2: T6 = 2 × 3^{5} = 2 × 243 = 486
Answer: 486
10. Find the 8th term of the G.P.: 1/2, –1, 2, –4, …
Step 1: a = 1/2, r = –1 / (1/2) = –2
Step 2: T8 = (1/2) × (–2)^{7} = (1/2) × (–128) = –64
Answer: –64

(ii) 10 JAMB Examples on Sum of A.P. and G.P. (5 AP + 5 GP)

1. (JAMB 2003) The sum of the first n terms of an A.P. is 252. First term –16, last term 72. Find n.
Step 1: S_n = n/2 (a + l)
Step 2: 252 = n/2 (–16 + 72)
Step 3: 252 = n/2 × 56
Step 4: 252 = 28n
Step 5: n = 9
Answer: 9
2. (JAMB 2009) The sum of the first n terms of the A.P. 5, 11, 17, 23, … is
Step 1: a = 5, d = 6
Step 2: S_n = n/2 [2a + (n–1)d]
Step 3: S_n = n/2 [10 + (n–1)6]
Step 4: S_n = n/2 [10 + 6n – 6]
Step 5: S_n = n/2 [6n + 4] = n(3n + 2)
Answer: n(3n + 2)
3. Sum of first 16 terms of an A.P. is –506, sum of first 20 terms is –770. Find the first term and common difference.
Step 1: S16 = 16/2 [2a + 15d] = 8(2a + 15d) = –506 → 2a + 15d = –506/8 = –63.25
Step 2: S20 = 20/2 [2a + 19d] = 10(2a + 19d) = –770 → 2a + 19d = –77
Step 3: Subtract step 1 from step 2: 4d = –77 + 63.25 = –13.75 → d = –3.4375
Step 4: 2a + 15(–3.4375) = –63.25 → 2a – 51.5625 = –63.25 → 2a = –11.6875 → a = –5.84375
Answer: a = –5.84375, d = –3.4375
4. Find the sum of the first 10 terms of the A.P.: 3, 7, 11, 15, …
Step 1: a = 3, d = 4, n = 10
Step 2: S10 = 10/2 [2×3 + 9×4] = 5 [6 + 36] = 5×42 = 210
Answer: 210
5. The sum of the first n terms of an A.P. is n(3n + 2). Find the first term and common difference.
Step 1: S_n = n/2 [2a + (n–1)d] = n(3n + 2)
Step 2: n/2 [2a + (n–1)d] = 3n² + 2n
Step 3: Multiply left by 2/n: 2a + (n–1)d = 6n + 4
Step 4: Expand: 2a + nd – d = 6n + 4
Step 5: Coefficient of n: d = 6, constant: 2a – d = 4 → 2a – 6 = 4 → 2a = 10 → a = 5
Answer: a = 5, d = 6
6. Find the sum of the first 5 terms of the G.P. 2, 6, 18, 54, …
Step 1: a = 2, r = 3, n = 5
Step 2: S_n = a(1 – r^n)/(1 – r)
Step 3: S5 = 2(1 – 3^5)/(1 – 3)
Step 4: 3^5 = 243, 1 – 243 = –242
Step 5: 2(–242)/(–2) = 242
Answer: 242
7. Sum of first 4 terms of G.P. is 15, first term 3. Find common ratio.
Step 1: S4 = 3(1 – r^4)/(1 – r) = 15
Step 2: 1 – r^4 = 15(1 – r)/3 = 5(1 – r)
Step 3: 1 – r^4 = 5 – 5r
Step 4: r^4 – 5r + 4 = 0
Step 5: Factor: (r – 1)(r + 1)(r^2 + 4) = 0 → r = 1 or –1 (discard r=1 as sum infinite, check –1)
Answer: r = –1
8. Three consecutive terms of a G.P. are x, 2x, 4x. Find the sum of the first 6 terms.
Step 1: r = 2x / x = 2
Step 2: a = x
Step 3: S6 = x(1 – 2^6)/(1 – 2) = x(1 – 64)/(–1) = 63x
Answer: 63x
9. Find S₁₀ of G.P.: 1, 1/2, 1/4, 1/8, …
Step 1: a = 1, r = 1/2, n = 10
Step 2: S10 = 1(1 – (1/2)^10)/(1 – 1/2) = [1 – 1/1024] / (1/2) = 2(1023/1024) = 1023/512
Answer: 1023/512
10. The sum of the first two terms of a G.P. is x and sum of the last two terms is y (n terms total). Find r.
Step 1: S2 = a(1 + r) = x
Step 2: Last two: T_{n-1} + T_n = a r^{n-2} + a r^{n-1} = a r^{n-2}(1 + r) = y
Step 3: Divide: y / x = r^{n-2}
Step 4: r = (y/x)^{1/(n–2)}
Answer: r = (y/x)^{1/(n–2)}

(iii) 10 JAMB Examples on Sum to Infinity of a Given G.P.

1. (JAMB 2012) The sum to infinity of a G.P. is –1/10 and the first term is –1/8. Find r.
Step 1: S∞ = a / (1 – r) = –1/10
Step 2: –1/8 / (1 – r) = –1/10
Step 3: 1/8 / (1 – r) = 1/10
Step 4: 1/8 = 1/10 (1 – r)
Step 5: 1 – r = 10/8 = 5/4 → r = 1 – 5/4 = –1/4
Answer: –1/4
2. (JAMB 1999) The first term of a G.P. is twice its common ratio. Sum to infinity is 8. Find the sum of the first two terms.
Step 1: a = 2r
Step 2: S∞ = 2r / (1 – r) = 8
Step 3: 2r = 8(1 – r) → 2r = 8 – 8r → 10r = 8 → r = 0.8
Step 4: a = 1.6
Step 5: First two terms: 1.6 + 1.6×0.8 = 1.6 + 1.28 = 2.88
Answer: 2.88
3. Sum to infinity of a G.P. is 9, first term 6. Find r.
Step 1: S∞ = 6 / (1 – r) = 9
Step 2: 6 = 9(1 – r)
Step 3: 1 – r = 6/9 = 2/3
Step 4: r = 1 – 2/3 = 1/3
Answer: 1/3
4. Find the sum to infinity of the series: 4 + 2 + 1 + ½ + …
Step 1: a = 4, r = 2/4 = 1/2
Step 2: |r| = 1/2 < 1, so converges
Step 3: S∞ = 4 / (1 – 1/2) = 4 / (1/2) = 8
Answer: 8
5. The sum to infinity is 10 and common ratio is –1/2. Find the first term.

10. A ball rebounds to 2/3 of the height from which it fell. If it is dropped from 27 m, find the total distance travelled before coming to rest (infinite bounces).
Step 1: First drop: 27 m down
Step 2: Rebound: 27 × 2/3 up, then down same
Step 3: Total after first: 27 + 2 × (27 × 2/3) + 2 × (27 × (2/3)^2) + ...
Step 4: GP for rebounds: a = 27 × 2/3 = 18, r = 2/3
Step 5: Sum of GP × 2 (up and down) + first drop: 27 + 2 × (18 / (1 – 2/3)) = 27 + 2 × (18 / (1/3)) = 27 + 2 × 54 = 27 + 108 = 135 m
Answer: 135 m
