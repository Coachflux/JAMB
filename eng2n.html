🧠 One-line recall

“He cried because his family had moved to the UK and he was being forced to join them, even though he loved Stardom.”

1️⃣ Context (Link to Chapter 1)

Chapter 2 explains the real reason behind Mr. Bepo Adewale’s shocking breakdown in Chapter 1.

  • For about five days, he remains distressed and unusually silent
  • Staff members keep questioning him
  • Eventually, he opens up

2️⃣ Main Revelation: The UK Relocation Pressure

Mr. Bepo reveals he plans to leave Nigeria and relocate to the UK.

  • He says he can no longer resist his family’s pressure
  • His delay (“foot-dragging”) has almost broken his marriage
  • His wife Seri has already moved to the UK for nursing work
  • She took their two children: Nike and Kike
  • Wife + children are “keen” on him joining them

Key detail

  • The children team up with their mother to pressure him to relocate.

3️⃣ Why He’s Torn: Love for Stardom & Nigeria

Mr. Bepo loves his students deeply and feels he is abandoning his mission in Nigerian education.

His service record at Stardom (20+ years)

  • 4 years as headmaster at Stardom Kiddies (nursery/primary)
  • Remaining years as principal of the secondary school

Core conflict: family reunion & better opportunities abroad vs his commitment to students and community in Nigeria.

4️⃣ Origin of “The Lekki Headmaster” Nickname

His calm, humanistic, conflict-resolving style (especially in Stardom Kiddies) earned him the nickname.

  • Nickname was first used jokingly by Mr. Audu (Fine Arts teacher)
  • Mr. Audu admired how Bepo handled issues fairly and calmly
🧠 MCQ trap to avoid

It wasn’t the MD or students that coined it first—remember: Mr. Audu.

5️⃣ Main Themes in Chapter 2

  • Family obligations vs professional passion
  • Migration (“Japa”) as enticement: money, opportunity, reunion
  • Emotional/internal conflict and sacrifice
  • Dedication to education and community in Nigeria
🧠 Theme line

Chapter 2 turns Chapter 1’s tears into a real-life dilemma: stay and serve, or leave and reunite.
