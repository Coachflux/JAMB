– JAMB Topic 3: Natural Habitats Made Super Easy

1. What are Natural Habitats?

A habitat is the natural home or environment where an organism lives and obtains everything it needs (food, water, shelter, mates).

JAMB divides habitats into two main types:

  • Aquatic (water-based)
  • Terrestrial / Arboreal (land-based or tree-based)

Candidates must know plants and animals that live in each habitat and their adaptive features.

2. Aquatic Habitats

Types of Aquatic Habitats

  • Freshwater: ponds, streams, lakes
  • Marine / Brackish: seashores, mangrove swamps

Common Organisms & Their Adaptations

Organism Habitat Key Adaptive Features
Water lily / Water hyacinth Ponds, lakes Floating leaves with air spaces, stomata on upper surface, flexible petioles
Tilapia / Catfish Streams, ponds, lakes Gills for oxygen extraction, streamlined body, lateral line for detecting movement
Crabs / Mudskippers Mangrove swamps Strong legs for walking on mud, gills + skin breathing, burrowing ability
Red mangrove Mangrove swamps Prop roots / pneumatophores for support and oxygen intake in salty, muddy soil

3. Terrestrial / Arboreal Habitats

Types

  • Tree-tops (arboreal)
  • Abandoned farmland / dry grassy field (savanna)
  • Burrow / hole

Common Organisms & Their Adaptations

Organism Habitat Key Adaptive Features
Oil palm / Iroko tree Tree-tops / forest Tall trunk, broad leaves for light capture, deep roots
Monkey / Parrot Tree-tops Grasping hands/feet, strong tail, sharp claws, excellent vision
Elephant grass / Guinea grass Dry grassy field (savanna) Deep fibrous roots, fire-resistant base, fast growth after burning
Grasshopper / Antelope Savanna field Camouflage colour, long legs for jumping/running, large eyes
Earthworm / Termite Burrow / soil Moist skin for breathing, segmented body, strong setae for movement
Rodent (rat, rabbit) Burrow / hole Sharp incisors for digging, nocturnal activity, keen smell/hearing
