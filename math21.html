📏 Chapter 21: Measures of Dispersion

This chapter covers range, mean deviation, variance, standard deviation, and coefficient of variation - essential statistics describing how spread out data values are from the center.

1️⃣ TYPES OF MEASURES OF DISPERSION

A. Range 📐

Definition: Simplest measure - difference between highest and lowest values
Formula: Range = Highest Value − Lowest Value

🔢 Example 1: Range

Find the range of: 12, 18, 25, 30, 15, 22, 28

Highest = 30, Lowest = 12
Range = 30 − 12 = 18
Answer: Range = 18
💡 Range = Max − Min (simplest but affected by extreme values)

B. Mean Deviation (Average Deviation) 📊

Definition: Average distance of each data point from the mean
Formula: Mean Deviation = ∑|x − x̄|n

Where:
• x = each data value
• x̄ = mean
• n = number of values
• |...| = absolute value (always positive)

🔢 Example 2: Mean Deviation

Find the mean deviation of: 5, 8, 12, 15, 10

Step 1: Calculate the mean
x̄ = 5 + 8 + 12 + 15 + 105 = 505 = 10
x x − x̄ |x − x̄|
5 5 − 10 = −5 5
8 8 − 10 = −2 2
12 12 − 10 = 2 2
15 15 − 10 = 5 5
10 10 − 10 = 0 0
Step 2: Sum of absolute deviations = 5 + 2 + 2 + 5 + 0 = 14

Step 3: Mean Deviation = 145 = 2.8
Answer: Mean Deviation = 2.8

C. Variance 📈

Definition: Average of the squared deviations from the mean
Formula: Variance (σ²) = ∑(x − x̄)²n

🔢 Example 3: Variance

Find the variance of: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12

Step 1: Calculate the mean
x̄ = 4 + 6 + 8 + 10 + 125 = 405 = 8
x (x − x̄) (x − x̄)²
4 4 − 8 = −4 16
6 6 − 8 = −2 4
8 8 − 8 = 0 0
10 10 − 8 = 2 4
12 12 − 8 = 4 16
Step 2: Sum of squared deviations = 16 + 4 + 0 + 4 + 16 = 40

Step 3: Variance = 405 = 8
Answer: Variance = 8

D. Standard Deviation 📉

Definition: Square root of variance - most commonly used measure
Returns to original units of measurement
Formula: σ = √Variance = √∑(x − x̄)²n

🔢 Example 4: Standard Deviation

Continuing from Example 3 (Variance = 8):

σ = √8 ≈ 2.83
Answer: Standard Deviation ≈ 2.83
💡 Standard Deviation = √Variance (most reliable measure of spread)

E. Coefficient of Variation 📊

Definition: Relative measure to compare dispersion of different datasets
Formula: CV = σx̄ × 100%
2️⃣ FORMULAS FOR GROUPED DATA

Frequency Distribution Tables 📋

Measure Formula
Mean x̄ = ∑fx∑f
Variance σ² = ∑fx²∑f − x̄²
Standard Deviation σ = √(∑fx²∑f − x̄²)

Where:

• f = frequency

• x = midpoint of each class interval

• fx = frequency × midpoint

• fx² = frequency × (midpoint)²

3️⃣ WORKED EXAMPLE: GROUPED DATA

🔢 Example 5: Mean & Standard Deviation (Grouped)

Calculate the mean and standard deviation:

Class Interval Frequency (f)
10-14 2
15-19 5
20-24 8
25-29 4
30-34 1
Step 1: Complete the calculation table

Class | f | Midpoint (x) | fx | x² | fx²
------+---+--------------+----+----+-----
10-14 | 2 | 12 | 24 | 144 | 288
15-19 | 5 | 17 | 85 | 289 | 1445
20-24 | 8 | 22 | 176 | 484 | 3872
25-29 | 4 | 27 | 108 | 729 | 2916
30-34 | 1 | 32 | 32 | 1024 | 1024
------+---+--------------+----+----+-----
Total | 20 | | 425 | | 9545
Step 2: Calculate Mean
x̄ = ∑fx∑f = 42520 = 21.25
Step 3: Calculate Variance
σ² = ∑fx²∑f − x̄²
= 954520 − (21.25)²
= 477.25 − 451.5625
= 25.6875
Step 4: Calculate Standard Deviation
σ = √25.6875 ≈ 5.07
Answers: Mean = 21.25, Standard Deviation ≈ 5.07

⚡ Quick Calculation Steps

For Ungrouped Data:

1. Find mean (x̄)

2. Calculate deviations (x − x̄)

3. Square them for variance, absolute for MD

4. Average appropriately

5. Take √ for standard deviation

🎯 JAMB EXAM TIPS

1. Standard deviation is most reliable: Always use when comparing spread

2. Range is simplest but risky: Easily affected by extreme values (outliers)

3. Variance is always positive: Squaring eliminates negatives

4. Units matter: SD has same units as data, variance has squared units

5. Grouped data: Always use midpoint of class intervals

6. CV for comparison: Use coefficient of variation to compare different datasets

⚠️ Common Mistakes:

• Forgetting to use absolute values in mean deviation

• Using class limits instead of midpoints for grouped data

• Confusing variance (σ²) with standard deviation (σ)

• Not squaring deviations when calculating variance

• Taking square root too early in calculations

Pre-Exam Checklist:

  • Range calculation (Max − Min)
  • Mean deviation (absolute deviations)
  • Variance (squared deviations)
  • Standard deviation (√variance)
  • Coefficient of variation (relative measure)
  • Grouped data formulas (using midpoints)
  • Calculation table method (∑fx, ∑fx²)
  • Alternative variance formula (∑fx²/∑f − x̄²)
