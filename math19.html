Measures of Location (also called Measures of Central Tendency)

These tell us the “centre” or typical value in a set of data. JAMB loves this topic because it is straightforward once you know the simple rules. We will cover everything in the syllabus with easy language, formulas, and exactly 10 JAMB-style examples with full solutions for (a) and 10 for (b).

(a) Mean, Mode and Median of Ungrouped and Grouped Data (simple cases only)

Quick Summary Table (memorise this!)

  • Measure: Mean
    Ungrouped Data: Σx / n
    Grouped Data (simple cases): Σ(f × midpoint) / Σf
  • Measure: Mode
    Ungrouped Data: Value that appears most often
    Grouped Data (simple cases): Modal class (highest f). Formula: L + [(f₁ – f₀)/(2f₁ – f₀ – f₂)] × h
  • Measure: Median
    Ungrouped Data: Arrange in order → middle value(s)
    Grouped Data (simple cases): Median class (where cum. freq. first ≥ N/2). Formula: L + [(N/2 – CF)/f] × h

Key words
n or N = total number of items/frequency
L = lower boundary of the class
f = frequency of that class
CF = cumulative frequency before that class
h = class width (upper – lower)
Midpoint = (lower + upper)/2

Ungrouped examples are very easy – just add and divide.
Grouped examples need a table with midpoints or cumulative frequency.

10 JAMB-Style Examples for (a) with Solutions

Example 1 (Ungrouped Mean – very common)
The scores of 8 students in a test are: 5, 7, 8, 8, 9, 10, 12, 15. What is the mean score?
Step 1: Sum the scores: 5 + 7 + 8 + 8 + 9 + 10 + 12 + 15 = 74.
Step 2: n = 8.
Step 3: Mean = 74 / 8 = 9.25.
Answer: 9.25
Example 2 (Ungrouped Mode)
The ages of some pupils are: 12, 13, 14, 14, 15, 15, 15, 16. Find the mode.
Step 1: Count frequencies: 12:1, 13:1, 14:2, 15:3, 16:1.
Step 2: Highest frequency = 3 for 15 → Mode = 15.
Answer: 15
Example 3 (Ungrouped Median – odd number)
Arrange: 20, 25, 30, 35, 40. Find the median.
Step 1: Number of values = 5 (odd).
Step 2: Middle position = (5+1)/2 = 3rd value = 30.
Answer: 30
Example 4 (Ungrouped Median – even number)
Arrange: 4, 6, 8, 10. Find the median.
Step 1: Number of values = 4 (even).
Step 2: Middle positions = 2nd and 3rd: 6 and 8.
Step 3: Average = (6 + 8)/2 = 7.
Answer: 7
Example 5 (Grouped Mean)
Marks | 0–9 | 10–19 | 20–29 | 30–39
Freq | 4 | 6 | 8 | 2
Calculate the mean.
Step 1: Midpoints: (0+9)/2 = 4.5, 14.5, 24.5, 34.5.
Step 2: fx = 4×4.5=18, 6×14.5=87, 8×24.5=196, 2×34.5=69.
Step 3: Σfx = 18+87+196+69 = 370.
Step 4: Σf = 4+6+8+2 = 20.
Step 5: Mean = 370 / 20 = 18.5.
Answer: 18.5
Example 6 (Grouped Mode)
Using the same table as Example 5. Find the mode.
Step 1: Modal class = 20–29 (highest f=8).
Step 2: L = 20, f₁=8, f₀=6, f₂=2, h=10.
Step 3: Mode = 20 + [(8–6)/(2×8 –6–2)] ×10 = 20 + [2/ (16–8)] ×10 = 20 + (2/8)×10 = 20 + 2.5 = 22.5.
Answer: 22.5
Example 7 (Grouped Median)
Using the same table. Find the median.
Step 1: N=20, N/2=10.
Step 2: Cum. freq: 4 (0–9), 10 (up to 19), 18 (up to 29), 20 (up to 39).
Step 3: Median class = 10–19 (first cum ≥10).
Step 4: L=10, CF before=4, f=6, h=10.
Step 5: Median = 10 + [(10–4)/6] ×10 = 10 + (6/6)×10 = 10 + 10 = 20.
Answer: 20
Example 8 (Grouped – mixed)
Class: 1–5 (f=3), 6–10 (f=5), 11–15 (f=7), 16–20 (f=5)
(a) Mean (b) Mode (c) Median
Step 1: Midpoints: 3, 8, 13, 18.
Step 2: Σfx = 3×3=9, 5×8=40, 7×13=91, 5×18=90 = 230.
Step 3: Σf = 20 → Mean = 230/20 = 11.5.
Step 4: Modal class = 11–15 (f=7).
Step 5: L=11, f₁=7, f₀=5, f₂=5, h=5 → Mode = 11 + [(7–5)/(14–5–5)]×5 = 11 + (2/4)×5 = 11 + 2.5 = 13.5.
Step 6: N/2=10, cum freq: 3,8,15,20 → median class 11–15.
Step 7: L=11, CF=8, f=7, h=5 → Median = 11 + [(10–8)/7]×5 = 11 + (2/7)×5 ≈ 11 + 1.43 ≈ 12.43.
Answer: Mean 11.5, Mode 13.5, Median ≈12.43
Example 9 (JAMB-style MCQ type)
The mean of the numbers 3, 6, 4, x, 7 is 5. Find x.
Step 1: Sum = 3+6+4+x+7 = 20 + x.
Step 2: Mean = (20 + x)/5 = 5 → 20 + x = 25 → x = 5.
Answer: 5
Example 10 (Advanced grouped – common in JAMB)
Class interval: 20–24 (f=5), 25–29 (f=8), 30–34 (f=12), 35–39 (f=5)
Find (i) mean (ii) modal mark (iii) median mark.
Step 1: Mid: 22, 27, 32, 37.
Step 2: Σfx = 5×22=110, 8×27=216, 12×32=384, 5×37=185 = 895.
Step 3: Σf=30 → Mean = 895/30 ≈ 29.83.
Step 4: Modal class 30–34 (f=12).
Step 5: L=30, f₁=12, f₀=8, f₂=5, h=5 → Mode = 30 + [(12–8)/(24–8–5)]×5 = 30 + (4/11)×5 ≈ 30 + 1.82 ≈ 31.82.
Step 6: N/2=15, cum freq:5,13,25,30 → median class 30–34.
Step 7: L=30, CF=13, f=12, h=5 → Median = 30 + [(15–13)/12]×5 = 30 + (2/12)×5 ≈ 30 + 0.83 ≈ 30.83.
Answer: Mean ≈29.83, Mode ≈31.82, Median ≈30.83

(b) Cumulative Frequency & Use of Ogive

Cumulative Frequency (CF) = running total of frequencies (add from the top).
It tells “how many are less than or equal to this value”.

Ogive = graph of cumulative frequency plotted against upper class boundaries.
It is an S-shaped curve. In JAMB exam they give the graph; you just read it.

How to read Ogive (very important for JAMB):

  • Median → CF = N/2
  • Lower Quartile (Q₁) → CF = N/4
  • Upper Quartile (Q₃) → CF = 3N/4
  • kth Percentile → CF = (k/100) × N

Draw horizontal line from the CF value to the curve, then vertical line down to the x-axis to read the value.

10 JAMB-Style Examples for (b) with Solutions

Example 1 (Construct CF table)
Marks: 0–9 (4), 10–19 (6), 20–29 (8), 30–39 (2)
Construct the cumulative frequency table.
Step 1: Upper limits: 9, 19, 29, 39.
Step 2: CF: 4 (up to 9), 4+6=10 (up to 19), 10+8=18 (up to 29), 18+2=20 (up to 39).
Answer: CF table as above
Example 2 (Median from CF)
Total N=40. From the ogive, the median is the value at CF=20. If the curve crosses at 25 on x-axis, what is the median?
Step 1: Draw horizontal from CF=20 to curve.
Step 2: Vertical down to x-axis = 25.
Step 3: Median = 25.
Answer: 25
Example 3 (Quartiles)
N=100 students. On the ogive, CF=25 gives 45 marks, CF=50 gives 62 marks, CF=75 gives 78 marks.
Find Q₁, median, Q₃.
Step 1: Q₁ at CF=25 = 45.
Step 2: Median at CF=50 = 62.
Step 3: Q₃ at CF=75 = 78.
Answer: Q₁=45, Median=62, Q₃=78
Example 4 (Percentile)
Using Example 3 above, find the 36th percentile.
Step 1: CF for 36th percentile = (36/100) × 100 = 36.
Step 2: Read mark at CF=36 on ogive, suppose 52.
Step 3: 36th percentile = 52.
Answer: 52
Example 5 (Full ogive question – common in JAMB)
50 students, scores grouped. N/2 = 25. From the given ogive the point at CF=25 is 48.
What is the median score?
Step 1: Median at CF=25.
Step 2: Read from ogive = 48.
Answer: 48
Example 6
From an ogive of 200 candidates, the 80th percentile is read as 65 marks. How many candidates scored 65 or less?
Step 1: 80th percentile means 80% scored ≤65.
Step 2: 80% of 200 = 0.8 × 200 = 160.
Answer: 160
Example 7 (Construct + read)
Class: 10–14(3), 15–19(7), 20–24(12), 25–29(8)
Draw CF table and state the CF value for median.
Step 1: CF: 3 (up to 14), 3+7=10 (up to 19), 10+12=22 (up to 24), 22+8=30 (up to 29).
Step 2: N=30, N/2=15 → CF for median = 15 (in 20–24 class).
Answer: CF table as above, median CF=15
Example 8
N=80. Q₁ is at CF=20, Q₃ at CF=60. Inter-quartile range = Q₃ – Q₁. If Q₁=35, Q₃=65, what is IQR?
Step 1: Read Q₁ at CF=20 = 35.
Step 2: Read Q₃ at CF=60 = 65.
Step 3: IQR = 65 – 35 = 30.
Answer: 30
Example 9 (JAMB 2019 style)
Cumulative frequency curve shows distribution of 50 students. Find the 36th percentile score (read from graph as 42).
Step 1: 36th percentile CF = (36/100) × 50 = 18.
Step 2: Read value at CF=18 on ogive = 42.
Answer: 42
Example 10 (Combined)
Using the 800 candidates marks example that appears often:
From the ogive, the 50th percentile (median) is read as 47 marks.
What does this mean?
Step 1: 50th percentile CF = 400 (50% of 800).
Step 2: Half the candidates (400) scored 47 marks or less.
Answer: 400 candidates scored ≤47

Quick Revision Tips for JAMB

  • Always arrange ungrouped data first.
  • For grouped, always make midpoint and cumulative frequency columns.
  • In the exam, when they give an ogive graph, just use a ruler: horizontal from CF value → curve → down to x-axis.
  • Practice drawing one ogive at home – it takes 2 minutes once you know upper boundaries.
