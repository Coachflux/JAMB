Quick Recap – Easy Understanding

Rate of Change: Derivative = instantaneous rate of change.
If s = distance, ds/dt = velocity.
Related rates: When quantities are connected (e.g. area ↔ radius), differentiate the relation w.r.t. time t using chain rule.
Example equation: A = πr² → dA/dt = 2πr dr/dt

Maxima and Minima: Find critical points: dy/dx = 0
Nature: Second derivative test
d²y/dx² > 0 → minimum
d²y/dx² < 0 → maximum
Word problems: Express quantity (profit, area, volume) in terms of one variable, then optimize.

(a) Rate of Change

10 JAMB-Style Examples with Solutions

1. (Similar to JAMB 2004)
The volume V of a sphere is V = (4/3)πr³. Find the rate of change of V with respect to r when r = 3 cm.
Step 1: dV/dr = d/dt [(4/3)πr³] = 4πr².
Step 2: At r = 3: 4π(9) = 36π.
Answer: 36π
2. A particle moves so that displacement s = 4t³ – 6t² + 5t + 2 (in metres). Find its velocity at t = 1 s.
Step 1: Velocity v = ds/dt = 12t² – 12t + 5.
Step 2: At t = 1: 12(1) – 12(1) + 5 = 12 – 12 + 5 = 5 m/s.
Answer: 5 m/s
3. (Common JAMB pattern – area increasing)
The area of a circle increases at 4 cm²/s. Find dr/dt when the radius r = 5 cm (take π ≈ 3.14).
Step 1: A = πr² → dA/dt = 2πr dr/dt.
Step 2: 4 = 2 × 3.14 × 5 × dr/dt.
Step 3: 4 = 31.4 dr/dt → dr/dt ≈ 4 / 31.4 ≈ 0.127 cm/s ≈ 0.128 cm/s.
Answer: 0.128 cm/s
4. The side of a square increases at 2 cm/s. Find the rate of increase of area when side = 8 cm.
Step 1: A = x² → dA/dt = 2x dx/dt.
Step 2: At x = 8, dx/dt = 2: 2(8)(2) = 32 cm²/s.
Answer: 32 cm²/s
5. Given s = t⁴ – 8t³ + 12t², find the acceleration when t = 2.
Step 1: v = ds/dt = 4t³ – 24t² + 24t.
Step 2: a = dv/dt = 12t² – 48t + 24.
Step 3: At t = 2: 12(4) – 48(2) + 24 = 48 – 96 + 24 = –24 units/s².
Answer: –24 units/s²
6. (Classic related rates – cone)
Water flows into a conical tank at 20 cm³/s. The cone has radius r = h/3 (height h). Find dh/dt when h = 9 cm.
Step 1: V = (1/3)πr²h = (1/3)π(h/3)²h = (π/27)h³.
Step 2: dV/dt = (π/9)h² dh/dt = 20.
Step 3: At h = 9: (π/9)(81) dh/dt = 20 → 9π dh/dt = 20 → dh/dt = 20/(9π) cm/s.
Answer: 20/(9π) cm/s
7. Radius of a balloon increases at 0.4 cm/s. Find dV/dt when r = 10 cm (sphere).
Step 1: V = (4/3)πr³ → dV/dt = 4πr² dr/dt.
Step 2: At r = 10, dr/dt = 0.4: 4π(100)(0.4) = 160π cm³/s.
Answer: 160π cm³/s
8. (Ladder problem – very common)
A 17 m ladder slides down a wall. Base moves away at 1.5 m/s. Find how fast the top descends when base is 8 m from wall.
Step 1: x² + y² = 17² = 289.
Step 2: 2x dx/dt + 2y dy/dt = 0.
Step 3: At x = 8, y = √(289 – 64) = √225 = 15.
Step 4: 2(8)(1.5) + 2(15) dy/dt = 0 → 24 + 30 dy/dt = 0 → dy/dt = –24/30 = –0.8 m/s.
Answer: –0.8 m/s
9. Volume of cube increases at 54 cm³/s. Find rate of side increase when side = 6 cm.
Step 1: V = x³ → dV/dt = 3x² dx/dt.
Step 2: 54 = 3(36) dx/dt → 54 = 108 dx/dt → dx/dt = 0.5 cm/s.
Answer: 0.5 cm/s
10. If y = 5x⁴ – 12x³ + 8x², find dy/dx at x = 1.
Step 1: dy/dx = 20x³ – 36x² + 16x.
Step 2: At x = 1: 20(1) – 36(1) + 16(1) = 20 – 36 + 16 = 0.
Answer: 0

(b) Maxima and Minima

10 JAMB-Style Examples with Solutions

1. (JAMB 2009 exact type)
What value of x gives maximum for y = x(6 – x)?
Step 1: y = 6x – x².
Step 2: dy/dx = 6 – 2x = 0 → x = 3.
Step 3: d²y/dx² = –2 < 0 → maximum at x = 3.
Answer: 3
2. (JAMB 2008 similar)
Find minimum value of y = x² + 4x + 7.
Step 1: dy/dx = 2x + 4 = 0 → x = –2.
Step 2: d²y/dx² = 2 > 0 → minimum.
Step 3: y at x=–2: 4 – 8 + 7 = 3.
Answer: 3
3. A rectangle has perimeter 80 m. Find dimensions for maximum area.
Step 1: Perimeter 2(l + w) = 80 → l + w = 40 → w = 40 – l.
Step 2: A = l w = l(40 – l) = 40l – l².
Step 3: dA/dl = 40 – 2l = 0 → l = 20 m, w = 20 m.
Step 4: d²A/dl² = –2 < 0 → maximum (square).
Answer: 20 m by 20 m
4. Profit P = 120x – 2x² – 400 from selling x items. Find number of items for max profit.
Step 1: dP/dx = 120 – 4x = 0 → x = 30.
Step 2: d²P/dx² = –4 < 0 → maximum at 30 items.
Answer: 30 items
5. Find maximum value of y = 8x – x² – 10.
Step 1: dy/dx = 8 – 2x = 0 → x = 4.
Step 2: d²y/dx² = –2 < 0 → maximum.
Step 3: y = 8(4) – 16 – 10 = 32 – 16 – 10 = 6.
Answer: 6
6. y = x³ – 6x² + 9x + 2. Find coordinates of maximum and minimum points.
Step 1: dy/dx = 3x² – 12x + 9 = 0 → x² – 4x + 3 = 0 → (x–1)(x–3)=0 → x=1 or 3.
Step 2: d²y/dx² = 6x – 12.
Step 3: At x=1: 6–12 = –6 < 0 → max, y=1–6+9+2=6.
Step 4: At x=3: 18–12=6 > 0 → min, y=27–54+27+2=2.
Answer: Max (1,6), Min (3,2)
7. A company’s cost function is C(x) = x² + 40x + 500, revenue R(x) = 80x. Find output for max profit.
Step 1: P = R – C = 80x – x² – 40x – 500 = –x² + 40x – 500.
Step 2: dP/dx = –2x + 40 = 0 → x = 20.
Step 3: d²P/dx² = –2 < 0 → maximum at x=20.
Answer: 20
8. Find turning points of y = 2x³ – 9x² + 12x – 5 and their nature.
Step 1: dy/dx = 6x² – 18x + 12 = 0 → x² – 3x + 2 = 0 → (x–1)(x–2)=0 → x=1 or 2.
Step 2: d²y/dx² = 12x – 18.
Step 3: At x=1: 12–18 = –6 < 0 → max.
Step 4: At x=2: 24–18 = 6 > 0 → min.
Answer: Max at x=1, Min at x=2
9. The function y = –4x² + 24x – 35 has a maximum value. Find it.
Step 1: dy/dx = –8x + 24 = 0 → x = 3.
Step 2: d²y/dx² = –8 < 0 → maximum.
Step 3: y = –4(9) + 24(3) – 35 = –36 + 72 – 35 = 1.
Answer: 1
10. A box (open top, square base) has volume 32 cm³. Find base side for minimum surface area.
Step 1: Base x, height h: x² h = 32 → h = 32/x².
Step 2: Surface A = x² + 4 x h = x² + 4x (32/x²) = x² + 128/x.
Step 3: dA/dx = 2x – 128/x² = 0 → 2x³ = 128 → x³ = 64 → x = 4 cm.
Step 4: d²A/dx² = 2 + 256/x³ > 0 at x=4 → minimum.
Answer: 4 cm
