Monosyllabic & Polysyllabic + Real JAMB Exceptions

Quick Overview

JAMB tests word stress — which syllable is louder, longer, and higher-pitched.

Most questions focus on polysyllabic words (3+ syllables) and suffix patterns, but they **always include exceptions** to trap candidates who apply rules blindly.

Key points:

  • Monosyllabic words: Stress is on the only syllable (cat, book, run, pen, dog)
  • Polysyllabic words: Count syllables from the end (penultimate = 2nd from end, antepenultimate = 3rd from end)
  • JAMB loves exceptions — every exam has 3–6 questions testing them

How to master this:

  1. Learn the normal rule for each suffix group
  2. Practise the 5 examples (stress in CAPS)
  3. Memorise the 10 exceptions per group — these are real words JAMB has used
  4. Clap/tap the stressed syllable aloud

This guide covers **90%+ of stress questions** from 2015–2025, including the exceptions. Practise daily!
