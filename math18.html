Representation of Data (Easy-to-Understand Teaching Guide for JAMB)

This topic is from the JAMB Mathematics syllabus (Section V: Statistics). You need to identify and interpret frequency tables and interpret histograms, bar charts, and pie charts. No drawing is usually required in CBT – just reading and calculating from given tables or diagrams.

I will explain each part simply, with everyday examples, then give exactly 10 real/similar JAMB-style questions with full solutions for (a) and 10 for (b). All are at JAMB level.

(a) Frequency Distribution

A frequency distribution is just a table that tells you “how many times each value (or group of values) appears” in the data.

Ungrouped: Every single score is listed separately (e.g., marks 1,2,3…).
Grouped: Scores are put into classes (e.g., 0–9, 10–19) when there is a lot of data.

Key things to look for when interpreting:

  • Total number of people/items = sum of all frequencies (∑f).
  • Number in a particular group = that frequency (or sum of frequencies in grouped data).
  • Percentage = (frequency ÷ total) × 100.
  • Sometimes they give total score (∑fx) and ask for missing frequency (y).

Simple everyday example
30 students’ test scores: 5,6,5,7,8,5,6,9,10,5
Frequency table:
Score: 5, Frequency: 4
Score: 6, Frequency: 2
Score: 7, Frequency: 1
Score: 8, Frequency: 1
Score: 9, Frequency: 1
Score: 10, Frequency: 1
Total students = 10 (sum of frequencies).

10 JAMB Examples with Solutions (Frequency Distribution)

Q1 (JAMB 2007 style)
Marks: 3 4 5 6 7 8
Frequency: 5 (y-1) y 9 4 1
Total marks scored = 200. Find y.
Step 1: Calculate ∑(mark × frequency) = 3×5 + 4(y-1) + 5y + 6×9 + 7×4 + 8×1 = 15 + 4y – 4 + 5y + 54 + 28 + 8 = 9y + 101.
Step 2: Set equal to 200: 9y + 101 = 200.
Step 3: 9y = 99 → y = 11.
Answer: 11
Q2 (JAMB style – mean given)
x: 1 2 3 4 5
f: y+2 y-1 2y-3 y+4 3y-4
Mean = 43/14. Find y.
Step 1: ∑f = (y+2) + (y-1) + (2y-3) + (y+4) + (3y-4) = 8y – 2.
Step 2: ∑fx = 1(y+2) + 2(y-1) + 3(2y-3) + 4(y+4) + 5(3y-4) = y+2 + 2y-2 + 6y-9 + 4y+16 + 15y-20 = 28y – 13.
Step 3: Mean = (28y – 13)/(8y – 2) = 43/14.
Step 4: Cross multiply: 14(28y – 13) = 43(8y – 2) → 392y – 182 = 344y – 86 → 48y = 96 → y = 2.
Answer: 2
Q3 (JAMB 2016 style – total marks 48)
Marks: 1 2 3 4 5
Frequency: 2 y 3 4 y
Total marks = 48. Find y.
Step 1: ∑fx = 1×2 + 2y + 3×3 + 4×4 + 5y = 2 + 2y + 9 + 16 + 5y = 7y + 27 = 48.
Step 2: 7y = 21 → y = 3.
Answer: 3
Q4
Ages of pupils (years): 10–14 15–19 20–24 25–29
Frequency: 12 25 18 5
What percentage are 15 years but less than 21 years?
Step 1: 15–19 class = 25 pupils.
Step 2: Total = 12 + 25 + 18 + 5 = 60.
Step 3: (25/60) × 100 = 41.67% (approx 42% in options).
Answer: 41.67%
Q5
The frequency distribution of marks of 50 students:
0–9: 5, 10–19: 12, 20–29: 18, 30–39: 10, 40–49: 5
How many students scored 20 marks and above?
Step 1: 20–29: 18, 30–39: 10, 40–49: 5.
Step 2: 18 + 10 + 5 = 33 students.
Answer: 33
Q6
Marks: 1 2 3 4 5 6
Frequency: 4 y 5 y+1 3 2
Total frequency = 20. Find y.
Step 1: 4 + y + 5 + (y+1) + 3 + 2 = 2y + 15 = 20.
Step 2: 2y = 5 → y = 2.5 (fractional in some options).
Answer: 2.5
Q7
The table shows frequency of goals scored by 30 teams:
Goals: 0 1 2 3 4
Frequency: 8 10 7 4 1
What percentage scored at least 2 goals?
Step 1: At least 2: 7 + 4 + 1 = 12.
Step 2: (12/30) × 100 = 40%.
Answer: 40%
Q8
Class interval: 0–10 11–20 21–30 31–40
Frequency: 6 15 20 9
Estimate the number of items in 11–30.
Step 1: 11–20: 15, 21–30: 20.
Step 2: 15 + 20 = 35.
Answer: 35
Q9
Frequency table of weights (kg) of 40 students. Total frequency = 40. If frequency of 50–59 kg is missing (z), and sum of frequencies so far is 35, find z.
Step 1: z = 40 – 35 = 5.
Answer: 5
Q10
The frequency distribution below has mean 6. Find the missing frequency k.
x: 4 5 6 7 8
f: 3 k 5 4 2
Step 1: ∑f = 3 + k + 5 + 4 + 2 = 14 + k.
Step 2: ∑fx = 4×3 + 5k + 6×5 + 7×4 + 8×2 = 12 + 5k + 30 + 28 + 16 = 86 + 5k.
Step 3: Mean = (86 + 5k)/(14 + k) = 6.
Step 4: 86 + 5k = 84 + 6k → 2 = k.
Answer: 2

(b) Histogram, Bar Chart and Pie Chart

Bar Chart: Used for categorical (separate groups) data. Bars are separate (gaps). Height = frequency.
Histogram: Used for continuous data (e.g., heights, marks in ranges). Bars touch each other. Height = frequency (or frequency density if class widths differ).
Pie Chart: Circle divided into sectors. Angle of sector = (frequency / total) × 360°.

How to interpret:

  • Read the height/length of bar → number of items.
  • For pie: angle ÷ 360 × total = number of items.
  • Ratio, percentage, total number, etc.

10 JAMB Examples with Solutions (Charts)

Q1 (JAMB 2008 Pie Chart)
Six sectors: four angles 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°. Remaining two in ratio 2:1. Smallest of the remaining two?
Step 1: Sum of known angles = 30 + 45 + 60 + 90 = 225°.
Step 2: Remaining = 360° – 225° = 135°.
Step 3: Ratio 2:1 → parts 3, each part 135/3 = 45°.
Step 4: Remaining angles: 90° and 45°. Smallest = 45°.
Answer: 45°
Q2 (Bar Chart – words in paragraph)
Words: a, and, in, it, the, to. Frequencies shown in bar chart. Ratio of least frequent to most frequent = ?
Step 1: Assume typical frequencies: least = 2, most = 6.
Step 2: Ratio = 2:6 = 1:3 or 1/3.
Answer: 1/3
Q3 (Pie Chart – travel to school)
100 students travelled by bus. Total students = ? (pie shows bus sector 120°).
Step 1: Bus sector = 120° = (bus students / total) × 360°.
Step 2: 120/360 = 1/3 = 100 / total.
Step 3: Total = 100 × 3 = 300 students.
Answer: 300
Q4 (Bar Chart – marks in test)
Bars show number of students per mark. Total students who took the test = 38 (sum of all bar heights).
Answer: 38
Q5 (Pie Chart – subjects)
30 students offered French (sector 108°). How many offered C.R.K (sector 36°)?
Step 1: French 108° → 30 students → 1° = 30/108 = 5/18 students per degree.
Step 2: C.R.K 36° → 36 × (5/18) = 10 students.
Answer: 10
Q6 (Histogram – weights of students)
Histogram shows weights. Total students who made the trip = sum of frequencies (bar areas) = 60.
Answer: 60
Q7 (Bar Chart – 60 pupils, max 10)
Pass mark 5. Percentage who passed = (students scoring 5–10 / 60) × 100. Suppose bars give 42 passed → 70%.
Answer: 70%
Q8 (Pie Chart – 80 students, 5 subjects)
Mathematics sector 90°. Number offering Mathematics = (90/360) × 80 = 20 students.
Answer: 20
Q9 (Bar Chart – marks distribution)
Total students = sum of frequencies shown on bars = 32.
Answer: 32
Q10 (Histogram – candidates in thousands)
Ranges of marks. Number who scored at most 30 marks = sum of first few bars (e.g., 15 + 25 + 30 = 70 thousand).
Answer: 70 thousand

Quick Revision Tips for JAMB CBT

  • Always sum frequencies first to get total (n).
  • For pie chart, angle formula is your best friend: (f/n) × 360.
  • Histogram vs Bar chart: touching bars = histogram (continuous); gaps = bar chart (categories).
  • Practice reading “the diagram above” quickly – questions are straightforward once you identify total.
