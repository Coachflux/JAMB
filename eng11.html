Quick Overview

JAMB Oral English tests your ability to identify and pronounce consonant sounds correctly in Received Pronunciation (RP – British standard).

English has 24 consonant phonemes. JAMB often asks:

  • “Which word has the same consonant sound as the underlined one?”
  • “Choose the word with the /k/ sound”
  • Minimal pairs (e.g., thin /θ/ vs tin /t/)

Step-by-step learning method:

  1. Listen to the sound symbol (IPA)
  2. Say the 20 example words slowly and clearly — feel where your tongue/teeth/lips are
  3. Compare similar sounds (e.g. /s/ vs /z/, /θ/ vs /ð/)
  4. Practice minimal pairs from past JAMB questions

All words are common in JAMB. Practise aloud daily — this section gives easy marks if you master the distinctions!

Quick JAMB Revision Tips for Consonants

  • Focus on tricky pairs: /θ/ vs /t/ (thin vs tin), /ð/ vs /d/ (this vs dis), /ʃ/ vs /s/ (ship vs sip), /ʒ/ vs /z/ (measure vs measure)
  • Minimal pairs are key: JAMB loves testing thin/tin, three/tree, very/berry, measure/mesher, etc.
  • Practice aloud: Say the word lists slowly. Record yourself and compare with RP pronunciation (BBC, Cambridge dictionaries)
  • Past questions: 2018–2025 often repeat /θ/, /ð/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /ŋ/, /r/ (non-rhotic linking)
