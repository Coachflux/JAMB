4. Sets

Abumchukwu, sets are very easy. A set is just a group of things that belong together. We use capital letters for the set name and curly braces { } for the members. No repeats allowed, and order does not matter.

Example: Your favourite fruits = {mango, orange, banana}

(a) Types of Sets

Very easy types to remember

  • Empty set (∅ or {}): No members at all
  • Universal set (U): All possible things in the question
  • Complement (A'): Things in U that are NOT in A
  • Subset (A ⊆ B): Every member of A is in B
  • Finite set: You can count the members
  • Infinite set: Goes on forever (cannot finish counting)
  • Disjoint sets: No common members (A ∩ B = ∅)
  • Singleton set: Only one member, e.g. {5}

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: Which is an empty set?
Step 1: Even primes greater than 2?
Step 2: Only even prime is 2. No even prime > 2.
Step 3: The set has no members → empty set.
Answer: Empty set
Example 2: U = {1,2,3,4,5,6}, A = {1,3,5}. Find A'.
Step 1: A' = everything in U that is not in A.
Step 2: Not in A = 2,4,6.
Step 3: A' = {2,4,6}
Answer: {2,4,6}
Example 3: Is ∅ a subset of every set?
Step 1: ∅ has no members.
Step 2: For subset, all members of ∅ must be in the other set.
Step 3: There are no members to check → always true.
Step 4: Yes.
Answer: Yes
Example 4: Classify {1,2,3,...} (natural numbers).
Step 1: Can we count all members?
Step 2: No — it never ends.
Step 3: Infinite set.
Answer: Infinite
Example 5: A = {2,4,6}, B = {1,3,5}. Are they disjoint?
Step 1: Disjoint means no common members.
Step 2: Look at both sets — no number appears in both.
Step 3: Yes, disjoint.
Answer: Yes
Example 6: n(U) = 50, n(A) = 30. Find n(A').
Step 1: A' = members not in A.
Step 2: n(A') = n(U) - n(A)
Step 3: 50 - 30 = 20
Answer: 20
Example 7: P = {a,b,c}, Q = {b,c,d}. Is P a proper subset of Q?
Step 1: Proper subset means all members of P are in Q and P ≠ Q.
Step 2: a is in P but not in Q.
Step 3: Not even a subset.
Answer: No
Example 8: Cardinality of vowels in "MATHEMATICS".
Step 1: Vowels = A, E, I
Step 2: 3 different vowels.
Step 3: n = 3
Answer: 3
Example 9: What symbol means "A is subset of B"?
Step 1: ⊆ means subset (can be equal)
Step 2: ⊂ means proper subset (not equal)
Answer: ⊆
Example 10: A and B disjoint, n(A)=12, n(B)=18. Find n(A ∪ B).
Step 1: Disjoint → n(A ∩ B) = 0
Step 2: n(A ∪ B) = 12 + 18 - 0 = 30
Answer: 30

Step 1: Look at each option.

Step 2: Yes — both 1 and 2 are there.
Step 3: Yes.
Answer: Yes

Step 1: ∅ is empty set.
Step 2: It has no members.
Step 3: n(∅) = 0
Answer: 0
JAMB 2017: n(U) = 40, n(A) = 25. Find n(A').
Step 1: n(A') = n(U) - n(A)
Step 2: 40 - 25 = 15

JAMB 2018: A = {vowels in "RHYTHM"}. What type of set is A?
Step 1: "RHYTHM" has no vowels.
Step 2: A has no members.
Step 3: Empty set.
Answer: Empty set
JAMB 2019: Is {a} a subset of {a,b,c}?
Step 1: Check if a is in {a,b,c}.
Step 2: Yes.
Step 3: Yes, subset.
Answer: Yes
JAMB 2020: n(A) = 15, n(B) = 20, n(A ∪ B) = 30. Find n(A ∩ B).
Step 1: Formula: n(A ∪ B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A ∩ B)
Step 2: 30 = 15 + 20 - n(A ∩ B)
Step 3: n(A ∩ B) = 35 - 30 = 5
Answer: 5

(b) Algebra of Sets (Operations & Symbols)

Easy operations

  • Union (A ∪ B): members in A or B or both
  • Intersection (A ∩ B): members in both
  • Difference (A − B): members in A but not in B
  • Complement (A'): members in U but not in A

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: P = {1,2,3}, Q = {2,4,6}. Find P ∪ Q
Step 1: Union = all members from both sets.
Step 2: No repeats.
Step 3: {1,2,3,4,6}
Answer: {1,2,3,4,6}
Example 2: Same P and Q. Find P ∩ Q
Step 1: Intersection = members in both.
Step 2: Only 2.
Step 3: {2}
Answer: {2}
Example 3: Find P − Q
Step 1: Members in P but not in Q.
Step 2: 1 and 3.
Step 3: {1,3}
Answer: {1,3}
Example 4: n(A) = 15, n(B) = 12, n(A ∩ B) = 7. Find n(A ∪ B)
Step 1: Formula: n(A ∪ B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A ∩ B)
Step 2: 15 + 12 - 7 = 20
Answer: 20
Example 5: A = {1,2,3,4}, B = {3,4,5,6}. Find A − B
Step 1: Members in A but not in B.
Step 2: 1 and 2.
Step 3: {1,2}
Answer: {1,2}
Example 6: U = {1 to 10}, A = {2,4,6,8,10}. Find A'
Step 1: A' = members in U not in A.
Step 2: Odd numbers = {1,3,5,7,9}
Answer: {1,3,5,7,9}
Example 7: Simplify A ∪ (A ∩ B)
Step 1: Anything union with a part of itself is the whole thing.
Step 2: A ∪ (anything inside A) = A
Answer: A
Example 8: Simplify (A ∪ B) ∩ A
Step 1: Intersection with A means only keep members in A.
Step 2: (A ∪ B) ∩ A = A
Answer: A
Example 9: If A ⊆ B, simplify A ∪ B
Step 1: All members of A are already in B.
Step 2: Adding A to B doesn't add anything new.
Step 3: A ∪ B = B
Answer: B
Example 10: If A ∩ B = ∅, simplify A − B
Step 1: No common members.
Step 2: A − B = all members of A (since none are in B).
Step 3: A − B = A
Answer: A

JAMB Past Questions for (b) Algebra of Sets (8 questions with step-by-step)

JAMB 2000: P = {1,2,u,v,w,x}, Q = {2,3,u,v,w,5,6,y}, R = {2,3,4,v,x,y}. Find (P − Q) ∩ R
Step 1: P − Q = members in P but not in Q = {1,x}
Step 2: {1,x} ∩ R = members common to both = {x}
Step 3: Answer = {x}
Answer: {x}
JAMB 2008: X = {n² + 1 : n = 0,2,3}, Y = {n + 1 : n = 2,3,5}. Find X ∩ Y
Step 1: X = {0²+1, 2²+1, 3²+1} = {1,5,10}
Step 2: Y = {2+1, 3+1, 5+1} = {3,4,6}
Step 3: No common members → ∅
Answer: ∅
JAMB 2012: Simplify (A ∪ B) ∩ (A ∪ B')
Step 1: Distributive law: A ∪ (B ∩ B')
Step 2: B ∩ B' = ∅
Step 3: A ∪ ∅ = A
Answer: A
JAMB 2015: n(A) = 20, n(B) = 15, n(A ∩ B) = 5. Find n(A ∪ B)
Step 1: n(A ∪ B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A ∩ B)
Step 2: 20 + 15 - 5 = 30
Answer: 30
JAMB 2017: If A ⊆ B, simplify A ∩ B
Step 1: All members of A are already in B.
Step 2: A ∩ B = A
Answer: A
JAMB 2018: Simplify A − (A ∩ B)
Step 1: A − (A ∩ B) = members in A but not in A ∩ B.
Step 2: That means members in A but not in B.
Step 3: A − B
Answer: A − B
JAMB 2019: If A ∩ B = ∅, simplify A ∪ B
Step 1: No common members.
Step 2: A ∪ B = all members of A plus all members of B.
Step 3: No overlap to remove.
Answer: n(A) + n(B)
JAMB 2020: Simplify (A' ∪ B')'
Step 1: De Morgan’s law: (A' ∪ B')' = A ∩ B
Step 2: Answer = A ∩ B
Answer: A ∩ B

(c) Venn Diagrams and Their Applications

Venn diagrams are pictures with circles that show how sets overlap. They make word problems very easy.

Left circle = A only, right = B only, middle = both (A ∩ B).

3-set Venn has 7 inner regions + outside. Label them carefully.

10 Easy Examples with Venn diagrams

Example 1: 30 students, 18 like football, 15 like basketball, 8 like both. How many like at least one?
Step 1: Draw 2-set Venn.
Step 2: Only football = 18 - 8 = 10
Step 3: Only basketball = 15 - 8 = 7
Step 4: Both = 8
Step 5: Total = 10 + 7 + 8 = 25
Answer: 25
Example 2: Same class. How many like neither?
Step 1: Total = 30
Step 2: At least one = 25
Step 3: Neither = 30 - 25 = 5
Answer: 5
Example 3: 50 students, 35 read novels, 28 read comics, 12 read both. How many read only novels?
Step 1: Only novels = 35 - 12 = 23
Answer: 23

JAMB Past Questions for (c) Venn Diagrams (8 questions with step-by-step)

JAMB 1999: 12 maize, 10 yam, 14 plantain; 5 plantain+maize, 4 yam+maize, 2 yam+plantain only, 3 all three. How many women?
Step 1: Draw 3-set Venn diagram.
Step 2: Centre (all three) = 3
Step 3: Only yam+plantain = 2
Step 4: Only plantain+maize = 5 - 3 = 2
Step 5: Only yam+maize = 4 - 3 = 1
Step 6: Only maize = 12 - (1 + 3 + 2) = 6
Step 7: Only yam = 10 - (1 + 3 + 2) = 4
Step 8: Only plantain = 14 - (2 + 3 + 2) = 7
Step 9: Total = 6 + 4 + 7 + 1 + 2 + 2 + 3 = 25
Answer: 25
JAMB 2000: 40 students, 30 buy Maths, 20 English, 10 both. How many buy at least one subject?
Step 1: n(M ∪ E) = n(M) + n(E) - n(M ∩ E)
Step 2: 30 + 20 - 10 = 40
Answer: 40
JAMB 2008: 50 readers, 40 Champion, 30 Guardian (all read at least one). How many read both?
Step 1: n(C ∪ G) = 50
Step 2: 50 = 40 + 30 - both
Step 3: both = 70 - 50 = 20
Answer: 20
JAMB 2012: In a class of 60, 45 like tea, 38 like coffee, 20 like both. How many like neither?
Step 1: At least one = 45 + 38 - 20 = 63 - 20 = 43
Step 2: Neither = 60 - 43 = 17
Answer: 17
JAMB 2015: n(A) = 25, n(B) = 20, n(C) = 15, n(A∩B) = 10, n(A∩C) = 8, n(B∩C) = 6, n(A∩B∩C) = 4. Find n(A ∪ B ∪ C).
Step 1: Formula for 3 sets: n(A∪B∪C) = n(A) + n(B) + n(C) - n(A∩B) - n(A∩C) - n(B∩C) + n(A∩B∩C)
Step 2: 25 + 20 + 15 - 10 - 8 - 6 + 4 = 60 - 24 + 4 = 40
Answer: 40
JAMB 2017: 100 people, 70 like rice, 55 like beans, 30 like both. How many like only rice?
Step 1: Only rice = n(rice) - both = 70 - 30 = 40
Answer: 40
JAMB 2019: 80 students, 50 play football, 40 play basketball, 15 play both. How many play exactly one game?
Step 1: Only football = 50 - 15 = 35
Step 2: Only basketball = 40 - 15 = 25
Step 3: Exactly one = 35 + 25 = 60
Answer: 60
JAMB 2020: In a survey of 120 people, 85 like tea, 70 like coffee, 45 like both. How many like neither?
Step 1: At least one = 85 + 70 - 45 = 110
Step 2: Neither = 120 - 110 = 10
Answer: 10
