🧠 One-line recall

Chapter 3 is Bepo’s “reality check”: migration pays more, but it can also reduce status and increase struggle.

1️⃣ Context & Focus of the Chapter

After deciding to join Seri and the children in the UK, Mr. Bepo begins to listen carefully to migration tales from Nigerians in the UK, USA, and Canada.

  • His decision is firm, but he wants to understand “what is really there”
  • He learns from success stories and warning stories

2️⃣ Payment Systems Abroad vs Nigeria

Bepo discovers that payment abroad can be hourly/daily/weekly/monthly—unlike Nigeria’s fixed monthly salary system.

Why he likes the hourly system

  • Flexibility to change jobs easily
  • Employers can monitor performance closely
  • Earn more by working extra hours

3️⃣ Success Stories vs Struggle Stories

  • Some migrants earn a lot (figures sound huge when converted to naira)
  • But many face downgrading because Nigerian qualifications may not fully transfer

Downgrading examples: university lecturers driving Uber/taxis; professionals doing night office cleaning.

4️⃣ The High Cost of Living Abroad

Bepo notices that high income abroad can be reduced by expenses.

  • Taxes
  • High rent
  • Maintenance and bills
  • General cost of living
🧠 Reality check line

Higher pay doesn’t always mean more comfort, because expenses are also higher.

5️⃣ “Japa” Phenomenon & Brain Drain

The chapter presents migration as a mass movement driven by Nigeria’s economic and security challenges.

  • Economic hardship
  • Insecurity
  • Poor infrastructure
  • Lack of opportunities
  • Brain drain (e.g., doctors leaving every year)

6️⃣ Bepo’s Identity Conflict (“Principoo”)

Bepo loves his identity at Stardom Schools—his routines, respect, and the feeling of being useful.

  • Students call him “Principoo”
  • He greets students at the gate (Mondays/Wednesdays)
  • Signature posture: left hand in pocket while speaking
  • Signature phrase: “other things being equal…”

Key quote(s) mentioned in this chapter

“If you say education is too expensive, try ignorance!”
— Mr. Bepo Adewale (motivational quote used with students)
“other things being equal…”
— Mr. Bepo’s signature phrase

Identity fear

  • He worries about becoming “a nobody trying to become a somebody” abroad (as described by a returned doctor friend).

7️⃣ Who Shares the Migration Tales?

  • Seri (his wife) shares experiences and reassurance
  • Others like a former colleague Sola also share stories
  • Bepo believes transition may be smoother because Seri is already settled

8️⃣ Themes in Chapter 3

  • Migration as temptation (“enticement”) vs reality
  • Identity and status crisis for professionals
  • Brain drain and national loss
  • Economic survival vs emotional attachment
  • Family reunion vs personal purpose
