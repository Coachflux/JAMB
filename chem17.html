Introduction – What is a Chemical Industry?

A chemical industry is any industry that uses chemical reactions to convert raw materials (natural substances) into useful products.

These industries are the backbone of Nigeria’s economy — fertilizers for farming, petrochemicals for plastics, pharmaceuticals for health.

Step 1: Classification of Chemical Industries by Products

Chemical industries are grouped according to what they produce.

Type Products Examples
Basic (heavy) chemicals Acids, alkalis, salts in huge quantities H₂SO₄, NaOH, NH₃, HCl
Petrochemical industries Plastics, synthetic rubber, fibres Polythene, PVC, benzene
Fertilizer industries Urea, ammonium nitrate Superphosphate, NPK
Fine chemicals Drugs, dyes, perfumes Aspirin, penicillin
Polymer/plastic industries Polythene, nylon PVC, synthetic fibres

Easy way to remember: Heavy chemicals = bulk raw materials for other industries. Everything else = finished or specialty products.

Step 2: Distinction Between Fine and Heavy Chemicals

Feature Heavy Chemicals Fine Chemicals
Quantity produced Very large (bulk) Small quantities
Purity Lower (industrial grade) Very high purity
Cost Cheap Expensive
Use Raw materials for other industries Specialty (drugs, dyes, reagents)
Examples H₂SO₄, NaOH, NH₃ Aspirin, penicillin, lab reagents

Mnemonic: Heavy = “Huge tonnage, low price”; Fine = “Few bottles, high price”.

Step 3: Raw Materials for Major Industries

Industry Raw Materials
Sulphuric acid Sulphur or iron pyrite
Ammonia / Fertilizer Nitrogen from air + Hydrogen from natural gas
Chlor-alkali (NaOH + Cl₂) Brine (concentrated NaCl solution)
Petrochemical / Plastic Crude oil or natural gas
Glass Sand + limestone + soda ash

Quick tip: Almost every heavy chemical starts from air, water, salt, sulphur, or petroleum.

Step 4: Relevance of Each Industry

  • Fertilizer industry → Food security (more crop yield)
  • Pharmaceutical industry → Better health care (life-saving drugs)
  • Petrochemical/plastic industry → Packaging, building materials, clothing
  • Soap/detergent industry → Hygiene and cleanliness

General relevance: employment, economic growth, raw materials for other sectors, improved quality of life.

Step 5: Link to Biotechnology in Industry

Biotechnology = using living things (microbes, enzymes, genetically modified organisms) in chemical processes.

  • Fermentation: Yeast converts sugar → ethanol (alcohol, beer, wine)
  • Antibiotic production: Penicillium mould produces penicillin
  • Enzyme technology: Protease in detergents, amylase in food industry
  • Biofuels: Microbes ferment biomass → bioethanol

Why it matters: cheaper, cleaner, more specific than traditional methods.
