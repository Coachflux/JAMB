🧠 One-line recall

“Normal assembly → principal freezes → tears for 30+ minutes → escorted to office → rumours explode.”

1️⃣ Setting & Opening Mood

The chapter opens at Stardom Schools, an elite school in Lekki, Lagos, during a routine morning assembly in the school hall/chapel.

  • Students expect the usual pep talk from the principal
  • The chapel prefect Angel handles the microphone
  • School routines: anthem, prayers, structured assembly culture

2️⃣ Plot Summary (What Happened in “Dusk”)

Mr. Bepo Adewale (principal) approaches the podium in an unusual, awkward, ponderous way, unlike his normal charismatic self.

Key breakdown moment: he holds the microphone but cannot speak, stares blankly, drops his head, then begins to cry uncontrollably.

  • He attempts to speak several times but fails
  • Tears stream down his face; microphone slips and clatters
  • He sobs for over 30 minutes, shaking and devastated
  • Students and staff are shocked; atmosphere becomes tense and puzzled

Likely “atmosphere” answer words

  • Shocking
  • Tense
  • Confusing
  • Emotional
  • Suspenseful

3️⃣ Staff Reaction & Immediate Response

  • Mrs. Grace Apeh (Vice Principal) steps forward, questions him, tries to console him
  • Nurse Titi offers a handkerchief and helps
  • Other staff (e.g., Mr. Oyelana, Mr. Justus Israel, Mrs. Oyelana) assist
  • The principal is escorted off the podium to his office
  • Mrs. Apeh dismisses assembly early and sends students to class
🧠 Quick recall

VP + Nurse = first responders in the school setting.

4️⃣ Rumours, Gossip & Community Dynamics

Even though nobody knows the cause, rumours spread quickly among staff and parents.

  • Staff gossip and exaggerate events
  • Parents hear more dramatic versions (e.g., rolling on the floor)
  • Mr. Audu (Fine Arts teacher) jokes about the MD, Mrs. Ibidun Gloss, being a “witch/wizard” linked to policy changes

Point: the school behaves like a small society—news travels fast and gets distorted.

5️⃣ Other Details Mentioned (Usually for MCQs)

  • SS1 student Ikenna Egbu gives a presentation about excursion to Jos (Plateau State)
  • He references the 2nd stanza of the National Anthem and Jos’ climate/rocks/tourism
  • School routines: structured prayers (Muslim/Christian) on specific days
