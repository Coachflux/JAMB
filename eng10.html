Quick Overview

JAMB Oral English tests your ability to identify and pronounce vowel sounds correctly in Received Pronunciation (RP – British standard).

There are:

  • 12 Monophthongs (pure single vowels)
  • 8 Diphthongs (gliding two-vowel sounds)
  • 5 Triphthongs (gliding three-vowel sounds)

Step-by-step learning method:

  1. Listen to the sound symbol
  2. Say the 20 example words slowly and clearly
  3. Compare similar sounds (e.g. /ɪ/ vs /iː/)
  4. Practice minimal pairs from past JAMB questions

All words chosen are common in JAMB questions. Practise aloud daily — this section can give you easy marks!

Quick JAMB Revision Tip

  • Focus on distinguishing short vs long (e.g., ship /ɪ/ vs sheep /iː/)
  • Many JAMB questions test minimal pairs or “which word has the same sound as the underlined vowel”
  • Practise with past questions from 2018–2025 (many repeat patterns like /æ/ vs /e/, /ɔɪ/ vs /aɪ/)
  • Memorise 5–10 words per sound first, then test yourself by grouping similar sounds
