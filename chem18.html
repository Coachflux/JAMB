1️⃣ Composition of the Solar System

The solar system is the family of the Sun. It includes:

  • The Sun (a star; ~99.8% of total mass)
  • Planets (8 major)
  • Natural satellites (moons)
  • Asteroids, comets, meteoroids, dwarf planets (e.g., Pluto)

Simple definition: Sun + everything that orbits the Sun.

Solar system: Sun at the center with 8 planets in orbit

2️⃣ List the Planets (8) — In Order from the Sun

  1. Mercury
  2. Venus
  3. Earth
  4. Mars
  5. Jupiter (largest)
  6. Saturn (rings)
  7. Uranus
  8. Neptune

3️⃣ Segments (Spheres) of the Earth

The Earth is divided into 3 main segments:

Segment Description Composition
Atmosphere Thin layer of air surrounding Earth N₂ (78%), O₂ (21%)
Lithosphere Solid rocky part of Earth Rocks, soil, minerals (crust + upper mantle)
Hydrosphere All water on Earth Oceans, rivers, lakes, ice, groundwater (~71% surface)

🧠 Easy memory

Atmosphere = Air • Lithosphere = Land • Hydrosphere = Water.

Earth domains: air (atmosphere), land (lithosphere), water (hydrosphere)

4️⃣ Natural Satellite of the Earth

The Earth has only one natural satellite — the Moon.

It orbits the Earth once every 27.3 days.

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite

5️⃣ Smallest Planet vs Farthest Planet (Important JAMB Note)

Key point

  • There is no single planet that is both the smallest and the farthest from the Sun.
  • Smallest planet: Mercury (also closest to Sun)
  • Farthest planet: Neptune
🧠 Don’t fall for old Pluto trick

Pluto was once taught as smallest & farthest, but it’s now a dwarf planet (2006).
