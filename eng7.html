Word Classes & Sentences

Word Classes and their Sentences

JAMB Use of English – Step-by-step guide

Quick Overview

Word classes (parts of speech) are the foundation of English grammar. JAMB tests them in:

  • Lexis & Structure (sentence completion, error spotting)
  • Concord / subject-verb agreement
  • Phrasal verbs & idioms
  • Comprehension (understanding word function)

There are 8 main word classes. Learn their definitions, common JAMB words, positions, and rules.

1. Nouns

What it is: Names a person, place, thing, animal, idea or quality.

Common JAMB collective / uncountable / always-plural nouns:

  • committee, family, police, luggage, furniture, information, news, mathematics, equipment, advice, baggage, stationery, poultry, cattle, people, scissors, trousers, glasses, economics, physics, measles, athletics, headquarters, government, audience, crowd, team, jury, staff, public, majority, minority, youth, offspring, aircraft, deer, sheep, series, species

Key rules for JAMB:

  • Collective nouns usually take singular verb (committee is…)
  • Uncountable nouns → no “a/an” and no plural “s” (information is…, not informations)
  • Some nouns always plural: police have…, scissors are…, trousers are…

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

The committee is meeting next week.
“Committee” = collective noun → treated as singular unit → verb is
All the information you need is on the website.
“Information” = uncountable → singular verb is, never “informations”
The police have arrested the suspect.
“Police” = always plural → verb have, never “the police has”
His luggage was lost at the airport.
“Luggage” = uncountable → singular verb was, never “luggages”
Mathematics is my favourite subject.
“Mathematics” = uncountable subject → singular verb is

2. Verbs

What it is: Shows action, state or occurrence. Many JAMB questions test phrasal verbs (verb + particle = new meaning).

Common high-frequency phrasal & single verbs in JAMB:

  • prefer, resemble, deliver (of a baby), circumvent, coax, coerce, cash in on, run on (petrol), postpone, break down, give up, look forward to, turn down, put up with, come across, call off, break into, look into, take after, bring about, put off, make up, fall out, get along, run out of, look up to, turn up, carry out, find out, pick up, set up, go on, come up with, back up

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

The thief tried to circumvent the security system.
circumvent = get round / avoid → correct formal verb for JAMB
Ayayi cashed in on our defensive error to score.
cash in on = take advantage of → very common JAMB phrasal verb
The car runs on petrol, not diesel.
run on = use as fuel → typical JAMB usage
She was delivered of a bouncing baby boy yesterday.
deliver of = give birth to (formal JAMB expression)
The match was called off because of rain.
call off = cancel → very frequent in JAMB

3. Adjectives

What it is: Describes or modifies a noun/pronoun.

Common high-frequency adjectives in JAMB:

  • beautiful, repulsive, early, lovely, white, best, more expensive, little, few, affluent, benevolent, candid, frugal, elated, adamant, hostile, lucid, meticulous, sluggish, mischievous, phenomenal, bureaucratic, eccentric, exhilarating, unprecedented, connoisseur, unanimous, irresistible, immaculate, excruciating, inquisitive, unpredictable, sophisticated, obsolete, diligent, ambiguous, audible, skeptical, arrogant, malicious, conspicuous

Positions: Before noun OR after linking verb (is, seems, looks, feels…)

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

She bought a beautiful dress for the party.
Before noun → correct position
Everybody hates him for his repulsive looks.
Before noun → correct
It is more expensive to travel on Friday.
Comparative form after linking verb “is” → correct
That was a lovely evening.
Before noun → correct
Jasmines are white in colour.
After linking verb “are” → correct

4. Adverbs

What it is: Modifies verb, adjective or another adverb (how, when, where, to what extent…).

Common JAMB adverbs:

  • beautifully, pleasantly, quickly, later, early, more, slowly, very, quite, rather, too, extremely, frequently, seldom, always, never, soon, yesterday, here, there, everywhere, upstairs, downstairs, well, badly, hard, fast, almost, nearly, hardly, scarcely, fortunately, unfortunately, honestly, carefully, suddenly, immediately, eventually

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

She sang beautifully on stage.
Modifies verb “sang” → manner adverb
I was pleasantly surprised when she arrived.
Modifies adjective “surprised”
John came later than Peter.
Comparative adverb of time
If we finish our work quickly, we can go to the movies.
Modifies verb “finish” → manner
He runs more quickly than his brother.
Comparative form

5. Pronouns

What it is: Replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

Common JAMB pronouns:

  • you, theirs, it, he, she, they, we, I, me, him, her, us, them, mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, whose, who, whom, which, that, everyone, somebody, anybody, nobody, something, anything, this, that, these, those, one, each, either, neither

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

It happened that our dog is male but theirs are all females.
theirs replaces “their dogs” – plural possessive
Where are you going?
you = second person pronoun
Everyone has his own book.
his agrees with singular “everyone”
The book is mine, not yours.
Possessive pronouns – no noun after them
Neither the teacher nor the students are ready.
Neither…nor construction – verb agrees with nearer subject

6. Prepositions

What it is: Shows relationship of noun/pronoun to other words (time, place, direction…).

Common JAMB prepositions & phrases:

  • under, for, on (radio/petrol), in spite of, beneath, with, by, from, at, in, to, of, about, above, across, against, along, among, around, before, behind, below, beside, between, beyond, during, except, inside, into, near, off, onto, outside, over, since, through, throughout, toward, until, up, upon, within

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

I left my shoes under the kitchen table.
under = position below
We heard the news on the radio.
on = media (radio, TV)
The car runs on petrol, not diesel.
on = fuel source
We went out in spite of the rain.
in spite of = despite (prepositional phrase)
He is beneath his dignity to join the queue.
beneath = below (formal JAMB usage)

7. Conjunctions

What it is: Joins words, phrases or clauses.

Common JAMB conjunctions:

  • but, although, in spite of (phrase), rather than, because, since, for (because), and, or, yet, so, if, unless, while, whereas, even though, as, when, whenever, where, wherever, before, after, until, till, as soon as, provided that, in order that, so that

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

Although the problem was simple, few students solved it.
Although = subordinating conjunction (contrast) → comma after clause
I prefer tea rather than coffee.
rather than = preference
We went out although it was raining.
although = contrast
He ran fast because he was late.
because = reason
Since you are here, let us start the meeting.
Since = reason or time

8. Interjections

What it is: Expresses sudden emotion or feeling.

Common JAMB interjections:

  • Ouch, Wow, Ah, Oh, Hey, Alas, Hurray, Bravo, Oops, Aha, Eh, Well, Gosh, Gee, Yippee, Hurrah, Phew, Ugh, Yay, Boo

5 JAMB-style examples with explanation:

Ouch! That hurts.
Ouch = pain
Wow! That is amazing.
Wow = surprise/admiration
Alas! The team lost the match.
Alas = sorrow/regret
Bravo! You have done well.
Bravo = praise/approval
Phew! I finally finished the exam.
Phew = relief

Rules of Concord (Subject-Verb Agreement) – JAMB Focus

Concord = subject and verb must agree in number and person. JAMB loves testing tricky cases.

15 Key Rules (with examples):

  • Basic: Singular subject → singular verb; plural → plural. The boy plays. / The boys play.
  • Collective nouns: Usually singular. The committee is divided.
  • As well as / with / together with / along with: Ignore extra words. The teacher, as well as students, is here.
  • Either…or / Neither…nor: Agree with nearer subject. Neither boys nor teacher is coming.
  • A number of (plural) vs The number of (singular). A number of students are absent.
  • Indefinite pronouns (everyone, each, somebody…): Singular. Everyone has arrived.
  • Uncountable & pluralia tantum: Singular for news/maths; plural for police/cattle. News is good. / Police have come.
  • More than one: Singular. More than one student has failed.
  • The + adjective (the poor, the rich): Plural. The poor are helped.
  • Amounts / Time / Money / Titles: Singular. Ten thousand naira is enough.
  • Not / Not only…but also: Agree before “not”. The man, not workers, is responsible.
  • Relative pronouns (who/which/that): Agree with antecedent. Boys who play are here.
  • One of / Each of: Singular. One of the boys has gone.
  • None of: Often singular in JAMB. None of the food is left.
  • And compounds: Plural unless one idea. Bread and butter is ready. / My friend and brother has arrived.

20 JAMB-Style Concord Questions with Answers & Explanation

The teacher, as well as his students, ………… working very hard.
A. is B. have been C. are D. were
Answer: A. is
Explanation: Ignore “as well as” → main subject “teacher” singular → verb singular.
The farmer, with his sons, ………… millet.
A. grows B. grow C. were growing D. have been growing
Answer: A. grows
Explanation: Ignore “with his sons” → main subject “farmer” singular.
Chief Owonikoko as well as his men ………… arrived.
A. have B. has C. are D. were
Answer: B. has
Explanation: Main subject “Chief Owonikoko” singular.
The man with his wives ………… coming.
A. were B. are C. is D. been
Answer: C. is
Explanation: Main subject “man” singular.
Julius Berger and Associates ………… not employ technicians.
A. do B. does C. has D. have
Answer: A. do
Explanation: Compound subject with “and” → plural verb.
Either my friends or my brother ………… taken the car out.
A. have B. has C. was D. were
Answer: B. has
Explanation: Agree with nearer subject “brother” (singular).
I was still sleeping when the national news ………… this morning.
A. were broadcasted B. was broadcasted C. was broadcast D. were broadcast
Answer: C. was broadcast
Explanation: “News” = uncountable singular → verb singular.
The man, not his workers, ………… to blame.
A. are B. is C. were D. them
Answer: B. is
Explanation: Ignore “not his workers” → main subject “man” singular.
The number of women ………… more than that of men in every society.
A. are B. is C. were D. been
Answer: B. is
Explanation: “The number of” = singular.
It is on record that more than one person ………… in road accidents in Nigeria every day.
A. die B. dying C. dies D. dead
Answer: C. dies
Explanation: “More than one” = singular.
The committee ………… divided in their opinion.
A. are B. is C. were D. have
Answer: B. is
Explanation: Collective noun → singular verb.
Everyone in the class ………… submitted his assignment.
A. have B. has C. are D. is
Answer: B. has
Explanation: “Everyone” = singular indefinite pronoun.
A number of students ………… absent today.
A. is B. are C. was D. were
Answer: B. are
Explanation: “A number of” = plural.
Neither the teacher nor the students ………… coming to the party.
A. is B. are C. was D. were
Answer: B. are
Explanation: Agree with nearer subject “students” (plural).
Twenty thousand naira ………… too much for the book.
A. are B. is C. were D. have
Answer: B. is
Explanation: Money amount = singular.
The police ………… arrested the suspect.
A. has B. have C. is D. are
Answer: B. have
Explanation: “Police” = always plural.
Mathematics ………… my favourite subject.
A. are B. is C. were D. have
Answer: B. is
Explanation: “Mathematics” = uncountable singular.
One of the students ………… failed the examination.
A. have B. has C. are D. were
Answer: B. has
Explanation: “One of” = singular.
The poor ………… always with us.
A. is B. are C. was D. were
Answer: B. are
Explanation: “The poor” = plural (the + adjective).
My wife and comforter ………… arrived.
A. have B. has C. are D. were
Answer: B. has
Explanation: One person (wife = comforter) → singular verb.
