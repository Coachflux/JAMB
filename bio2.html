Overall Evolutionary Trend üåç

Life started simple and got more complex over time: From unicellular prokaryotes (Monera) ‚Üí unicellular eukaryotes (Protista) ‚Üí multicellular heterotrophs (Fungi) ‚Üí multicellular autotrophs (Plantae) ‚Üí highly complex multicellular heterotrophs (Animalia).

Key trends: Cells get fancier (prokaryotic to eukaryotic), bodies organize better (unicellular to multicellular with tissues/organs/systems), plants develop vascular tissues, and life moves from water to land.

a. Monera (Prokaryotes) ü¶†

Simplest life; no true nucleus or organelles.

Examples: Bacteria (E. coli, Streptococcus), Blue-green algae (Cyanobacteria like Anabaena).

Shapes: Spherical (coccus), rod (bacillus), spiral (spirillum), comma (vibrio).

External features: Cell wall of peptidoglycan, some have flagella for movement; cyanobacteria have pigments for photosynthesis.

Evolutionary position: First life on Earth ‚Äì primitive prokaryotes.

Reproduction: Binary fission (asexual); quick and simple.

Life history: No complex stages; just divide in good conditions.

b. Protista (Protozoans and Algae) üî¨

Unicellular eukaryotes; have nucleus and organelles.

Examples: Amoeba, Euglena, Paramecium, Chlamydomonas (alga), Trypanosoma (parasite).

Shapes: Amoeba (irregular, changing), Euglena (elongated, spindle-like), Paramecium (slipper-shaped, cigar-like).

External features: Pseudopodia for movement (Amoeba), flagella (Euglena), cilia (Paramecium); some like Euglena have chloroplasts.

Evolutionary position: Bridge to multicellular life; first eukaryotes.

Life history: Mostly asexual (fission); some sexual; in water or moist places.

Complexity: Organelles like vacuoles for water balance.

c. Fungi üçÑ

Mostly multicellular eukaryotes; eat by absorbing (heterotrophs), no chlorophyll.

Examples: Mushroom (Agaricus), Rhizopus (bread mold), Yeast (unicellular), Penicillium (mold).

Shapes: Mushroom (umbrella with cap and stalk), Rhizopus (thread-like hyphae with sporangia).

External features: Hyphae form mycelium; cell wall of chitin; mushrooms have gills for spores.

Evolutionary position: After Protista; first multicellular but no vascular system.

Reproduction: Spores (asexual/sexual); Rhizopus forms zygospores.

Life history: Spore ‚Üí mycelium ‚Üí fruiting body (like mushroom).

d. Plantae (Plants) ‚Äì Evolutionary Trend üåø

Plants evolve from water to land, getting more complex structures.

i. Thallophyta (Algae)

Simplest plants; no roots/stems/leaves (thallus body), mostly aquatic.

Examples: Spirogyra, Ulothrix, Chlorella, Seaweed.

Shapes: Filamentous (Spirogyra ‚Äì spiral chloroplasts in threads).

Features: Chlorophyll for photosynthesis; no vascular tissues.

Life history: Asexual (fragmentation); sexual (conjugation in Spirogyra).

Evolutionary: Fully water-based; primitive.

ii. Bryophyta (Mosses and Liverworts)

No vascular tissues; simple stems/leaves; rhizoids instead of roots.

Examples: Brachymenium (moss), Marchantia (liverwort), Funaria (moss), Riccia (liverwort).

Shapes: Moss (leafy, erect), Liverwort (flat, lobed thallus).

Features: Live in moist places; alternation of generations (gametophyte main).

Life history: Green gametophyte ‚Üí gametes ‚Üí zygote ‚Üí dependent sporophyte.

Evolutionary: First land plants; need water for sperm to swim.

iii. Pteridophyta (Ferns)

Vascular plants; true roots, stems, leaves (fronds).

Examples: Dryopteris, Nephrolepis, Selaginella, Equisetum.

Shapes: Fronds (feathery leaves), coiled young leaves (fiddleheads).

Features: No seeds; produce spores; sporophyte dominant.

Life history: Fern (sporophyte) ‚Üí spores ‚Üí small gametophyte ‚Üí gametes.

Evolutionary: Better on land with vascular tissues; still water for fertilization.

iv. Spermatophyta (Seed Plants)

Advanced with seeds; no need for water to fertilize.

Gymnosperms (Naked Seeds)

Examples: Cycads, Pines (conifers), Ginkgo, Fir.

Shapes: Cones (male small, female large woody).

Features: Cones for reproduction; woody stems.

Evolutionary: Fully land-adapted; pollen tubes for sperm.

Angiosperms (Flowering Plants)

Seeds in fruits; flowers for pollination.

Monocots: One cotyledon, parallel veins (e.g. Maize, Grass, Onion).

Dicots: Two cotyledons, net veins (e.g. Water leaf, Bean, Sunflower).

Evolutionary: Most advanced; fruits help spread seeds.

v. Plants by Life Cycle

Annuals: 1 year (Maize, Rice).

Biennials: 2 years (Carrot, Beetroot).

Perennials: Many years (Trees like Mango, Shrubs).

Trend in plants: Water (Thallophyta) ‚Üí half-land (Bryophyta) ‚Üí land with pipes (Pteridophyta) ‚Üí seeds (Spermatophyta) ‚Üí flowers/fruits.

e. Animalia (Animals) ‚Äì Evolutionary Trend üêæ

From simple no-backbone to complex with backbone; water to land.

i. Invertebrates (No Backbone)

Coelenterata: e.g. Hydra, Jellyfish, Sea anemone.

Shapes: Hydra (tubular polyp), Jellyfish (umbrella medusa).

Radial symmetry, tissue level, aquatic.

Platyhelminthes (Flatworms): e.g. Taenia (tapeworm), Planaria, Liver fluke.

Shapes: Flat, ribbon-like (Taenia long tape).

Bilateral symmetry, organ level, mostly parasitic.

Nematoda (Roundworms): e.g. Ascaris, Hookworm, Pinworm.

Shapes: Round, cylindrical, tapered ends.

Pseudocoelom, complete gut.

Annelida: e.g. Earthworm, Leech, Nereis.

Shapes: Segmented tube (Earthworm ringed body).

True coelom, setae for movement.

Arthropoda: e.g. Mosquito, Cockroach, Housefly, Bee, Butterfly, Spider, Crab.

Shapes: Jointed legs, segmented (Mosquito slender with wings, Cockroach flat oval).

Exoskeleton, metamorphosis (e.g. butterfly: egg ‚Üí larva ‚Üí pupa ‚Üí adult).

Economic importance of insects: Good (bees pollinate, honey; silkworms silk); Bad (mosquito spreads malaria, pests damage crops).

Environment: Decompose waste, food for others, pollination.

Mollusca: e.g. Snails, Octopus, Squid, Clam.

Shapes: Snail (coiled shell, soft body).

Soft body, mantle, some shells.

Advancement: Radial to bilateral; tissues to systems; specialization in Arthropoda.

ii. Vertebrates (With Backbone)

Pisces (Fish): e.g. Shark (cartilaginous), Tilapia (bony).

Shapes: Streamlined body, fins, scales.

Gills, fully water.

Amphibia: e.g. Toads, Frogs, Salamanders.

Shapes: Frog (jumping legs, moist skin).

Metamorphosis (tadpole to adult); lungs + skin; semi-land.

Reptilia: e.g. Lizards, Snakes, Turtles, Crocodiles.

Shapes: Lizard (scaled, four legs), Snake (elongated, no legs).

Scales, amniotic eggs; fully land.

Aves (Birds): e.g. Eagle, Pigeon, Ostrich.

Shapes: Feathers, wings, beaks.

Endothermic, fly (most).

Mammalia: e.g. Humans, Dogs, Whales, Bats.

Shapes: Hair/fur, varied (e.g. dog four-legged).

Milk glands, placenta; smart brains.

Advancement: Water (fish) ‚Üí land-water (amphibians) ‚Üí full land (reptiles, birds, mammals with eggs/lungs/parent care).

Key JAMB Tips üìù

  • Describe features showing complexity (e.g. from no nucleus to organs).
  • Life histories: Metamorphosis in animals, alternation in plants.
  • Water to land: Amphibians/plants need water, higher don't.
  • Economic: Insects good/bad; earthworms aerate soil.
