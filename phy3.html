Type Description Examples

Oscillatory Back-and-forth motion about a point Pendulum, swing

2️⃣ Scalars vs Vectors (Quick Review) ↔️

  • Scalars: Magnitude only (speed, distance, mass, time)
  • Vectors: Magnitude + direction (velocity, displacement, acceleration, force)

Quantity Symbol Definition Formula Unit

4️⃣ The BIG FIVE Equations of Motion 📝

⚡ MUST MEMORIZE THESE! ⚡

Equation When to Use
v = u + at When displacement (s) is not given
s = ½(u+v)t When acceleration (a) is not given
s = ut + ½at² When final velocity (v) is not given
v² = u² + 2as When time (t) is not given
s = vt - ½at² When initial velocity (u) is not given

🔢 Example 1: Basic Acceleration

Question: A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly at 2 m/s² for 10 seconds. Calculate the distance covered.

Given: u = 0 m/s, a = 2 m/s², t = 10 s

Using: s = ut + ½at²
s = (0)(10) + ½(2)(10)²
s = 0 + ½(2)(100) = 100 m
Answer: s = 100 m

🔢 Example 2: Finding Final Velocity

Question: A train moving at 20 m/s accelerates at 0.5 m/s² for 30 seconds. Find its final velocity.

Given: u = 20 m/s, a = 0.5 m/s², t = 30 s

Using: v = u + at
v = 20 + (0.5)(30) = 20 + 15 = 35 m/s
Answer: v = 35 m/s

🔢 Example 3: Motion Under Gravity

Question: A stone is thrown vertically upward with velocity 30 m/s. Calculate: (a) Maximum height (b) Time to reach maximum height. (Take g = 10 m/s²)

At max height, v = 0 m/s

(a) Maximum height:
Using: v² = u² + 2as (a = -g = -10 m/s²)
0 = (30)² + 2(-10)s
0 = 900 - 20s
20s = 900 → s = 45 m

(b) Time to reach max height:
Using: v = u + at
0 = 30 + (-10)t
10t = 30 → t = 3 s
Answers: (a) 45 m (b) 3 s

🔢 Example: Graph Interpretation

Question: Velocity-time graph shows: 0-5s: velocity increases 0 to 20 m/s; 5-10s: constant at 20 m/s; 10-15s: decreases to 0. Calculate total distance.

Solution:
• 0-5s (triangle): Area = ½ × 5 × 20 = 50 m
• 5-10s (rectangle): Area = 5 × 20 = 100 m
• 10-15s (triangle): Area = ½ × 5 × 20 = 50 m

Total distance s = 50 + 100 + 50 = 200 m
Answer: 200 m
🎯 SECTION D: PROJECTILE MOTION

6️⃣ Projectile Key Quantities 🎯

Quantity Symbol Description
Initial velocity u Launch velocity
Horizontal component ux = ucosθ Constant throughout flight
Vertical component uy = usinθ Changes due to gravity
Launch angle θ Angle with horizontal
Maximum height H Highest vertical point
Horizontal range R Total horizontal distance
Time of flight T Total time in air

Projectiles Launched Horizontally from Height h:

• Time of flight: T = √(2h/g)
• Horizontal range: R = uT = u√(2h/g)
• Velocity at ground: v = √(u² + 2gh)

Projectiles Launched at Angle θ:

• Time of flight: T = (2usinθ)/g
• Maximum height: H = (u²sin²θ)/(2g)
• Horizontal range: R = (u²sin2θ)/g
• Max range (θ = 45°): Rmax = u²/g

🔢 Example 4: Horizontal Projectile

Question: A ball is projected horizontally from 45 m high cliff with velocity 10 m/s. Calculate: (a) Time to reach ground (b) Horizontal distance. (Take g = 10 m/s²)

(a) Time of flight:
T = √(2h/g) = √(2×45/10) = √9 = 3 s

(b) Horizontal range:
R = u × T = 10 × 3 = 30 m
Answers: (a) 3 s (b) 30 m
🎯 SECTION E: NEWTON'S LAWS OF MOTION

7️⃣ The Three Laws ⚖️

Law Statement Formula
First Law (Inertia) Body remains at rest or moves with constant velocity unless acted upon by net external force —
Second Law Rate of change of momentum is proportional to applied force F = ma = dp/dt
Third Law Action and reaction are equal and opposite Faction = -Freaction

Additional Force Notations:

• Weight: W = mg
• Normal reaction: R or N
• Tension: T
• Frictional force: f = μR
• Coefficient of friction: μ = f/R

🔢 Example 5: Force Calculation

Question: A 5 kg mass is accelerated from rest to 10 m/s in 2 seconds. Calculate the force applied.

Given: m = 5 kg, u = 0, v = 10 m/s, t = 2 s

First find acceleration:
a = (v-u)/t = (10-0)/2 = 5 m/s²

Then: F = ma = 5 × 5 = 25 N
Answer: F = 25 N

🔢 Example 6: Connected Bodies

Question: Two masses 3 kg and 2 kg are connected by a string over a smooth pulley. Find the acceleration.

Given: m₁ = 3 kg, m₂ = 2 kg, g = 10 m/s²

Net force = (m₁g - m₂g) = (3×10) - (2×10) = 10 N
Total mass = m₁ + m₂ = 3 + 2 = 5 kg
Acceleration: a = F/m = 10/5 = 2 m/s²
Answer: a = 2 m/s²
🎯 SECTION F: MOMENTUM & IMPULSE

8️⃣ Key Quantities 🚀

Quantity Symbol Formula Unit
Momentum p or p⃗ p = mv kg·m/s
Impulse I or J I = Ft = Δp = m(v-u) N·s or kg·m/s
Force F F = Δp/t N

Conservation of Momentum:

• General collision: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
• Perfectly inelastic (stick together): m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁+m₂)v
• Explosion: 0 = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂

🔢 Example 7: Collision

Question: A 2 kg mass moving at 5 m/s collides with a stationary 3 kg mass. After collision, they move together. Find their common velocity.

Given: m₁ = 2 kg, u₁ = 5 m/s, m₂ = 3 kg, u₂ = 0

Before: Momentum = (2×5) + (3×0) = 10 kg·m/s
After: Combined mass = 2 + 3 = 5 kg
Conservation: 10 = 5 × v → v = 2 m/s
Answer: v = 2 m/s
🎯 SECTION G: CIRCULAR MOTION

9️⃣ Circular Motion Quantities 🎡

Quantity Symbol Formula Unit
Angular displacement θ θ = s/r rad
Angular velocity ω ω = θ/t = v/r = 2πf rad/s
Linear velocity v v = ωr = 2πr/T m/s
Period T T = 1/f = 2π/ω s
Centripetal acceleration ac ac = v²/r = ω²r m/s²
Centripetal force Fc Fc = mv²/r = mω²r N

🔢 Example 8: Circular Motion

Question: A 0.5 kg stone is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1 m at 2 rev/s. Calculate the centripetal force.

Given: m = 0.5 kg, r = 1 m, f = 2 Hz

Angular velocity: ω = 2πf = 2π × 2 = 4π rad/s
Centripetal force: Fc = mω²r = 0.5 × (4π)² × 1
Fc = 0.5 × 16π² = 8π² ≈ 78.96 N
Answer: Fc ≈ 79 N or 8π² N
🎯 SECTION H: SIMPLE HARMONIC MOTION

🔟 SHM Key Quantities 🎵

Quantity Symbol Formula Unit
Amplitude A Maximum displacement m
Displacement x x = Asin(ωt) or Acos(ωt) m
Velocity v v = ω√(A²-x²) m/s
Max velocity vmax vmax = Aω m/s
Acceleration a a = -ω²x m/s²
Angular frequency ω ω = 2π/T = √(k/m) rad/s
Period (pendulum) T T = 2π√(L/g) s
Period (spring) T T = 2π√(m/k) s

🔢 Example 9: Simple Pendulum

Question: A simple pendulum has period of 2 seconds. Calculate its length. (Take g = π² m/s²)

Given: T = 2 s, g = π² m/s²

Using: T = 2π√(L/g)
2 = 2π√(L/π²)
1 = π × √(L)/π = √L
L = 1 m
Answer: L = 1 m

🎓 JAMB Exam Tips for Motion

  1. Always draw diagrams for projectile and circular motion problems
  2. Check units carefully - convert km/h to m/s (multiply by 5/18)
  3. For graphs: Remember area = distance/change, slope = rate of change
  4. Projectile at angle: Maximum range always occurs at θ = 45°
  5. Conservation of momentum: Total before = Total after
  6. SHM: Velocity max at equilibrium (x=0), acceleration max at extremes (x=A)
  7. Circular motion: Centripetal force always points toward center

  2. A ball is thrown upward at 20 m/s. How long until it returns to the thrower?

  4. A stone of mass 2 kg is whirled in a vertical circle of radius 0.5 m at 4 m/s. Calculate the tension at the lowest point.
Answers: 1) a = 4 m/s², 2) t = 4 s, 3) v = 0 m/s, 4) T = 78.4 N
