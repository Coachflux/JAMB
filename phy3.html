Motion in Physics

Motion is the change in position of an object with respect to time and a reference point. Based on New School Physics, we'll cover the subtopics step by step in a concise manner.

(a) Newton’s Laws of Motion

Step 1: Inertia, Mass, and Force — Inertia is the tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion. Mass measures inertia (scalar, kg). Force is a push or pull causing motion change (vector, N).

Step 2: First Law (Inertia) — An object at rest stays at rest, or in uniform motion stays in motion, unless acted upon by a net external force.

Step 3: Second Law — Net force F = ma, where a is acceleration. Acceleration is directly proportional to net force and inversely proportional to mass.

Step 4: Third Law — For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Step 5: Impulse and Momentum — Momentum p = mv. Impulse J = Ft = Δp (change in momentum).

Step 6: Force-Time Graph — Area under the graph gives impulse (J = ∫F dt).

Step 7: Conservation of Linear Momentum — In isolated systems (no external forces), total momentum before = total momentum after collision. For two bodies: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂.

(b) Types of Motion

Step 1: Translational — Body moves along a straight or curved path without rotation (e.g., car moving).

Step 2: Oscillatory — To-and-fro motion about a mean position (e.g., pendulum).

Step 3: Rotational — Motion around a fixed axis (e.g., wheel spinning).

Step 4: Spin — Rotation about own axis (e.g., top spinning).

Step 5: Random — Irregular, unpredictable path (e.g., Brownian motion).

(f) Linear Motion

Step 1: Speed, Velocity, Acceleration — Speed = distance/time (scalar). Velocity = displacement/time (vector). Acceleration = Δv/time (vector).

Step 2: Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

  • v = u + at
  • s = ut + ½ at²
  • v² = u² + 2as
  • s = (u + v)t / 2

Step 3: Motion Under Gravity — a = g ≈ 10 m/s² downward.

Step 4: Distance-Time Graph — Slope = velocity.

Step 5: Velocity-Time Graph — Slope = acceleration. Area = displacement.

Step 6: Instantaneous Velocity/Acceleration — Slope of the graph at a point.

(i) Simple Harmonic Motion (S.H.M)

Step 1: Definition - Periodic motion where restoring force ∝ -displacement (e.g., F = -kx).

Step 2: Examples - Simple pendulum, mass-spring, floating object.

Step 3: Period T = time for one cycle; Frequency f = 1/T; Amplitude A = max displacement.

Step 4: Velocity v = ω √(A ² - x²); Acceleration a = -ω² x (ω = 2π/T).

Step 5: Energy — Total E = ½ k A² = KE + PE; KE = ½ m v²; PE = ½ k x².

Step 6: Forced Vibration - External periodic force.

Step 7: Resonance - When driving f = natural f, amplitude max.
