Non-metals & Their Compounds

JAMB 2026 – New School Chemistry

Introduction to Non-metals

Non-metals are elements that do not easily form positive ions and are poor conductors of heat and electricity.

Examples of Non-metals

  • Hydrogen (H)
  • Oxygen (O)
  • Nitrogen (N)
  • Carbon (C)
  • Sulphur (S)
  • Halogens (F, Cl, Br, I)

General Properties of Non-metals

  • Usually gases or brittle solids
  • Low melting and boiling points
  • Poor conductors of heat and electricity
  • Tend to gain electrons
  • Form acidic oxides

(A) Hydrogen

Hydrogen is the lightest element and the most abundant element in the universe.

Laboratory Preparation

Hydrogen is prepared by reacting metals with dilute acids.

  • Zinc + Dilute HCl → Hydrogen
  • Zinc + Dilute H₂SO₄ → Hydrogen

Hydrogen is collected over water because it is insoluble in water.

Commercial Production

  • Water Gas Method – Steam passed over hot coke.
  • Cracking of Petroleum – Hydrogen produced during petroleum refining.

Properties of Hydrogen

  • Colourless gas
  • Odourless
  • Very light
  • Slightly soluble in water
  • Burns in air
  • Acts as a reducing agent

Uses of Hydrogen

  • Manufacture of ammonia
  • Hydrogenation of vegetable oils
  • Rocket fuel
  • Production of methanol

Test for Hydrogen

Bring a lighted splint near the gas.

Observation: Squeaky pop sound.

(B) Halogens

Halogens are elements in Group 17 of the periodic table.

  • Fluorine
  • Chlorine
  • Bromine
  • Iodine

Chlorine (Cl₂)

Chlorine is a greenish-yellow poisonous gas.

Laboratory Preparation

Heating manganese (IV) oxide with concentrated HCl.

Industrial Preparation

Chlorine is produced by electrolysis of brine (NaCl solution).

Products Produced

  • Chlorine gas
  • Hydrogen gas
  • Sodium hydroxide

Uses of Chlorine

  • Water sterilization
  • Bleaching agent
  • Manufacture of plastics (PVC)
  • Production of hydrochloric acid
  • Insecticides

Test for Chloride Ion

Add Silver Nitrate (AgNO₃)

Result: White precipitate

(C) Oxygen

Oxygen supports combustion and respiration.

Laboratory Preparation

Heating potassium chlorate with MnO₂ catalyst.

Commercial Production

Obtained from fractional distillation of liquid air.

Uses

  • Respiration
  • Welding
  • Steel manufacture
  • Medical oxygen

Types of Oxides

Water

Water forms when hydrogen burns in oxygen.

Composition by volume:

Hydrogen : Oxygen = 2 : 1

(D) Nitrogen

Nitrogen makes about 78% of air.

Ammonia (NH₃)

Prepared industrially using the Haber Process.

  • Nitrogen + Hydrogen → Ammonia
  • High pressure and temperature

Uses

  • Fertilizer production
  • Manufacture of nitric acid
  • Refrigeration

Test for Ammonia

Turns moist red litmus paper blue.

(E) Carbon

Allotropes of Carbon

  • Diamond
  • Graphite
  • Charcoal
  • Coke

Carbon (IV) Oxide

CO₂ turns limewater milky.

Carbon (II) Oxide

CO is colourless, odourless and poisonous.
