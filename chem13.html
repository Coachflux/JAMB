1️⃣ Introduction to Non-metals

Non-metals are elements that do not easily form positive ions and are poor conductors of heat and electricity.

Examples

  • Hydrogen (H), Oxygen (O), Nitrogen (N), Carbon (C), Sulphur (S)
  • Halogens: F, Cl, Br, I

General properties of non-metals

  • Usually gases or brittle solids
  • Low melting and boiling points (many of them)
  • Poor conductors of heat and electricity
  • Tend to gain electrons
  • Form acidic oxides
Non-metals are mostly at the top-right of the periodic table (plus hydrogen)

2️⃣ (A) Hydrogen (H₂)

Hydrogen is the lightest element and the most abundant element in the universe.

Laboratory preparation

Zinc + dilute HCl → hydrogen

Zinc + dilute H₂SO₄ → hydrogen

Hydrogen is collected over water because it is insoluble (only slightly soluble) in water.

Commercial production

  • Water gas method: steam passed over hot coke
  • Cracking of petroleum: produced during refining

Properties

  • Colourless, odourless, very light
  • Slightly soluble in water
  • Burns in air
  • Acts as a reducing agent

Uses

  • Manufacture of ammonia
  • Hydrogenation of vegetable oils
  • Rocket fuel
  • Production of methanol

Test for hydrogen

Lighted splint test

  • Bring a lighted splint near the gas → squeaky pop sound

3️⃣ (B) Halogens (Group 17) + Chlorine

Halogens are elements in Group 17: fluorine, chlorine, bromine, iodine.

Chlorine (Cl₂)

Chlorine is a greenish-yellow poisonous gas.

Laboratory preparation

Heat manganese(IV) oxide with concentrated hydrochloric acid:

MnO₂ + 4HCl → MnCl₂ + Cl₂ + 2H₂O

Industrial preparation (very important)

Produced by electrolysis of brine (NaCl solution).

Products formed

  • Chlorine gas (Cl₂)
  • Hydrogen gas (H₂)
  • Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)

Uses of chlorine

  • Water sterilization
  • Bleaching agent
  • Manufacture of plastics (PVC)
  • Production of hydrochloric acid
  • Insecticides

Test for chloride ion (Cl⁻)

Silver nitrate test

  • Add AgNO₃ → white precipitate (AgCl)

4️⃣ (C) Oxygen (O₂)

Oxygen supports combustion and respiration.

Laboratory preparation

Heat potassium chlorate with MnO₂ catalyst:

2KClO₃ → 2KCl + 3O₂ (MnO₂ catalyst)

Commercial production

  • Fractional distillation of liquid air

Uses

  • Respiration
  • Welding
  • Steel manufacture
  • Medical oxygen
🧠 Quick test (often used)

O₂ relights a glowing splint.

6️⃣ (D) Nitrogen (N₂) + Ammonia (NH₃)

Nitrogen makes about 78% of air.

Ammonia (NH₃) — industrial preparation

Prepared by the Haber process (high pressure and temperature).

N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃

Uses of ammonia

  • Fertilizer production
  • Manufacture of nitric acid
  • Refrigeration

Test for ammonia

Litmus test

  • Turns moist red litmus paper blue

7️⃣ (E) Carbon (C) + Oxides of Carbon

Allotropes of carbon

  • Diamond
  • Graphite
  • Charcoal
  • Coke

Carbon(IV) oxide (CO₂)

Test: CO₂ turns limewater milky.

Carbon(II) oxide (CO)

Danger note

  • CO is colourless, odourless and poisonous.
