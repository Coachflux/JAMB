1. Mechanics (Spelling, Punctuation & Capitalisation)

What it is: Rules of correct written English (British standard). JAMB tests spelling errors, punctuation placement, and capitalisation in names/titles/days.

Key Tips:

  • British spelling only: colour, realise, programme, defence
  • Apostrophe: boy’s (singular), boys’ (plural), don’t (contraction)
  • Direct speech: comma before closing quote in British style
  • Capitalise: I, proper nouns, days (Monday), months, titles (President when specific)

2. Ordinary Usage (Literal Meaning)

What it is: Straightforward dictionary meaning — no metaphor, no idiom, just literal sense.

Key Tip: Ask: “What do the words actually mean if taken exactly as written?”

10 JAMB-style examples with explanations:

“The bank of the river was slippery.” “bank” means:
A. financial institution B. side or edge of the river C. a place to keep money D. a type of cloud
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal geographical feature (riverbank).
“The light in the room was too dim to read.” “light” means:
A. not heavy B. brightness/illumination C. a matchstick D. a lamp that is switched off
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal sense = illumination/brightness level.
“He ran to catch the bus.” “ran” means:
A. managed a business B. moved quickly on foot C. escaped from prison D. competed in an election
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal physical action of running.
“The head of the school addressed the students.” “head” means:
A. the top part of the body B. the principal/leader C. a coin side D. a river source
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal position of authority (headmaster/principal).
“She bought a new pair of shoes.” “pair” means:
A. two matching items B. a fruit C. a group of people D. to repair
Answer: A
Explanation: Literal quantity = two matching shoes.
“The cold weather made him shiver.” “cold” means:
A. unfriendly B. low temperature C. a viral illness D. calm
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal weather condition = low temperature.
“The bark of the dog woke me up.” “bark” means:
A. outer covering of a tree B. the sound a dog makes C. a small sailing boat D. a type of chocolate
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal animal sound (dog barking).
“He drew a picture of his house.” “drew” means:
A. pulled something B. made a sketch with a pencil C. ended a game in a tie D. attracted attention
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal artistic action (drawing/sketching).
“The key to the door was missing.” “key” means:
A. the most important thing B. a metal device to open a lock C. a musical scale D. a computer button
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal object used to unlock a door.
“She planted flowers in the garden.” “planted” means:
A. placed secretly B. put seeds or plants into soil C. established a company D. hit someone
Answer: B
Explanation: Literal gardening action (putting plants in soil).

3. Figurative Usage (Figures of Speech)

What it is: Imaginative, non-literal language. JAMB asks you to identify the figure and explain the intended meaning.

Key Tip: If the sentence cannot be true literally → it is figurative.

1–100 Most Occurring Figures of Speech in JAMB:

1. As brave as a lion – Simile – Meaning: Extremely courageous / very brave
Example: He fought as brave as a lion in the battle.
2. Her smile is like sunshine – Simile – Meaning: Very bright, warm and cheerful
Example: Her smile is like sunshine on a rainy day.
3. As cold as ice – Simile – Meaning: Extremely unfriendly, emotionless or lacking warmth
Example: His stare was as cold as ice when he was angry.
4. Busy as a bee – Simile – Meaning: Very hardworking and active
Example: She works busy as a bee from morning till night.
5. As light as a feather – Simile – Meaning: Extremely light in weight
Example: The box felt as light as a feather when I lifted it.
6. He ran like the wind – Simile – Meaning: Very fast / at great speed
Example: He ran like the wind to catch the departing bus.
7. As strong as an ox – Simile – Meaning: Very powerful and physically strong
Example: The wrestler is as strong as an ox.
8. Sweet as honey – Simile – Meaning: Very kind, pleasant or charming
Example: Her voice is sweet as honey when she sings.
9. Quiet as a mouse – Simile – Meaning: Very silent or making no noise at all
Example: The baby slept quiet as a mouse all night.
10. As white as snow – Simile – Meaning: Pure white or very pale
Example: Her dress was as white as snow on her wedding day.
11. Life is a roller coaster – Metaphor – Meaning: Life has many ups and downs / unpredictable changes
Example: Life is a roller coaster full of surprises.
12. Time is money – Metaphor – Meaning: Time is valuable and should not be wasted
Example: Time is money, so don’t waste it on unimportant things.
13. The world is a stage – Metaphor – Meaning: Life is like a theatrical play with roles we perform
Example: The world is a stage and we are all actors.
14. Her eyes are diamonds – Metaphor – Meaning: Eyes sparkle beautifully / very bright and precious
Example: Her eyes are diamonds when she smiles.
15. He is a night owl – Metaphor – Meaning: Someone who stays up very late at night
Example: He is a night owl who never sleeps before 2 a.m.
16. Love is a battlefield – Metaphor – Meaning: Love involves conflict, struggle and fighting
Example: Love is a battlefield full of emotional wars.
17. The classroom was a zoo – Metaphor – Meaning: Very noisy, chaotic and disorderly
Example: The classroom was a zoo during the break.
18. She has a heart of gold – Metaphor – Meaning: Very kind, generous and good-hearted
Example: She has a heart of gold and helps everyone.
19. Books are windows to the world – Metaphor – Meaning: Reading opens up new knowledge and experiences
Example: Books are windows to the world of knowledge.
20. His words were daggers – Metaphor – Meaning: Words hurt deeply / very painful to hear

21. The wind whispered through the trees – Personification – Meaning: Wind blowing gently and softly (as if speaking quietly)
Example: The wind whispered through the trees at night.
22. The sun smiled down on us – Personification – Meaning: Sun shining warmly and pleasantly (as if happy)
Example: The sun smiled down on us during the picnic.
23. Time flies when you're happy – Personification – Meaning: Time passes very quickly (as if it has wings)
Example: Time flies when you're happy with friends.
24. The flowers danced in the breeze – Personification – Meaning: Flowers moving gracefully in the wind (as if dancing)
Example: The flowers danced in the breeze happily.
25. The moon danced in the night sky – Personification – Meaning: Moon appearing to move playfully (as if dancing)
Example: The moon danced in the night sky above us.
26. Death laid its cold hand – Personification – Meaning: Death taking someone’s life (as if using a hand)

27. The clock ticked impatiently – Personification – Meaning: Clock sounding urgent (as if impatient)

29. The stars winked at me – Personification – Meaning: Stars twinkling playfully (as if winking)

30. Anger boiled inside him – Personification – Meaning: Anger growing strong and intense (as if boiling)

32. I’m so hungry I could eat a horse – Hyperbole – Meaning: Extremely hungry (exaggerated amount of food)

33. My bag weighs a ton – Hyperbole – Meaning: Very heavy (exaggerated weight)

34. She cried an ocean of tears – Hyperbole – Meaning: Cried a very large amount (exaggerated volume)

52. Economical with the truth – Euphemism – Meaning: Lied or did not tell the full truth

54. Big-boned – Euphemism – Meaning: Overweight / fat (polite way to describe)
Example: He is just big-boned, not fat.
55. Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers – Alliteration – Meaning: Repetition of initial ‘p’ sound for musical effect

56. She sells seashells by the seashore – Alliteration – Meaning: Repetition of ‘s’ sound

58. Wild and wonderful – Alliteration – Meaning: Repetition of ‘w’ sound for poetic effect

60. The clock went tick-tock – Onomatopoeia – Meaning: Word imitates the actual sound of a clock
