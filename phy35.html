1️⃣ Introduction to Electromagnetic Induction 🔄

Electromagnetic Induction is the process by which an electromotive force (e.m.f) is induced in a conductor when there is a change in magnetic flux linking the conductor.

  • Key Principle: Discovered by Michael Faraday (1831) - A changing magnetic field induces an electric current.

2️⃣ Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction 📐

Statement

The magnitude of the induced e.m.f is directly proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux linking the circuit.

ℰ = -N(dΦ/dt)

Where:
• ℰ = Induced e.m.f (volts)
• N = Number of turns in the coil
• dΦ/dt = Rate of change of magnetic flux
• The negative sign indicates direction (Lenz's Law)

Magnetic Flux (Φ)

Φ = BA cos θ

Where:
• B = Magnetic flux density (Tesla, T)
• A = Area of the coil (m²)
• θ = Angle between magnetic field and normal to the plane

3️⃣ Lenz's Law 🔄

Statement

The direction of the induced current is such that it opposes the change producing it.

  • If magnetic flux increases → Induced current creates opposing field
  • If magnetic flux decreases → Induced current creates supporting field
  • This is the Law of Conservation of Energy in action

🔢 Calculation 1: Basic Faraday's Law

Question: A coil of 50 turns and area 0.02 m² is placed perpendicular to a magnetic field. If the magnetic flux density changes from 0.1 T to 0.4 T in 0.5 seconds, calculate the induced e.m.f.

ℰ = -N(ΔΦ/Δt) = -N[Δ(BA)/Δt]
= -50 × [(0.4 - 0.1) × 0.02]/0.5
= -50 × [0.3 × 0.02]/0.5
= -50 × 0.006/0.5
= -50 × 0.012 = -0.6 V

The magnitude is 0.6 V (negative sign shows direction per Lenz's Law)
Answer: 0.6 V

🔢 Calculation 2: Rotating Coil (AC Generator)

Question: A rectangular coil of 100 turns, dimensions 0.1 m × 0.05 m, rotates at 50 rev/s in a uniform magnetic field of 0.2 T. Calculate the maximum e.m.f induced.

Maximum e.m.f occurs when coil is parallel to field:
ℰmax = NBAω

Where ω = 2πf = 2π × 50 = 100π rad/s
Area A = 0.1 × 0.05 = 0.005 m²

ℰmax = 100 × 0.2 × 0.005 × 100π
= 100 × 0.2 × 0.005 × 314.16
= 31.4 V
Answer: 31.4 V

🔢 Calculation 3: Moving Conductor

Question: A conductor of length 0.5 m moves at 20 m/s perpendicular to a magnetic field of 0.4 T. Calculate the induced e.m.f.

ℰ = Blv
= 0.4 × 0.5 × 20
= 4.0 V
Answer: 4.0 V

🔢 Calculation 4: Mutual Inductance

Question: Two coils have a mutual inductance of 0.5 H. If the current in the primary coil changes from 2 A to 8 A in 0.2 s, calculate the induced e.m.f in the secondary coil.

ℰs = -M(dIp/dt)
= -0.5 × [(8-2)/0.2]
= -0.5 × (6/0.2)
= -0.5 × 30
= -15 V

Magnitude = 15 V
Answer: 15 V

🔢 Calculation 5: Self-Inductance

Question: A coil with self-inductance 0.4 H carries a current that changes at 5 A/s. Calculate the back e.m.f.

ℰ = -L(dI/dt)
= -0.4 × 5
= -2.0 V

Magnitude = 2.0 V
Answer: 2.0 V

4️⃣ Applications of Electromagnetic Induction 🏭

Application How It Works
AC Generator/Dynamo Coil rotates in magnetic field → alternating e.m.f
DC Generator Split-ring commutator converts AC to DC
Transformer Changing current in primary induces e.m.f in secondary
Induction Coil Interrupted DC produces high voltage spikes
Moving Coil Microphone Sound waves move coil in magnetic field
Eddy Current Brake Induced currents oppose motion (Lenz's Law)
Induction Cooking Changing magnetic field induces currents in pot

5️⃣ Transformers 🔌

Principle

Mutual induction between two coils wound on the same iron core.

Voltage Ratio:
Vs/Vp = Ns/Np

Current Ratio (Ideal):
Is/Ip = Np/Ns = Vp/Vs

Efficiency:
η = (Output Power/Input Power) × 100%
= (VsIs)/(VpIp) × 100%

🔢 Calculation 6: Transformer

Question: A transformer has 1000 turns in the primary and 200 turns in the secondary. If the primary voltage is 240 V and current is 2 A, calculate: (a) Secondary voltage, (b) Secondary current (assuming 100% efficiency).

(a) Vs/240 = 200/1000
Vs = 240 × 0.2 = 48 V

(b) VpIp = VsIs
240 × 2 = 48 × Is
Is = 480/48 = 10 A
Answer: (a) 48 V, (b) 10 A

6️⃣ Energy Losses in Transformers ⚡

Loss Type Cause Reduction Method
Copper Loss Resistance of windings Use thick copper wires
Iron/Eddy Current Loss Induced currents in core Laminated core
Hysteresis Loss Repeated magnetization Soft iron core
Flux Leakage Not all flux links secondary Proper core design

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

  1. Remember the negative sign in Faraday's Law indicates Lenz's Law direction

  2. Maximum e.m.f occurs when coil is parallel to magnetic field (sinθ = 1 or cosθ = 0 depending on orientation)

  3. Zero e.m.f occurs when coil is perpendicular to field

  4. Step-up transformer: Ns > Np, Vs > Vp, Is < Ip

  5. Step-down transformer: Ns < Np, Vs < Vp, Is > Ip

  6. DC cannot be transformed - transformers work only with changing current

    Answer: 20 turns
