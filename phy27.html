📚 Chapter 27: Dispersion of Light and Colors

🎯 Learning Objectives: Understand dispersion by prisms, calculate angular dispersion and dispersive power, learn color mixing principles, and master the electromagnetic spectrum for JAMB success.

1️⃣ What is Dispersion? 🌈

Principle

Dispersion is the splitting of white light into its constituent colors (spectrum) when passed through a medium like a glass prism. This happens because different colors (wavelengths) of light travel at different speeds in the medium, causing them to refract (bend) by different amounts.

  • Key Concept: White light is actually a mixture of seven colors: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, and Violet (ROYGBIV)

Dispersion by a Triangular Prism

When white light enters a triangular prism:

  1. It refracts (bends) towards the normal as it enters the glass
  2. Different colors bend by different amounts because the refractive index varies with wavelength
  3. Violet light bends the most (highest refractive index)
  4. Red light bends the least (lowest refractive index)
  5. The light refracts again as it exits the prism, spreading the colors into a spectrum

Why Does This Happen? Cauchy's Formula

The refractive index n of a material depends on wavelength λ according to:

n = n₀ + A/λ²

Where:
• n₀ = constant (refractive index at infinite wavelength)
• A = positive constant
• λ = wavelength of light

Since violet light has shorter wavelength than red light:
nviolet > nred

2️⃣ Deviation and Angular Dispersion 📐

For a small-angled prism with angle A:

Mean Deviation (for yellow light):
δy = (ny - 1)A

Angular Dispersion (between violet and red):
θ = (nv - nr)A

Where:
• ny = refractive index for yellow light
• nv = refractive index for violet light
• nr = refractive index for red light
• A = prism angle (apex angle)

Dispersive Power (ω)

Dispersive power measures how much a prism spreads light relative to its deviation:

ω = (nv - nr)/(ny - 1) ≈ θ/δy

🔢 Calculation 1: Angular Dispersion

Question: A prism has an apex angle of 6° and refractive indices of 1.514 for red light and 1.523 for violet light. Calculate the angular dispersion.

Using θ = (nv - nr)A:
θ = (1.523 - 1.514) × 6°
θ = 0.009 × 6°
θ = 0.054°
Answer: 0.054°

🔢 Calculation 2: Dispersive Power

Question: A glass prism has refractive indices: nr = 1.51, ny = 1.52, and nv = 1.53. Calculate its dispersive power.

Using ω = (nv - nr)/(ny - 1):
ω = (1.53 - 1.51)/(1.52 - 1)
ω = 0.02/0.52
ω = 0.0385
Answer: 0.0385 (no units)

🔢 Calculation 3: Minimum Deviation

Question: For a prism with angle A = 60°, find the angle of refraction at minimum deviation.

At minimum deviation:
The light ray passes symmetrically through the prism

r = A/2 = 60°/2
r = 30°

Note: This angle of refraction is independent of color (refractive index), though the angle of incidence and minimum deviation angle vary with color.
Answer: 30°

🔢 Calculation 4: Deviation Without Dispersion

Question: Two prisms are combined to produce deviation without dispersion. Prism 1 has A = 5°, nr = 1.51, nv = 1.53. Prism 2 has n'r = 1.61, n'v = 1.63. Find the angle A' of the second prism.

Condition for deviation without dispersion:
(nv - nr)A = (n'v - n'r)A'

(1.53 - 1.51) × 5 = (1.63 - 1.61) × A'
0.02 × 5 = 0.02 × A'
0.1 = 0.02 × A'
A' = 5°
Answer: 5°

3️⃣ Production of Pure Spectrum 🔬

A pure spectrum is one where colors are clearly separated without overlapping.

  • To produce one: Use a collimator (lens + slit) to produce parallel rays, pass through a prism, use a telescope or screen to view the spectrum
  • Impure spectrum: Occurs when colors overlap due to non-parallel rays or wide slit

4️⃣ Colour Mixing 🎨

Primary Colours of Light (Additive)

  • Red, Green, Blue (RGB)
  • Adding all three produces white light

Secondary Colours

  • Red + Green = Yellow
  • Red + Blue = Magenta
  • Green + Blue = Cyan

Subtractive Mixing (Pigments/Filters)

  • Primary pigments: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow (CMY)
  • A filter subtracts (absorbs) its complementary color

5️⃣ Colour of Objects 👁️

  • An object appears a certain color because it reflects that color and absorbs others
  • A red object reflects red light and absorbs other colors
  • A white object reflects all colors
  • A black object absorbs all colors
  • Colour Filters: A red filter only allows red light to pass through, absorbing all other colors

6️⃣ The Rainbow 🌈

A rainbow is a natural spectrum formed by:

  1. Dispersion of sunlight by water droplets
  2. Refraction as light enters the droplet
  3. Internal reflection inside the droplet
  4. Refraction as light exits
  • Primary rainbow: One internal reflection (red on top, violet inside)
  • Secondary rainbow: Two internal reflections (colors reversed)

7️⃣ Electromagnetic Spectrum 📡

Type Wavelength Source Uses
Radio waves > 1 m Electronic circuits Communication, radio, TV
Microwaves 1 mm - 1 m Magnetron Cooking, mobile phones, radar
Infrared 700 nm - 1 mm Hot objects Remote controls, thermal imaging
Visible light 400-700 nm Sun, lamps Vision, photography
Ultraviolet 10-400 nm Sun, mercury lamps Sterilization, fluorescence
X-rays 0.01-10 nm X-ray tubes Medical imaging, security
Gamma rays < 0.01 nm Radioactive nuclei Cancer treatment, sterilization
Key Relationships:

λ × f = c (speed of light, 3 × 10⁸ m/s)
Higher frequency = Higher energy = More penetrating power

🎯 JAMB Exam Tips

  1. Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂

  2. Critical angle: sin θc = n₂/n₁ (where n₁ > n₂)

  3. Refractive index: n = c/v = sin i/sin r

  4. Prism formulas: Master deviation and dispersion calculations

  5. Remember: Violet always bends more than red (nv > nr)

  6. At minimum deviation: r = A/2 (same for all colors)

  7. Dispersive power: Has no units!

  8. EM spectrum order: Radio → Microwave → IR → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma (increasing frequency)

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Confusing deviation with dispersion - deviation is bending, dispersion is separation into colors
  • ❌ Forgetting that dispersive power has no units
  • ❌ Mixing up primary colors of light (RGB) with pigments (CMY)
  • ❌ Thinking all colors travel at the same speed in glass - they don't!
  • ❌ Forgetting the order of colors in the spectrum (ROYGBIV)
  • ❌ Confusing primary and secondary rainbows - secondary has reversed colors and is fainter
