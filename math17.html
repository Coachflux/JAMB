Integration Made Super Easy for JAMB!

Integration is just the reverse of differentiation. If differentiation asks “What is the slope?”, integration asks “What was the original function before we took the slope?”

Think of it like this:
Differentiation: “Break down” a function → get its derivative.
Integration: “Build back up” → add the +C (constant of integration) because many functions can give the same derivative.

Quick Rules You Must Know (for algebraic & simple trig functions):

  • Function: Constant k → Integral (Indefinite): kx + C
  • Function: xⁿ (n ≠ –1) → Integral: xⁿ⁺¹/(n+1) + C (Power rule)
  • Function: (ax + b)ⁿ → Use substitution u = ax + b, du = a dx
  • Function: sin(ax) → Integral: –(1/a) cos(ax) + C
  • Function: cos(ax) → Integral: (1/a) sin(ax) + C
  • Sum/Difference → Integrate term by term

Definite Integral (from a to b):
∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx = [F(x)]ₐᵇ = F(b) – F(a)
(No +C needed)

Area under the curve (simple cases only):
If the curve is above the x-axis from a to b → Area = ∫ₐᵇ y dx
If it crosses the x-axis → split the integral or take absolute value (JAMB usually gives regions above the axis).

Now let’s practise with real & typical JAMB-style examples.

(a) Integration of explicit algebraic and simple trigonometric functions

10 JAMB-style examples with full step-by-step solutions

1. Evaluate ∫₁³ (x² – 1) dx (JAMB-style definite algebraic)
Step 1: Antiderivative F(x) = x³/3 – x.
Step 2: At upper limit x=3: (27/3) – 3 = 9 – 3 = 6.
Step 3: At lower limit x=1: (1/3) – 1 = 1/3 – 3/3 = –2/3.
Step 4: F(3) – F(1) = 6 – (–2/3) = 6 + 2/3 = 18/3 + 2/3 = 20/3.
Answer: 20/3
2. Evaluate ∫₂³ (x² – 2x) dx
Step 1: F(x) = x³/3 – x².
Step 2: At x=3: 27/3 – 9 = 9 – 9 = 0.
Step 3: At x=2: 8/3 – 4 = 8/3 – 12/3 = –4/3.
Step 4: 0 – (–4/3) = 4/3.
Answer: 4/3
3. If dy/dx = 2x – 3 and y = 3 when x = 0, find y in terms of x.
Step 1: y = ∫(2x – 3) dx = x² – 3x + C.
Step 2: At x=0, y=3: 0 – 0 + C = 3 → C=3.
Step 3: y = x² – 3x + 3.
Answer: y = x² – 3x + 3
4. Evaluate ∫ sin(3x) dx
Step 1: Let u = 3x, du = 3 dx → dx = du/3.
Step 2: ∫ sin u (du/3) = (1/3) ∫ sin u du = (1/3)(–cos u) + C.
Step 3: –(1/3) cos(3x) + C.
Answer: –(1/3) cos(3x) + C
5. Evaluate ∫ 2(2x – 3)²/³ dx (JAMB 2001 style)
Step 1: Let u = 2x – 3, du = 2 dx → dx = du/2.
Step 2: 2 ∫ (u²/³) (du/2) = ∫ u²/³ du.
Step 3: ∫ u²/³ du = u^(5/3)/(5/3) + C = (3/5) u^(5/3) + C.
Step 4: (3/5)(2x – 3)^(5/3) + C.
Answer: (3/5)(2x – 3)^(5/3) + C
6. A function f(x) passes through the origin and f'(x) = 3x + 2. Find f(x).
Step 1: f(x) = ∫(3x + 2) dx = (3/2)x² + 2x + C.
Step 2: f(0) = 0: C = 0.
Step 3: f(x) = (3/2)x² + 2x.
Answer: (3/2)x² + 2x
7. Evaluate ∫₋₁² (2x² + x) dx
Step 1: F(x) = (2/3)x³ + (1/2)x².
Step 2: At x=2: (2/3)(8) + (1/2)(4) = 16/3 + 2 = 16/3 + 6/3 = 22/3.
Step 3: At x=–1: (2/3)(–1) + (1/2)(1) = –2/3 + 1/2 = –4/6 + 3/6 = –1/6.
Step 4: 22/3 – (–1/6) = 44/6 + 1/6 = 45/6 = 15/2.
Answer: 15/2
8. Integrate (2x + 1)³
Step 1: Let u = 2x + 1, du = 2 dx → dx = du/2.
Step 2: ∫ u³ (du/2) = (1/2) ∫ u³ du = (1/2)(u⁴/4) + C = (1/8)u⁴ + C.
Step 3: (1/8)(2x + 1)⁴ + C.
Answer: (1/8)(2x + 1)⁴ + C
9. Integrate 4x³ + 2x + cos x
Step 1: ∫ 4x³ dx = x⁴.
Step 2: ∫ 2x dx = x².
Step 3: ∫ cos x dx = sin x.
Step 4: x⁴ + x² + sin x + C.
Answer: x⁴ + x² + sin x + C
10. Integrate (1 + x)/x³ dx
Step 1: (1 + x)/x³ = x^{-3} + x^{-2}.
Step 2: ∫ x^{-3} dx = x^{-2}/(-2) = –1/(2x²).
Step 3: ∫ x^{-2} dx = x^{-1}/(-1) = –1/x.
Step 4: –1/(2x²) – 1/x + C.
Answer: –1/(2x²) – 1/x + C

(b) Area under the curve (simple cases only)

10 JAMB-style examples with full solutions

1. Find the area bounded by y = x(2 – x), the x-axis, x = 0 and x = 2 (real JAMB).
Step 1: y = 2x – x².
Step 2: Area = ∫₀² (2x – x²) dx = [x² – (x³/3)]₀².
Step 3: At 2: 4 – (8/3) = 12/3 – 8/3 = 4/3.
Step 4: At 0: 0.
Step 5: Area = 4/3 sq units.
Answer: 4/3 sq units
2. Find the area under y = x² from x = 0 to x = 1.
Step 1: ∫₀¹ x² dx = [x³/3]₀¹ = (1/3) – 0 = 1/3 sq unit.
Answer: 1/3 sq unit
3. Find the area under y = 4 – x² from x = –2 to x = 2 (symmetric).
Step 1: ∫₋₂² (4 – x²) dx = [4x – (x³/3)]₋₂².
Step 2: At 2: 8 – (8/3) = 24/3 – 8/3 = 16/3.
Step 3: At –2: –8 – (–8/3) = –24/3 + 8/3 = –16/3.
Step 4: 16/3 – (–16/3) = 32/3 sq units.
Answer: 32/3 sq units
4. Area bounded by y = 2x, x-axis, x=1 to x=4.
Step 1: ∫₁⁴ 2x dx = [x²]₁⁴ = 16 – 1 = 15 sq units.
Answer: 15 sq units
5. Area under y = sin x from 0 to π (above x-axis).
Step 1: ∫₀^π sin x dx = [–cos x]₀^π = (–cos π) – (–cos 0) = (–(–1)) – (–1) = 1 + 1 = 2 sq units.
Answer: 2 sq units
6. Area under y = x³ from x=0 to x=2.
Step 1: ∫₀² x³ dx = [x⁴/4]₀² = (16/4) – 0 = 4 sq units.
Answer: 4 sq units
7. Find the area between y = x² + 1 and x-axis from x=0 to x=1 (always above).
Step 1: ∫₀¹ (x² + 1) dx = [x³/3 + x]₀¹ = (1/3 + 1) – 0 = 4/3 sq units.
Answer: 4/3 sq units
8. Area bounded by y = 3x – x² and x-axis (find limits first: roots at 0 and 3).
Step 1: Roots: 3x – x² = 0 → x(3 – x)=0 → x=0,3.
Step 2: ∫₀³ (3x – x²) dx = [(3/2)x² – (1/3)x³]₀³.
Step 3: At 3: (3/2)(9) – (1/3)(27) = 27/2 – 9 = 13.5 – 9 = 4.5 sq units.
Answer: 4.5 sq units
9. Area under y = cos(2x) from 0 to π/4.
Step 1: ∫₀^{π/4} cos(2x) dx = [ (1/2) sin(2x) ]₀^{π/4}.
Step 2: At π/4: (1/2) sin(π/2) = (1/2)(1) = 1/2.
Step 3: At 0: (1/2) sin 0 = 0.
Step 4: 1/2 sq unit.
Answer: 1/2 sq unit
10. Find the area bounded by y = 6x – x² and the x-axis.
Step 1: Roots: 6x – x² = 0 → x(6 – x)=0 → x=0,6.
Step 2: ∫₀⁶ (6x – x²) dx = [3x² – (1/3)x³]₀⁶.
Step 3: At 6: 3(36) – (1/3)(216) = 108 – 72 = 36 sq units.
Answer: 36 sq units

Quick Tips for JAMB Success

  • Always check if the curve is above the x-axis (positive area).
  • For (ax + b)ⁿ use substitution — it appears every year.
  • Definite integrals for area never need +C.
  • Practice writing the integral first, then antiderivative, then plug in limits.
  • Time yourself: these should take you less than 2 minutes each in the exam.
