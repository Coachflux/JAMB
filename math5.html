Polynomials Made Super Easy for JAMB

Abumchukwu, polynomials are expressions like 3x² + 5x – 7. We only go up to degree 3 in JAMB. Think of them as “power expressions” with x. I will teach each part very slowly and simply, like we are in class together. Read one example at a time. Try it on paper first, then check my steps. You will understand everything fast.

(a) Change of Subject of Formula

Very easy way to do it

Step 1: Get rid of fractions (multiply both sides by denominator).
Step 2: Get rid of roots or squares (square or cube both sides).
Step 3: Move all terms with the new subject to one side, others to the other side.
Step 4: Factor or divide to isolate the new subject.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: Make Q the subject of L = (4/3)M√(PQ)
Step 1: L = (4/3)M √(PQ)
Step 2: L / M = (4/3) √(PQ)
Step 3: (3L) / (4M) = √(PQ)
Step 4: Square both sides → 9L² / 16M² = PQ
Step 5: Q = 9L² / (16M² P)
Answer: Q = 9L² / (16M² P)
Example 2: Make q the subject of t = √(pq/r - r²q)
Step 1: Square → t² = pq/r - r²q
Step 2: t² = q (p/r - r²)
Step 3: q = t² r / (p - r³)
Answer: q = t² r / (p - r³)
Example 3: Make Q the subject of p = (M/5)(X + Q) + 1
Step 1: p - 1 = (M/5)(X + Q)
Step 2: 5(p - 1)/M = X + Q
Step 3: Q = 5(p - 1)/M - X
Answer: Q = [5(p-1) - MX]/M
Example 4: Make u the subject of x = (2u - 3)/(3u + 2)
Step 1: x(3u + 2) = 2u - 3
Step 2: 3xu + 2x = 2u - 3
Step 3: 3xu - 2u = -3 - 2x
Step 4: u(3x - 2) = -(2x + 3)
Step 5: u = (2x + 3)/(2 - 3x)
Answer: u = (2x + 3)/(2 - 3x)
Example 5: Make r the subject of V = (4/3)πr³
Step 1: 3V/(4π) = r³
Step 2: r = ∛[3V/(4π)]
Answer: r = ∛[3V/(4π)]
Example 6: Make t the subject of s = ut + (1/2)at²
Step 1: s - ut = (1/2)at²
Step 2: 2(s - ut)/a = t²
Step 3: t = √[2(s - ut)/a]
Answer: t = √[2(s - ut)/a]
Example 7: Make x the subject of y = (x + 3)/(x - 2)
Step 1: y(x - 2) = x + 3
Step 2: yx - 2y = x + 3
Step 3: yx - x = 2y + 3
Step 4: x(y - 1) = 2y + 3
Step 5: x = (2y + 3)/(y - 1)
Answer: x = (2y + 3)/(y - 1)
Example 8: Make k the subject of T = 2π√(l/kg)
Step 1: T/(2π) = √(l/kg)
Step 2: T²/(4π²) = l/kg
Step 3: k = 4π² l / (g T²)
Answer: k = 4π² l / (g T²)
Example 9: Make m the subject of E = (1/2)mv²
Step 1: 2E = mv²
Step 2: m = 2E / v²
Answer: m = 2E / v²
Example 10: Make b the subject of A = (1/2)(a + b)h
Step 1: 2A = (a + b)h
Step 2: 2A - ah = bh
Step 3: b = (2A - ah)/h
Answer: b = (2A - ah)/h

(b) Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication & Division of Polynomials

Very easy way

Add/subtract: collect like terms (same powers of x).
Multiply: distribute every term.
Divide: long division or synthetic division.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: Simplify (2x² + 3x – 1) + (x² – 5x + 4) – (3x² + x – 2)
Step 1: x² terms: 2 + 1 – 3 = 0
Step 2: x terms: 3 – 5 – 1 = –3
Step 3: constants: –1 + 4 – 2 = 1
Step 4: –3x + 1
Answer: –3x + 1
Example 2: Multiply (x + 2)(x² – 3x + 5)
Step 1: x(x² – 3x + 5) = x³ – 3x² + 5x
Step 2: 2(x² – 3x + 5) = 2x² – 6x + 10
Step 3: Combine → x³ – x² – x + 10
Answer: x³ – x² – x + 10
Example 3: Multiply (2x – 1)(3x + 4)
Step 1: 6x² + 8x – 3x – 4
Step 2: 6x² + 5x – 4
Answer: 6x² + 5x – 4
Example 4: Divide 2x³ + 5x² – 3x + 1 by (x – 1)
Step 1: Synthetic division (root 1)
Step 2: 1 | 2 5 –3 1
Step 3: 2 7 4 5
Step 4: Quotient 2x² + 7x + 4, remainder 5
Answer: 2x² + 7x + 4 + 5/(x – 1)
Example 5: Divide x³ – 6x² + 11x – 6 by (x – 1)
Step 1: Synthetic division (root 1)
Step 2: 1 | 1 –6 11 –6
Step 3: 1 –5 6 0
Step 4: Quotient x² – 5x + 6
Answer: x² – 5x + 6
Example 6: Simplify (3x² – 2x + 1) – (x² + 4x – 3) + 2(x – 1)
Step 1: 3x² – 2x + 1 – x² – 4x + 3 + 2x – 2
Step 2: 2x² – 4x + 2
Answer: 2x² – 4x + 2
Example 7: Multiply (x – 3)(x + 3)(x + 1)
Step 1: (x – 3)(x + 3) = x² – 9
Step 2: (x² – 9)(x + 1) = x³ + x² – 9x – 9
Answer: x³ + x² – 9x – 9
Example 8: Divide 4x³ – 2x² + 5 by (2x – 1)
Step 1: Long division
Step 2: Quotient 2x² + (1/2)x + 1/4, remainder 21/4
Answer: 2x² + (1/2)x + 1/4 + 21/4 / (2x – 1)
Example 9: Expand (2x + 1)³
Step 1: (2x + 1)(2x + 1) = 4x² + 4x + 1
Step 2: (4x² + 4x + 1)(2x + 1) = 8x³ + 12x² + 6x + 1
Answer: 8x³ + 12x² + 6x + 1
Example 10: Divide x⁴ – 1 by (x – 1)
Step 1: Synthetic division (root 1)
Step 2: 1 | 1 0 0 0 –1
Step 3: 1 1 1 1 0
Step 4: Quotient x³ + x² + x + 1
Answer: x³ + x² + x + 1

(c) Factorization of Polynomials (degree ≤ 3)

Very easy way

Step 1: Take common factor.
Step 2: Difference of squares or perfect squares.
Step 3: Grouping.
Step 4: Sum or difference of cubes.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: Factor 9x² + 6xy + 4y²
Step 1: Perfect square trinomial
Step 2: (3x + 2y)²
Answer: (3x + 2y)²
Example 2: Factor x³ + 8
Step 1: Sum of cubes
Step 2: (x + 2)(x² – 2x + 4)
Answer: (x + 2)(x² – 2x + 4)
Example 3: Factor 2x³ – 3x² – 11x + 6
Step 1: Try x = 2 (p(2) = 0)
Step 2: (x – 2) is factor
Step 3: Divide → (x – 2)(2x² + x – 3)
Step 4: Factor quadratic → (2x – 3)(x + 1)
Step 5: (x – 2)(2x – 3)(x + 1)
Answer: (x – 2)(2x – 3)(x + 1)
Example 4: Factor x² – 5x + 6
Step 1: Numbers: –2 and –3
Step 2: (x – 2)(x – 3)
Answer: (x – 2)(x – 3)
Example 5: Factor 4x² – 9
Step 1: Difference of squares
Step 2: (2x – 3)(2x + 3)
Answer: (2x – 3)(2x + 3)
Example 6: Factor x³ – 6x² + 11x – 6
Step 1: Try x = 1 (p(1) = 0)
Step 2: (x – 1) is factor
Step 3: Divide → (x – 1)(x² – 5x + 6)
Step 4: (x – 1)(x – 2)(x – 3)
Answer: (x – 1)(x – 2)(x – 3)
Example 7: Factor 6x³ + 11x² – 10x – 3
Step 1: Try x = –1/2 or –3/2
Step 2: (2x + 3) is factor
Step 3: Divide → (2x + 3)(3x² + x – 1)
Step 4: (2x + 3)(3x – 1)(x + 1)
Answer: (2x + 3)(3x – 1)(x + 1)
Example 8: Factor x⁴ – 16
Step 1: (x² – 4)(x² + 4)
Step 2: (x – 2)(x + 2)(x² + 4)
Answer: (x – 2)(x + 2)(x² + 4)
Example 9: Factor 27y³ – 8
Step 1: Difference of cubes
Step 2: (3y – 2)(9y² + 6y + 4)
Answer: (3y – 2)(9y² + 6y + 4)
Example 10: Factor x³ + x² – 4x – 4
Step 1: Try x = 2 (p(2) = 0)
Step 2: (x – 2) is factor
Step 3: (x – 2)(x² + 3x + 2)
Step 4: (x – 2)(x + 1)(x + 2)

(d) Roots of Polynomials (degree ≤ 3)

Very easy way

Roots = values of x that make polynomial = 0.
Quadratic → use formula or factor.
Cubic → find one root by trial, then divide.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: Find roots of x² – 5x + 6 = 0
Step 1: (x – 2)(x – 3) = 0
Step 2: x = 2 or x = 3
Answer: 2 and 3
Example 2: Solve 2x² – 7x + 3 = 0
Step 1: Discriminant = 49 – 24 = 25
Step 2: x = [7 ± 5]/4
Step 3: x = 3 or x = 1/2
Answer: 3 and 1/2
Example 3: Find roots of x³ – 6x² + 11x – 6 = 0
Step 1: Try x = 1: 0
Step 2: (x – 1) is factor
Step 3: Divide → (x – 1)(x² – 5x + 6) = (x – 1)(x – 2)(x – 3)
Step 4: Roots = 1, 2, 3
Answer: 1, 2, 3
Example 4: Find roots of x³ + x² – 4x – 4 = 0

Step 2: (x – 2) is factor

Answer: –2, –1, 2

(e) Factor & Remainder Theorems

Very easy way

Remainder Theorem: When p(x) divided by (x – a), remainder = p(a).
Factor Theorem: If p(a) = 0, then (x – a) is a factor.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: If 2x² – kx – 12 is divisible by (x – 4), find k
Step 1: p(4) = 0
Step 2: 32 – 4k – 12 = 0
Step 3: 20 = 4k → k = 5
Answer: k = 5
Example 2: Remainder when x³ + 2x² + 5x + 4 divided by (x – 1)
Step 1: p(1) = 1 + 2 + 5 + 4 = 12
Answer: 12
Example 3: Show (x + 3) is factor of x³ + 3x² + nx – 12
Step 1: p(–3) = 0
Step 2: –27 + 27 – 3n – 12 = 0
Step 3: n = –4
Answer: n = –4
Example 4: Remainder when 3x³ – 2x² + 5 divided by (x + 1)
Step 1: p(–1) = –3 – 2 + 5 = 0
Answer: 0 (so (x + 1) is a factor)
Example 5: Find k so (x – 2) is factor of 2x³ + kx² – 5x + 6
Step 1: p(2) = 0
Step 2: 16 + 4k – 10 + 6 = 0
Step 3: 4k + 12 = 0 → k = –3
Answer: k = –3
Example 6: Remainder when x³ – x² + 2x – 3 divided by (x – 3)
Step 1: p(3) = 27 – 9 + 6 – 3 = 21
Answer: 21
Example 7: Remainder when pm² + qm + 1 divided by (m – 1) is 2
Step 1: p(1) + q(1) + 1 = 2
Step 2: p + q = 1
Answer: p + q = 1
Example 8: If p(x) = x³ + 2x² + 5x + k, remainder when divided by (x + 2) is 5, find k
Step 1: p(–2) = 5
Step 2: –8 + 8 – 10 + k = 5
Step 3: k = 15
Answer: k = 15
Example 9: Show (x – 1) is factor of x³ – 6x² + 11x – 6
Step 1: p(1) = 1 – 6 + 11 – 6 = 0
Step 2: Yes
Answer: Yes
Example 10: Remainder when 4x³ – 3x + 2 divided by (x + 1)
Step 1: p(–1) = –4 + 3 + 2 = 1
Answer: 1

(f) Simultaneous Equations (one linear, one quadratic)

Very easy way

Solve linear for one variable → substitute into quadratic → solve quadratic → find corresponding values.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: y = 2x + 1 and y = x² – 3x + 5
Step 1: x² – 3x + 5 = 2x + 1
Step 2: x² – 5x + 4 = 0
Step 3: (x – 1)(x – 4) = 0
Step 4: x = 1 → y = 3; x = 4 → y = 9
Answer: (1,3) and (4,9)
Example 2: x + y = 5, xy = 6
Step 1: t² – 5t + 6 = 0
Step 2: (t – 2)(t – 3) = 0
Step 3: 2 and 3
Answer: 2 and 3
Example 3: y = x – 3, y = x² – 5x + 6
Step 1: x² – 6x + 9 = 0
Step 2: (x – 3)² = 0
Step 3: x = 3, y = 0
Answer: x = 3, y = 0
Example 4: 2x + y = 7, x² + y² = 25
Step 1: y = 7 – 2x
Step 2: x² + (7 – 2x)² = 25
Step 3: 5x² – 28x + 24 = 0
Step 4: x = 4 or x = 6/5
Answer: (4, –1) and (6/5, 11/5)
Example 5: Two numbers sum to 11, product 30
Step 1: t² – 11t + 30 = 0
Step 2: (t – 5)(t – 6) = 0
Step 3: 5 and 6
Answer: 5 and 6
Example 6: Rectangle: length = width + 4, area = 45
Step 1: l(l – 4) = 45
Step 2: l² – 4l – 45 = 0
Step 3: l = 9 or l = –5 (discard negative)
Step 4: l = 9, w = 5
Answer: length 9 cm, width 5 cm
Example 7: y = 3x – 1, 2x² – y = 5

(g) Graphs of Polynomials (degree ≤ 3)

Very easy way

Quadratic: parabola (U if a > 0, ∩ if a < 0). Vertex at x = –b/2a.
Cubic: S-shape, can have local max + min.

10 Easy Examples with solutions

Example 1: Sketch y = x² – 4x + 3
Step 1: Vertex x = –b/2a = 4/2 = 2
Step 2: y = 4 – 8 + 3 = –1 (minimum)
Step 3: Opens upward
Answer: Parabola, minimum at (2, –1)
Example 2: Sketch y = –x² + 6x – 5
Step 1: Vertex x = 6/2 = 3
Step 2: y = –9 + 18 – 5 = 4 (maximum)
Step 3: Opens downward
Answer: Parabola, maximum at (3, 4)
Example 3: Profit P = –2x² + 20x – 30. Find maximum
Step 1: Vertex x = –20/(–4) = 5
Step 2: P = –2(25) + 20(5) – 30 = 20
Answer: Maximum profit 20 at x = 5
Example 4: Sketch y = x³ – 3x² – x + 3
Step 1: Roots approx –1, 1, 3
Step 2: Local max at x ≈ 0.3, local min at x ≈ 2.3
Answer: S-shape cubic
Example 5: Area under y = x² from x = 0 to 2
Step 1: ∫x² dx = x³/3
Step 2: [8/3] – 0 = 8/3
Answer: 8/3
Example 6: Sketch y = (x – 1)(x – 2)(x – 3)
Step 1: Roots at 1, 2, 3
Step 2: Positive for x > 3
Answer: Cubic with roots 1,2,3
Example 7: Find minimum of y = 2x² + 4x + 5
Step 1: Vertex x = –4/4 = –1
Step 2: y = 2(1) – 4 + 5 = 3
Answer: Minimum 3 at x = –1
Example 8: Revenue R = 50x – 2x². Find maximum
Step 1: Vertex x = 50/4 = 12.5
Step 2: R = 50(12.5) – 2(156.25) = 312.5
Answer: Maximum 312.5 at x = 12.5
Example 9: Sketch y = x³ – 6x² + 9x
Step 1: Turning points at x = 1 (local max), x = 3 (local min)
Answer: Cubic with local max at x=1, min at x=3
Example 10: Sketch y = x³ – x
Step 1: Roots at –1, 0, 1
Step 2: Local max at x = –1/√3, local min at x = 1/√3
Answer: Cubic with roots –1,0,1
